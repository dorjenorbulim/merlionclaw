# ICF Core Competencies Flashcards

**Study Format:** Spaced Repetition  
**Method:** Review cards in order, mark ones you know vs. need work

---

## Domain A: Foundation

### Card 1.1
**Front:** What is Competency 1.1: Demonstrates Ethical Practice?
**Back:** Understands and consistently applies coaching ethics and standards. Key behaviors: knows ICF Code of Ethics, shares confidentiality terms, maintains confidentiality, recognizes when coaching isn't appropriate, refers to other professionals.
**Domain:** A

---

### Card 1.2
**Front:** What is the ICF Code of Ethics based on?
**Back:** Four pillars: Confidentiality, Conflict of Interest, Professional Conduct, Professional Competence.
**Domain:** A

---

### Card 1.3
**Front:** How does Competency 1.1 apply to AI coaching?
**Back:** Must disclose AI involvement, maintain data security (prefer local processing), define scope boundaries (AI supports reflection, NOT therapy/diagnosis/crisis intervention).
**Domain:** A

---

### Card 1.4
**Front:** What is Competency 1.2: Embodies a Coaching Mindset?
**Back:** Develops open, curious, flexible, client-centered mindset. Acknowledges client is expert in their life, manages own biases, maintains presence, uses reflection for self-awareness.
**Domain:** A

---

### Card 1.5
**Front:** Can AI "embody a coaching mindset"?
**Back:** No. AI can prep context, but presence and mindset are human-only. AI tracks patterns; human maintains curiosity.
**Domain:** A

---

## Domain B: Co-Creating the Relationship

### Card 2.1
**Front:** What is Competency 2.1: Establishes and Maintains Agreements?
**Back:** Partners with client to create clear agreements about relationship, process, plans, goals. Reaches agreement on what is appropriate in coaching.
**Domain:** B

---

### Card 2.2
**Front:** What should an AI coaching agreement include?
**Back:** Disclosure of AI use, data storage/deletion policy, what AI does vs. human coach, client right to request data deletion.
**Domain:** B

---

### Card 2.3
**Front:** What is Competency 2.2: Cultivates Trust and Safety?
**Back:** Partners with client to create safe, supportive environment. Seeks to understand context, shows appropriate vulnerability, respects client identity and decisions.
**Domain:** B

---

### Card 2.4
**Front:** How does AI affect trust and safety?
**Back:** Technology shouldn't feel intrusive. Client must feel safe knowing data is secure. AI enhances, not replaces, human connection.
**Domain:** B

---

### Card 2.5
**Front:** What is Competency 2.3: Maintains Presence?
**Back:** Is fully conscious, present, open, flexible, grounded. Remains focused, observant, empathetic, responsive. Manages emotions to stay present.
**Domain:** B

---

### Card 2.6
**Front:** Can AI "maintain presence"?
**Back:** No. Presence is a core human competency. AI can support prep/context, but being present with the client is human-only.
**Domain:** B

---

## Domain C: Communicating Effectively

### Card 3.1
**Front:** What is Competency 3.1: Listens Actively?
**Back:** Focuses on what client is/is not saying. Considers context, reflects/summarizes, recognizes emotions, allows silence.
**Domain:** C

---

### Card 3.2
**Front:** What is Competency 3.2: Evokes Awareness?
**Back:** Facilitates client insight through powerful questioning, silence, metaphor. Asks questions beyond current thinking. Demonstrates curiosity about client's perspective.
**Domain:** C

---

### Card 3.3
**Front:** Critical AI boundary: Can AI interpret client emotions?
**Back:** NO. AI should NOT interpret emotional states or suggest what client "really means." This replaces client self-discovery — coaching's core value.
**Domain:** C

---

### Card 3.4
**Front:** What is Competency 3.3: Reflects and Interprets?
**Back:** Shares observations, insights to enhance awareness. Clearly states when sharing intuition. Invites client to accept, reject, or revise.
**Domain:** C

---

### Card 3.5
**Front:** AI role in reflection?
**Back:** AI-generated interpretations must be labeled. Client has authority to accept/reject. Human coach validates/reframes AI observations.
**Domain:** C

---

## Domain D: Cultivating Learning and Growth

### Card 4.1
**Front:** What is Competency 4.1: Facilitates Client Growth?
**Back:** Partners with client to transform learning/insight into action. Recognizes progress, sets goals/actions/accountability, celebrates success.
**Domain:** D

---

### Card 4.2
**Front:** AI applications for facilitating growth?
**Back:** Tracks goals/progress over time, sends accountability reminders, documents action items, flags obstacle patterns.
**Domain:** D

---

### Card 4.3
**Front:** What is Competency 4.2: Partners with Client in Goal-Setting?
**Back:** Supports client in creating clear, achievable goals. Ensures goals are client-generated (not coach-imposed), aligned with values, broken into steps.
**Domain:** D

---

### Card 4.4
**Front:** AI role in goal-setting?
**Back:** Stores/organizes goals, tracks milestones, prompts reflection on goal alignment. Does NOT set goals for client.
**Domain:** D

## Ethics & Scope

### Card E.1
**Front:** What is the ICF scope boundary for coaches?
**Back:** Coaching focuses on forward movement, client-generated goals, non-directive support. NOT: therapy (healing past), diagnosis, treatment, advice-giving.
**Domain:** Ethics

---

### Card E.2
**Front:** When must you refer to a therapist?
**Back:** Mental health concerns, trauma, crisis/safety concerns, deep emotional healing needs, when client needs treatment/diagnosis.
**Domain:** Ethics

---

### Card E.3
**Front:** Client mentions suicidal ideation. What do you do?
**Back:** STOP coaching immediately. Refer to mental health professional/crisis resources. Document if appropriate. Follow up. This is beyond coaching scope.
**Domain:** Ethics

---

### Card E.4
**Front:** What is "confidentiality" in ICF ethics?
**Back:** Information shared in coaching is private. Exceptions: legal requirements, imminent harm, client permission. Includes secure data storage.
**Domain:** Ethics

---

### Card E.5
**Front:** Client requests deletion of all coaching data. What do you do?
**Back:** Confirm request, comply promptly (client owns data), document deletion, review policies. ICF + Singapore PDPA support this right.
**Domain:** Ethics

---

### Card E.6
**Front:** What is "conflict of interest" in ICF ethics?
**Back:** Avoid situations where personal interests conflict with client welfare. Disclose potential conflicts. Withdraw if can't be managed.
**Domain:** Ethics

---

### Card E.7
**Front:** What does "professional competence" mean?
**Back:** Practice within scope of training. Refer when client needs exceed competence. Continue professional development. Maintain current knowledge.
**Domain:** Ethics

## AI Coaching Ethics

### Card A.1
**Front:** Before using AI with clients, what must you do?
**Back:** Disclose AI involvement, explain what AI does/doesn't do, have clear data policy, use local processing when possible, define AI scope.
**Domain:** AI Ethics

---

### Card A.2
**Front:** During AI-assisted sessions, what must be true?
**Back:** Human maintains presence. AI observations labeled. Client can reject AI suggestions. Human validates/reframes AI output. Emotional interpretation stays human-led.
**Domain:** AI Ethics

---

### Card A.3
**Front:** AI coaching red flags?
**Back:** AI interpreting client emotions, storing data without clear policy, not disclosing AI use, AI making suggestions that cross into advice-giving, AI replacing client self-discovery.
**Domain:** AI Ethics

---

### Card A.4
**Front:** What is the "client owns data" principle?
**Back:** Client has right to: know how data is stored, request deletion, understand AI's role, reject AI suggestions, own the meaning-making process.
**Domain:** AI Ethics

---

### Card A.5
**Front:** Sample AI coaching disclosure language?
**Back:** "I use AI tools to support session prep, follow-up notes, accountability tracking. All data is processed locally. You may request deletion anytime. You will always know when AI is involved."
**Domain:** AI Ethics

## Quick Reference

### Card Q.1
**Front:** 4 Domains of ICF Core Competencies?
**Back:** A. Foundation (Ethics/Mindset), B. Co-Creating Relationship (Trust), C. Communicating Effectively (Listening), D. Cultivating Learning and Growth (Action).
**Domain:** Overview

---

### Card Q.2
**Front:** Total competencies across all domains?
**Back:** 46 competencies (2025 model, evolved from 8 domains to 4).
**Domain:** Overview

---

### Card Q.3
**Front:** When in doubt, what guides ICF decision-making?
**Back:** Protect client welfare first. Ethics over expediency. When uncertain, refer to scope-appropriate professional.
**Domain:** Overview

---

### Card Q.4
**Front:** Difference between coaching and mentoring?
**Back:** Coaching: client-generated, non-directive, forward-focused. Mentoring: sharing experience, advice-giving, guidance from mentor's expertise.
**Domain:** Overview

---

### Card Q.5
**Front:** Difference between coaching and therapy?
**Back:** Coaching: forward movement, goals, non-clinical. Therapy: healing past, diagnosis, treatment, clinical intervention.
**Domain:** Overview

## How to Use These Flashcards

### Method: Spaced Repetition
1. **Review 10-20 cards per day**
2. **Sort into piles:**
   - Know it cold → review less often
   - Getting there → review in 2 days
   - Need work → review tomorrow
3. **Focus on AI-specific cards** — this is your edge
4. **Practice scenarios** — use cards to quiz yourself on "what would you do?"

### Daily Practice
- Morning: 10 new cards
- Evening: Review missed cards from yesterday
- Weekend: Full review of all "need work" cards
