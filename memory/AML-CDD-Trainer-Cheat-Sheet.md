## 📋 The 4 CDD Triggers (Memorize)

1. **New business relationship** (account opening, first loan)
2. **Transactions ≥ S$20,000** (single or linked)
3. **Suspicion of ML/TF/PF** (regardless of amount)
4. **Doubt about identity** (forged docs, mismatched info)

---

## 📋 The 5 CDD Elements (Memorize)

1. **Customer Identity** (name, reg number, address, NRIC/passport)
2. **Beneficial Ownership** (humans with 25%+ or control)
3. **Purpose & Nature of Business** (what they actually do)
4. **Source of Wealth** (how they got rich overall)
5. **Source of Funds** (where THIS money came from)

**Key distinction:** SoW = how they accumulated wealth. SoF = where this transaction's money originated. Different questions. Both matter.

## 🚩 Top 3 Red Flags by Product

### Corporate Accounts
1. Company < 3 months old + large expected turnover
2. Complex ownership with no business reason
3. Virtual office + foreign directors + no Singapore presence

### Remittances
1. Missing originator information (MAS requirement)
2. Structuring (below S$20K threshold)
3. Rapid movement (in/out 24–48 hours)

### Loans
1. Early repayment without explanation
2. Proceeds to unrelated third parties
3. Customer indifferent to interest rates

### Insurance
1. Single-premium, large lump sum
2. Early surrender (within 6–12 months)
3. Surrender proceeds to third parties

## ⚠️ When to Escalate (8 Triggers)

- [ ] Any HIGH-risk customer rating
- [ ] 2+ red flags identified
- [ ] Customer refuses to provide CDD information
- [ ] Inconsistencies in customer explanations
- [ ] Suspicion of ML/TF/PF (regardless of amount)
- [ ] PEP identified (customer or BO)
- [ ] High-risk jurisdiction connection
- [ ] Unusual transaction patterns

**Escalation Pathway:**
1. Document concerns (email + system notes)
2. Notify branch manager
3. Submit to AML Compliance Team via [system name]
4. **DO NOT tip off the customer**

- [ ] Customer identity verified (originals sighted)
- [ ] BO chart completed and attached

- [ ] Purpose/nature of business documented
- [ ] Expected transaction patterns recorded
- [ ] SoW verified and documented
- [ ] SoF verified and documented

- [ ] Your name/ID and date recorded

**Retention:** Minimum 5 years (MAS requirement)

## 🎤 Delivery Tips

### Voice & Presence
- **Pace:** Slow down. Breathe. Nerves speed you up.
- **Volume:** Project to back of room.
- **Eye contact:** Scan room. Don't fixate on slides.
- **Pauses:** Use silence. Let key points sink in (3 seconds).

### Handling Questions
- **If you know:** Answer clearly.
- **If you don't know:** "Good question. Let me check with AML Compliance and get back to you." Document it. Follow up within 24 hours.
- **If challenging:** Stay calm. "I understand your concern. Here's the regulatory requirement..."

### Time Management
- **Watch the clock:** Keep timer visible.
- **If running long:** Skip detailed quiz explanations.
- **If running short:** Expand on case studies.
- **Never rush closing:** Key takeaways matter.

### Hybrid-Specific
- **Acknowledge virtual:** "For those on Zoom..."
- **Repeat questions:** "Someone on Zoom asked..."
- **Check tech:** "Can everyone see/hear?"
- **Assign co-host:** Monitor Zoom chat while you present.

## 🪷 Buddhist-Informed Framing (Optional)

Use these if they resonate with your style:

**Opening:**
> "In Buddhist practice, there's 'clear seeing' — seeing things as they are, not as we wish them to be. CDD is clear seeing. You're not being suspicious. You're being clear."

**On Red Flags:**
> "Red flags aren't accusations. They're invitations to look deeper. When you see a red flag, don't react with fear or judgment. Respond with curiosity. Ask the question. Get the answer. Document it."

**On Escalation:**
> "Escalation is not failure. It's wisdom. In the Buddhist tradition, there's no shame in asking for help. The shame is in pretending you know when you don't."

**On Documentation:**
> "Documentation is mindfulness. Being present with what you did, why you did it, and what you found. Future-you — and the MAS examiner — will thank present-you for writing it down."

**Closing:**
> "You're not compliance officers. You're relationship managers. But you're also the first line of defense for your clients, your institution, and yourself. Protect all three. With clarity. With wisdom. With care."
