# ICF Techniques: Working with Resistant or "Know-It-All" Coachees

**Created:** April 5, 2026  
**Context:** Supporting coaches working with coachees who defend positions, appear certain, and resist deep inquiry

---

## 🎯 Understanding the Pattern

**What you're seeing:**
- Coachee has ready answers for every question
- Unwilling to say "I don't know"
- Worldview may be outdated or biased (but unexamined)
- Conversation drifts toward casual catch-up vs. transformative coaching
- Defensiveness masked as confidence

**What's underneath:**
- Fear of vulnerability ("not knowing" = weakness)
- Ego protection (certainty = safety)
- Possible mismatch: using coaching for validation vs. growth
- Habitual intellectualizing vs. feeling/reflecting

---

## 🪷 ICF Core Competencies in Play

### 1. **Active Listening (Domain 2)**
*Listen for what's NOT being said.*

**Technique:** Notice the gap between certainty and curiosity.
- When coachee answers quickly → pause before next question
- Listen for rigidity in tone, not just content
- Hear the fear beneath the confidence

**Example:**
> Coachee: "I already know how to handle this."
> Coach: *[silent pause, 3-5 seconds]* "What's it like to already know?"

---

### 2. **Powerful Questioning (Competency 7)**
*Questions that invite inquiry, not defense.*

**Avoid:**
- ❌ "Have you considered...?" (implies they haven't)
- ❌ "What if you tried...?" (leading toward your answer)
- ❌ "Why do you think that?" (can feel confrontational)

**Try Instead:**
- ✅ "What's alive in this for you right now?"
- ✅ "If you didn't already know the answer, what might you be curious about?"
- ✅ "What would it be like to not have an answer here?"
- ✅ "What part of this feels certain? What part might have more to explore?"
- ✅ "If a trusted friend saw this situation, what might they notice that you haven't?"

**The "One Question at a Time" Rule:**
- Ask ONE question, then wait
- Resist the urge to clarify or add follow-ups
- Let silence do the work (7-10 seconds is okay)

---

### 3. **Evoking Awareness (Domain 3)**
*Help them see their own blind spots without telling them.*

**Technique: Reflect the Pattern**
> "I'm noticing that you have clear answers for each question I ask. I'm curious—what would it be like if there was something you *didn't* have an answer for yet?"

**Technique: Invite Meta-Awareness**
> "As you share all these insights, what's your experience of our conversation right now? Is this moving you toward something new, or confirming what you already know?"

**Technique: The "Both/And" Frame**
> "It sounds like you have a lot of wisdom here. AND I'm wondering if there's a place where that wisdom might be keeping you from seeing something fresh."

---

### 4. **Cultivating Trust & Safety (Competency 4)**
*Make "I don't know" safe enough to say.*

**Explicit Permission:**
> "In this space, not knowing is welcome. In fact, some of the best coaching happens in the uncertainty. You don't need to have answers here."

**Model Vulnerability:**
> "I don't have an answer for what you should do next. And I'm okay with that. Are you?"

**Reframe Uncertainty as Strength:**
> "The fact that you're willing to sit with this question—even without an answer—shows real courage."

---

### 5. **Maintaining Presence (Domain 2)**
*Stay centered when they pull away from depth.*

**When you feel frustration:**
- Notice it (don't act on it)
- Breathe. Return to curiosity.
- Ask yourself: "What is this coachee needing right now?"

**When conversation goes surface-level:**
- Don't pull harder. Invite gently.
- "We could talk about this as a catch-up. Or we could go deeper. What would serve you best today?"

---

## 🛠️ Specific Techniques for "Know-It-All" Coachees

### **Technique 1: The Curiosity Challenge**
> "I'm curious: If you were to approach this topic as if you knew *nothing* about it—beginner's mind—what might you notice that you haven't before?"

*(Buddhist lens: Shoshin, 初心)*

---

### **Technique 2: The Certainty Audit**
> "On a scale of 1-10, how certain are you that this belief/approach is serving you? What would it take to move it one point lower?"

---

### **Technique 3: The Alternative Perspective**
> "If someone who loved you AND disagreed with you was here, what would they say about this situation?"

---

### **Technique 4: The Body Check**
> "As you say that with such confidence, what do you notice in your body? Is there any tension, tightness, or sensation?"

*(Bypasses the intellectual mind)*

---

### **Technique 5: The Coaching Agreement Reset**
> "I want to check in about how we're using this time together. Are you looking for a sounding board to think out loud, or are you seeking transformation and new awareness? Both are valid—I want to make sure I'm serving you well."

## 🚩 When to Consider Renegotiating the Relationship

**Signs this may not be coaching:**
- Coachee consistently rejects invitations to depth
- Sessions feel like casual conversation every time
- No accountability or goal progress over multiple sessions
- You're doing more work than the coachee
- Coachee uses sessions for validation, not growth

**Options:**
1. **Direct conversation:** "I'm noticing a pattern. Let's talk about what you want from these sessions."
2. **Reframe as mentorship/peer support:** Release the coaching frame if it's not serving
3. **End the coaching relationship:** "I don't think I'm the right coach for where you are right now."

## 🪷 Buddhist Lens on Working with Certainty

**Right Speech (Samma Vaca):**
- Is the coachee speaking truthfully, or defending ego?
- Can you respond with kindness, not frustration?

**Beginner's Mind (Shoshin):**
- "In the beginner's mind there are many possibilities. In the expert's mind there are few." — Shunryu Suzuki
- Invite this as practice, not criticism

**Compassion for Defensiveness:**
- Certainty often masks fear
- Meet rigidity with warmth, not challenge
- The coachee is protecting something vulnerable

## 📝 Practice Exercises for Michael

### **Before Next Session:**
1. Review ICF Core Competencies on Evoking Awareness
2. Write 3-5 powerful questions you could use with Tze Wei
3. Set intention: "I will stay curious, not frustrated"

### **During Session:**
1. Notice when you feel the pull to "break through"
2. Pause. Breathe. Return to presence.
3. Try ONE technique from above (don't overload)

### **After Session:**
1. Document what worked/didn't work
2. Note your own emotional state
3. Ask: "Did I serve this coachee well today?"
