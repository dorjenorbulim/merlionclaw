## The 4 AI Leadership Archetypes

| Archetype | Description | % of Leaders (Estimated) | Coaching Need |
|-----------|-------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| **The Resister** | "AI won't affect my industry." Avoids AI, dismisses its impact, clings to old methods. | 20% | High — needs mindset shift before skills |
| **The Clinger** | "I need to control everything AI touches." Over-involved, anxious, micromanages AI implementation. | 35% | High — needs non-attachment practice |
| **The Experimenter** | "Let's try this AI tool!" Enthusiastic but scattered, lacks strategy, pilots without purpose. | 30% | Medium — needs focus + wisdom framework |
| **The Wise Leader** | "AI reveals who I really am." Balanced, clear, uses AI without clinging or resisting. | 15% | Low — needs peer cohort, not 1:1 coaching |

## The 7 Questions

### Question 1: Your AI Beliefs
**Question:** "When you think about AI's impact on your role, what's your gut reaction?"

**Options:**
- A) "Honestly, I don't think it'll affect my industry much." *(Resister)*
- B) "I'm worried about losing control over how work gets done." *(Clinger)*
- C) "Excited! We're piloting several tools right now." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "It's revealing what work I'm really here to do." *(Wise Leader)*

**Follow-up (optional text):** "Tell me more about what's behind that reaction."

---

### Question 2: Your AI Knowledge
**Question:** "How would you describe your current relationship with AI tools?"

**Options:**
- A) "I haven't really used them. Not sure I need to." *(Resister)*
- B) "I use them, but I review every output carefully. Can't trust AI completely." *(Clinger)*
- C) "I'm constantly testing new tools. Some stick, some don't." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "I use AI strategically for specific tasks. I know its limits and mine." *(Wise Leader)*

---

### Question 3: Your Team's AI Adoption
**Question:** "How is your team approaching AI right now?"

**Options:**
- A) "We haven't discussed it. It hasn't come up." *(Resister)*
- B) "I've set strict guidelines on what they can and can't use." *(Clinger)*
- C) "Everyone's experimenting. It's a bit chaotic but energetic." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "We've had honest conversations about what AI handles and what humans own." *(Wise Leader)*

---

### Question 4: Your Biggest AI Concern
**Question:** "What keeps you up at night about AI?"

**Options:**
- A) "Nothing, really. I think the hype is overblown." *(Resister)*
- B) "That we'll make a mistake AI could have prevented. Or that AI will make mistakes we can't catch." *(Clinger)*
- C) "That we'll miss out on something game-changing by moving too slow." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "That my team will lose their sense of purpose if I don't frame this wisely." *(Wise Leader)*

---

### Question 5: Your Decision-Making Style
**Question:** "When facing AI-related decisions, how do you typically proceed?"

**Options:**
- A) "I defer to my team or IT. This isn't really my domain." *(Resister)*
- B) "I need to be involved in every decision. Too much risk otherwise." *(Clinger)*
- C) "I say yes to most pilots. We can always course-correct later." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "I pause, reflect on what matters most, then decide from clarity." *(Wise Leader)*

---

### Question 6: Your Energy Level
**Question:** "How do you feel after a day of managing AI-related changes?"

**Options:**
- A) "Fine. Nothing's really changed." *(Resister)*
- B) "Exhausted. There's so much to monitor and control." *(Clinger)*
- C) "Energized but scattered. Lots of moving pieces." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "Centered. Change is constant; I've made peace with that." *(Wise Leader)*

---

### Question 7: Your Leadership Identity
**Question:** "Complete this sentence: 'My value as a leader comes from...'"

**Options:**
- A) "My experience and track record. That's what got me here." *(Resister)*
- B) "My attention to detail and quality control. Someone has to ensure standards." *(Clinger)*
- C) "My ability to innovate and stay ahead of trends. I'm an early adopter." *(Experimenter)*
- D) "My wisdom, judgment, and ability to develop people. That can't be automated." *(Wise Leader)*

---

## Scoring Methodology

**Mostly A's = The Resister**  
**Mostly B's = The Clinger**  
**Mostly C's = The Experimenter**  
**Mostly D's = The Wise Leader**  

**Mixed Results:** If no clear majority, assign based on Questions 1, 4, and 7 (belief, concern, identity are most predictive).

**Tiebreaker:** Question 7 (leadership identity) is the strongest predictor of archetype.

### Result 1: The Resister

**Headline:** "You're betting AI won't affect you. Are you sure?"

**Description:**
You haven't felt AI's impact yet — or you've convinced yourself it won't touch your role. There's comfort in this position. But comfort can become complacency.

The leaders I coach who started as Resistors had one thing in common: they didn't resist because they lacked capability. They resisted because they were clinging to an identity that was already changing.

**Your Strengths:**
- You have deep experience and wisdom
- You're not easily swayed by hype
- You protect your team from unnecessary disruption

**Your Blind Spots:**
- You may be dismissing AI's relevance prematurely
- Your team might be experimenting without your awareness
- "Waiting it out" rarely works in rapid change

**Recommended Next Steps:**
1. Have one honest conversation with your team about where they're using AI (you might be surprised)
2. Try one AI tool for a task you currently do manually (experience beats theory)
3. Ask yourself: "What am I protecting by not engaging with this?"

### Result 2: The Clinger

**Headline:** "You're carrying the weight of AI on your shoulders. You don't have to."

**Description:**
You care deeply about getting AI right. That's admirable. But your need for control is exhausting you — and possibly slowing your team down.

In Buddhist terms, you're clinging. Not to the old way, but to the idea that you can control the new way. The irony? Control is the one thing AI makes impossible.

**Your Strengths:**
- You protect your team from AI mistakes
- You maintain quality standards
- You think through risks carefully

**Your Blind Spots:**
- You're exhausted from monitoring everything
- Your team may feel micromanaged
- You're clinging to control in a domain that requires non-attachment

**Recommended Next Steps:**
1. Identify 3 decisions you can delegate to your team (with AI support)
2. Practice "good enough" review on one AI output this week (not perfect, just functional)
3. Ask yourself: "What am I afraid will happen if I let go?"

**Coaching Fit:** High. Non-attachment practice is core to my coaching approach. We can work on releasing control without losing accountability. [Book a free 30-min chemistry call]

**Resources:**

- Framework: "The AI Leadership Mindset Shift" (downloadable PDF)

### Result 3: The Experimenter

**Headline:** "You're all in on AI. But is there wisdom beneath the enthusiasm?"

**Description:**
You're excited about AI — maybe more excited than anyone on your team. You're piloting tools, attending webinars, pushing for adoption. That energy is valuable. But enthusiasm without strategy creates chaos.

The question isn't "What AI tools should we try?" The question is "What work are we trying to transform, and why?"

**Your Strengths:**
- You're not afraid of change
- You encourage innovation
- You stay ahead of trends

**Your Blind Spots:**
- You may be piloting without clear outcomes
- Your team might feel whiplash from constant tool changes
- You're avoiding the deeper question: "What is my role when AI handles so much?"

**Recommended Next Steps:**
1. Pause new pilots for 2 weeks. Evaluate what's already in motion.
2. For each tool, ask: "What human work does this free up? Where is that energy going?"
3. Define your AI leadership philosophy in one paragraph (I can help with this)

**Coaching Fit:** Medium-High. You'd benefit from a wisdom framework to ground your enthusiasm. Let's talk. [Book a free 30-min chemistry call]

### Result 4: The Wise Leader

**Headline:** "You've found balance. Now it's time to deepen and share."

**Description:**
You're navigating AI with clarity, presence, and wisdom. You're not clinging, not resisting, not scattering. You're leading.

But here's the challenge for Wise Leaders: isolation. You might be the only person in your organization thinking this way. That's lonely. And it limits your impact.

**Your Strengths:**
- You see AI clearly, without fear or hype
- You hold change lightly while staying fully engaged
- Your team trusts your judgment

**Your Growth Edge:**
- You may be underestimating how rare your perspective is
- You could be scaling your wisdom beyond your immediate team
- You might benefit from peer community at your level

**Recommended Next Steps:**
1. Document your AI leadership philosophy (share it internally or publicly)
2. Mentor 1–2 leaders who are struggling with AI transition
3. Consider joining a peer cohort of leaders at your level (confidential, high-trust)

**Coaching Fit:** Medium. You don't need 1:1 coaching as much as peer community. My AI Leadership Peer Cohort might be a fit. [Learn more]
