## SECTION 1: RED FLAG IDENTIFICATION

### 1.1 What is a Red Flag?

A red flag is a **warning sign** that indicates potential money laundering, terrorism financing, or proliferation financing risk.

**Important:** A red flag is NOT proof of wrongdoing. It's an invitation to look deeper.

---

### 1.2 Red Flags by Product Type

#### Corporate Cash Accounts

| Red Flag | Why It Matters | What to Ask |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| ⚠ Company incorporated < 3 months ago | New companies have no track record | "Can you show me your business pipeline? Contracts? Invoices?" |
| ⚠ Expected turnover seems unrealistic | S$5M/month for a new F&B company? | "Help me understand how you'll achieve this volume." |
| ⚠ Registered address is residential/virtual office | May indicate shell company | "Where do you actually operate from? Can I see utility bills?" |
| ⚠ Complex ownership with no business reason | 5 layers across 3 jurisdictions? | "What's the business purpose of this structure?" |
| ⚠ Reluctance to provide beneficial ownership | Who are we really dealing with? | "I need to identify all humans with 25%+ ownership or control." |
| ⚠ Multiple companies with same directors/addresses | Potential mule accounts | "What's the relationship between these companies?" |
| ⚠ Business activity doesn't match profile | Manufacturing company expecting S$10M FX/month | "Walk me through your typical transaction flow." |

#### Remittances & Wire Transfers

| Red Flag | Why It Matters | What to Ask |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| ⚠ Missing/incomplete originator information | MAS requirement for cross-border transfers | "I need full originator details before I can process this." |
| ⚠ Frequent transfers to high-risk jurisdictions | FATF grey/black list countries | "What's the business purpose of transfers to [jurisdiction]?" |
| ⚠ Round-dollar amounts repeatedly | Exactly S$50,000 each time? | "I notice a pattern. Can you help me understand?" |
| ⚠ Transfers inconsistent with business | Retail company sending to offshore gambling jurisdiction | "How does this relate to your core business?" |
| ⚠ Structuring (multiple transfers just below S$20K) | Avoiding CDD thresholds | "These transfers together exceed S$20K. I need to do CDD." |
| ⚠ Rapid movement (funds in, out within 24–48 hours) | Layering — classic laundering technique | "Why the urgency? Can the funds stay in the account longer?" |
| ⚠ Third-party payments with no clear relationship | Who are we really dealing with? | "What's your relationship with the sender/receiver?" |

#### Loans

| Red Flag | Why It Matters | What to Ask |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| ⚠ Early repayment without explanation | Borrowing at 5% then repaying immediately? | "What's driving the early repayment?" |
| ⚠ Loan proceeds to unrelated third parties | Why not to the borrower? | "What's the relationship with the third party?" |
| ⚠ Collateral source unclear | Where did this asset come from? | "Can you document how you acquired this collateral?" |
| ⚠ Customer indifferent to interest rates | Legitimate borrowers care about cost | "You seem unconcerned about the rate. Can you help me understand?" |
| ⚠ Loan purpose vague ("business expansion") | No specifics = no verification possible | "Can you be more specific? Which business? What expansion?" |
| ⚠ Guarantor relationship unclear | Why is this person/company guaranteeing? | "What's the relationship between borrower and guarantor?" |
| ⚠ Disbursement to multiple accounts/jurisdictions | Layering risk | "Why multiple accounts? What's the business need?" |

| Red Flag | Why It Matters | What to Ask |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| ⚠ Single-premium policies with large lump sums | S$100K+ paid upfront | "Can you explain the source of these funds?" |

## SECTION 2: RISK RATING METHODOLOGY

### 2.1 Why Risk Rating Matters

Risk rating determines:
- **Level of CDD required** (standard vs. enhanced)
- **Frequency of reviews** (annual vs. quarterly)
- **Approval level needed** (RM vs. senior management)
- **Monitoring intensity** (standard vs. enhanced transaction monitoring)

**Key Principle:** Better to over-flag than under-flag. MAS penalties are for under-flagging, never for over-flagging.

---

### 2.2 Risk Rating Matrix

| Factor | LOW Risk | MEDIUM Risk | HIGH Risk |
|--------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Customer Type** | Singapore government entities, SGX-listed companies | Private Singapore companies (normal business) | PEPs, shell companies, cash-intensive businesses |
| **Jurisdiction** | Singapore, FATF white-list countries | Standard jurisdictions | FATF grey/black list, high-risk jurisdictions |
| **Product/Service** | Savings accounts, standard corporate accounts | Standard loans, insurance policies | Private banking, correspondent banking, remittances |
| **Transaction Pattern** | Predictable, matches business profile | Some variation, explainable | Unusual, inconsistent with profile, rapid movement |
| **Ownership Structure** | Simple, transparent | Some complexity, business reason clear | Complex, no clear business reason, offshore layers |
| **Delivery Channel** | Face-to-face, verified in person | Mixed (some remote, verified) | Non-face-to-face, remote onboarding |

---

### 2.3 Risk Rating Decision Tree

```
START: New Customer Application
       │
       ▼
Is customer a Singapore government entity or SGX-listed company?
       │
       ├── YES ──→ Rate: LOW (Standard CDD)
       │
       └── NO
            │
            ▼
Are any HIGH-risk factors present?
(PEP, high-risk jurisdiction, complex ownership,
cash-intensive, dual-use goods, unusual patterns)
       │
       ├── YES ──→ Rate: HIGH (Enhanced Due Diligence Required)
       │              │
       │              ├── Senior management approval
       │              ├── Deeper SoW/SoF verification
       │              ├── More frequent reviews (quarterly)
       │              └── Enhanced transaction monitoring
       │
       └── NO
            │
            ▼
Rate: MEDIUM (Standard CDD + Periodic Review)
       │
       ├── Annual review
       ├── Standard monitoring
       └── RM-level approval
```

---

### 2.4 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Triggers

**When EDD is REQUIRED:**

- [ ] Customer or beneficial owner is a PEP (Politically Exposed Person)
- [ ] Customer is from a FATF grey/black-list jurisdiction
- [ ] Complex ownership structures with no clear business reason
- [ ] Cash-intensive businesses (casinos, money changers, precious metals)
- [ ] Dual-use goods traders (civilian + military applications) — **NEW 2025**
- [ ] Non-face-to-face onboarding without additional verification
- [ ] Unusual transaction patterns inconsistent with business profile
- [ ] Third-party funding with unclear relationship

**EDD Requirements:**

| Requirement | What You Must Do |
|-------------|------------------|
| Senior Management Approval | Obtain written approval before onboarding |
| Source of Wealth Verification | Verify through independent sources (not just customer declaration) |
| Source of Funds Verification | Document where funds for THIS transaction originate |
| Enhanced Monitoring | Review transactions quarterly (not annually) |
| Additional Documentation | Request contracts, invoices, bank statements, tax returns |
| Ongoing Review | Re-assess risk rating every 6–12 months (not 2–3 years) |

## SECTION 3: DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS

### 3.1 The Golden Rule

> **"If it's not documented, it didn't happen."**

MAS examiners do not take your word for it. They look at the system. If it's not in the system, it doesn't exist.

---

### 3.2 What Must Be Documented

#### For Every Customer Onboarding

| Item | Where to Document | Example |
|------|-------------------|---------|
| **Customer Identity** | CRM/KYC system | "Verified NRIC S1234567A via original sighted on 15 Mar 2026" |
| **Beneficial Ownership** | CRM/KYC system + attachment | "BO chart attached. 2 individuals: John Tan (50%), Mary Lim (50%). Verified via ACRA search." |
| **Purpose & Nature of Business** | CRM/KYC system | "IT consulting services. Primary clients: Singapore SMEs. Expected 10–15 transactions/month, avg S$20K each." |
| **Source of Wealth** | CRM/KYC system | "SoW: Business profits from IT consulting (10 years). Verified via audited financial statements 2023–2025." |
| **Source of Funds** | CRM/KYC system | "SoF: Initial capital S$500K from DBS account XXXXXX. Verified via bank statement dated 10 Mar 2026." |
| **Risk Rating Rationale** | CRM/KYC system | "Rated MEDIUM: Established SG company, transparent ownership, normal business. No high-risk factors identified." |
| **Red Flags Identified** | CRM/KYC system | "Red flag: New company (<3 months). Mitigation: Requested business plan + contracts. Satisfied with explanation." |
| **Escalation Notes** (if applicable) | CRM/KYC system + email | "Escalated to AML team on 20 Mar 2026. Approval received from [name] on 22 Mar 2026. See email attachment." |

---

### 3.3 Documentation Checklist

**Before Submitting Application:**

- [ ] Customer identity verified (original documents sighted)
- [ ] Beneficial ownership chart completed and attached
- [ ] All beneficial owners identified (25%+ ownership or control)
- [ ] Purpose and nature of business documented
- [ ] Expected transaction patterns recorded
- [ ] Source of Wealth verified and documented
- [ ] Source of Funds verified and documented
- [ ] Risk rating assigned with rationale
- [ ] Red flags identified and addressed (or escalated)
- [ ] All documents uploaded to system
- [ ] Your name/ID and date recorded

**If HIGH Risk (EDD Required):**

- [ ] Senior management approval obtained
- [ ] Enhanced SoW/SoF verification completed
- [ ] Additional documentation collected (contracts, invoices, tax returns)
- [ ] AML Compliance Team notified
- [ ] Enhanced monitoring flag set in system

---

### 3.4 Documentation Examples

#### Example 1: Good Documentation

```

- Clear, concise, complete
- All 5 CDD elements documented

- Next review date set

- No detail on what documents

```

│  WHAT INFORMATION MUST YOU COLLECT?                         │
│                                                             │
│  1. Customer Identity                                       │
│     • Name, registration number, registered address         │
│     • For individuals: NRIC/passport, DOB, nationality      │
│                                                             │
│  2. Beneficial Ownership (BO)                               │
│     • Who ultimately owns or controls?                      │
│     • Typically 25%+ ownership or significant control       │
│     • Must identify human beings, not companies             │
│                                                             │
│  3. Purpose & Nature of Business                            │
│     • What does this company actually do?                   │
│     • Expected transaction patterns                         │
│                                                             │
│  4. Source of Wealth (SoW)                                  │
│     • How did the customer accumulate their wealth?         │
│     • Business profits, inheritance, investments, etc.      │
│                                                             │
│  5. Source of Funds (SoF)                                   │
│     • Where is the money for THIS transaction coming from?  │
│     • Bank account, sale of asset, loan, etc.               │
│                                                             │
│  REMEMBER: SoW = how they got rich. SoF = where this money │
│            came from. Different questions. Both matter.     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

2. No change in beneficial ownership declared (but control changed)
3. Customer unusually eager to complete quickly
4. Nephew's background not verified

---

#### Scenario 3: The Structuring Pattern

**Correct Answers (minimum 3):**
1. Deposits just below S$20,000 threshold (structuring)
2. Pattern change (no history of large cash deposits)
3. Retail business with suddenly high cash volume (inconsistent with profile)
4. Customer evasive when asked for sales records
5. 8 deposits in 10 days (unusual frequency)
