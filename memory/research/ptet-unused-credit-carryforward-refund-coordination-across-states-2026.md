# PTET Unused Credit Carryforward and Refund Coordination Across States 2026

**Research Date:** April 6, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~720 words

---

## Executive Summary

When pass-through entities elect to pay state-level Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET), owners receive refundable or nonrefundable credits on their individual returns. A critical planning gap emerges when PTET credits exceed owner-level tax liability: **state treatment varies dramatically**. Some states allow multi-year carryforwards; others offer refunds; many provide neither. For multi-state coaching practices with owners residing across different jurisdictions, coordinating unused PTET credits requires systematic tracking and state-specific strategies to avoid leaving tax benefits on the table.

---

## The Core Problem: Excess PTET Credits

PTET credits can exceed individual tax liability when:

- **Entity operates in high-tax states** (CA, NY, NJ) but owners reside in low/no-tax states
- **Owner has other credits** (R&D, solar, low-income housing) that reduce tax below PTET credit amount
- **Owner moves mid-year** from high-tax to low-tax state after PTET election
- **Multi-state entities** make PTET elections in several states, creating stacked credits in one resident state
- **Practice income drops** after PTET payment is made (economic downturn, client loss)

Without proper planning, excess credits expire worthless in nonrefundable states with no carryforward provisions.

## State-by-State Treatment (2026 Rules)

### **Refundable Credit States** (Best Case)

These states refund excess credits in cash:

- **New York**: Fully refundable; excess credits generate cash refunds on IT-606
- **New Jersey**: Refundable for tax years 2021-2026 (scheduled to sunset)
- **Massachusetts**: Refundable with limitations; carryforward allowed for 5 years
- **Connecticut**: Refundable; no carryforward needed
- **Maryland**: Refundable for taxable years beginning after 2020

**Planning Implication:** In refundable states, excess PTET payments create immediate cash flow benefits. No complex tracking required beyond standard documentation.

### **Nonrefundable with Carryforward** (Moderate Flexibility)

These states don't refund but allow multi-year carryforward:

- **California**: **5-year carryforward** (R&TC §19906); no refund; credits expire after year 5
- **Illinois**: 5-year carryforward; nonrefundable
- **Georgia**: 5-year carryforward; nonrefundable
- **Louisiana**: 5-year carryforward; nonrefundable
- **Arizona**: 5-year carryforward; nonrefundable
- **Colorado**: 10-year carryforward; nonrefundable (most generous carryforward period)
- **Oklahoma**: 5-year carryforward; nonrefundable

**California Deep Dive** (Most Common for Coaching Practices):

- **Form 3804-CR** claims the PTET credit
- Excess credits carry forward automatically (no election required)
- **Tracking requirement:** Taxpayers must maintain separate schedules for each tax year's credit origination (FIFO ordering rules apply)
- **Departing owners:** Credits remain with the individual, not the entity; moving out of state doesn't forfeit credits but California-source income must exist to utilize them
- **Composite filers:** Nonresident owners included in composite returns still receive K-1 credit allocations

**Critical Risk:** California credits expire after 5 years. Practices with consistent excess credits should model whether accelerating PTET payments (to create current-year deductions) vs. deferring (to match credit utilization) maximizes after-tax value.

### **Nonrefundable, No Carryforward** (Worst Case)

These states provide no relief for excess credits:

- **Wisconsin**: Nonrefundable; use-it-or-lose-it in current year
- **Kansas**: Nonrefundable; no carryforward
- **South Carolina**: Nonrefundable; no carryforward

**Planning Implication:** In these states, PTET elections require precise liability modeling. Overpayment creates permanent value loss. Consider reducing PTET elections or coordinating with other tax attributes to ensure full utilization.

## Multi-Owner Coordination Challenges

### **Scenario 1: Owners in Different Resident States**

Entity operates in California (PTET elected). Owner A lives in California; Owner B lives in Texas (no income tax).

- **Owner A:** Full California tax liability; PTET credit likely fully utilized
- **Owner B:** No California tax liability; PTET credit carries forward 5 years but may expire unused

**Strategy:** Consider **asymmetric PTET elections** if state law permits (some states allow owner-by-owner elections). Alternatively, adjust distributions to compensate Owner B for stranded credits.

### **Scenario 2: Owner Relocates Mid-Year**

Owner moves from New York (refundable PTET) to Florida (no income tax) in July.

- **New York credit:** Refundable, so excess generates cash refund regardless of residency
- **Timing consideration:** File New York part-year resident return; claim full-year PTET credit

**Strategy:** In refundable states, mid-year moves don't strand credits. In carryforward states, model whether accelerating income into high-tax state residency period maximizes credit utilization.

### **Scenario 3: Multi-State PTET Stack**

Entity elects PTET in California, New York, and New Jersey. Owner resides in California.

- **California resident tax:** Must claim credits for all three states
- **Risk:** Total credits may exceed California tax liability
- **Ordering rules:** California applies credits in specific sequence (own CA PTET first, then other states)

**Strategy:** Model combined credit impact before making multi-state PTET elections. In some cases, skipping PTET in one state (and paying owner-level tax) prevents credit stacking problems.

## Documentation and Tracking Requirements

### **Essential Records**

1. **PTET Election Confirmations** (state filing receipts)
2. **Payment Confirmations** (cancelled checks, EFT records)
3. **Schedule K-1 Allocations** (Box for PTET credits paid on behalf of owner)
4. **Credit Tracking Schedules** (by origination year for carryforward states)
5. **Residency Documentation** (if owners move during credit carryforward period)

### **Technology Solutions**

- **Tax software:** Most professional platforms (Drake, Lacerte, ProSeries) track PTET carryforwards automatically
- **Spreadsheets:** For multi-state practices, maintain master tracker with:
  - State
  - Credit origination year
  - Original credit amount
  - Utilized to date
  - Remaining balance
  - Expiration year

## Practical Planning Strategies

### **1. Pre-Election Modeling**

Before making PTET elections:

- Project owner-level tax liability in each state
- Model credit utilization rates (what % will be used vs. carried forward vs. expired)
- Compare PTET deduction value (federal) against stranded credit risk (state)

**Rule of Thumb:** If >30% of credits will carry forward beyond 3 years in nonrefundable states, reconsider election magnitude.

### **2. Accelerated Utilization**

For practices with excess carryforwards:

- **Accelerate owner compensation** (W-2 wages, guaranteed payments) into high-tax state years
- **Defer other deductions** to increase state tax liability and absorb credits
- **Consider entity restructuring** if credits are systematically stranded (e.g., converting to C-Corp in high-credit states)

### **3. Departing Owner Buyouts**

When owners leave practices with substantial PTET carryforwards:

- **Value the carryforwards** in buyout negotiations (NPV of future tax benefits)
- **Allocate credits explicitly** in purchase agreements
- **Consider installment sales** to match credit utilization with payment streams

### **4. Composite Return Coordination**

For nonresident owners included in composite filings:

- Verify composite returns properly allocate PTET credits to individual owners
- Ensure K-1s reflect credit amounts (some states require separate credit allocation statements)
- Track whether composite tax payment includes PTET or requires separate election

## IRS Audit Defense Considerations

PTET credits face heightened scrutiny because:

- **Large dollar amounts** (often 5-10% of entity income)
- **Novel provisions** (many states enacted PTETs in 2021-2022)
- **Cross-state coordination** creates complexity

**Documentation Best Practices:**

1. Maintain board/owner consent resolutions for PTET elections
2. Document state-specific election deadlines and compliance
3. Retain proof of payment (EFT confirmations, cancelled checks)
4. Reconcile K-1 credit allocations to state filings annually
5. Model federal SALT deduction impact (PTET reduces federal taxable income)

## Key Takeaways for Coaching Practices

1. **Know your state's rules:** Refundable vs. carryforward vs. use-it-or-lose-it
2. **Track by origination year:** FIFO rules apply; credits expire in sequence
3. **Model before electing:** Don't assume all credits will be utilized
4. **Coordinate across owners:** Asymmetric situations require distribution adjustments
5. **Document everything:** PTET audits are increasing as states ramp up enforcement
