# QBI Deduction Optimization and PTET Coordination for Multi-State Coaching Practices 2026

**Research Date:** April 6, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), signed July 4, 2025, fundamentally changed the calculus for pass-through entity tax (PTET) elections by making the Section 199A QBI deduction permanent and raising the SALT cap from $10,000 to $40,000. For multi-state coaching practices, this creates a nuanced optimization problem: PTET deductions reduce K-1 income, which directly shrinks the QBI deduction base. The optimal strategy now requires scenario modeling rather than automatic PTET elections.

---

## 2026 QBI Deduction Thresholds

Under IRS Rev. Proc. 2025-32, the 2026 phase-out ranges are:

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Ends |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Single | $201,750 | $276,750 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $403,500 | $553,500 |

**Key changes under OBBBA:**
- Phase-out window expanded from $50,000 to $75,000 (single) and $100,000 to $150,000 (MFJ)
- New $400 minimum deduction for taxpayers with ≥$1,000 QBI from material participation
- 20% deduction rate unchanged

Coaching practices qualify as Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs), meaning the QBI deduction phases out completely above the upper threshold.

## The PTET-QBI Tradeoff

### How PTET Reduces QBI

When an entity elects PTET:
1. The entity pays state income tax at the entity level
2. This payment is deductible under IRC §164(a)(3) as a business expense
3. Each owner's K-1 reflects reduced distributive share income
4. **Lower K-1 income = lower QBI base = smaller 20% deduction**

### The Math

For a coaching practice with $500,000 net income:
- **Without PTET:** QBI = $500,000 → QBI deduction = $100,000 (20%)
- **With $45,000 PTET election:** QBI = $455,000 → QBI deduction = $91,000
- **Lost QBI deduction:** $9,000 × marginal tax rate (e.g., 32%) = ~$2,880 federal tax increase

This lost QBI benefit must be weighed against the PTET's SALT cap bypass value.

## Decision Framework for 2026

### Scenario 1: SALT Under $40,000

**Recommendation: Skip PTET**

If total state and local taxes are below the $40,000 cap:
- Itemizing on Schedule A already captures the full deduction
- PTET would reduce QBI without providing additional SALT relief
- Net result: higher federal tax liability

### Scenario 2: SALT Exceeds $40,000

**Recommendation: Model Both Scenarios**

For coaching practices with state tax liability above $40,000 (typically net income >$450,000 in high-tax states):

**PTET still wins when:**
- MAGI exceeds $500,000 (SALT cap phases out to $10,000 by $600,000)
- Entity operates in CA, NY, NJ with tax rates exceeding 8%
- Owners are subject to AMT (PTET survives AMT calculation; Schedule A SALT does not)
- Multi-state operations create nexus in multiple PTET states

**PTET may lose when:**
- Coaching practice is near QBI phase-out threshold ($276,750/$553,500)
- Reduced K-1 income pushes owner into SSTB zero-deduction territory
- State PTET rates are low (<5%) and SALT is only slightly above $40,000

## Multi-State Coordination Challenges

### State Election Deadlines (2026)

| State | Deadline | Key Requirement |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| New York | March 15 | Irrevocable after first estimated payment |
| California | June 15 | 50% prepayment of prior-year tax or $1,000 minimum |
| New Jersey (BAIT) | April (return due date) | Election on PTE-100 return |
| Michigan | December 31 | Election on annual return |

**Critical:** Missing deadlines means waiting a full year. Multi-state practices must track each jurisdiction separately.

### Cross-Border Optimization

For coaches serving clients across state lines:
- PTET elections apply only to income sourced to electing states
- Nonresident owners receive credits in their home state for PTET paid
- Coordination required to avoid double taxation or missed credits
- Some states (e.g., NJ) offer favorable credit treatment for taxes paid to other states

## Practical Planning Strategies

### 1. Run Side-by-Side Projections

Before making 2026 PTET elections, model:
- Federal tax with PTET (lower QBI, higher business deduction)
- Federal tax without PTET (higher QBI, Schedule A SALT deduction capped at $40,000 or less)
- Include state-level impacts (some states don't conform to federal PTET treatment)

### 2. Consider Partial PTET Elections

Some states allow entities to elect PTET for only certain owners. This can preserve QBI for owners below thresholds while providing SALT relief for high-income owners.

### 3. Align with Retirement Contributions

PTET reduces K-1 income, which may shrink the base for SEP-IRA or solo 401(k) contributions for partnership/LLC owners. Model the combined impact on retirement savings capacity.

### 4. S-Corp Salary Optimization

For S-corp coaching practices above the QBI phase-out floor:
- Higher W-2 salaries increase the W-2 wage limitation base
- This can preserve some QBI deduction even in the phase-out range
- Trade-off: more FICA taxes vs. larger QBI deduction

---

## Bottom Line

PTET elections are no longer automatic defaults in 2026. The raised SALT cap and permanent QBI deduction create a complex optimization problem requiring:

1. **Income threshold analysis** (where does the owner fall relative to $201,750/$403,500 phase-out floors?)
2. **State tax liability modeling** (does SALT exceed $40,000 after phase-outs?)
3. **Multi-state coordination** (which states have PTET, and what are the deadlines?)
4. **Holistic planning** (how does PTET interact with retirement contributions, AMT, and other deductions?)

For multi-state coaching practices with net income exceeding $400,000, PTET coordination with QBI optimization should be a quarterly planning discussion, not an annual afterthought.
