# GDPR Legitimate Interest Assessments for Coaching Marketing 2026

**Research Date:** April 5, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~720 words

---

## Executive Summary

Coaches often assume consent is the only lawful basis for email marketing under GDPR. However, **legitimate interest** can be appropriate for certain B2B coaching communications, existing client relationships, and lead nurturing—provided a proper Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) is documented. The key distinction: **B2B corporate emails** may qualify for legitimate interest; **B2C personal emails** generally require consent under PECR/ePrivacy rules.

---

## The Three-Part Test for Legitimate Interest

Under GDPR Article 6(1)(f), coaches must pass all three prongs:

### 1. Purpose Test: Is There a Legitimate Interest?

Valid interests for coaching practices include:
- **Client relationship management**: Communicating with current/past clients about relevant services
- **B2B networking**: Contacting corporate decision-makers about executive coaching services
- **Lead nurturing**: Following up with inquiry forms where no explicit consent was given (context-dependent)
- **Business development**: Reasonable outreach to organizations that may benefit from coaching

**Invalid interests:**
- Buying email lists and cold-emailing individuals
- Marketing to personal email addresses without prior relationship
- Sending promotional content unrelated to original collection purpose

### 2. Necessity Test: Is Processing Necessary?

Ask: Could you achieve the same goal with less intrusive means?

**Document:**
- Why consent wasn't obtained (e.g., business card exchange at networking event)
- Why legitimate interest is more appropriate than consent for this specific use
- Whether you could reasonably use postal mail, LinkedIn messaging, or phone instead

**Best practice:** If you're collecting emails via website forms, use consent instead. Legitimate interest is for **pre-existing relationships** or **reasonable B2B outreach** where consent would be impractical.

### 3. Balancing Test: Do Your Interests Override Individual Rights?

Weigh your coaching business interests against the data subject's privacy rights.

**Factors favoring legitimate interest:**
- Contact is a corporate email (name@company.com), not personal (gmail.com)
- Content is directly relevant to their role (e.g., leadership coaching for HR directors)
- You have an existing professional relationship
- Emails are infrequent (monthly or less)
- Clear opt-out is provided in every message

**Factors favoring individual rights:**
- Personal email addresses
- Sensitive data involved (health, financial status)
- High-frequency messaging
- No reasonable expectation of receiving marketing

Document your assessment using this structure:

```markdown

**Date:** [Date]
**Data Controller:** [Your Coaching Business Name]
**Purpose:** [e.g., B2B email marketing to HR professionals about executive coaching]

### 1. Purpose Test
- Legitimate interest claimed: [Business development for executive coaching services]
- Who benefits: [Coaching practice + potential corporate clients]
- Is it lawful: [Yes, not prohibited by statute or contract]

### 2. Necessity Test
- Why necessary: [Corporate decision-makers benefit from knowing available services; consent impractical for initial B2B contact]
- Alternative means considered: [LinkedIn outreach, postal mail, phone—email most efficient]
- Data minimization: [Only name, corporate email, job title processed]

### 3. Balancing Test
- Nature of data: [Business contact information only, not sensitive]
- Reasonable expectation: [HR professionals expect B2B service outreach]
- Impact on individuals: [Low—one email per month max, clear opt-out]
- Safeguards: [Suppression list maintained, opt-out honored within 24 hours]

**Outcome:** ✅ Legitimate interest appropriate
**Review Date:** [12 months from assessment]
```

## PECR/ePrivacy Interaction: Critical Distinction

**GDPR governs lawful basis; PECR governs electronic communications.**

### For B2B Corporate Emails:
- **PECR allows** unsolicited marketing to **limited company** addresses without consent
- Legitimate interest (GDPR) + corporate email (PECR) = compliant
- **Must still provide opt-out** in every message

### For B2C Personal Emails:
- **PECR requires prior consent** for unsolicited marketing emails
- Legitimate interest **cannot override** PECR consent requirement
- Applies to: gmail.com, yahoo.com, personal domains, sole trader emails

### For Existing Clients (Soft Opt-In):
- PECR allows marketing similar services to **existing customers** without fresh consent
- Must have collected email **in course of sale/negotiation**
- Must have offered opt-out at collection and in every message
- **Coaching clients qualify** if they purchased services from you

## Practical Implementation Checklist

### Before Sending:
- [ ] Complete written LIA and store with compliance records
- [ ] Verify email addresses are corporate (not personal) for B2B outreach
- [ ] Ensure content is relevant to recipient's role/industry
- [ ] Set up suppression list for opt-outs
- [ ] Limit frequency (recommend: max 1-2 emails/month)

### In Every Email:
- [ ] Clear sender identification (your name, business, contact details)
- [ ] Unsubscribe link that works within 24 hours
- [ ] Privacy policy link explaining data use
- [ ] No deceptive subject lines or content

### Ongoing Compliance:
- [ ] Review LIA annually or when purposes change
- [ ] Honor opt-outs immediately (add to suppression list)
- [ ] Don't share/transfer email lists without fresh legal basis
- [ ] Document all LIAs—ICO can request during investigations

## When to Use Consent Instead

**Use consent (not legitimate interest) when:**
- Collecting emails via website forms
- Building newsletter subscriber lists
- Marketing to consumers (B2C)
- Sending frequent promotional content
- Using third-party purchased lists (generally non-compliant anyway)

**Consent requirements:**
- Freely given (not bundled with service terms)
- Specific (clear what they're signing up for)
- Informed (they know who you are and how you'll use data)
- Unambiguous (active opt-in, no pre-ticked boxes)
- Easy to withdraw (opt-out as easy as opt-in)

---

## Risk Assessment

**Low Risk:** B2B emails to corporate addresses, existing client communications, infrequent relevant content  
**Medium Risk:** Lead nurturing sequences, networking follow-ups without explicit consent  
**High Risk:** Purchased lists, personal email addresses, frequent promotional blasts, sensitive data processing

**Penalty context:** ICO fines consider documentation quality. A well-documented LIA shows accountability even if challenged.

---

## Key Takeaways for Coaches

1. **Legitimate interest is viable for B2B coaching marketing** but requires documented LIA
2. **PECR overrides GDPR** for electronic mail—corporate vs. personal email distinction is critical
3. **Existing clients** can receive similar service marketing under soft opt-in rules
4. **Document everything**—LIA is your defense if questioned
5. **When in doubt, use consent**—it's clearer and lower risk for most coaching practices
6. **Review annually**—legitimate interests can change as your practice evolves
