# PTET Revocation and Amendment Procedures Across States 2026

**Research Date:** April 6, 2026  
**Topic:** Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) Election Revocation and Amendment Procedures  
**Focus:** State-by-state procedures for revoking or amending PTET elections after initial filing

---

## Executive Summary

Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) elections are generally **annual decisions** that must be made fresh each tax year. However, business circumstances can change after an election is filed—ownership shifts, income projections prove inaccurate, or multi-state coordination reveals unintended consequences. Understanding revocation and amendment procedures is critical for audit defense and avoiding costly mistakes.

**Key Finding:** PTET elections are **largely irrevocable** once filed in most states, with narrow exceptions for timely amendments before original return due dates. Planning must happen upfront.

---

## State-by-State Revocation Procedures

### New York

**Election Window:** Annual election via NY PTET Web File system  
**Revocation Policy:** Elections are **irrevocable** once filed for the tax year  
**Amendment Process:**
- Amended PTET returns can be filed if the original return contained errors
- Must use amended return Web application (available January 1 following tax year)
- Overpayments result in refunds or credits applied to subsequent year
- No mid-year revocation permitted even if entity dissolves

**Critical Deadline:** Election must be made by original due date (March 15 for calendar-year entities)

### California

**Election Window:** Annual election on Form 3809  
**Revocation Policy:** **Irrevocable** once the original due date passes  
**Amendment Process:**
- Amended returns accepted for mathematical corrections or federal changes
- FTB Form 3809 (Amended) must reference original election
- Refunds processed within 6-12 months typically
- No revocation for change of business circumstances

**Note:** California's 9.3% PTET rate makes erroneous elections costly—verify income projections before filing.

### New Jersey

**Election Window:** Annual election via NJ-1065/1120S  
**Revocation Policy:** **Irrevocable** after due date (including extensions)  
**Amendment Process:**
- Amended returns permitted for corrections
- NJ Division of Taxation requires written explanation for amendments
- Credit refunds available for overpayments
- Entities must notify all partners/shareholders of amendment

### Indiana

**Election Window:** Annual election required (does not carry over)  
**Revocation Policy:** Election is **binding** once filed  
**Amendment Process:**
- Amended IT-20S/IT-65 returns accepted
- DOR FAQ confirms annual election requirement—each year is independent
- No retroactive revocation permitted

### Montana

**Election Window:** Annual election via new SB 554 framework  
**Revocation Policy:** **Irrevocable** after due date  
**Amendment Process:**
- Newly implemented (2025-2026)
- Amended returns follow standard corporate amendment procedures
- Limited guidance on PTET-specific revocations

## Amendment vs. Revocation: Critical Distinction

**Amendment** = Correcting errors in a filed PTET return (generally permitted)  
**Revocation** = Undoing the election itself (rarely permitted)

Most states allow amendments for:
- Mathematical errors
- Changes in federal taxable income (after IRS audit)
- Correction of partner/shareholder allocations
- Clerical mistakes

Most states **do not allow** revocation for:
- Change in business strategy
- Lower-than-expected income
- Owner preference changes
- Multi-state coordination issues discovered post-filing

---

## Handling Overpayments and Refunds

When PTET payments exceed actual tax liability:

1. **Refund Claims:** File amended return showing correct tax due; refund issued automatically in most states
2. **Credit Application:** Some states (NY, CA) allow overpayment to credit toward next year's PTET
3. **Processing Time:** 6-12 months typical for refund issuance
4. **Interest:** Most states do NOT pay interest on PTET overpayments

**Cash Flow Warning:** PTET overpayments tie up capital for extended periods—model conservatively.

---

## Multi-State Coordination Risks

**Scenario:** Entity files PTET in NY and CA, then discovers one state doesn't provide usable resident credits.

**Problem:** Neither state permits revocation. Entity is locked into both elections.

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- **Pre-filing analysis:** Model resident state credit usability before any PTET election
- **Staggered elections:** In uncertain situations, file in highest-benefit state first
- **Documentation:** Retain analysis showing good-faith planning (audit defense)
- **Owner communication:** Ensure all owners understand irrevocability before filing

---

## IRS Guidance on PTET Election Reversals

**Current Status:** No formal IRS guidance specifically addresses PTET revocation.

**General Principles:**
- PTET deductions are claimed on federal returns via Schedule K-1
- If state disallows PTET refund, federal deduction remains valid (tax was paid)
- If state issues refund, federal return may need amendment (income was overstated)
- **Audit Risk:** Frequent amendments or inconsistent PTET positions across years may trigger scrutiny

**Best Practice:** Treat PTET elections as final. Only amend for genuine errors, not planning second-guesses.

## Practical Checklist for PTET Election Decisions

**Before Filing:**

- [ ] Confirm all owners support PTET election (unanimous consent often required)
- [ ] Model regular tax vs. PTET scenario for each owner's resident state
- [ ] Verify resident state allows credit for PTET taxes paid
- [ ] Check for AMT interactions (PTET deductions may increase AMT exposure)
- [ ] Confirm entity type qualifies (some states exclude certain partnership types)
- [ ] Document decision rationale for audit trail

**After Filing:**

- [ ] Retain confirmation of election submission
- [ ] Notify all owners of irrevocable election
- [ ] Track payment deadlines (quarterly vs. annual varies by state)
- [ ] Coordinate with estimated tax calculations (PTET credits reduce owner-level estimates)

---

## Audit Defense Documentation

If PTET elections are audited, retain:

1. **Election confirmations** from each state
2. **Owner consent documentation** (meeting minutes, written consents)
3. **Tax calculations** showing PTET vs. non-PTET scenarios
4. **Resident state credit worksheets** demonstrating benefit
5. **Payment confirmations** and bank records
6. **Amended return explanations** (if applicable)

**Key Point:** Consistency matters. Don't elect PTET in high-income years and skip in low-income years without clear business rationale.

---

## Bottom Line for Multi-State Coaching Practices

PTET elections are **use-it-or-lose-it annual decisions** with limited undo options. The window for careful planning is before filing—not after. For coaching practices operating across multiple states:

1. **Model first, file second**—run numbers for all owners before any election
2. **Assume irrevocability**—plan as if you cannot change your mind
3. **Document everything**—audit defense starts with contemporaneous records
4. **Communicate with owners**—ensure they understand the commitment
5. **Coordinate across states**—avoid double taxation or unusable credits

When in doubt, consult state-specific guidance or tax counsel before filing. The cost of prevention is far lower than the cost of correction.
