# Cryptocurrency Estate Tax Valuation and Step-Up in Basis for Trust Beneficiaries 2026

**Research Date:** April 5, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

Cryptocurrency held in trust structures receives favorable tax treatment upon the grantor's death: beneficiaries inherit digital assets with a **step-up in cost basis to fair market value (FMV) on the date of death** under IRC Section 1014. This eliminates capital gains tax on all prior appreciation. However, proper valuation methodologies and documentation are critical to withstand IRS scrutiny, especially for illiquid or low-volume tokens.

---

## IRC Section 1014: The Step-Up Basis Rule

**Core Principle:** When a beneficiary inherits cryptocurrency from a trust or estate, the tax basis resets to the asset's FMV on the date of the decedent's death (or alternate valuation date, if elected).

**Example:**
- Original purchase: 10 BTC at $15,000 each ($150,000 total basis)
- Value at death: 10 BTC at $95,000 each ($950,000 FMV)
- **New basis for beneficiary:** $950,000
- **Capital gains eliminated:** $800,000 of appreciation never taxed

**Key Point:** This applies equally to cryptocurrency held in revocable trusts, irrevocable grantor trusts, and direct ownership. The step-up occurs regardless of the holding structure, provided the assets are includible in the decedent's gross estate.

## Valuation Methodologies for Cryptocurrency

The IRS requires **reasonable, consistent, and documented** valuation approaches. Methods vary by asset type:

### 1. **Liquid, Exchange-Traded Tokens (Bitcoin, Ethereum)**
- **Method:** Use closing price on major exchanges (Coinbase, Kraken, Binance) on date of death
- **Documentation:** Screenshot exchange prices, download trade history, retain API data
- **Best Practice:** Average prices across 2-3 major exchanges to mitigate exchange-specific anomalies

### 2. **Illiquid or Low-Volume Tokens**
- **Method:** Apply discounts for lack of marketability (DLOM) and lack of control
- **Approaches:**
  - **Restricted stock studies:** Reference SEC Rule 144 discount analyses (typically 20-35%)
  - **Put option studies:** Compare to traded put options on similar assets
  - **Pre-IPO transaction analysis:** Use recent private sale prices if available
- **Documentation:** Retain whitepapers, tokenomics documents, trading volume data, lock-up agreements

### 3. **DeFi Positions, Staked Assets, LP Tokens**
- **Valuation Challenge:** These positions may have impermanent loss, unstaking periods, or complex payoff structures
- **Method:** Calculate net asset value (NAV) of underlying assets minus any exit penalties or unstaking delays
- **Documentation:** Screenshot protocol dashboards, retain smart contract addresses, document unstaking timelines

### 4. **NFTs and Unique Digital Collectibles**
- **Method:** Use recent comparable sales (comps) from same collection, adjusted for rarity traits
- **If no comps:** Obtain qualified appraisal from certified digital asset appraiser (ASA, CVA credentials)
- **IRS Threshold:** Appraisal required for single items valued over $50,000 (Form 8283)

## Estate Tax Exposure and Minimization Strategies

### **Federal Estate Tax Threshold (2026):**
- **Exemption amount:** $13.61 million per individual (indexed for inflation)
- **Portability:** Unused exemption transfers to surviving spouse
- **Tax rate:** 40% on amounts exceeding exemption

### **State Estate Taxes:**
- **Varies by jurisdiction:** Some states (e.g., Massachusetts, Oregon, Washington) have lower thresholds ($1-2 million)
- **Trust situs matters:** Holding crypto in trusts established in no-income-tax states (Nevada, South Dakota, Wyoming) can reduce exposure

### **Minimization Strategies:**

1. **Annual Exclusion Gifts:** Gift up to $18,000 per beneficiary annually (2026 amount) to remove appreciation from estate

2. **SLATs (Spousal Lifetime Access Trusts):** Transfer crypto to irrevocable trust for spouse's benefit, removing future appreciation from both spouses' estates

3. **IDGTs (Intentionally Defective Grantor Trusts):** Sell crypto to trust in exchange for promissory note; grantor pays taxes on trust income, effectively transferring wealth tax-free

4. **Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs):** Donate appreciated crypto to CRT, receive income stream, remainder goes to charity—avoids capital gains and estate tax

5. **Family Limited Partnerships (FLPs):** Transfer crypto to FLP, gift limited partnership interests with valuation discounts (typically 20-40% for lack of control/marketability)

## Form 1099-DA: New Reporting Requirements (2026)

**Critical Update:** IRS Form 1099-DA ("Digital Asset Proceeds") is now mandatory for cryptocurrency transactions in 2026.

**Implications for Estates:**
- Executors must report disposition of crypto assets on final Form 1041 (estate tax return)
- Beneficiaries receive 1099-DA when they sell inherited crypto
- **Basis reporting:** Executors must provide beneficiaries with date-of-death FMV documentation to support stepped-up basis claims

**Action Item:** Executors should download complete transaction histories from all exchanges and wallets immediately upon death to establish pre-death basis and date-of-death valuations.

## Practical Checklist for Trustees and Executors

1. **Inventory all digital assets:** Exchanges, hardware wallets, DeFi protocols, staking positions
2. **Secure access:** Obtain private keys, seed phrases, exchange credentials (use password manager or encrypted vault)
3. **Document valuations:** Screenshot prices on date of death from multiple sources
4. **File Form 706:** Estate tax return if estate exceeds exemption threshold
5. **Issue Schedule K-1:** Report income and basis to beneficiaries
6. **Retain records:** Keep valuation documentation for 3-7 years (IRS audit window)

## Risk Areas and Audit Triggers

**High-Risk Scenarios:**
- **Large valuations without appraisals:** Tokens valued over $500,000 without third-party appraisal
- **Inconsistent pricing:** Using different exchanges or dates for similar assets
- **Missing documentation:** No screenshots, trade histories, or wallet statements
- **PFIC complications:** Foreign-issued tokens may trigger PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) reporting on Form 8621

**Defense Strategy:** Engage a qualified digital asset appraiser early, maintain contemporaneous records, and file all required forms (3520, 8938, FBAR if applicable).

---

## Bottom Line

Cryptocurrency in trust structures receives step-up in basis at death, eliminating capital gains on prior appreciation. However, **valuation methodology and documentation are critical**. Use exchange prices for liquid tokens, apply appropriate discounts for illiquid assets, and engage qualified appraisers for high-value or unique holdings. With the introduction of Form 1099-DA in 2026, the IRS has enhanced visibility into crypto transactions—proper planning and record-keeping are no longer optional.
