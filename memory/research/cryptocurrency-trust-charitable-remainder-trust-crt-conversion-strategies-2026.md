## Executive Summary

Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs) offer cryptocurrency holders a powerful tax deferral strategy for appreciated digital assets exceeding $500,000 in unrealized gains. By contributing crypto to a CRT, donors can eliminate immediate capital gains tax (up to 23.8% federal + state), receive an income stream for life or term, and claim a partial charitable deduction—all while the trust sells assets tax-free and reinvests proceeds into diversified income-producing investments.

---

## How CRTs Work with Cryptocurrency

### The Basic Structure

A CRT is an irrevocable trust that splits benefits between income beneficiaries (you or designated recipients) and remainder beneficiaries (qualified charities). The trust operates in three phases:

1. **Contribution:** You transfer appreciated cryptocurrency into the CRT
2. **Sale:** The CRT sells the crypto **tax-free** (no immediate capital gains recognition)
3. **Distribution:** You receive annual payments (5-7% typical) for life or up to 20 years
4. **Remainder:** Remaining assets pass to designated charity(ies) at termination

### CRUT vs. CRAT: Which for Crypto?

**Charitable Remainder Unitrust (CRUT)** — Recommended for cryptocurrency:
- Pays fixed **percentage** of trust assets annually (revalued each year)
- Allows **additional contributions** (flexible for ongoing crypto acquisitions)
- Better hedge against inflation and portfolio growth
- Most common choice for volatile assets like crypto

**Charitable Remainder Annuity Trust (CRAT):**
- Pays fixed **dollar amount** annually
- No additional contributions permitted
- Simpler but less flexible
- Risk: inflation erodes purchasing power over long terms

For cryptocurrency holders, the **CRUT structure dominates** due to contribution flexibility and growth participation.

## Tax Benefits and Optimization

### Immediate Tax Savings

| Benefit | Typical Value |
|---------|---------------|
| Capital gains tax avoided | 20-33.8% (federal + NIIT + state) |
| Charitable income tax deduction | 10-30% of contributed value |
| Estate tax reduction | 40% on removed assets |

**Example:** Contributing $1M Bitcoin with $200K basis:
- **Without CRT:** Sell → $800K gain × 23.8% = ~$190K tax due immediately
- **With CRT:** $0 immediate tax, full $1M reinvested, ~$150K charitable deduction

### Payout Rate Optimization (5-7% Standard)

IRS rules require minimum 5% and maximum 50% payout rates. Optimal range for crypto CRTs:

- **5-6%:** Maximizes charitable deduction, preserves principal longer, better for younger donors
- **6-7%:** Balances income needs with deduction size, standard recommendation
- **7%+:** Higher income but smaller deduction, faster principal depletion

**Key constraint:** The 10% probability-of-exhaustion test (CRATs) and 5% minimum (all CRTs) must pass at inception using IRS discount rates.

### Income Stream Characterization

CRT distributions follow a **four-tier taxation system** (worst-to-best):

1. **Ordinary income** (interest, non-qualified dividends)
2. **Capital gains** (short-term then long-term)
3. **Tax-free return of basis**
4. **Tax-free distributions** (once all income/gains exhausted)

**Strategy:** After tax-free crypto sale inside CRT, reinvest in tax-efficient assets (municipal bonds, growth stocks) to minimize ordinary income tier, pushing distributions into lower-tax categories.

## CRT vs. IDGT Installment Sale: Comparison for Crypto Portfolios

| Feature | CRT | IDGT Installment Sale |
|---------|-----|----------------------|
| **Capital gains tax** | Eliminated (tax-free sale) | Deferred (installment method) |
| **Charitable deduction** | Yes (partial) | No |
| **Beneficiary** | Charity receives remainder | Family trust receives assets |
| **Income stream** | Required (5%+ annually) | Optional (note payments) |
| **Estate tax removal** | Yes | Yes (if structured properly) |
| **Flexibility** | Irrevocable, fixed terms | More flexible trust terms |
| **Best for** | Philanthropically-inclined, high-income | Wealth transfer to heirs, no charity goal |

**Decision framework:**
- Choose **CRT** if: You want charitable impact + tax deferral + lifetime income
- Choose **IDGT** if: Primary goal is wealth transfer to family with minimal tax

For portfolios exceeding $500K in unrealized crypto gains, **CRTs provide superior tax elimination** (not just deferral) when charitable intent exists.

## Setup Requirements and Practical Steps

### Minimum Thresholds

- **Practical minimum:** $250K-$500K in appreciated assets (setup costs ~$5K-$15K)
- **Optimal range:** $1M+ crypto holdings
- **Remainder requirement:** At least 10% of initial value must go to charity (IRS test)

### Setup Process

1. **Select trustee:** Self-trusteed (you manage investments) or institutional trustee
2. **Draft trust agreement:** Specify payout rate, beneficiaries, termination event
3. **File for EIN:** IRS Form SS-4 (trust tax ID)
4. **Transfer crypto:** Execute assignment deed, update wallet custody
5. **Obtain appraisal:** Required for crypto >$500K deduction claim (IRC §170)
6. **File Form 8283:** With tax return for charitable deduction
7. **Trust sells crypto:** Tax-free liquidation, reinvest per investment policy
8. **Receive distributions:** Annual payments begin (prorated first year)

### Remainder Charity Selection

- Can name multiple charities (including donor-advised funds)
- Can retain right to change remainder beneficiaries
- Charity must be IRS-qualified 501(c)(3)
- Consider naming your own DAF for maximum flexibility

## Risks and Considerations

### Irrevocability

CRTs cannot be undone. Once crypto is contributed:
- You cannot reclaim the assets
- Terms are fixed (payout rate, beneficiaries)
- Only flexibility: investment management and charity selection

### Low-Interest Rate Environment Impact

IRS discount rates affect charitable deduction calculations:
- **Higher rates** → larger deductions
- **Lower rates** → smaller deductions
- Monitor applicable federal rate (AFR) at time of funding

### Cryptocurrency-Specific Issues

- **Valuation challenges:** Use contemporaneous exchange prices; document thoroughly
- **Custody:** Transfer to trustee-controlled wallet before sale
- **PFIC concerns:** If CRT holds crypto funds/ETFs, potential PFIC complications
- **Audit risk:** Large crypto CRT contributions may trigger IRS scrutiny

## Bottom Line for 2026

Charitable Remainder Trusts remain one of the most tax-efficient exit strategies for appreciated cryptocurrency when:

✅ Unrealized gains exceed $500K  
✅ You have charitable intent (even if modest)  
✅ You want lifetime income plus tax diversification  
✅ You're in high tax brackets (24%+ federal)

**Typical outcome:** A $2M crypto CRT at age 55 with 6% payout generates ~$120K/year income, eliminates ~$400K+ in capital gains tax, provides ~$300K charitable deduction, and leaves ~$1M+ to charity at death—all while you sleep well knowing the tax bomb was defused.
