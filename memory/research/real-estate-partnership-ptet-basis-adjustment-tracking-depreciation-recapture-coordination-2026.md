# Real Estate Partnership PTET Basis Adjustment Tracking and Depreciation Recapture Coordination 2026

**Research Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Topic:** Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) elections and their interaction with depreciation recapture (Section 1250/1245) and basis adjustment tracking in real estate partnerships

---

## Executive Summary

Pass-through entity tax (PTET) elections create significant complexity for real estate partnerships holding depreciable property. The core challenge: PTET payments reduce partnership inside basis, but the timing and characterization of these reductions interact awkwardly with depreciation recapture calculations under Sections 1245 and 1250. This research identifies coordination gaps, state-specific variations, and practical tracking methodologies for partnerships navigating dual PTET and depreciation recapture regimes.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. PTET's Effect on Inside Basis

PTET payments are deductible at the entity level under IRC Section 164, reducing federal taxable income. This deduction flows through to partners as a reduction in their distributive share of partnership income. Critically:

- **Inside basis reduction:** PTET payments reduce partnership inside basis in assets proportionally across all partnership property
- **Outside basis reduction:** Partners' outside basis is reduced under Section 705(a)(2)(B) as distributions or deemed contributions, depending on state credit treatment
- **Timing mismatch risk:** When PTET is paid in Year 1 but property sales with recapture occur in Year 2+, partnerships face basis tracking disparities between federal and state depreciation schedules

### 2. Depreciation Recapture Coordination Challenges

**Section 1250 Recapture (Real Property):**
- Unrecaptured Section 1250 gain is taxed at a maximum 25% rate
- PTET payments that reduce inside basis before recapture calculations can inadvertently increase the amount of Section 1250 gain recognized
- **Practical issue:** If PTET reduces depreciable basis in Year 1, but the property is sold in Year 2, the partnership must determine whether PTET basis reductions apply before or after computing accumulated depreciation for recapture purposes

**Section 1245 Recapture (Personal Property):**
- Applies to depreciable personal property (HVAC systems, appliances, fixtures in cost segregation studies)
- Full recapture of depreciation taken as ordinary income
- PTET basis reductions can accelerate Section 1245 recapture by reducing the adjusted basis against which gain is measured

### 3. State-Specific Treatment Variations

**New York:**
- PTET is imposed on PTE taxable income including all income, gain, loss, or deduction items
- Partners receive refundable credits against NY resident tax
- Basis adjustment tracking requires separate NY vs. federal depreciation schedules

**California:**
- PTET elective with specific basis adjustment rules
- Non-conformity with federal treatment creates book-tax disparities
- Requires dual tracking for partnerships with CA real estate holdings

**New Jersey:**
- PTET mandatory for some entities, elective for others
- Basis adjustment timing differs from NY/CA approaches
- Partners must track PTET payments separately for credit carryforward calculations

### 4. Cost Segregation Study Interactions

Partnerships that have performed cost segregation studies face amplified complexity:

- Accelerated depreciation from cost segregation increases Section 1245 recapture exposure
- PTET basis reductions may apply differently to segregated assets vs. building structure
- **Documentation requirement:** Separate PTET basis adjustment tracking for each cost segregation asset class

### 5. Like-Kind Exchange (Section 1031) Complications

When PTET-electing partnerships defer gain through Section 1031 exchanges:

- PTET basis adjustments must carry over to replacement property
- Deferred recapture potential becomes embedded in replacement property basis
- **Risk:** If PTET elections are made inconsistently across exchange cycles, basis tracking errors compound over time

## Practical Recommendations

### For Tax Preparers

1. **Create dual depreciation schedules:** Maintain separate federal and state (PTET-adjusted) depreciation tracks for each property
2. **Document PTET payment timing:** Record exact payment dates and amounts to correlate with basis adjustment events
3. **Model recapture scenarios:** Before property sales, run projections showing PTET's impact on Section 1245/1250 recapture amounts
4. **Track partner-level credits:** Maintain ledgers showing each partner's PTET credit allocation, carryforwards, and refundable amounts

### For Partnership Agreements

1. **Specify PTET allocation methodology:** Clarify whether PTET benefits are allocated proportionally or specially to partners bearing state tax burden
2. **Address basis adjustment timing:** Define whether PTET basis reductions apply at payment date, year-end, or K-1 issuance
3. **Include recapture sharing rules:** Specify how Section 1245/1250 recapture is allocated among partners when PTET has altered basis calculations

### For Audit Defense

1. **Maintain contemporaneous records:** Document PTET election decisions, payment confirmations, and basis adjustment calculations
2. **Reconcile federal vs. state basis:** Prepare annual reconciliations showing PTET's impact on inside vs. outside basis
3. **Preserve cost segregation studies:** Keep detailed asset classifications to support PTET basis allocation across property components

---

## Unresolved Questions

- **IRS guidance gap:** No definitive federal guidance on whether PTET basis reductions apply before or after depreciation recapture calculations
- **State conformity variance:** Some states may not recognize PTET basis adjustments for state-level recapture purposes
- **Transferee partner treatment:** Unclear how PTET basis adjustments affect partners who purchase interests mid-cycle, post-PTET payment but pre-sale

---

## Conclusion

PTET elections add a layer of basis tracking complexity that real estate partnerships cannot afford to ignore. The interaction between PTET basis reductions and depreciation recapture creates timing mismatches, characterization risks, and state-specific compliance burdens. Partnerships holding depreciable real estate should implement dual-track depreciation systems, document PTET payment timing meticulously, and model recapture scenarios before property dispositions. As state PTET regimes continue evolving, expect increased IRS scrutiny on basis adjustment coordination—particularly for partnerships using cost segregation studies or like-kind exchanges.
