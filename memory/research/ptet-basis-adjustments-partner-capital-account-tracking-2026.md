# PTET Basis Adjustments and Partner Capital Account Tracking 2026

**Research Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) payments create complex basis adjustment questions under IRC Section 705. The core issue: whether PTET payments increase partner basis (as deemed contributions) or decrease basis (as distributions), and when these adjustments occur. This research clarifies the treatment across federal and state jurisdictions, with practical guidance for multi-state coaching practices.

---

## IRC Section 705 Framework

Under IRC Section 705(a), a partner's outside basis is adjusted by:

1. **Increases:** Additional contributions + distributive share of income/gain
2. **Decreases:** Distributions + distributive share of losses/deductions + nondeductible expenditures

**PTET Treatment:** PTET payments are generally treated as **state income taxes paid by the partnership**, which flow through to partners as separately stated items. The critical question is timing and characterization.

### Current IRS Position

The IRS has not issued definitive guidance specific to PTET basis adjustments. However, established partnership tax principles suggest:

- **PTET payments reduce partnership taxable income** (as business expenses or taxes paid)
- **Partners' distributive shares reflect the reduction**, decreasing outside basis under Section 705(a)(2)(B)
- **PTET credits claimed by partners** on resident state returns do not create additional basis adjustments—they are personal tax credits, not partnership-level items

**Key Insight:** PTET payments decrease partner basis in the year paid (or accrued, depending on partnership accounting method), not when K-1s are issued. This timing mismatch creates tracking challenges.

## Section 704(b) Capital Account Maintenance

**Book vs. Tax Capital Accounts:** Section 704(b) requires partnerships to maintain capital accounts reflecting economic arrangements, separate from tax basis capital accounts.

### PTET Impact on 704(b) Accounts

1. **Book Capital Accounts:** PTET payments reduce book capital accounts as a tax expense allocation
2. **Tax Capital Accounts:** PTET payments reduce tax capital accounts consistent with Section 705 basis reductions
3. **Special Allocations:** If PTET benefits vary among partners (e.g., nonresident vs. resident partners), special allocations under Section 704(b) may be required to maintain substantial economic effect

**Practical Issue:** Multi-state coaching practices with partners in different states may have PTET elections in some states but not others. This creates **asymmetric tax burdens** that require careful capital account tracking.

## State-Specific Variations

### New York PTET

- Partners receive Form IT-204-IP showing PTET credit share
- Credit is claimed on resident state return (not partnership level)
- **Basis Impact:** PTET payment reduces partner basis in year of entity payment; credit claim has no additional basis effect

### California PTET

- Elective tax at entity level (9.3% for S-corps, variable for partnerships)
- Credit carryforward allowed (5 years) if credit exceeds tax due
- **Basis Impact:** Same as NY—basis reduced when tax paid, not when credit utilized

### New Jersey PTET

- Mandatory for some entities, elective for others
- Credits flow through to partners via Schedule NJ-K-1
- **Basis Impact:** Consistent treatment—basis reduction at payment

## Section 704(c) Built-In Gains Interaction

**Scenario:** Partnership has appreciated property with built-in gain under Section 704(c). PTET payments are made on income that includes Section 704(c) gain.

**Treatment:** PTET allocations should follow the same method as the underlying income allocation (traditional, curative, or remedial). Mismatched PTET allocations can distort partner economics and trigger IRS scrutiny.

---

## PTET Credit Mismatches and Basis

**Common Problem:** Partner resides in non-PTET state, or PTET credit exceeds resident state tax liability.

**Basis Consequences:**

- **Excess credits carried forward:** No additional basis adjustment (credit is personal, not partnership item)
- **Refunds received by partners:** Reduce basis in year received (treated as distribution/recovery)
- **Refunds retained by partnership:** Increase basis if treated as partnership asset (uncommon)

## Practical Tracking Recommendations

### 1. Separate PTET Tracking by State

Maintain separate schedules for each state's PTET:
- Payment date and amount
- Allocation method (pro rata vs. special)
- Partner-specific credit amounts
- Carryforward balances (if applicable)

### 2. Basis Adjustment Timing

**Best Practice:** Adjust partner basis on **payment date** (cash basis) or **accrual date** (accrual basis), not K-1 issuance date. This prevents year-end mismatches and ensures accurate basis for distribution planning.

### 3. K-1 Disclosure

Include detailed PTET disclosures on Schedule K-1:
- State where PTET paid
- Partner's share of PTET
- Whether PTET was deducted in computing ordinary income
- Credit amount available to partner

### 4. Capital Account Reconciliation

Annually reconcile:
- Book capital accounts (704(b))
- Tax capital accounts (705)
- Outside basis (705 + debt share)
- PTET-related adjustments (separate line item)

## Audit Defense Considerations

**Red Flags:**

- Inconsistent PTET treatment across years
- Special allocations without economic effect documentation
- Basis adjustments timed to K-1 issuance rather than payment
- Failure to track state-specific PTET variations

**Documentation Requirements:**

- PTET election forms (state-specific)
- Payment confirmations
- Partner consent records (some states require unanimous consent)
- Capital account computation worksheets
- K-1 disclosure statements

---

## Conclusion

PTET basis adjustments follow standard partnership tax principles: payments reduce partner basis when made, credits are personal items with no additional basis effect, and refunds reduce basis when received. Multi-state coaching practices must maintain granular tracking by state and partner to avoid mismatches and ensure audit-ready documentation.

**Action Item:** Review PTET elections annually with tax advisors, especially when partner residency changes or new state nexus is established.
