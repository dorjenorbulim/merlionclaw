# Cryptocurrency Trust Partial Termination and Decanting Strategies 2026

**Research Date:** April 6, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

Trust decanting—the process of "pouring over" assets from an existing irrevocable trust into a new trust with modified terms—has emerged as a critical strategy for cryptocurrency holders seeking to adapt to evolving tax landscapes, jurisdictional advantages, and beneficiary needs. For digital asset trusts, partial termination and decanting offer flexibility without triggering full liquidation events or losing stepped-up basis benefits.

---

## When to Decant vs. Fully Terminate

### Partial Termination Thresholds

Most states permit partial termination when:

- **Trust value exceeds $500,000** and distribution of a portion won't materially impair remaining trust purposes
- **Beneficiary consent** is obtained from all affected parties (required in Delaware and Nevada; South Dakota allows trustee discretion in many cases)
- **Material purpose test** is satisfied—the remaining trust still serves its original intent

### Full Termination Triggers

Consider complete termination when:

- Trust assets have depreciated significantly below original basis (realize losses before distribution)
- All beneficiaries are competent adults who prefer direct ownership
- Administrative costs exceed 1-2% of trust value annually
- Cryptocurrency custody technology has evolved beyond the trust's original distribution protocols

## State-by-State Decanting Authority

### South Dakota (Most Flexible)

South Dakota's decanting statute (SDCL §55-2-14) permits trustees to decant without beneficiary consent if:

- The trustee has discretionary distribution authority
- The new trust doesn't reduce vested interests
- Notice is provided to qualified beneficiaries 60 days prior

**2025 Update:** Senate Bill 69 expanded decanting authority to include changes to trustee succession and administrative provisions without court approval.

### Delaware (Beneficiary-Friendly)

Delaware (12 Del. C. §3528) requires:

- Beneficiary consent for decanting that alters beneficial interests
- 60-day notice period for all qualified beneficiaries
- No reduction of fixed income or annuity interests

Delaware uniquely permits decanting to extend trust duration beyond the original perpetuity period if all beneficiaries consent.

### Nevada (Trustee-Discretion Focused)

Nevada (NRS 163.550-566) allows trustees to decant without court approval when:

- The trustee holds discretionary distribution powers
- The new trust doesn't add new beneficiaries (but can remove them)
- The decanting doesn't violate the rule against perpetuities

Nevada is particularly favorable for cryptocurrency trusts due to its strong asset protection statutes and no state income tax.

## Tax Consequences of Decanting

### Grantor Trust Status Preservation

If the original trust is a grantor trust (IRC §671-679), decanting generally preserves this status if:

- The same grantor remains treated as owner
- No taxable exchange occurs under IRC §1001
- The new trust instrument maintains grantor trust powers (e.g., power to substitute assets)

**Critical:** File a new Form 1041 for the decanted trust with a separate EIN, but no gain/loss recognition should occur if structured properly.

### Basis Carryover Rules

Under IRC §1015, beneficiaries receiving distributed cryptocurrency take a **carryover basis** from the trust, not fair market value at distribution. This means:

- Appreciated crypto distributed to beneficiaries retains the trust's original purchase basis
- Beneficiaries inherit the trust's holding period (important for long-term capital gains rates)
- Losses are preserved but subject to related-party loss disallowance rules if distributed to grantor or spouse

### Generation-Skipping Transfer (GST) Tax

Decanting can reset or preserve GST exemption allocation:

- **Original trust has GST exemption allocated:** Decanted trust retains exempt status if no new beneficiaries are added
- **No GST exemption previously allocated:** Decanting creates opportunity to allocate current exemption ($13.61M per person in 2026)
- **Caution:** Adding new beneficiaries or extending trust term may trigger GST tax at the highest federal rate (40%)

## Sequencing Strategies for Multi-Beneficiary Trusts

When distributing cryptocurrency across multiple beneficiaries:

### 1. **High-Basis Assets First**

Distribute cryptocurrency with the highest cost basis to minimize built-in gains that beneficiaries will eventually recognize upon sale.

### 2. **Coordinate with Beneficiary Tax Brackets**

Time distributions to beneficiaries in lower income tax brackets (e.g., college students, retired parents) who can sell at 0% or 15% long-term capital gains rates rather than the trust's compressed 20% rate (kicked in at ~$15,200 taxable income in 2026).

### 3. **Retain Appreciated Assets for Step-Up**

If the grantor is elderly or has health concerns, retain highly appreciated cryptocurrency in a grantor trust to receive a **step-up in basis at death** under IRC §1014, eliminating capital gains tax entirely for heirs.

### 4. **Stagger Distributions Across Tax Years**

Avoid dumping all distributions into a single tax year. Spread distributions over 2-3 years to prevent beneficiaries from being pushed into higher tax brackets.

## Practical Implementation Checklist

1. **Review original trust instrument** for decanting authority or amendment powers
2. **Obtain certified copies** of trust agreements and all amendments
3. **Prepare decanting affidavit** documenting trustee's authority and intent
4. **Draft new trust instrument** with updated terms (jurisdiction, trustee powers, distribution standards)
5. **Provide statutory notice** to all qualified beneficiaries (timing varies by state)
6. **Transfer cryptocurrency** via wallet-to-wallet transfer with detailed transaction records
7. **Update custody documentation** (multi-sig signers, hardware wallet storage locations)
8. **File Form 1041** for both old and new trusts with explanatory statements
9. **Notify exchanges** of trust ownership change if assets are held on platforms
10. **Document basis information** for each cryptocurrency position transferred

## Red Flags to Avoid

- **Decanting to add beneficiaries** not in original trust (triggers gift tax)
- **Decanting without notice** when state statute requires it (voidable by beneficiaries)
- **Mixing grantor and non-grantor trust assets** in the same decanting transaction
- **Failing to update beneficiary designations** on related accounts (insurance, retirement)
- **Ignoring state-specific perpetuity rules** when extending trust duration

---

## Bottom Line for Coaches and Advisors

Cryptocurrency trust decanting is a powerful tool for adapting to changing circumstances, but it requires careful coordination between estate attorneys, tax advisors, and custody specialists. For coaches working with high-net-worth clients holding digital assets, understanding when decanting makes sense—and when full termination or no action is preferable—is essential for competent guidance.

**Key question to ask clients:** *"Has your trust's jurisdiction, trustee structure, or distribution terms become misaligned with your current goals or the evolving cryptocurrency landscape?"* If yes, decanting may be worth exploring.
