# IRC Section 734(b) Basis Adjustments and PTET Distribution Interactions 2026

**Research Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

The interaction between Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) elections and IRC Section 734(b) basis adjustments represents an emerging complexity in partnership taxation. While PTET has received substantial attention for SALT cap workaround benefits, its impact on optional basis adjustments under Section 734(b) remains underexplored in current guidance. This research identifies key coordination challenges and practical planning considerations for partnerships holding appreciated assets while making PTET elections.

---

## Section 734(b) Fundamentals

Section 734(b) permits partnerships to adjust the basis of undistributed partnership property when:

1. **A Section 754 election is in effect**, and
2. **A distribution triggers gain/loss recognition** under Section 731, **or**
3. **The partnership's basis in distributed property differs from the partner's outside basis** (substantial basis reduction scenario)

The adjustment amount equals the gain/loss recognized by the distributee partner or the difference between the partner's outside basis and the partnership's inside basis in distributed assets.

---

## PTET's Impact on Basis Calculations

### The Core Tension

PTET creates a potential mismatch in basis tracking:

- **Federal Level:** PTET payments are generally deductible by the partnership, reducing federal taxable income that flows through to partners
- **Partner Level:** Partners typically receive a corresponding tax credit on their resident state returns
- **Basis Effect:** The deduction reduces partner outside basis under Section 705(a)(2)(B) as a distribution of money

**Critical Question:** Does the PTET deduction reduce the amount of Section 734(b) basis adjustment available when distributions occur?

### Current Understanding (2026)

Based on available guidance and practitioner analysis:

1. **PTET Reduces Outside Basis First:** When a partnership pays PTET, partners' outside bases decrease proportionally to their share of the deduction. This occurs regardless of whether the partner ultimately claims the credit.

2. **Section 734(b) Calculation Uses Post-PTET Basis:** The basis adjustment under Section 734(b) is calculated using the partner's outside basis *after* accounting for PTET-related deductions. This means PTET payments effectively reduce the potential Section 734(b) adjustment amount.

3. **No Separate PTET Add-Back:** There is no authority suggesting PTET should be added back to outside basis for Section 734(b) purposes, even though the partner receives a state tax credit.

## Practical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Appreciated Real Estate Partnership

**Facts:**
- Partnership holds property with $500,000 inside basis, $2M FMV
- Partner A has $600,000 outside basis
- Partnership makes $50,000 PTET payment (deductible)
- Partner A's outside basis drops to $575,000 (assuming 50% share)
- Partnership distributes $100,000 cash to Partner A (no gain recognized)

**Section 734(b) Impact:**
- Without PTET: Outside basis would be $600,000; distribution reduces to $500,000
- With PTET: Outside basis is $575,000; distribution reduces to $475,000
- **Result:** The Section 734(b) adjustment (if triggered) would be $25,000 smaller due to PTET

### Scenario 2: Transferee Partner Mid-Year Purchase

**Facts:**
- Partner B purchases 50% interest for $1M when inside basis of assets is $800,000
- Partnership has Section 754 election in effect
- PTET payment of $40,000 made before year-end
- Partnership distributes property with $200,000 inside basis to Partner B

**Coordination Challenge:**
- Partner B's outside basis reflects PTET deduction share ($20,000 reduction)
- Section 734(b) adjustment must account for post-PTET basis
- **Timing Mismatch:** If PTET is paid in Year 1 but distribution occurs in Year 2, which year's basis controls?

**Best Practice:** Track PTET payments and basis adjustments on a contemporaneous basis. Don't wait until K-1 issuance to adjust basis for Section 734(b) calculations.

## State-Specific Variations

### California

- PTET is mandatory for electing partnerships (no opt-out once elected)
- Basis adjustments follow federal treatment
- **Documentation:** California requires separate Schedule K-1 reporting for PTET payments

### New York

- PTET is optional annually
- NY allows basis adjustment tracking consistent with federal
- **Key Difference:** NY PTET credit is refundable, which may create additional book-tax disparities

### New Jersey

- PTET is mandatory once elected (revocation requires consent)
- NJ specifically addresses basis reduction for PTET payments
- **Documentation:** NJ requires detailed PTET tracking on Form NJ-K-1

## Planning Recommendations

### 1. Coordinate PTET with Section 754 Elections

If your partnership holds appreciated assets and has a Section 754 election (or is considering one):

- **Model PTET impact** on potential Section 734(b) adjustments before electing PTET
- **Consider timing:** Accelerate or defer PTET payments relative to planned distributions
- **Document assumptions:** Keep contemporaneous records of basis calculations

### 2. Track Inside/Outside Basis Disparities

PTET can widen the gap between inside basis (partnership assets) and outside basis (partner interests):

- Maintain **separate ledgers** for federal basis, state basis, and PTET-adjusted basis
- Reconcile disparities **quarterly**, not just at year-end
- Flag situations where PTET creates or exacerbates negative capital accounts

### 3. Transferee Partner Protections

For partnerships with purchased interests:

- **Disclose PTET impact** in purchase agreements
- Consider **indemnification provisions** if PTET reduces expected basis adjustments
- Provide **estimated PTET allocations** to transferees before year-end

### 4. Audit Defense Documentation

Given the lack of explicit IRS guidance on PTET + Section 734(b) interactions:

- **Document methodology** for basis adjustments
- Retain **state PTET election forms** and payment confirmations
- Prepare **workpapers** showing PTET's effect on Section 734(b) calculations
- Consider **disclosure statements** if positions are aggressive

## Unresolved Questions (2026)

The following areas lack clear guidance:

1. **Credit vs. Deduction Mismatch:** If a partner cannot use the PTET credit (e.g., non-resident of PTET state), should basis still be reduced?

2. **Carryforward Credits:** When PTET credits are carried forward rather than claimed currently, does basis reduction still occur in the payment year?

3. **Tiered Partnerships:** How does PTET at the lower-tier affect Section 734(b) calculations at the upper-tier?

4. **Section 704(c) Interactions:** When PTET affects partners differently due to built-in gains, how should Section 734(b) adjustments be allocated?

---

## Conclusion

PTET elections reduce partner outside basis, which in turn reduces potential Section 734(b) basis adjustments on distributions. Partnerships holding appreciated assets should model this interaction before electing PTET, maintain detailed basis tracking, and document methodologies for audit defense. Until IRS guidance clarifies these interactions, conservative basis tracking and contemporaneous documentation are essential.
