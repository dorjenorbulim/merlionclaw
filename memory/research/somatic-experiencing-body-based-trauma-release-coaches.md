# Somatic Experiencing and Body-Based Trauma Release for Coaches

**Research Date:** April 4, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

Somatic approaches to trauma release are rapidly gaining traction in the coaching industry as practitioners recognize that cognitive-only interventions often fail to address stored trauma in the nervous system. This research examines three evidence-based modalities—Somatic Experiencing (SE), Tension & Trauma Releasing Exercises (TRE), and Polyvagal Theory-informed practices—that coaches can integrate to help clients release stored trauma and regulate their autonomic nervous systems.

---

## Key Modalities Overview

### 1. Somatic Experiencing (SE)

**Developer:** Dr. Peter Levine (1970s)  
**Core Premise:** Trauma is stored in the body as incomplete defensive responses. SE helps clients complete these frozen survival responses through gentle bodily awareness.

**What Coaches Learn:**
- Tracking bodily sensations without overwhelm
- Titration: working with small doses of activation
- Pendulation: moving between resourced and activated states
- Understanding the three survival states: mobilization (fight/flight), immobilization (freeze), and social engagement

**Training Path:** Multiple somatic coaching certifications now incorporate SE principles, including Pause Breathwork's Somatic Coaching Certification and Body-Based Breakthrough's 12-week program. Full SEP (Somatic Experiencing Practitioner) training requires 3+ years and is licensed for therapists, but coaches can learn SE-informed techniques through shorter certifications.

**Best For:** Clients with chronic stress, anxiety, or who feel "stuck" despite years of talk therapy.

---

### 2. Tension & Trauma Releasing Exercises (TRE)

**Developer:** Dr. David Berceli  
**Core Premise:** The body has a natural tremor mechanism that releases deep muscular tension and trauma. TRE activates this through simple exercises.

**What Coaches Learn:**
- Seven exercises to induce therapeutic tremors
- How to safely guide clients through the shaking process
- Understanding the psoas muscle's role in trauma storage
- Session structure: activation, tremoring, integration

**Training Path:** TRE Global offers a structured certification pathway:
- Module 1: Public workshop (2 days) — learn the exercises for personal use
- Modules 2-4: Progressive training for working with others
- Full certification: ~6-12 months depending on pace

**Cost:** Module 1 typically USD $400-600; full certification USD $3,000-5,000  
**Next Training:** Multiple 2026 cohorts available (e.g., Yoga Calm, March 7-8, 2026; Red Beard TRE, October 2025 start)

**Best For:** High-stress professionals, first responders, athletes, or anyone with physical tension patterns. TRE is highly scalable and can be taught in group settings.

---

### 3. Polyvagal Theory Applications

**Developer:** Dr. Stephen Porges (1994)  
**Core Premise:** The autonomic nervous system has three hierarchical states that determine our sense of safety and social connection.

**Three States:**
1. **Ventral Vagal (Safe & Social):** Calm, connected, creative, collaborative
2. **Sympathetic (Mobilized):** Fight/flight, anxious, angry, reactive
3. **Dorsal Vagal (Immobilized):** Freeze, shut down, depressed, disconnected

**What Coaches Learn:**
- Helping clients identify their current nervous system state
- "Glimmers": micro-moments of ventral vagal activation
- Co-regulation techniques (using your own regulated state to help clients)
- Practices to shift from sympathetic/dorsal states back to ventral vagal

**Training Path:** 
- Unyte Integrated Listening offers on-demand webinars
- Positive Success Group published new coaching implications (September 2025)
- Many somatic coaching certifications now integrate polyvagal theory as core curriculum

**Best For:** All coaching contexts. Polyvagal theory provides a universal language for understanding client states and selecting appropriate interventions.

## Practical Integration for Coaches

### Quick Techniques You Can Use Immediately

**1. State Check-In (2 minutes)**
Before diving into content, ask: "Where are you right now—calm and connected, revved up, or shut down?" Normalize all states and adjust your approach accordingly.

**2. Grounding Through Feet (1 minute)**
Have clients feel their feet on the floor, notice pressure points, and imagine roots extending downward. This activates the ventral vagal system.

**3. Orienting Practice (30 seconds)**
Invite clients to slowly look around the room, noticing colors, shapes, and objects. This signals safety to the nervous system.

**4. Breath as Anchor (ongoing)**
Teach clients to notice their breath without changing it. This builds interoceptive awareness without overwhelm.

### When to Refer Out

Coaches should refer to licensed therapists when:
- Clients disclose significant trauma history (abuse, assault, combat)
- Dissociation or severe destabilization occurs during sessions
- Clients are actively suicidal or self-harming
- Complex PTSD symptoms emerge

**Best Practice:** Build relationships with 2-3 trauma-informed therapists in your network for warm handoffs.

## Ethical Considerations

1. **Scope of Practice:** Coaches are not therapists. Frame work as "nervous system regulation" and "stress release," not "trauma treatment."

2. **Informed Consent:** Explain what somatic work involves (bodily sensations, possible emotional release) before beginning.

3. **Resourcing First:** Always establish safety and coping skills before exploring activation.

4. **Cultural Sensitivity:** Some cultures view bodily expression differently. Adapt language and techniques accordingly.
