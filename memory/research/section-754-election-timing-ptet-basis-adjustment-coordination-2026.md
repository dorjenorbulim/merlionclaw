# Section 754 Election Timing and PTET Basis Adjustment Coordination 2026

**Research Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

The intersection of Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) elections and IRC Section 754 basis adjustments creates complex coordination challenges for partnerships holding appreciated assets. This research examines strategic timing considerations, basis tracking implications, and compliance requirements when both elections are active.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Section 754 Election Timing and Mechanics

**Deadline:** A Section 754 election must be made by filing a written statement with the partnership's timely-filed Form 1065 (including extensions) for the tax year in which the distribution or transfer occurs. The election is made at the partnership level and, once made, applies to all subsequent transfers and distributions unless revoked with IRS approval.

**Revocation:** Revoking a Section 754 election requires filing Form 15254 with the IRS National Office. The IRS grants revocation requests only when there is a substantial change in circumstances (e.g., significant shift in partnership business, change in partnership composition, or administrative burden that outweighs benefits). Once revoked, the partnership cannot re-elect Section 754 for 5 years without IRS consent.

**Practical Implication:** Partnerships should make the Section 754 election decision before the first PTET election, as PTET payments affect partner outside basis under Section 705, which in turn impacts Section 743(b) and Section 734(b) calculations.

### 2. PTET's Impact on Partner Outside Basis (Section 705)

**Basis Reduction Mechanism:** When a partnership pays PTET at the entity level, partners' outside basis in their partnership interests is reduced under Section 705(a)(2)(B) as a nondeductible expenditure. This occurs regardless of whether the partner claims the PTET credit on their resident state return or carries it forward.

**Timing Mismatch:** The basis reduction occurs in the tax year the PTET is paid, but partners may not receive Schedule K-1 reporting the PTET payment until the following year. This creates potential for partners to underestimate basis reductions when calculating gain/loss on partnership interest sales or distributions.

**Key Coordination Point:** When a Section 754 election is active, the partnership must track:
- Partner outside basis reductions from PTET payments (Section 705)
- Section 743(b) adjustments for transferee partners (purchase of partnership interest)
- Section 734(b) adjustments for partnership distributions

### 3. Section 743(b) Adjustments for Transferee Partners

**Calculation Framework:** When a partnership interest is transferred and a Section 754 election is in place, the transferee partner receives a Section 743(b) basis adjustment equal to:

```
Section 743(b) Adjustment = Purchase Price - Transferee's Share of Inside Basis
```

**PTET Complication:** PTET payments reduce the partnership's inside basis in its assets (via Section 705 outside basis reduction, which flows through to inside basis tracking). This means:

- Transferee partners purchasing interests in PTET-electing partnerships may receive smaller Section 743(b) positive adjustments (or larger negative adjustments) than expected
- The timing of PTET payments relative to the transfer date affects the calculation
- State-specific PTET rules create disparities: some states (CA, NY, NJ) have different PTET basis treatment, requiring separate Section 743(b) tracking per state

**2025-2026 Regulatory Context:** Final regulations issued in early 2025 tightened rules on partnership basis-shifting transactions, particularly for related-party transfers. Partnerships with PTET elections should document Section 743(b) calculations separately for federal and state purposes to avoid audit exposure.

### 4. Section 734(b) Adjustments and Partnership Distributions

**Trigger Events:** Section 734(b) adjustments occur when a partnership makes a distribution to a partner and there is:
- A Section 754 election in place, AND
- A substantial basis reduction (or increase) resulting from the distribution

**PTET Interaction:** When PTET has reduced partner outside basis prior to a distribution:
- The partnership must determine whether the distribution creates a Section 734(b) adjustment based on post-PTET basis
- If PTET was paid in Year 1 but the distribution occurs in Year 2, the partnership must track the timing mismatch
- Liquidating distributions are particularly complex: PTET basis reductions may trigger larger Section 734(b) adjustments than would otherwise occur

**Documentation Requirement:** Partnerships should maintain separate Section 734(b) tracking schedules showing:
- Pre-PTET and post-PTET inside basis in partnership assets
- Partner outside basis before and after PTET payments
- Section 734(b) adjustment calculations for each distribution

### 5. State-Specific Coordination Challenges

**Non-Uniform Treatment:** PTET regimes vary significantly by state:

| State | PTET Basis Treatment | Section 754 Recognition |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------|
| California | Reduces outside basis | Conforms to federal |
| New York | Reduces outside basis | Conforms to federal |
| New Jersey | Reduces outside basis | Partial conformity |
| Massachusetts | Credit-based (no basis reduction) | Conforms to federal |

**Practical Impact:** Partnerships operating in multiple PTET states must:
- Maintain separate basis books for each state
- Calculate state-specific Section 743(b) and Section 734(b) adjustments
- Track PTET credit carryforwards separately from basis adjustments

### 6. Strategic Planning Considerations

**Before Making PTET Election:**
1. Evaluate whether Section 754 election is already in place
2. Model basis reduction impact on anticipated transfers/distributions
3. Consider accelerating Section 754 election if partnership expects partner turnover

**After PTET Election:**
1. Update partnership tax basis tracking systems immediately
2. Communicate basis reductions to partners before they sell interests
3. Document PTET payment timing relative to transfer/distribution dates

**For Transferee Partners:**
1. Request PTET payment history before purchasing partnership interest
2. Negotiate purchase price adjustments for PTET-induced basis reductions
3. Require indemnification for state-specific PTET basis disparities

---

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] File Section 754 election statement with Form 1065 (if not already elected)
- [ ] Track PTET payments separately for basis reduction calculations
- [ ] Maintain state-specific basis books for multi-state PTET partnerships
- [ ] Document Section 743(b) calculations for all partner interest transfers
- [ ] Document Section 734(b) calculations for all partnership distributions
- [ ] Communicate basis adjustments to affected partners before year-end
- [ ] Review partnership agreement for PTET allocation provisions

---

## Audit Defense Strategies

1. ** contemporaneous documentation:** Maintain written records of PTET payment dates, amounts, and basis reduction calculations
2. **Separate state books:** Keep federal and state basis tracking separate to avoid commingling errors
3. **Partner communications:** Document all basis-related communications with partners (especially transferees)
4. **Consistency:** Apply PTET basis reduction methodology consistently across all partners and tax years

---

## Conclusion

The coordination of Section 754 elections and PTET basis adjustments requires proactive planning, robust tracking systems, and clear partner communication. Partnerships holding appreciated assets should model the combined tax impact of both elections before making PTET elections, and should maintain detailed documentation to support basis adjustment calculations in the event of an IRS or state audit.

The key insight: **PTET is not just a state tax election—it's a basis event that cascades through federal partnership tax calculations.** Treating it as such prevents costly errors and audit exposure.
