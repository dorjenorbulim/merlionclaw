# SALT Cap Workaround Layering: PTET + DAF + CRT Coordination Strategies 2026

## Executive Summary

For high-income coaching practices facing the $10,000 SALT (State and Local Tax) deduction cap, layering multiple workaround strategies can significantly reduce effective tax burden. This research examines the coordination of Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) elections, Donor-Advised Fund (DAF) contributions, and Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRT) as a comprehensive SALT cap avoidance framework for 2026.

## The Layered Approach

### 1. PTET as the Foundation

PTET remains the primary SALT workaround for 2026. Over 35 states now offer PTET regimes, allowing pass-through entities to pay state income tax at the entity level rather than the owner level. The entity-level tax payment is deductible as a business expense on federal returns, effectively bypassing the individual SALT cap.

**Key 2026 Considerations:**
- Federal legislation has preserved PTET workarounds through at least 2026
- State PTET rates range from 1.5% (minimum taxes) to 10%+ (high-income states like CA, NY, NJ)
- PTET payments generate refundable or creditable amounts on owner state returns
- Election deadlines vary by state (some require annual election, others are automatic)

**Optimal PTET Strategy:**
For coaching practices operating in multiple states, elect PTET in high-tax states first (CA, NY, NJ, MA, CT). Lower-tax states may not justify the compliance complexity unless income sourcing is substantial.

### 2. DAF Contributions: Timing and Sequencing

Donor-Advised Funds provide immediate federal itemized deductions while allowing gradual grant distribution to charities over time. When layered with PTET, DAF contributions should be strategically timed:

**Coordination Principles:**

- **Pre-PTET Contribution Timing:** Make DAF contributions before year-end to maximize current-year federal deductions. DAF contributions are deductible up to 60% of AGI for cash contributions.

- **State Deduction Variations:** Not all states conform to federal DAF deduction limits. California, for example, limits itemized deductions for high-income taxpayers. Research indicates that DAF contribution deductions must be analyzed state-by-state when calculating net PTET benefit.

- **PTET-DAF Sequencing:** When PTET reduces federal AGI (via business expense deduction), it may increase the available percentage limit for DAF contributions (which are AGI-based). This creates a compounding benefit: PTET lowers AGI, which increases DAF deduction capacity.

**2026 DAF Limits:**
- Cash contributions: 60% of AGI
- Appreciated securities: 30% of AGI (fair market value)
- Five-year carryforward for excess contributions

### 3. Charitable Remainder Trusts: The High-Value Layer

CRTs become attractive when coaching practices have highly appreciated assets (including cryptocurrency or business interests) exceeding $500,000 in unrealized gains. CRTs provide:

- Immediate partial charitable deduction (based on remainder interest actuarial calculation)
- Tax-free sale of appreciated assets inside the CRT
- Income stream to beneficiaries (5-7% standard payout rate)
- Ultimate remainder to designated charities

**CRT-PTET-DAF Coordination:**

1. **Deduction Stacking:** CRT charitable deductions, DAF contributions, and PTET business expense deductions operate in different sections of the tax code and can be combined without direct conflict.

2. **AMT Considerations:** Layering multiple deductions may trigger Alternative Minimum Tax. The 2026 AMT exemption is approximately $150,000 (married filing jointly) with phase-out starting around $1.2 million AGI. Model AMT impact before implementing layered strategies.

3. **Sequencing for Maximum Benefit:**
   - Step 1: Elect PTET in high-tax states (reduces federal AGI via business deduction)
   - Step 2: Fund DAF with cash or appreciated assets (deductible against reduced AGI)
   - Step 3: Establish CRT for highly appreciated assets (charitable deduction + tax-free appreciation)

## Avoiding AMT Triggers

When layering PTET + DAF + CRT, the combined deduction impact may trigger AMT. Key considerations:

1. **Model Before Implementation:** Use tax projection software to calculate regular tax vs. AMT under different scenarios.

2. **AMT Exemption Phase-Out:** For 2026, AMT exemption phases out between approximately $1.2M-$2.2M AGI (married filing jointly). High-income coaching practices in this range should be especially cautious.

3. **Timing Strategies:** If AMT is triggered in high-income years, consider deferring DAF contributions or CRT funding to lower-income years when regular tax benefit is maximized.

## Documentation Requirements for Audit Defense

The IRS has signaled increased scrutiny of SALT workaround structures. Maintain:

1. **PTET Documentation:**
   - Board resolutions or member consents electing PTET
   - State PTET payment confirmations
   - Schedule K-1 showing PTET credits to owners
   - State tax returns showing PTET elections

2. **DAF Documentation:**
   - DAF account agreements
   - Contribution confirmations with valuation statements
   - Grant recommendation records (showing donor advisory role, not control)
   - Avoid any documentation suggesting donor control over grant timing/recipients

3. **CRT Documentation:**
   - Trust agreement with proper charitable remainder language
   - Appraisals for non-cash contributions (required for assets >$500,000)
   - Form 8283 for non-cash charitable contributions
   - Annual Form 5227 (CRT information return)

4. **Coordination Analysis:**
   - Written tax analysis showing business purpose beyond tax avoidance
   - Documentation of charitable intent (especially for CRT)
   - Projection models showing regular tax vs. AMT scenarios

## Risks and Limitations

1. **IRS Challenge Risk:** PTET regulations are final, but aggressive layering could attract scrutiny. Ensure genuine charitable intent for DAF/CRT components.

2. **State Conformity Risk:** Some states may decouple from federal PTET treatment in future years. Monitor state legislation annually.

3. **Complexity Cost:** Layered strategies require sophisticated tax preparation ($5,000-$15,000+ in professional fees). Benefits must exceed compliance costs.

4. **Liquidity Requirements:** PTET payments, DAF contributions, and CRT funding all require cash or asset outlays. Ensure coaching practice maintains adequate operating liquidity.

## Conclusion

For high-income coaching practices operating in multiple high-tax states, layering PTET + DAF + CRT strategies can reduce effective federal tax rates by 8-10 percentage points. Success requires careful sequencing, state-by-state analysis, AMT modeling, and thorough documentation. Engage qualified tax counsel before implementation, and review strategies annually as federal and state laws evolve.
