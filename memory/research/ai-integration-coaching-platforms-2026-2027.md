## Ethical Framework & ICF Guidelines (2025-2026)

The **ICF Artificial Intelligence (AI) Coaching Framework and Standards** (released March 2025) provides the definitive ethical guidance:

### Core Ethical Principles

**1. Informed Consent**
- Clients must understand how AI tools function
- Clear disclosure of what data is collected and how it's used
- Explicit consent for session recording and AI analysis
- Option to opt-out without service penalty

**2. Data Privacy & Security**
- Enterprise-grade encryption for session data
- Clear data retention and deletion policies
- Compliance with GDPR, PDPA (Singapore), and regional regulations
- No training of public AI models on client session data without explicit consent

**3. AI as Assistant, Not Replacement**
- AI cannot replace coach judgment or presence
- Coaches remain responsible for all interpretations and interventions
- AI insights must be validated by human coaches before action
- Transparency about AI's role in the coaching relationship

**4. Bias & Fairness**
- Awareness of AI model limitations across cultures and contexts
- Regular auditing of AI recommendations for bias
- Human oversight for high-stakes interpretations

### ICF's "What's OK and What's Not" (October 2025)

**Acceptable Uses:**
- Session transcription for note-taking efficiency
- Pattern identification to support coach observation
- Administrative automation (scheduling, follow-ups)
- Progress visualization for clients and stakeholders

**Unacceptable Uses:**
- AI making coaching decisions without human oversight
- Using client data to train public AI models without consent
- Replacing human coaching with AI-only interactions (while claiming ICF credential)
- Concealing AI use from clients

## Practical Implementation for Coaches

### Getting Started Checklist

1. **Select a Platform**
   - Evaluate based on ICF alignment, security, and feature fit
   - Request demo with actual session scenarios
   - Check integration with existing tools (calendar, CRM, video)

2. **Create AI Use Policy**
   - Document what AI tools you use and why
   - Define data handling procedures
   - Prepare client consent forms

3. **Client Communication**
   - Discuss AI use during contracting phase
   - Provide written policy with clear opt-out options
   - Explain benefits (better notes, more presence, deeper insights)

4. **Start Small**
   - Begin with transcription only
   - Gradually add insight features as comfort grows
   - Regularly review AI outputs for accuracy

5. **Ongoing Ethics Check**
   - Quarterly review of AI tool practices
   - Stay updated on ICF guidance changes
   - Participate in coach communities discussing AI ethics

## Key Takeaways for Practice

1. **AI is now table stakes** for competitive coaching practices—clients expect efficiency and insight depth that AI enables.

2. **Ethics must lead implementation**—ICF's framework provides clear guardrails; follow them rigorously.

3. **Transparency builds trust**—clients appreciate knowing how AI enhances their coaching; hiding it damages the relationship.

4. **Start with transcription**—it's the highest-ROI, lowest-risk entry point for most coaches.

5. **Stay human-centered**—AI amplifies great coaching; it doesn't create it. The coach's presence, intuition, and wisdom remain irreplaceable.
