# Section 734(b) and 743(b) Basis Adjustments with PTET Elections: Coordination Rules 2026

**Research Date:** April 7, 2026  
**Word Count:** ~650 words

---

## Executive Summary

Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) elections create significant complexity when partnerships have made Section 754 elections, triggering potential basis adjustments under IRC Sections 734(b) (distributions) and 743(b) (transfers of partnership interests). This research identifies critical coordination gaps, timing mismatches, and state-specific treatment variations that practitioners must navigate in 2026.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Inside Basis vs. Outside Basis Treatment

**Inside basis** (partnership's basis in its assets) and **outside basis** (partner's basis in partnership interest) diverge when PTET elections interact with Section 754 adjustments:

- **PTET payments reduce outside basis** when treated as deemed distributions to partners (per IRC Section 705(a)(2)(B))
- **Inside basis treatment varies by state**: Some states (NY, NJ) treat PTET as a partnership-level tax that reduces inside basis before Section 734(b)/743(b) calculations; others (CA) allow partners to elect whether PTET reduces inside or outside basis
- **Critical gap**: No definitive IRS guidance exists on whether PTET payments should be treated as Section 704(c) remedial items that trigger basis adjustments

### 2. Section 734(b) Distribution Adjustments

When partnerships make distributions after PTET elections:

- **Timing mismatch risk**: PTET paid in Year 1 may reduce distributable cash, but basis adjustments under Section 734(b) are calculated at distribution time in Year 2+
- **Adjustment reduction**: PTET payments that reduce inside basis decrease the amount of Section 734(b) adjustment available on property distributions
- **Documentation requirement**: Partnerships must track PTET payments separately from other tax payments to calculate accurate basis adjustments

**Practical example**: A partnership with $1M inside basis in appreciated real estate pays $50K PTET in 2025. In 2026, it distributes property with $200K built-in gain. The Section 734(b) adjustment is reduced by the $50K PTET payment if inside basis was reduced, creating a $50K mismatch between partner expectations and actual tax outcomes.

### 3. Section 743(b) Transfer Adjustments

When partnership interests are transferred (sale, gift, death):

- **Transferee partner impact**: Purchasers of partnership interests in PTET-electing partnerships receive reduced Section 743(b) adjustments if PTET has already reduced inside basis
- **Mid-year transfer complexity**: If PTET is paid after the transfer date but before year-end, allocation between transferor and transferee requires special tracking
- **State-specific variations**:
  - **New York**: PTET reduces inside basis before Section 743(b) calculations
  - **California**: Partners can elect to treat PTET as reducing outside basis only (preserving inside basis for Section 743(b))
  - **New Jersey**: Follows federal treatment but requires separate state-level basis tracking

### 4. Section 754 Election Coordination

**Strategic timing considerations**:

- **Make Section 754 election BEFORE PTET election** if partnership expects significant distributions or interest transfers (preserves maximum basis adjustment potential)
- **Delay PTET election** if partnership is close to Section 754 deadline and basis adjustments are material
- **Dual election documentation**: Partnerships must maintain separate schedules for:
  - Federal basis tracking (pre-PTET)
  - State basis tracking (post-PTET)
  - Section 743(b) adjustment schedules for each transferee partner

### 5. Audit Defense and Documentation

**Required documentation for PTET + Section 754 coordination**:

1. **Basis tracking worksheets** showing inside/outside basis before and after PTET payments
2. **Section 743(b) allocation schedules** for each transferee partner with PTET impact noted
3. **State election records** documenting which partners elected PTET treatment in non-conforming states
4. **K-1 disclosure statements** explaining PTET impact on basis adjustments (currently no standardized Box code exists—use Box 13 or attach statement)

**Red flags for IRS audit**:
- Large discrepancies between inside and outside basis without clear Section 754 documentation
- PTET payments not reflected in partner capital account tracking
- Inconsistent treatment across multiple states for same partnership

## Practical Recommendations

### For Tax Preparers

1. **Request PTET election status** before preparing any partnership return with Section 754 implications
2. **Calculate basis adjustments twice**: once assuming PTET reduces inside basis, once assuming it reduces outside basis; compare outcomes and document choice
3. **Use Form 7217** (Partner's Report of Property Distributed by a Partnership) to document PTET impact on distributed property basis

### For Partnership Agreements

1. **Add PTET-specific language** to Section 754 election provisions addressing basis adjustment coordination
2. **Require partner consent** for PTET elections when Section 754 adjustments are material (>5% of inside basis)
3. **Specify allocation methodology** for PTET-induced basis disparities in partnership accounting policies

### For Transferee Partners

1. **Request PTET payment history** before purchasing partnership interests
2. **Model Section 743(b) adjustment scenarios** with and without PTET impact
3. **Negotiate purchase price adjustments** if PTET elections reduce expected basis step-up

## Unresolved Questions (2026)

- **IRS guidance gap**: No revenue ruling or notice addresses PTET + Section 754 coordination directly
- **State conformity uncertainty**: Several states (IL, MA, WI) have not clarified whether PTET affects basis adjustments
- **Remedial allocation interaction**: Unclear whether PTET creates Section 704(c) disparities requiring remedial allocation methods
