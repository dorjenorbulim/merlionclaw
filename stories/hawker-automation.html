Automating Hawker Order Taking with OpenClaw

How a Chinatown hawker reduced order errors by 90% and saved 2 hours daily

Anonymous • Bedok March 2026 5 min read

The Problem

Running a hawker stall in Singapore is no joke. The heat, the rush, the constant stream of customers — and then there are the WhatsApp orders. Every day, I'd receive 50-80 messages from regulars asking for:

  • "Uncle, tomorrow 12pm want chicken rice 2 packet"
  • "Add extra chilli, no cucumber"
  • "My office 5 people, different orders leh"

I was spending 2-3 hours every evening manually writing down orders, checking payment screenshots, and preparing ingredient lists. Errors happened. Customers got upset. I got stressed.

The Solution

With OpenClaw, I built a simple workflow that handles the entire process:

1

Customer sends WhatsApp order

↓
2

OpenClaw parses message, extracts items

↓
3

Auto-generates order confirmation with total

↓
4>

Creates daily prep list sorted by pickup time

The Tools

📱 WhatsApp Business API

For receiving and sending messages

🤖 OpenClaw

Natural language processing, order extraction

📊 Google Sheets

Order tracking and prep list generation

🔔 Pushover

Notifications for new orders

The Impact

90% Reduction in order errors
2 hrs Saved daily
150+ Regular customers

Ethical Considerations

✅ What I Did Right

  • Transparency: Customers know it's automated — I tell them "Uncle got robot helper now"
  • Human oversight: I review every order before confirming
  • Data privacy: No customer data stored beyond what's needed for orders
  • No deception: Customers still talk to me when they collect — the personal touch remains

The Code

Here's a simplified version of my OpenClaw skill:

# Hawker Order Parser Skill
# Parses WhatsApp messages into structured orders

name: hawker-order-parser
description: Parse hawker orders from WhatsApp messages

---

When I receive a WhatsApp message like:
"Uncle tomorrow 12pm want chicken rice 2 packet extra chilli no cucumber"

Extract:
- Date: tomorrow
- Time: 12pm  
- Items: [
    { name: "chicken rice", qty: 2, modifiers: ["extra chilli", "no cucumber"] }
  ]

Then:
1. Calculate total: 2 × $4.50 = $9.00
2. Generate confirmation message in Singlish:
   "OK uncle! 2 chicken rice ($9) pickup 12pm. Extra chilli no cucumber. PayNow 9123 4567"
3. Add to Google Sheet with timestamp

Always confirm unusual orders with me first.

Want to adapt this for your business?

This approach works for any WhatsApp-based ordering: home bakers, tuition teachers, personal shoppers, etc.
