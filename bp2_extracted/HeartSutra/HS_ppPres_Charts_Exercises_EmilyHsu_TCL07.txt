snying po
Bhagavati (Tib. bcom ldan ‘das ma)  – female Buddha or 
Blessed Mother. In this context, the meaning is the realization of 
emptiness in the continuum of a Buddha. 
Tibetan
bcom – destroyer, to overcome or conquer
ldan – to possess
‘das – to transcend or go beyond
ma – mother
5Heart Sutra – Title of the text
Sanskrit: Bhagavati prajnaparamita  hridaya 
Tibetan: bCom ldan ‘das ma shes rab khi pha rol tu phyin par’i  
snying po
prajna (Tib. shes rab)  – wisdom
paramita (Tib. pha rol tu phyin pa)  – 
perfection, or literally gone to the other shore
hridaya (Tib. snying po)  – heart or essence
6Prajnaparamita (or Perfection of Wisdom)
Four nominal types of perfection of wisdom
1. resultant perfection of wisdom (the actual 
perfection of wisdom)
2. path perfection of wisdom
3. scriptural (or textual) perfection of wisdom
4. natural perfection of wisdom

Vast – 100,000 stanzas

All of the meanings of the vast, intermediate, 
and brief mother sutras (perfection of wisdom

16Heart Sutra – structure of the text
1. Prologue
Thus did I hear at one time. The Bhagavan was dwelling on Mass of Vultures Mountain in 
Rajagriha together with a great community of monks and a great community of bodhisattvas. 
At that time, the Bhagavan was absorbed in the concentration on the categories of phenomena called 
“Profound Perception.” Also, at that time, the bodhisattva mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara looked 
upon the very practice of the profound perfection of wisdom and beheld those five aggregates also as 
empty of inherent nature. 
2. Actual sutra
Then, through the power of Buddha, the venerable Shariputra said this to the bodhisattva 
mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara: “How should any son of the lineage train who wishes to practice 
the activity of the profound perfection of wisdom?”
He said that and the bodhisattva mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara said this to the venerable 
Sharadvatiputra. “Shariputra, any son of the lineage or daughter of the lineage who wishes to 
practice the activity of the profound perfection of wisdom should look upon it like this, correctly and 
repeatedly beholding those five aggregates also as empty of inherent nature....
Shariputra, the bodhisattva mahasattva should train in the profound perfection of wisdom like that.”
Then the Bhagavan arose from that concentration and commended the bodhisattva mahasattva arya 
Avalokiteshvara saying: “Well said, well said, son of the lineage, it is like that. It is like that; one 
should practice the profound perfection of wisdom just as you have indicated; even the tathagatas 
rejoice.” The Bhagavan having thus spoken, the venerable Sharadvatiputra, the bodhisattva 
mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara, those surrounding in their entirety along with the world of gods, 
humans, asuras, and gandharvas were overjoyed and highly praised that spoken by the Bhagavan.a

21Heart Sutra – Uncommon prologue
At that time, the Bhagavan was absorbed in 
the concentration on the categories of 
phenomena called “Profound Perception.”
categories of phenomena – aggregates, 
constituents, sources
profound – emptiness (object)
perception – wisdom realizing emptiness (subject)
22Heart Sutra – Uncommon prologue
Also, at that time, the bodhisattva mahasattva arya 
Avalokiteshvara looked upon the very practice of the 
profound perfection of wisdom and beheld those five 
aggregates also as empty of inherent nature.
bodhisattva  – one who has generated uncontrived bodhicitta
mahasattva  – bodhisattva who is on the bhumis/grounds
arya – being who has directly realized emptiness
Avalokiteshvara  – bodhisattva of compassion
perfection of wisdom  – emptiness  
also – the self as well as the five aggregates (and all 
phenomena) are empty of inherent nature.
23Heart Sutra – Uncommon prologue
Five aggregates  (in general vs. in the continuum of a person)
form – all objects of the senses (in general), or one’s body (for a person).
feeling – experience of pleasure, pain or neutrality
discrimination  – apprehends uncommon signs of an object; 
distinguishes between objects, good & bad, right & wrong, etc.
compositional factors  – all other mental factors and non-associated 
compositional factors
consciousness  – six main minds or primary consciousnesses (eye, ear, 
nose, tongue, body, and mental consciousnesses).
The five appropriated aggregates are the basis of imputation of the 
person

49Review of the five aggregates
Five aggregates  (in general vs. in the continuum of a person)
form – all objects of the senses (in general), or one’s body (re: a person).
feeling – experience of pleasure, pain or neutrality
discrimination  – apprehends uncommon signs of an object; 
distinguishes between objects, good & bad, right & wrong, etc.
compositional factors  – all other mental factors and non-associated 
compositional factors
consciousness  – six main minds or primary consciousnesses (eye, ear, 
nose, tongue, body, and mental consciousnesses).
The five appropriated aggregates are the basis of imputation of the 
person
50Self of persons
The self does not exist ultimately, truly, inherently, 
from its own side.
The self does exist conventionally, as merely 
imputed by the mind. The self is merely imputed in 
dependence on the aggregates.
Lama Tsongkhapa says that there are three ways to 
conceive of objects: 
conceiving objects to truly exist
conceiving objects to not truly exist, or
conceiving objects in neither of these two ways. (Neutral)
51Importance of identifying the object of 
negation

1. Prologue
Thus did I hear at one time. The Bhagavan was dwelling on Mass of Vultures Mountain in 
Rajagriha together with a great community of monks and a great community of bodhisattvas.

“Profound Perception.” Also, at that time, the bodhisattva mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara looked 
upon the very practice of the profound perfection of wisdom and beheld those five aggregates also as 
empty of inherent nature. 
2. Actual sutra

mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara: “How should any son of the lineage train who wishes to practice 
the activity of the profound perfection of wisdom?”

Sharadvatiputra. “Shariputra, any son of the lineage or daughter of the lineage who wishes to 
practice the activity of the profound perfection of wisdom should look upon it like this, correctly and 
repeatedly beholding those five aggregates also as empty of inherent nature....
Shariputra, the bodhisattva mahasattva should train in the profound perfection of wisdom like that.”

mahasattva arya Avalokiteshvara, those surrounding in their entirety along with the world of gods, 
humans, asuras, and gandharvas were overjoyed and highly praised that spoken by the Bhagavan.a

193Mantra
Therefore, the mantra of the perfection of wisdom, the mantra of great 
knowledge, the unsurpassed mantra,  the mantra equal to the 
unequaled, the mantra that thoroughly pacifies all suffering, should be known 
as truth since it is not false.  
mantra of great knowledge (path of preparation)
The realization of emptiness is the highest state of awareness because it is 
the awareness that directly perceives reality as it actually is.  (GND)
thoroughly understanding its meaning eliminates the three poisons of 
craving, hatred, and delusion. (HHDL).  
unsurpassed mantra  (path of seeing)
there is no more clear and accurate and subtle state of awareness, no more 
all-encompassing state of awareness. (GND)
there is no greater method than the perfection of wisdom for saving one from 
the extremes of cyclic existence and the isolated peace of individual nirvana. 
(HHDL) 
194Mantra
Therefore, the mantra of the perfection of wisdom, the mantra of great 
knowledge, the unsurpassed mantra , the mantra equal to the 
unequaled, the mantra that thoroughly pacifies all 
suffering, should be known as truth since it is not false.  
mantra equal to the unequaled (path of meditation)
there is nothing equal, nothing as powerful as this state of awareness 
and the mantra that embodies it . (GND) 
the Buddha’s enlightened state is unequalled, and, through the deepest 
realization of this mantra, one attains a state equal to that state. (HHDL)  
mantra that thoroughly pacifies all suffering  (path of no 
more learning)  –  practicing the perfection of wisdom quells manifest 
sufferings and also removes all propensities for future suffering. (HHDL)  
195Mantra
Therefore, the mantra of the perfection of wisdom, the 
mantra of great knowledge, the unsurpassed mantra , the 
mantra equal to the unequaled, the mantra that 
thoroughly pacifies all suffering,  should be known as 
truth since it is not false.  
should be known as truth since it is not false   
it is not false  because it sees things as they actually are, 
with no deception, no distortion and no falsity. 
should be known as truth  because this is the truth that 
liberates from all falsity, from all error, therefore it liberates 
from samsara. (GND)

Gate – “go”. Exhorts one to enter the path of accumulation
Gate – “go”. Exhorts one to enter the path of preparation .
Paragate  – “go beyond ”. Exhorts one to enter the path of seeing .
Parasamgate  – “go completely beyond ”. Exhorts one to enter the path 
of meditation .

198How that was admired by the Teacher
Then the Bhagavan arose from that concentration 
and commended the bodhisattva mahasattva arya 
Avalokiteshvara saying: “Well said, well said, son 
of the lineage, it is like that. It is like that; one 
should practice the profound perfection of wisdom 
just as you have indicated; even the tathagatas 
rejoice.”
199How that was admired by the Teacher
The Bhagavan having thus spoken, the venerable 
Sharadvatiputra, the bodhisattva mahasattva arya 
Avalokiteshvara, those surrounding in their 
entirety along with the world of gods, humans, 
asuras, and gandharvas were overjoyed and 
highly praised that spoken by the Bhagavan.

202Exercise for daily life

prideful, etc. try to remember that the three 
things are merely imputed by the mind and 
don’t exist from their own side:

three are constantly changing, impermanent.
