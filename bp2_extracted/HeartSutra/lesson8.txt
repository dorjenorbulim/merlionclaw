We have to think about the fourfold emptiness starting with “Form is empty.
Emptiness is form” and so forth.
What form essentially is, is the mere appearance to mind/consciousness.
Because form is a mere appearance to the consciousness, therefore it does
not exist by its own nature; it does not exist inherently.
Then the next one, “Emptiness is form”. Because form does not exist
inherently, it is empty of existing inherently. Therefore what form is, is
just a mere appearance to the consciousness. Therefore “Emptiness is form”.
Next, “Emptiness is not other than form”.  The very nature/entity of form,
of this appearance as form, is none other than emptiness – the emptiness of
existing inherently. Its nature as empty of existing inherently is not of a
separate nature from the form, the appearance as form.
While form is empty of existing inherently, it exists as form, the mere
appearance to the mind.
What form is, the entity of form is not something separate/different from
its emptiness.
Here form is used as the first illustration in this fourfold emptiness.  If
you substitute the form with the person or the “I” and then try to think
about the ultimate nature of the “I”, at the end of the day, the essence is
that while the “I” is empty of existing inherently, it functions. The “I”
exists and functions within it being empty of existing inherently.
The basis of designation of the “I” is the aggregates. So it is clear that
when you look for the “I” among the aggregates, you will not be able to
find the “I”.  At the end of the day, what the “I” is, is none other than
that which is merely imputed by a conventional valid cognizer in dependence
upon the aggregates.
If the “I” exist inherently, it should be found. Where do you look for the
“I”?
So when you look for the “I” on the aggregates (which is the basis of
designation of the “I”), is the “I” exists on the aggregates? Is the ‘I’
exists in the aggregates?  Is the “I” the aggregates? Is the ‘I’ any part
of the aggregates?
So you investigate. It should be very clear that when you look for such an
“I” on the aggregates, amongst the aggregates, such an “I” cannot be found.
Because it cannot be found, therefore “I” is empty.
For us, when we conceive of ourselves and when we think of us, how do we
exist? When we think of the “I”, how does it appear to us? It appears that
the “I” is the aggregates. Whether it is this particular aggregate or that
aggregate or whether it is the collection that put together or the body and
mind, we believe the “I” is that. There is this appearance, it is somewhere
isn’t it? Not only is there this appearance, we firmly believe it is the
case that the “I” exists in the way it appears to us.
How does it appear to us? Sometimes one with the aggregates, somewhere
among the aggregates, whether it is all from the parts or the collection of
the aggregates, we believe that is the “I”. This is how the “I” appears to
us. This is how we believe the “I” to be.
When we think of the self, this is how the “I” appears to us. We believe
that this is how the “I” exists, the feeling of the “I” is somewhere
inside, the collection of stuff. We have this appearance and this strong
belief. What we have to check is: this is what we believe, and this is how
the “I” appears. Is that exactly how the “I” exist? If it exists in the way
we believe it to exist, then it should be findable. So when we look for it,
it should be findable. Of course it has to be a detailed investigation, or
a detailed analysis. The thing is, supposedly, at the end of the analysis,
it is said that it cannot be found.
The “I” appears to us as one with the body and mind. Somewhere in this
composite of body and mind is the “I”. That is what we think, and that is
how it appears. That is what we believe. If it is there in this composite
of the body and mind, then it should be found. If you engage a surgeon to
cut and look for this “I”, if it is in this composite of body and mind, it
should be found.  In this collection of aggregates, you cut up individually
into parts, one cannot find the “I”. But it should be found if it existed
in the way we believe it to be.
With the detailed search for this “I”, if it is one with the body and mind,
it should be found. But if you look for it and yet you are not able to find
it, what does it show? What does it mean? It shows that the “I” does not
exist from the side of the basis of designation. The “I” does not exist
from the side of the body and mind. If “I” cannot be found, this shows that
“I” doesn’t exist from the side of the aggregates. If “I” doesn’t exist
from the side of the aggregates then that means what the “I” is none other
than that which is merely imputed in dependence on the aggregates (Ven
Gyurme: Not merely imputed on the aggregates).
This is what you can reflect on when you recite the phrase “Emptiness is
form” or “Emptiness is I”. So when you look for the “I”, if the “I” is
really existed in the way you believe, it should be inside the composite of
body and mind. Yet when you look for it, you will not able to find it. If
you cannot find the “I”, that shows clearly that the “I” does not exist
from the side of the aggregates. Although it does not exist from the side
of the aggregates, it doesn’t mean that the “I” doesn’t exist. The “I” does
exist.  At the end of the day, what “I” is, is a mere appearance of the
mind; that which is merely imputed in dependence on the aggregates. Whether
it is the “I” or the form, if you go back to the example of form, if you
look for the inherently existent form, you cannot find it. While you cannot
find the inherent existent form, it doesn’t mean that form does not exist.
Form exists. So what essentially is form? It is none other than that which
is mere appearance.  Form is a mere appearance to the mind. So that is
emptiness. That is what one can think of or reflect on when you recite the
phrase “Emptiness is form”.
“I” is empty of existing inherently, so “I” is in the nature of emptiness.
While it is empty, it exists. So form arises from emptiness.
Of course this is extremely challenging. It is not something that you can
just get it, but something you have to regularly reflect and think about.
So start with this fourfold emptiness. You can use different illustrations
such as “form”, “I” etc. You just try to understand by using this fourfold
emptiness. You can also try to understand on the basis of the analogies
like what we have talked about recently.

no compositional factors, no consciousness”- here it is talking about the
emptiness of inherent existence of the five aggregates
“No eye, no ear, no nose, no tongue, no body, no mind” – these are the six
sources, the sense powers.
“No visual form, no sound, no odor, no taste, no object of touch, and no
phenomenon” - these are the six objects. So six objects and six sense
powers constitute the twelve sources.
The five aggregates - form, feeling discrimination, compositional factors
and consciousness.
The six sources - eye source, the ear source, nose source, tongue source,
body source and mental source.
The six objects of the six respective consciousnesses - form, sound, odor,
taste, object of touch and phenomena. So altogether these are the twelve
sources.

“There is no eye element and so on up to and including no mind element and
no mental consciousness element – here element is translated as
constituent.
The six sense powers - eye source, ear source, nose source, tongue source,
body source and mental source
The six consciousnesses - the eye consciousness, the ear consciousness, the
nose consciousness, the tongue consciousness, the body consciousnesses and
mental consciousness
What also implied here are the six objects of the respective
consciousnesses, so altogether eighteen constituents are indicated.
“There is no eye element and so on up to and including no mind element– the
element/constituent refers to the eye source, ear source, nose source,
tongue source, body source and mental source. Altogether there are six
sources.
“no mental consciousness elements” - shows the six consciousnesses - the
ear consciousness, the eye consciousness, nose consciousness, tongue
consciousness, body consciousnesses and mental consciousness.
There are the six sense powers and the six consciousnesses. This is
explicitly stated. But what also implied in the meaning is the six objects
- form constituent, sound constituent, odor constituent, taste constituent,
tangible object constituent, and phenomenon constituent. These six are
implied and the meaning is indicated there.
So 6 sense powers plus 6 objects plus 6 consciousnesses, they are
collectively called the eighteen constituents. The twelve sources is not
something separate from the eighteen constituents.
“There is no ignorance, no extinction of ignorance, and so on up to and
including no aging and death and no extinction of aging and death” - this
is talking about the twelve links of dependent origination in the forward
and reverse process.
The twelve links of dependent origination are:
   1. Ignorance
   2. Compositional activities
   3. Consciousness
   4. Name and form
   5. Six sources
   6. Contact
   7. Feeling
   8. Craving
   9. Grasping
  10. Potential existence
  11. Birth
  12. Aging and death.
The twelve links of dependent origination can be discussed in the forward
order or the reverse order.
In this way, it is linked to the discussion on the class of the thoroughly
afflicted phenomena, and the class of the thoroughly purified phenomena.
In the forward order, the twelve links of dependent origination starts with
ignorance.
Because of ignorance, then there is compositional activity. It is because
of ignorance, one accumulates throwing karma/propelling karma/ projecting
karma and the imprint/ residue/ latency of that action is imprinted on and
supposedly planted on the consciousness and so forth.
Because of this, then there is that. Because of that, then there is next
and all the way up to resulting in birth. Because there is birth, then come
aging and death.
This is the forward order of the twelve links of dependent origination.
Forward order is the meaning translation. The literal translation is arisen
from scripture.
So this is the forward order of the twelve links of dependent origination.
Because of ignorance (  compositional activities ( consciousness ( name and
form ( six sources ( contact ( feeling ( craving ( grasping ( potential
existence ( birth  ( aging and death.
If you look at it in the reverse order and if you are asked; “From what did
aging and death come from?”
    ⇨ Birth has to come from potential existence.
    ⇨ Potential existence has to come from grasping.
    ⇨ Grasping has to come from craving.
    ⇨ Craving is preceded by feeling.
    ⇨ Feeling is preceded by contact.
    ⇨ Contact is preceded by six sources.
    ⇨ Six sources are preceded by name and form.
    ⇨ Name and form is preceded consciousness.
    ⇨ Consciousness is preceded by compositional activities.
    ⇨ Compositional activities came from ignorance.

In the commentary of The Heart of the Perfection Sutra, it talks about the
training in the perfection of wisdom and in the context of the Path of
Meditation, one meditates on the emptinesses of the five aggregates, twelve
sources, the eighteen constituents, the four noble truths, twelve-links of
dependent origination and so forth.
By training in the perfection of wisdom on the Path Of Accumulation, Path
Of Preparation, Path Of Seeing, Path Of Meditation, this is how one achieve
enlightenment.

Shariputra, therefore, because there is no attainment, bodhisattvas rely on
and dwell in the perfection of wisdom, the mind without obscuration and
without fear. Having completely passed beyond error, they reach the end-
point of nirvana.

By meditating on the emptiness of inherently existence of the phenomena (as
below), the mind becomes free of obscurations:
   1. Five aggregates
   2. Twelve sources
   3. Eighteen constituents
   4. Twelve-link of dependent origination
   5. Four noble truths
   6. Exalted wisdom
   7. Attainment
   8. Non-attainment
By the meditation on the emptiness of inherently existence, one eliminates
the defilements and the obscuration. Then one is without fear and through
passing completely beyond all errors. By meditating and familiarizing with
the emptiness of inherently existence, the mind is without obscuration.
“the mind without obscuration”  -in some commentaries, it mean that one is

In some commentaries, “obscuration” refers to the two types of obscuration.

“Without fear” - in some commentaries it is explained that one is without

When the sutra says “all the buddhas who dwell in the three times also
manifestly, completely awaken to unsurpassable, perfect, complete
enlightenment in reliance on the perfection of wisdom”,  it is saying that
anyone who has become a Buddha did so by realizing subtle emptiness – the
emptiness of inherently existence and directly perceiving it. That is the
perfection of wisdom. All the buddhas of the future will have to achieve
enlightenment by relying on the perfection of wisdom.
“All the buddhas who dwell in the three times” - basically the buddhas
achieve enlightenment in reliance on the perfection of wisdom. The
“perfection of wisdom” in this sentence refers to the path perfection of
wisdom which is a mind realizing emptiness. In this context, this
“perfection of wisdom” is the mind realizing emptiness – the path
perfection of wisdom. By depending on this, one achieves enlightenment.
“All the buddhas who dwell in the three times also manifestly, completely
awaken to unsurpassable, perfect, complete enlightenment in reliance on the
perfection of wisdom” - this “perfection of wisdom” is the path perfection
of wisdom. The perfection of wisdom here refers to a mind realizing
emptiness in general.
If you were asked a question: “If it is a mind realizing emptiness, is it
necessary a perfection of wisdom?”
The answer is: No.
On top of that if you were asked a question: “If it is a wisdom directly
perceiving emptiness, is it necessary a perfection of wisdom?”
The answer is also: No.
In order to be a perfection of wisdom, it has to be a mind realizing
emptiness in the continuum of a Mahayanist.
The perfection of wisdom is a bodhisattva deed/conduct. In order for
something to be a bodhisattva deed/conduct, it has to be conjoined with the
Mahayana mind generation.
So the perfection of wisdom is a mind realizing emptiness that is conjoined
with a Mahayana mind generation. Earlier on, we saw how the perfection of
wisdom can be divided into four:
   1. Natural
   2. Path
   3. Scriptural
   4. Resultant
Of the four, this “perfection of wisdom “is the path perfection of wisdom.
Path perfection of wisdom is the mind realizing emptiness that is conjoined
with Mahayana mind generation.
