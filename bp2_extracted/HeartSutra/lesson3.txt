The path of the Mahayana superior consists of:
    - The Mahayana Path of Seeing
    - The Mahayana Path of Meditation and
    - The Mahayana Path of No-more-learning
A person becomes a superior bodhisattva when he enters the Mahayana Path of
Seeing. Prior to that, on the Mahayana Path of Preparation, the ordinary
bodhisattva meditates on emptiness -the ultimate nature of reality- via
meaning generality of emptiness.
Comes the time when the ordinary bodhisattva perceives emptiness directly
for the first time, then he enters the Mahayana Path of Seeing. At that
time:
    • he becomes a superior,
    • he becomes a Sangha Jewel - the ultimate Sangha Jewel
    • he possesses the Dharma Jewel in his mental continuum
When a person realizes emptiness directly, in the case of a bodhisattva who
realizes emptiness directly for the first time, he becomes a superior, he
becomes the Sangha Jewel, and specifically he becomes an ultimate Sangha
Jewel. The ultimate Sangha Jewel is the real Sangha Jewel. Anybody who
possesses the Dharma Jewel in the mental continuum is an ultimate Sangha
Jewel.
Conventionally, a Sangha is constituted by four fully ordained monks. But
here you do not need four. In the case, as long it is a superior being,
that person is the real Sangha Jewel -the ultimate Sangha Jewel.
If you think about this, then it becomes apparent that an actual Sangha
Jewel or the ultimate Sangha Jewel need not be an ordained person. We
usually think a Sangha in terms of monks and nuns. If you think about who
exactly is an ultimate Sangha Jewel, it becomes clear that even in the case
of a non-ordained person who has directly perceived emptiness is an
ultimate Sangha Jewel.
When the bodhisattva realizes emptiness directly for the first time, this
wisdom directly perceiving emptiness becomes an actual antidote to the
afflictions because it can actually counteract and destroy the afflictions.
This wisdom directly perceiving emptiness is the Dharma Jewel.
The wisdom directly perceiving emptiness in the continuum of this
bodhisattva is the Dharma Jewel and is True Path. Simultaneous with
perceiving emptiness directly for the first time, the bodhisattva achieves
the 1st ground which is called Very Joyful. It is said that at this point
in time, the bodhisattva feels that his enlightenment is certain. He can
see it coming that he is definitely going to be enlightened.
The bodhisattva enters into the Mahayana Path of Seeing while in the
meditative equipoise. He is in meditative equipoise directly perceiving
emptiness.
At this point in time, his wisdom directly perceiving emptiness directly
perceives emptiness.
At the same time, this wisdom becomes an actual antidote to the afflictions
and is powerful enough to act as a direct antidote to the afflictions.
Such a mind is called an Uninterrupted Path.
An Uninterrupted Path is not just a wisdom directly perceiving emptiness.
It is the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness which can act as the actual
antidote to the afflictions.
If you look at the chart, there is the Uninterrupted Path on the Mahayana
Path of Seeing.
The Uninterrupted Path on the Mahayana Path of Seeing acts an actual
antidote to certain kinds of afflictions.
In general, afflictions can be divided into two:
    • the afflictions that are abandoned by the Mahayana Path of Seeing
    • the afflictions that are abandoned by the Mahayana Path of Meditation
The Uninterrupted Path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing acts as an actual
antidote to the afflictions.
Here, this Uninterrupted Path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing acts as an
actual antidote to which afflictions?
In short, there are the Path of Seeing abandonments and Path of Meditation
abandonments.
Of the two, the Uninterrupted Path of Mahayana Path of Seeing acts as an
actual antidote to the Path of Seeing abandonments.
What is the Path of Seeing abandonment? It is the intellectually acquired
afflictions.
If you compare the intellectually acquired afflictions with the innate
afflictions, the intellectually acquired afflictions are relatively
coarser.

The Uninterrupted Path of Mahayana Path of Seeing acts as the actual

Is it the affliction that is abandoned by the Path of Seeing or by the Path

The Uninterrupted Path of Mahayana Path of Seeing acts as the actual
antidote to the afflictions that are abandoned by the Path of Seeing. In
short, they are the Path of Seeing abandonment.
What exactly is the Path of Seeing abandonment?
What are the afflictions abandoned by the Path of Seeing?
Between intellectually acquired affliction and innate affliction, the
Uninterrupted Path of Mahayana Path of Seeing acts as the actual antidote
to the intellectually acquired affliction. This Uninterrupted Path is a
specific kind of wisdom in meditative equipoise.
When the intellectually acquired afflictions are abandoned, they are
destroyed, and this happens during meditative equipoise. Comes the time,
while in meditative equipoise, the intellectually acquired afflictions are
destroyed, and at that time, that mind is given the name the Path of
Release.
For sake of simplicity, there is an Uninterrupted Path and a Path of
Release associated with each of the ten grounds.
The Uninterrupted Path in general refers to the wisdom directly perceiving
emptiness which acts as an actual antidote to any of the afflictions.
The Path of Release in general refers to that mind that has been freed from
any of the afflictions.
There are two paths, the Uninterrupted Path and the Path of Release, both
occurs in meditative equipoise.
During the meditative equipoise directly focusing on emptiness, the mind is
capable of acting as an actual antidote to the afflictions. During that
time, that meditative equipoise is termed the Uninterrupted Path.
When the respective afflictions are already abandoned, for that duration of
the meditative equipoise which happens in one session, that meditative
equipoise is given the name the Path of Release.

It is like the two halves of a soccer game. It is one continuous game but
it is split into two halves. Uninterrupted Path and Path of Release both
are wisdom of meditative equipoise. When the individual arises from that
meditative equipoise, he enters into the post-meditative equipoise.
This is in general how the bodhisattva progress in terms of abandoning
afflictions.
The bodhisattva enters into meditative equipoise, and the meditative
equipoise consists of Uninterrupted Path and Path of Release. Both are
wisdoms of meditative equipoise.
When they arise from that meditative equipoise, they enter into the post-
meditative equipoise. During the post meditative-equipoise, they carry out
activities.
They then enter again into the next meditative equipoise which consists of
the Uninterrupted Path and Path of Release.
From there, again they arise from that meditative equipoise into the post
meditative-equipoise.
This is how they move along the path. This is like us going to sleep then
waking up and then going to sleep.
This is how the bodhisattva progress toward full enlightenment.
They enter into meditative equipoise where they generate the Uninterrupted
Path, which is then followed by Path of Release. Both are wisdom of
meditative equipoise.
When they arise from there, they enter into post-meditative equipoise where
they actualize the post-equipoise wisdom.
Later on, they enter into meditative equipoise again and arise from it and
so forth.
This is how they progress along the ten grounds.
Through progressing along the ten grounds, they gradually abandon the
obscurations.
First, they abandon the afflictive obscurations and then they go on to
abandoning the knowledge obscurations.
In short, when the bodhisattva realizes emptiness directly for the first
time, his wisdom directly perceiving emptiness becomes the Uninterrupted
Path, which can acts as the actual antidote to the intellectually acquired
afflictions. Then in that meditative equipoise he achieves the Path of
Release where the intellectually acquired afflictions are completely
abandoned.
When he arises from that meditative equipoise, he achieved the post-
equipoise wisdom of the 1st ground.
During the post-meditative equipoise the bodhisattva strives in amassing
huge collections of merit in order to be able to abandon the Path of
Meditation abandonments.
When he arises from the meditative equipoise, he achieves the 1st ground
post-equipoise wisdom.
So during the post-meditative equipoise, the bodhisattva strives in
accumulating huge collections of merit in order to abandon the Path of
Meditation abandonment, specifically in order to abandon the innate
afflictions; which are abandoned by the Path of Meditation.
The innate afflictions which are abandoned by the Mahayana Path of
Meditation are categorized into three types:
   1. The big innate afflictions
   2. The middling afflictions
   3. The small innate afflictions
These three are further divided into three each.
All together make a total of 9 objects of abandonments, or 9 levels of
innate afflictions, that are abandoned by the Mahayana Path of Meditation.
For the sake of simplicity, if we base our discussion on the chart (not
based on the note in the Handout), the bodhisattva after abandoning the

During the post-meditative equipoise he accumulates a lot of merits in
order to be able to abandon the innate afflictions that are abandoned by
the Mahayana Path of Meditation.
Come the time when the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness of this
bodhisattva becomes powerful enough to act as the actual antidote to the

When the big innate affliction is abandoned, he achieves the Path of

When the bodhisattva achieves the 8th ground Path of Release, the

afflictions have been destroyed in his mind, which is labeled as “H” in

If you remember the discussion from the previous module on Tenets,
afflictive obscurations are not necessary afflictions. Prior to abandoning
the afflictive obscurations, the bodhisattva is not able to act on
abandoning the knowledge obscurations. After achieving the 8th ground Path
of Release, the bodhisattva enters into post-meditative equipoise of the
8th ground. During the post meditative equipoise, the bodhisattva
accumulates huge collections of merit. Comes the time when his wisdom

antidote to the knowledge obscurations, then that wisdom becomes the 8th
ground Uninterrupted Path. It is only when the 8th ground Uninterrupted
Path is actualized, does the work on abandoning the knowledge obscurations
start. It is only then.
If you look at the chart, this particular chart shows an Uninterrupted Path
for the 8th, 9th and 10th grounds. So there are three Uninterrupted Paths.
These three Uninterrupted Paths are the actual antidotes to the knowledge
obscurations.
Why are there three Uninterrupted Paths that are the actual antidote to the
knowledge obscurations?
There are three because the knowledge obscurations are also divided into
three:
   1. Big knowledge obscurations
   2. Middling knowledge obscurations and
   3. Small knowledge obscurations
These three Uninterrupted Paths of the 8th, 9th and 10th grounds are the
actual antidote to these three levels of knowledge obscurations.
When the small knowledge obscurations are abandoned the bodhisattva becomes
the Buddha. He achieves the Mahayana Path of No-more-learning.

On each of the grounds the bodhisattva acquires very special qualities
associated with those Perfections. So on the 1st ground, the bodhisattva
achieves very special form of the practice of generosity. Then on the 2nd
ground, he achieves very special kind of qualities and practices associated
with ethics and so forth. Each of these grounds the bodhisattva achieves
very special many types of qualities. I think there are twelve distinctive
qualities.
For example, on the 1st ground, the bodhisattva, in just one moment:
- enters into hundred concentrations,
- can see a hundred Buddhas,
- can travel to a hundred pure lands and
- is able to emanate a hundred replicas of himself i.e. send out a hundred
emanations.
These qualities are further enhanced on the 2nd ground. So, on the 2nd
ground, the bodhisattva, in just one moment:
- can enter into a thousand concentrations,
- can sees a thousand Buddhas
- can travel to a thousand pure lands and
- can manifests a thousand emanations.
Then on the 3rd ground, those qualities are even greatly enhanced. So, on
the 3rd ground, the bodhisattva, in just one moment:
- can enter into a hundred thousand concentrations,
- can see a hundred thousand Buddhas,
- can travel to a hundred thousand Buddha pure lands and
- can manifests a hundred thousand emanations.
These qualities are increased when the bodhisattva progresses on the
ground. By the time they are on the 10th ground, I don’t think we would
even want to try expressing that number because probably in one moment they
can enter into literally zillions and zillions of huge number of
concentrations, see unbelievably number of Buddhas, go to unbelievably
number of pure lands and manifest so many kinds of emanations. This is just
talking about the bodhisattvas on the 10th ground.
These bodhisattvas on the 10th ground can enter into so many kinds of
concentrations, travel to so many pure lands and emanate so many
manifestations, all for the purpose of purifying their obscurations,
accumulating merit and to work for sentient beings.
If you look at how hard these bodhisattva superiors work in order to
accumulate merit, then at our level, we know how important it is for us to
accumulate merits. Now you have heard a little bit about how one becomes a
Buddha, so achieving enlightenment is not a stroll in the park. A Buddha is
not your ordinary guy next door. You have to think about what a Buddha
really is. In short, in order to achieve enlightenment to become a Buddha,
you have to abandon all these obscurations: the afflictive obscurations and
knowledge obscurations. One has to work so hard to accumulate the merits
and to abandon these obscurations.
The work starts from the beginning after you have bodhicitta and entered
into the Mahayana Path of Accumulation. At the Mahayana Path of
Accumulation, one focuses on the accumulations of the collections and one
focuses on hearing and reflection in order to generate the realization of
emptiness.
Then on the Mahayana Path of Preparation, whatever ascertainment or
realization of emptiness one has, one meditates on it in order to derive
the wisdom arisen from meditation. The meditation on emptiness on the Path
of Preparation is done primarily, at this time, is to counteract the
manifest conception of true existence. But still one has not realizing
emptiness directly yet, therefore one does not have the actual antidote to
the afflictions.
Come the time when the bodhisattva is able to see emptiness directly for
the first time, he enters into the Mahayana Path of Seeing. Only then does
he have in his hand the actual antidote to the afflictions. When the
bodhisattva abandons the afflictions with the actual antidote, the
affliction that is first abandoned is the intellectually acquired
affliction. Only when the intellectually acquired affliction is abandoned
does the work of abandoning the innate afflictions start.
Then when the bodhisattva starts the work of abandoning the innate
afflictions, all the innate afflictions are not abandoned at one go. The
bodhisattva has to abandon those nine levels or the nine types of the
innate afflictions. So it is a long process. Only after which is the
bodhisattva free from the afflictive obscurations.
Still the work is not done as there are the knowledge obscurations to be
abandoned. Only after abandoning the afflictive obscurations does the work
of abandoning the knowledge obscurations start. By progressing on the 8th,
9th and 10th ground, the bodhisattva gradually abandons the knowledge
obscurations.
Come the time when the small knowledge obscurations are abandoned, he
becomes a Buddha, the fully enlightened one.
From this you can see Buddha is not your ordinary guy next door.
When we think, even just a little bit, about the qualities the bodhisattvas
are said to achieve from the 1st to the 10th ground, already it is

Student: On the chart, about the Effortless Bodhicitta (aspiring or
engaging), after the ordinary bodhisattva obtain the uncontrived
bodhicitta, why is the bodhicitta still an aspiring bodhicitta?
Answer: If you look at the ritual for adopting bodhicitta, in terms of the
order, one literally generates the aspiring mind generation, bodhicitta.
That is the aspirational mind generation which involves generating the
thought “I will achieve enlightenment in order to accomplish the welfare of
sentient beings”. So that is generating the aspiring mind generation.
Next is generating aspirational bodhicitta with a commitment. That involves
making a pledge to never give up this aspiration to achieve full
enlightenment for others. Never to give up. So, it is generating
aspirational bodhicitta with a commitment.
First, you generate the aspirational bodhicitta which is just the thought
“I will achieve enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient beings,” and
that does not come with a commitment. You are just generating the mind.
The next step is generating aspirational bodhicitta with a pledge or
commitment. Essentially it is the same, generating the thought to achieve
enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient beings. Just that this time
you make a vow or pledge to never give up this aspiration; never ever give
up. So you are making a vow or pledge.
Only then do you take the bodhisattva vow, you could say engage mind
generation or the engage bodhicitta. When you take the bodhisattva vow, on
top of the earlier pledge of never giving up the aspiration to achieve
enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient beings, one is making a
pledge or vow to practice accordingly as it is intended the six perfections
which are the deeds of the bodhisattva. It is a step by step thing.
When we are training to develop or generate bodhicitta, this training is
all about effort. Therefore it is contrived because it requires effort.
First you train the mind accordingly in order to generate the thought
thinking “I will achieve full enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient
beings.”
Based on that, the next step is to make a pledge to never give up that
aspiration. But it could be contrived when you are still making effort.
Come the time when this thought to achieve enlightenment for the benefit of
all sentient beings is uncontrived, it arises effortlessly, and that is the
time when you become a bodhisattva. That is the time when you have
uncontrived bodhicitta. That is the real and actual bodhicitta.
Come the time when an individual actualizes the uncontrived bodhicitta he
becomes a bodhisattva. This uncontrived bodhicitta that is generated
consists of two: aspirational bodhicitta and engage bodhicitta.
In term of mind generation, there are aspirational mind generation and
engage mind generation. This is not to be confused with what I have said
earlier.
When we are talking about the uncontrived bodhicitta, which is the actual
fully qualified bodhicitta. This fully qualified bodhicitta or mind
generation is divided into two:
   1. Aspirational bodhicitta and
   2. Engage bodhicitta
This is not to be confused with what I have said earlier about generating
the thought to achieve enlightenment in general, then making a pledge and
then taking the bodhisattva vow. Many of us have taken bodhisattva vow but
we don’t have any kind of bodhicitta yet. This is not the same thing. If
the question is about the difference between the aspirational bodhicitta
and engage bodhicitta, then a different answer will be required.

Student: The general understanding for the two collections is usually the
wisdom and method. From the first lesson, my understanding is that the
first collection is for the ordinary bodhisattva and the second collection
is for the arya bodhisattva. Is this correct?
Answer: When we say accumulating the two collections - collection of merit
and collection of wisdom, these two collections are not done separately;
they are done in unison right from the beginning of the Mahayana path. The
accumulations of the two collections are done in unison starting from the
Path of Accumulation all the way up to the end of the Path of Meditation.
It is not that the ordinary bodhisattva only accumulates the collection of
merit and the bodhisattva superior only accumulate the collection of
wisdom. It is not like that.
