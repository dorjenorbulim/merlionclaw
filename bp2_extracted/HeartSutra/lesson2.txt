Referring to the above chart, there are the five Mahayana paths.
The five Mahayana paths can be divided into:
   1. The paths of an ordinary bodhisattva
   2. The paths of a superior or an arya

The path of a Mahayana ordinary bodhisattva consists of:
   1. Mahayana Path of Accumulation
   2. Mahayana Path of Preparation
The path of a Mahayana bodhisattva superior/ bodhisattva arya consists of:
   1. Mahayana Path of Seeing
   2. Mahayana Path of Meditation
The path of a Mahayana superior consists of:
   1. Mahayana Path of Seeing
   2. Mahayana Path of Meditation
   3. Mahayana Path of No-more-learning
There is a difference between a Mahayana superior and a Mahayana
bodhisattva superior.
Do you notice that, in the context of the Mahayana, these five paths are
called the Mahayana paths?
They are not called the five paths of the bodhisattvas?
So the path of the Mahayana superior consists of Mahayana Path of Seeing,
Mahayana Path of Meditation and Mahayana Path of No-more-learning.

The Path of Accumulation is divided into three:
   1. Small Path of Accumulation
   2. Medium Path of Accumulation
   3. Great Path of Accumulation
When an individual achieves the Mahayana small Path of Accumulation, that
individual becomes a bodhisattva simultaneous with the uncontrived
realization of bodhicitta (or the effortless bodhicitta). That individual:
    • enters the Mahayana path
    • enters the Mahayana path from the Mahayana small Path of Accumulation
    • becomes a bodhisattva
    • becomes a Mahayanist
If you were to ask, do the bodhisattvas at this point necessarily have to
realize emptiness or not?
The answer is: No.
Those who become bodhisattvas when they enter the Mahayana path from the
Mahayana small Path of Accumulation, there could be bodhisattvas who have
realized emptiness but there could be bodhisattvas who have not realized
emptiness yet. Based on this, you could say in general that there are
bodhisattvas who have not realized emptiness.
When the bodhisattva reaches the Mahayana medium Path of Accumulation,
whatever mind generation he has achieved becomes very stable and will never
degenerates. Hence, from that point of time onwards, it is not possible for
that bodhisattva to fall into the Hinayana.
Anybody who will become a bodhisattva has to work very hard in accumulating
merits, and training the mind in generating bodhicitta based on the
instruction such as the sevenfold cause and effect personal instructions
starting from recognizing all sentient beings as one’s mother, remembering
their kindness, generating the wish to repay their kindness and generating
the love that considers all sentient beings to be beloved, to be
attractive, to be very precious. On the basis of that, one generates great
compassion, and on the basis of that, one generates wholehearted resolved.
Only on the basis of that, one generates the mind of enlightenment –
bodhicitta.
Through a lot of hard work and perseverance, that individual through
depending on instruction such as the sevenfold cause and effect instruction
generates the actual realization of bodhicitta - an actual effortless/
uncontrived realization. Only then does this person enter the Mahayana Path
of Accumulation. Only then he becomes a bodhisattva and a Mahayanist.
Even then, this bodhisattva on the Mahayana small Path of Accumulation can
fall backward in terms of realization. In that his bodhicitta could
degenerates if he is not careful. It is possible that such bodhisattva
could also fall into the lower realms even though they have bodhicitta at
this point in time. If you think about this carefully, then it becomes very
clear that for us, there is no doubt that falling into the lower realms in
our next life is almost a certainty.

One of the qualities that the bodhisattvas will gain upon actualizing the
Mahayana great Path of Accumulation is a special kind of
concentration/meditative stabilization called the meditative stabilization
of the stream of doctrine (or the stream of dharma). It is said that when
the bodhisattvas achieve the meditative stabilization of the stream of
dharma, the coarse obscurations (such as feelings of not wanting to hear
the teachings) that hinders the hearing of the teachings are purified.
The bodhisattvas who achieved this meditative stabilization of the stream
of doctrine are able to listen to many, many teachings. They have this
ability. It is said that they can even receive teachings from the
representation of the buddhas such as statues and so forth. Bodhisattvas
who have achieved this meditative stabilization of the stream of doctrine
are able to remember every single word and meaning of the Buddha’s
teachings that they hear.
Khen Rinpoche: How wonderful! For us we hear today and forget today.
While achieving this meditative stabilization of stream of doctrine is no
easy feat, many prerequisites apply:
    • The person must be of Mahayana lineage.
    • The person must have accumulated huge collections of merits.
    • The person must abide in pure ethics/pure morality.
    • The person must have engaged in extensive hearing of the Buddha’s
      teachings.
This is the meditative stabilization of the stream of doctrine, which is a
special quality that the bodhisattvas achieve on the Mahayana great Path of
Accumulation.
The Mahayana Path of Accumulation is the time when the bodhisattvas are
accumulating the collections. The primary way that the bodhisattvas
accumulate the collections while on the Mahayana Path of Accumulation is
through extensive hearing and reflection. The bodhisattva on the Path of
Accumulation, after they have achieved the Mahayana great Path of
Accumulation, from there they move on to the Mahayana Path of Preparation.
The Mahayana Path of Preparation is divided into four stages:
   1. Heat
   2. Peak
   3. Forbearance
   4. Supreme Mundane Qualities
The bodhisattvas on the Mahayana Path of Preparation primarily focus on
meditation. What they achieve is the wisdom arisen from meditation. The
meditation is the meditation from the threefold activity of hearing,
reflection and meditation. Meditation is their main activity. It is not
that they do not engage in hearing and reflection at this juncture, it is
possible but the primary activity is meditation that is from the threefold
activity of hearing, reflection and meditation.
The Mahayana Path of Preparation is divided into four stages.
The first stage is called the heat stage of Mahayana Path of Preparation.
Why is it given such a name “heat”?
Just imagine that you are trying to start a fire. Before you can see the
actual fire you first feel the heat as heat comes before the fire. When you
sense the presence of heat, that is like the sign that the fire is about to
start. Likewise, the heat stage of the Path of Preparation is like the sign
that one will perceive emptiness directly soon. It is a sign that the
Mahayana Path of Seeing is going to come.
At the point of achieving the heat stage of Mahayana Path of Preparation,
one achieves a special concentration, which is a union of calm abiding and
special insight focusing or observing on emptiness - the ultimate nature of
reality. That is indicated by “D” in the chart.
So achieving the special concentration, which is a union of calm abiding
and special insight observing emptiness, is simultaneous with achieving the
heat stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation. Before you can achieve the
concentration, which is a union of calm abiding and special insight
focusing on emptiness, you first must achieve a calm abiding focusing on
emptiness. That has to happen on the Mahayana great Path of Accumulation.
The bodhisattva on the Mahayana great Path of Accumulation will achieves
this calm abiding/meditative serenity focusing on emptiness. Based on that,
you could say that the bodhisattva on the Mahayana great Path of
Accumulation has necessarily realized emptiness.
As for the bodhisattva on the Mahayana small Path of Accumulation, there
are those who have realized emptiness and there are those who have not
realized emptiness. But I do not know what to say about the bodhisattva on
the Mahayana medium Path of Accumulation. Do we say the bodhisattvas who
are on the Mahayana medium Path of Accumulation have necessarily realized
emptiness? This is something to think about.
Come the time when a bodhisattva achieves the special concentration which
is a union of calm abiding and special insight focusing on emptiness, that
bodhisattva simultaneously achieves the heat stage of the Mahayana Path of
Preparation. If you think about this special concentration which is a union
of calm abiding and special insight focusing on emptiness, and if you were
to relate back to lorig, the topic of mind and awarenesses, of the seven
kinds of awareness, which is the special concentration which is a union of
calm abiding and special insight focusing on emptiness? Those of you who
wish to relate this back to the topic of minds and awarenesses, it would be

Then the bodhisattva moves on to the peak stage of the Mahayana Path of
Preparation. At this point in time it is said that the roots of virtue can
never be severed. That means one’s roots of virtue at this point in time
can never be destroyed. The roots of virtue of the bodhisattva on the peak
stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation can never ever be severed
henceforth. Up to just before this stage, the roots of virtue of that being
can still be severed, but at this point in time, the bodhisattva has
reached the end of the possibility of having his roots of virtue severed.
So he has reached the peak of that, so it is called the peak of the
Mahayana Path of Preparation.
The bodhisattva who has achieved the peak stage of the Mahayana Path of
Preparation will never ever have their roots of virtue severed or destroyed
by wrong views. Such bodhisattva will never ever be reborn in the lower
realms. They will only have good rebirths. Of course bodhisattva at this
point in time they still take rebirth under their own karma and
afflictions. Having said that, the kinds of rebirth that they take in
samsara will only be good samsaric rebirths; no more lower realms for them.
The next stage is the forbearance stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation
The bodhisattva on the forbearance stage of the Mahayana Path of
Preparation acquires the confidence that they will never be reborn in the
lower realm. It is kind of guaranteed that the bodhisattva on the peak
stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation will not be reborn in the lower
realms, but they have not achieved that confidence. However, the
bodhisattva on the forbearance stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation
achieves this confidence. Having said this, you wonder why “F” being no
more rebirths in the lower realms is placed in the forbearance stage and
not in the peak stage.
The bodhisattva on the forbearance stage of Mahayana Path of Preparation
acquires this confidence, and they are so sure, it is a kind of guarantee
but not a guarantee from outside, they know “There is no way that I will be
reborn in the lower realms ever again.”  This is the meaning. Otherwise, if
you just look at milestone “F” on its own, you may get the idea that the
bodhisattva prior to forbearance stage may be reborn in the lower realms.
It is not like that. What “F” indicates is not just no more rebirths in
lower realms, but the bodhisattva acquires this confidence that they will
never be reborn in the lower realms. If you think about it, most likely
bodhisattva who has achieved the Path of Preparation, even starting from
the heat stage, you could probably say that such bodhisattva will never
ever be born in the lower realms.
As long as the bodhisattva is on the Mahayana Path of Preparation, you
could say that probably that the bodhisattva will never ever be reborn in
the lower realms. So it is for sure that bodhisattvas on the forbearance
stage of Mahayana Path of Preparation will not be born in the lower realms.
For bodhisattvas on the peak stage, it is for sure they will not be born in
the lower realms as well, because their roots of virtues can never ever be
severed. That means they will only get good rebirths because they cannot
destroy the roots of virtue, all they have is virtues. Their virtues are so
strong, and they don’t destroy it.  However, there are some texts that
mentioned the bodhisattvas on the heat stage of the Path of Preparation
will not fall into the lower realms.

The last stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation is the Supreme Mundane
Qualities.
Why this stage is called the Supreme Mundane Qualities (or Supreme Dharma)?

This is because of all the paths of ordinary beings, this is the highest
that one can achieve.
The notes (in the handout) pertaining to what is abandoned on the Mahayana
Path of Preparation, the different manifest form of the different
conceptions and so forth, is written from the perspectives of the
Sautrantika Middle Way Autonomist. In general study, most of the time the
paths and grounds are presented according to the Autonomist. You have to
take note on this. Whether everything in this section is in accord with the
assertion of the CMWS or not, it is something you have to think about and

So the heat, peak, forbearance and supreme mundane qualities stages of the
Mahayana Path of Preparation are essentially paths of an ordinary being.
The bodhisattvas on the Mahayana Path of Preparation have already realized
emptiness, but these bodhisattvas are not able to realize emptiness
directly. They have realized emptiness but they have not realized it
directly. By going through the four stages of the Mahayana Path of
Preparation, what the bodhisattva are trying to do on the Mahayana Path of
Preparation is to weaken/diminish the manifest conceptions of true
existence. The bodhisattvas on the Mahayana Path of Preparation depend
mainly on the wisdom arisen from meditation in order to suppress the
manifest conceptions of true existence.
The bodhisattvas on the heat and peak stages of the Mahayana Path of
Preparation have realized emptiness, although not directly. While they are
in the meditative equipoise focusing on emptiness, what appears to the
perspective of this meditative equipoise is not the subsidence of dualistic
appearances. That means that the bodhisattvas at this point do have
dualistic appearances. The bodhisattvas on these two stages know and are
aware that they have dualistic appearances. In short, basically the
bodhisattvas on heat and peak stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation,
while they are in meditative equipoise focusing on emptiness, there is
still dualistic appearances in their meditative equipoise. These
bodhisattvas are aware of the dualistic appearances.
While the bodhisattva on the forbearance stage and the supreme mundane
qualities stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation are in meditative
equipoise focusing on emptiness, they have the appearance of the subsidence
of dualistic appearances, but in reality dualistic appearances have not
subsided. They do get the appearance of as if dualistic appearances have
subsided, but in fact there are still dualistic appearances and the
bodhisattvas are not aware of that. So if you compare their experiences
with the experiences of the bodhisattva on the heat and peak stage, their
experiences are more subtle.
Basically on the Mahayana Path of Preparation, the manifest conception of
true existence gradually diminishes as one move through the Mahayana Path
of Preparation. When the bodhisattva on the supreme mundane qualities stage
of Mahayana Path of Preparation is in meditative equipoise focusing single-
pointedly on emptiness or the ultimate nature of reality, what appears to
that bodhisattva seemed to be the subsidence of dualistic appearance. This
is what appears to the bodhisattva as if there is no dualistic appearance
but in fact there is still dualistic appearances. Just that the bodhisattva
is not aware of it. The bodhisattvas on this supreme mundane qualities
stage of the Mahayana Path of Preparation realized emptiness, but they have
not realized emptiness directly yet. So essentially their mind that
realized emptiness is a conceptual mind, and if it is a conceptual mind
realizing emptiness, the realization of emptiness is necessarily via a
meaning generality/generic image/mental image of the object, and in this
case emptiness. If the realization is via this meaning generality, there
has to be dualistic appearances. So although there is still dualistic
appearance but the bodhisattvas are not aware. What they experience is as
if all dualistic appearances have subsided. So their mind has become very
subtle at this point.
What the bodhisattvas on the Mahayana Path of Preparation do is to
gradually diminish the manifest conceptions of true existence; so they are
suppressed. In short, the bodhisattva on the Mahayana Path of Preparation
by depending primarily on the wisdom arisen from meditation suppressed the
manifest conceptions of true existence. This mind that realizes emptiness
is a conceptual mind. It will come a time when this conceptual
consciousness realizing emptiness will transform into the entity of a
direct perceiver. So come the time when the bodhisattva generates this
direct perceiver apprehending emptiness then the bodhisattva achieves the
path of a superior.
When the bodhisattva first achieves the wisdom directly perceiving
emptiness that is the very time when true path comes into being. When we
talk about the Three Jewels –Buddha Jewel, Dharma Jewel and Sangha Jewel-
the Dharma Jewel consist of the true path as well. When the bodhisattva
first generates the wisdom directly realizing emptiness, at this point in
time, this is when the true path of the Dharma Jewel comes into being. When
the bodhisattva newly achieves this mind, the wisdom directly perceiving
emptiness becomes the actual refuge. That is the thing that actually
protects an individual.
Why is this true path in the mental continuum of this bodhisattva superior?
Why does this wisdom directly perceive emptiness is called the actual
refuge that protects any beings? This is because the root of all the
problems and sufferings is ignorance. This ignorance is the conception of
person and phenomena as inherent existence. This ignorance is the root of
all problems. The one and only thing that is powerful enough to counteract
this ignorance is the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness. Comes the time
when any individual generates the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness and
that is the very birth of the actual refuge in the mind.
Comes the time when this wisdom directly perceiving emptiness is newly
generated, that is the start of the true path, and that is the birth of the
true path, and that is the birth the Dharma Jewel, which is the actual
refuge. That is the time when one comes to possess in one’s own mind the
actual refuge, which is the Dharma Jewel. Such an individual who has
actualized true path becomes a superior Sangha, therefore becomes a Sangha
Jewel.
When an individual realizes emptiness directly for the very first time,
that wisdom becomes the actual refuge, becomes the Dharma Jewel. Then that
individual becomes the Sangha Jewel.
One becomes a refuge for oneself and one becomes the refuge for others as
well.
That is the start of becoming an actual refuge for oneself and an object of
refuge for other sentient beings.
It is only when the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness is generated, one
starts to be able to do something to actually save, and become a source of
refuge for oneself.
When you start becoming an actual savior of your own self, then you come to
possess some abilities and powers to actually to be of some actual help and
real refuge to others. Then of course you will come to be an object of
refuge for others. That is the start of you becoming the object of refuge
for others.
If we are not able to save ourselves, if we cannot really be a savior of
ourselves, it is quite difficult to be an effective object of refuge for
others.
The time when the bodhisattva newly realizes emptiness directly:
    • he becomes a Mahayana superior,
    • he becomes a bodhisattva superior,
    • he becomes a person of the Mahayana path of seeing
Why is it called the Path of Seeing? Because the Path of Seeing is the very
first time that an individual sees the truth directly for the first time.
Prior to the achievement of the Path of Seeing, one will not be able to see
the truth directly. The Path of Preparation is called the Path of
Preparation because it prepares an individual to see the truth directly.
When one is prepared enough to the point when one sees the truth directly
for the first time, then you achieves the Path of Seeing, when you are
seeing the truth directly for the first time.
When an individual realizes emptiness for the first time while in the
meditative equipoise focusing on emptiness, nothing appears except
emptiness to the perspective of this mind. There is no dualistic appearance
at all. All dualistic appearance has subsided. Only the emptiness of
inherent existence appears. In the perspective of this wisdom directly
perceiving emptiness, there is no dualistic appearance, there is no
appearance of true existence, no appearance of inherent existence and no
appearance of conventional phenomena.
According to the Prasangika, CMWS, this mind, the wisdom directly
perceiving emptiness is the real unmistaken mind. According to CMWS, all
the minds in the continuum of sentient beings with the exception of the
wisdom directly perceiving emptiness are mistaken consciousnesses. Why?
Because all the minds in the continuum of sentient beings, with the
exception of the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness, are necessarily
pervaded by the appearances of true existence.
If someone will to ask you, or if you are wondering to yourself: “Why is it
that every mind in the continuum of sentient beings, with the exception the
wisdom directly perceiving emptiness, is necessarily mistaken?
The answer is: Because all other consciousnesses in the continuum of
sentient beings, with the exception of the wisdom directly perceiving
emptiness, are necessarily pervaded by appearances of true existence. It is
for this reason, according to the CMWS, there is a common locus between a
mistaken consciousness and a valid cognizer.

When the bodhisattva newly generates the wisdom directly perceiving
emptiness, that wisdom directly perceiving emptiness becomes an actual
antidote to the afflictions. This wisdom directly perceiving emptiness is a
very powerful mind. Therefore it can acts as a direct antidote to the
afflictions. When the bodhisattva newly realizes emptiness directly for the
first time, he enters the Mahayana Path of Seeing, and that very mind
realizing emptiness directly is called the uninterrupted path in general,
but specifically is an uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing.
So the uninterrupted path of Mahayana Path of Seeing is that wisdom
directly perceiving emptiness.
The uninterrupted path of Mahayana Path of Seeing is a direct antidote to
the obscurations and the afflictions. It is not sufficient to say direct
antidote to afflictions. When we talk about obscurations, it is either
afflictive obscuration or knowledge obscuration. The uninterrupted path of
Mahayana Path of Seeing is an actual antidote to which obscuration? Is it
the afflictive obscuration or the knowledge obscuration?
The uninterrupted path of Mahayana Path of Seeing cannot act as the direct
antidote to the knowledge obscuration. The abandonment of the knowledge
obscuration starts only from the 8th grounds/ bhumi.
At this point in time, the uninterrupted path of Mahayana Path of Seeing
has no chance whatsoever or even smelling the knowledge obscuration.
Therefore the uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing can only
act as the direct antidote of the afflictive obscurations.
If you want to pursue this in more details, essentially we are saying that
the uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing is the direct
antidote to the afflictions (delusions/afflictive emotions/destructive
emotions), but the afflictions can be divided into innate afflictions and
intellectually acquired afflictions.
The uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing is the direct
antidote to which of these two afflictions?
The uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing cannot act as direct
antidote to the innate afflictions; it acts as direct antidote to the
intellectually acquired afflictions.
If you pursue it in greater details, you will realize that the
uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing is the direct antidote to
the intellectually acquired afflictions; and not the innate afflictions.

Student: Can the wisdom of directly perceiving emptiness be reversed? After
you have entered the Path of Seeing, do you stay in the state all the time
even after you have get out of meditative equipoise focusing on emptiness
forever?
Khen Rinpoche: That is not the question of President. You are saying that
person will stay there forever and never get enlightened?  No improvement?
Are you saying that?
Student: No. Does this state of Path of Seeing only last as long as you
stay in meditative equipoise, and when you are out of post meditative
equipoise you will not be able to sustain that state?
Khen Rinpoche: Once an individual becomes an arya bodhisattva, it is
impossible for that person to become an ordinary being again. He or she
will not lose that realization, that means that the realization will not
degenerates and will not be lost. When a bodhisattva achieves the Mahayana
Path of Seeing, the bodhisattva is definitely to achieve enlightenment. It
is just a matter of time. This will come later. The first bodhisattva
ground is called extremely joyful. The bodhisattva has such great joy
because finally he so sure he sees his own enlightenment. That is definite.
For that reason, we could say for sure that such an arya bodhisattva will
never degenerates into a worldly being again and it is impossible for him
to lose the realization of emptiness. This is stated very clearly by
Chandrakirti in his auto-commentary to his own text called “Engaging in the
Middle Way”.  When a bodhisattva enters the Mahayana Path of Seeing, he
enters through the uninterrupted path of the Mahayana Path of Seeing, which
is a state of meditative equipoise focusing directly on emptiness, but it
is not that the bodhisattva remains in the meditative equipoise directly
perceiving emptiness forever. He still has to get up and enters into the
post-meditative equipoise. In the post-meditative equipoise the bodhisattva
strives and works very hard in order to accumulate merits. The details will
come later.
