Answer: Like what I have mentioned before, it is said that the Perfection
of Wisdom Sutra is included in the sutra pitaka. If that is the case, is it
okay to say that The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra falls into the
sutra pitaka? In general, if the main subject matter of the sutra is
dealing with the higher training in concentration, then it falls into the
sutra pitaka. But the subject matter of The Heart of the Perfection of
Wisdom Sutra is talking about the wisdom realizing emptiness, so can we say
that the subject matter of The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra is
also concentration? This is something that needs to be thought about. I am
not 100% saying that The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra falls into
the sutra pitaka. This is something we need to think about. Perhaps there
are people whom we can enquire and ask about this.
In order to appreciate this question, and in order for the question to come
about, you first must have an understanding of a couple of things. First,
you must know how the three scriptural collections are classified, i.e. how
they are classified according to the subject matter. The second thing is
that you have to know, in general, the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra is
included in the sutra pitaka. The third thing is that if you look at The
Heart of Perfection of Wisdom Sutra, you could almost say for sure that the
subject matter is the higher training in wisdom. If you put all these three
things together then you will have the question: “How The Heart the
Perfection of Wisdom Sutra is included in the sutra pitaka? If “yes” why?”
In The Heart of Perfection of Wisdom Sutra it involves this questions and
answers between Avalokiteshvara and Shariputra. Both of them spoke due to
the blessing of Buddha. You could ask: “Why can’t the Buddha just say
everything himself? Why does he have to start this with suddenly two people
start talking to one another?” There is no straight forward answer for
this, and you just have to think about it.
Khen Rinpoche: Some example, some people want to ask question, but the next

side. Next to him, the person asked to ask. Many people do that. I don’t

It is possible that the Buddha did what he did in The Heart of the
Perfection of Wisdom Sutra because of the disposition and inclination of
the objects to be subdued - the disciples. The whole point about the
teaching of the Buddha is to benefit sentient beings, the objects to be
subdued - the disciples. He has to benefit them according to their
disposition and inclination. It is possible, in this case, it was perhaps
more beneficial, for whatever reason as the Buddha knows, for the teaching
to happen in the way as a conversation between these two. You could say
that perhaps it was more beneficial to the disciples for the teaching to be
given as a dialogue between these two individuals, and the dialogue was
inspired by the Buddha himself.

So far we have been talking about the way to train in the perfection of
wisdom on the Mahayana Path of Accumulation, Path of Preparation, Path of
Seeing, and Path of Meditation, and by depending on that one achieves
enlightenment. As it says in the sutra, all the Buddhas who dwell in the
three times achieve enlightenment in reliance on the perfection of wisdom.
The perfection of wisdom here refers to the path perfection of wisdom. So
it refers to wisdom realizing emptiness that is conjoined with the Mahayana
mind generation/ bodhicitta. In some places, it mentioned that it has to be
the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness. Perhaps it should be okay to
posit the perfection of wisdom to be the wisdom realizing emptiness that is
conjoined with bodhicitta.
It is mentioned in some commentaries that the path of perfection of wisdom
is the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness that is conjoined with
bodhicitta. But if you look at the sutra, one trains in the perfection of
wisdom in order to train in enlightenment. The training in the perfection
of wisdom starts from the Mahayana Path of Accumulation and Mahayana Path
of Preparation. In order to cater to the training of wisdom on the Path of
Accumulation and Path of Preparation, perhaps it would be better to say
that the path perfection of wisdom refers to the wisdom realizing emptiness
that is conjoined with bodhicitta; instead of saying that it has to be the
wisdom directly perceiving emptiness that is conjoined with bodhicitta. So
in order to be a perfection of wisdom, the wisdom realizing emptiness has
to be conjoined with method.
There are the five paths of the hearers, solitary realizers and the
bodhisattvas. All three has their respective Path of Seeing - the hearers’
Path of Seeing, the solitary realizers’ Path of Seeing and the Mahayana
Path of Seeing. Those individuals who have achieved those three paths do
realized emptiness directly, but the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness
in the continuum of the hearers and solitary realizers do not enable them
to achieve enlightenment. But it is the same wisdom, why? Why does the
wisdom directly perceive emptiness in the continuum of the hearers and
solitary realizers is unable to overcome the knowledge obstructions?
Because their wisdom directly perceiving emptiness are not conjoined with
method. In order for the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness to act as an
antidote to the knowledge obstructions it must be conjoined with method.
From this you can see that having this wisdom directly perceiving emptiness
alone does not necessarily mean that you will eliminate all the
obstructions. In order for the wisdom directly perceiving emptiness to be
able to eliminate all the obstructions, it must be conjoined with method,
i.e. it must be conjoined with a huge collection of merits. From this you
can understand that you need a huge collection of merit, which is the
support that enables this wisdom directly perceiving emptiness to act as an
antidote even to the knowledge obstructions.
In short, the perfection of wisdom refers to wisdom realizing emptiness
that is conjoined with method, or conjoined with bodhicitta/Mahayana mind
generation. From this you can see that if it is a wisdom realizing
emptiness, it is not necessarily a perfection of wisdom.
Khen Rinpoche: Alright? Okay.
Next is showing the greatness of the mantra of the perfection of wisdom. In
this context here, the mantra of the perfection of wisdom refers to the
path perfection of wisdom.  So the point is what the text is saying is the
path perfection of wisdom is a mantra, or the wisdom realizing emptiness is
a mantra.
In Sanskrit, the word “mantra” is made of two syllables “man” and “tra”.
What mantra means in Sanskrit is mind protection, protecting the mind, or
saving the mind,.
Mantra means protecting the mind, and it is protecting the mind from fear.
Showing the greatness of the mantra
Therefore, the mantra of the perfection of wisdom, the mantra of great
knowledge, the unsurpassed mantra, the mantra equal to the unequalled, the
mantra that thoroughly pacifies all suffering, should be known as truth
since it is not false.
“The mantra of the perfection of wisdom” refers to the wisdom realizing
emptiness. In English it is translated as perfection, but literally it
means gone over, crossover away out of samsara.
So the mantra of the perfection of wisdom is the wisdom realizing
emptiness. By depending on this wisdom realizing emptiness one cross over
the shore of the ocean of cyclic existence i.e. it takes one to the other
shore.
“The mantra of great knowledge” - the mantra here refers to the wisdom
realizing emptiness. This wisdom realizing emptiness is the mantra of great
knowledge because this wisdom realizing emptiness destroys ignorance and
its afflictions.
“The unsurpassed mantra” - there is no higher path leading to liberation
“The mantra equal to the unequalled” – here the unequalled or unparalleled
refers to Buddha. So this perfection of wisdom, the mind realizing
emptiness makes one equal to the Buddha.
Ven Gyurme: I am not sure if unequalled is better or unparalleled”
It is “the mantra that thoroughly pacifies all suffering” because it can
pacify all the sufferings and its causes.
It “should be known as truth since it is not false” - because the
perfection of wisdom sees suchness, therefore is true and undeceiving. This
wisdom realizing emptiness sees the truth, the reality as it is, therefore
it is not false.
In this paragraph here, the mantra, in general refers to mind protection,
or protecting the mind. So that is the meaning of mantra.
What is the mantra here? The mantra here is the path perfection of wisdom,
the wisdom realizing emptiness. This wisdom realizing emptiness is that
which protects the mind. It protects the mind from the suffering of samsara
and its causes. This wisdom realizing emptiness is the definitive mantra,
or the mantra of definitive meaning.
Most people only think of mantra as thing that is recited; there are all
these mantras. If you understand what mantra really means, it means
protecting the mind. Protecting the mind from what? Protecting the mind
from suffering and its causes. If you think about it, at the end of the
day, the things that people recite cannot really save them from all the
suffering. How can that thing you recite save you from suffering? In order
for our minds to be protected from all the fears, the whole variety of
sufferings, we have to eliminate the main root causes of these sufferings.
All of you have already done quite a fair bit of learning and study and all
the extensive discussion in Tenets and so forth. We have repeatedly seen
how the source of all our miseries and problems can be traced back to our
ignorance - the apprehension of a self. The only sole antidote to our
ignorance has always been and will always be the wisdom realizing
emptiness. Without this wisdom realizing emptiness, we cannot defeat
ignorance. If we don’t defeat ignorance, we cannot stop all the problems in
samsara. Then we cannot stop samsara. The actual real definitive mantra
that protects us from suffering is none other than the wisdom realizing
emptiness. It is not those mantras that we recite. From this you can see
the big difference between somebody who has learned as much as possible the
Buddha’s teachings and somebody who doesn’t learn, but just contend to
recite mantras. For a person who doesn’t learn, and when you tell them
‘mantra’, the only thing that comes to their mind is the thing that we
recite. A person who has studied and studied well, who has thought about it
and reflected well, his/hers understanding of mantra is so much deeper,
profound, extensive, of wide angle and wide perspective. A person, who
hasn’t studied and reflected, will not be able to see this. You tell them
‘mantra’ and the only thing that comes to their mind is mantra that they
recite, and that is about it.
The main reason why the Buddha gave all the teachings was to help us to
overcome the root cause of all our sufferings. When you think about it and
realized this for yourself, then you see the point behind learning. You
will see why it is so important to learn. Many times people wonder and ask,
“Why should I study all these so much; for what? Practice can already.” You
see it is not easy to see the point of what the Buddha is trying to say.
The core meaning of the Buddha’s teachings really requires learning and
thinking about it. Without doing reflections, and analysis, you will not be
able to see or you will not be able to gain an ascertainment of certain
knowledge in your heart.  Through learning, reflection and analysis, then
when you come to gain an ascertainment of what the Buddha has said “The
root of all our problems is this and the solution is this”, I think it
makes a huge difference to the practice because it gives weight to the
practice. If you are really practicing, then of course, relatively speaking
compare to other people, your enlightenment will definitely be quicker.

The mantra of the perfection of wisdom is declared:
TADYATHA [OM] GATE GATE PARAGATE PARASAMGATE BODHI SVAHA
Sometimes there is an “Om” there.
Actually I have explained the meaning of the mantra to you earlier on.
“Gate” means go.  As there are two “Gate”, so is go, go.
The first two “Gate” means go to the Path of Accumulation and go to the
Path of Preparation.
“Para” means other side, and “gate” means go.
So, “Paragate” means go to the other side, go on to the Path of Seeing.
“Parasamgate” means go perfectly to the Path of Meditation.
“Bodhi” is enlightenment.
“Bodhi Svaha” means go to enlightenment, go to the buddha ground.
One trains in the perfection of wisdom on the Path of Accumulation and Path
of Preparation.
Then on the Path of Seeing, one sees emptiness directly.
On the Path of Meditation one familiarizes with what one has seen on the
Path of Seeing.
Through that one achieves the enlightenment, Path of No More Learning.
In short, the mantra is kind of an explanation of how one progress on the
various paths by depending on the perfection of wisdom. In some
commentaries, it is explained that the explanation of the training in the
perfection of wisdom is in the paragraph “Form is empty. Emptiness is
form…… Similarly, there is no suffering, origination, cessation, and path;
there is no exalted wisdom, no attainment, and also no non-attainment” is
kind of an extensive explanation on the training in the perfection of
wisdom for the trainees of dull faculties. For the trainees of sharp
faculties, it is just the mantra: Tadyatha Om Gate Gate Paragate
Parasamgate Bodhi Svaha. The sharp faculties only need that mantra.
There are many Perfection of Wisdom Sutra. There is the extensive, middling
and short Perfection of Wisdom Sutra. The extensive one consists of twelve
volumes, and the middling one consists of three or four volumes. The twelve
volumes have the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra in 100,000 verses, and the
middling one in 20,000 verses. The third one, the condensed Perfection of
Wisdom Sutra is in one volume and is commonly known as the “The Perfection
of Wisdom Sutra in Eight Thousand Verses”. The subject is the same, whether
it is twelve, three or one volume. Why is there this varying length?
Perhaps it is due to the capacity of the disciple.
For the disciple or the intended recipient of the extensive Perfection of

“Well said” is mentioned two times because it is word of emphasizing.
“Well said, well said, son of the lineage, it is like that. It is like

have indicated; even the tathagatas rejoice” - the Buddha is saying that
the way to train in the perfection of wisdom is just as you

In general, there are three categories of the Buddha’s speech:
   1. Speech spoken by his mouth
   2. Inspired speech
   3. Permitted speech.
The prologue and the last paragraph of the sutra (starting with “The

why the order is definite. If you look at the Lamrim Chenmo it should be
