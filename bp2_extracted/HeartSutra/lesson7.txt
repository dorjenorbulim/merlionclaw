Shariputra begins by asking Avalokiteshvara how should anyone who wishes to
train in the perfection of wisdom do so. In response, Avalokiteshvara says:

Shariputra, any son of the lineage or daughter of the lineage who wishes to
practice the activity of the profound perfection of wisdom should look upon
it like this,
The brief explanation is given in the next sentence:
correctly and repeatedly beholding those five aggregates also as empty of
inherent nature.
That is the brief explanation on how one should trains in the perfection of
wisdom.
Essentially, the answer that is given by Avalokiteshvara shows how the
perfection of wisdom is practiced on the Mahayana path. Soon you will see
how the Perfection of Wisdom is practiced on the Mahayana Path of
Accumulation, on the Mahayana Path of Preparation, on the Mahayana Path of
Seeing and on the Mahayana Path of Meditation.
Avalokiteshvara gives an answer by giving an overview of how the perfection
of wisdom is to be practiced on the paths. He said that one should
“correctly and repeatedly beholding those five aggregates also as empty of
inherent nature.” Then what follows is an expanded explanation of that
brief statement.

The sutra continues:
Form is empty. Emptiness is form. Emptiness is not other than form; form is
also not other than emptiness. In the same way, feeling, discrimination,
compositional factors, and consciousness are empty.
This pertains to the training in perfection of wisdom on the Mahayana Path
of Accumulation and Mahayana Path of Preparation.
Avalokiteshvara says “Form is empty”. The “form” here should take it to
mean one’s own form aggregates. One’s form aggregate is empty. What is it
empty of? It is empty of inherent existence.
“Form is empty” - form is empty of existing inherently.
What is the aggregate of form of a person?
It is an appearance to the perspective of a non-investigating, non-
analyzing conventional consciousness.
For when the knower or the mind which engages in ultimate analysis looks at
what form is, it does not find form that is inherent existent.
So when form is investigated by a mind that is engaged in ultimate
analysis, the inherently existing form is not found. The form existing from
its own side is not found. What form essentially is, is none other than an
appearance to the perspective of a non-investigating, non-analyzing
conventional consciousness.
Form does not exist from its own side. It is like a dream elephant because
a dream elephant does not exist from its own side. What a dream elephant
is? It is just an appearance to that dreaming consciousness.
At the end of the day, what exactly is a dream elephant?
It is just an appearance to that dream consciousness. There is no real
elephant from its own side. There is no real elephant there or anywhere.
Likewise, what is the form aggregate? What is form?
The form aggregate does not exist from its own side. What form is, is none
other than a mere appearance to the consciousness.
Form is like a dream elephant. Just like the dream elephant, form does not
exist from its own side. Therefore, form is in the nature of emptiness. The
nature of form is emptiness.
Then doubt arises. If form does not exist inherently, does it mean that
form does not exist?
Does it mean that form is non-existent because it does not exist
inherently?
No, while form is empty of existing inherently, i.e. while form does not
exist inherently, form can be posited as a mere appearance conventionally.
Then you think about the analogy of the dream elephant. While it is true
that the dream elephant is not established as an elephant, nevertheless,
one can still posit the appearance of the dream elephant. While the dream
elephant is not an elephant, the dream elephant appears. Dream elephant is
there. There is an appearance of a dream elephant, and one can posit it.
The dream elephant is not established as an elephant. A dream elephant is
empty of being an elephant.
The dream elephant is not an elephant. It is empty of elephant. It is not
established as an elephant. But that fact does not eliminate or negate
dream elephant. The dream elephant still appears, and one can posit the
appearance of dream elephant.
So if you understand the analogy, likewise when you apply this to the
meaning of form aggregates, while form is empty of existing inherently,
that does not negate the mere appearance of form conventionally.
The very fact that form is empty of inherent existence does not negate or
stop one from being able to posit the form as mere appearance
conventionally.
If you find both the analogy and the meaning difficult, then it is really
difficult. The analogy is supposed to be easier than the meaning.

While a dream elephant is not a real elephant, one can posit a dream

Likewise while form is empty of inherent existence, while it is empty,
there is no contradiction in its existing as a mere appearance to a

If you are asked “Is a dream elephant an elephant?” You have to say “No!”
If subsequently you are asked: “Does dream elephant exist?” “Is there a
dream elephant?” You will say “Yes”
There is a dream elephant but the dream elephant is not an elephant.
If you say dream elephant does exists, and if you pursue the matter, then
what exactly is a dream elephant? What are you going to say?
Dream elephant is essentially the mere appearance to the mind, in this case
is the dreaming consciousness. Dream elephant is nothing other than that.
Can you posit something else as a dream elephant other than that?
So what is a dream elephant is? It is just that appearance of an elephant

Do you understand the analogy? Does a dream elephant exists?
Dream elephant is a phenomena source form. So what is this dream elephant?
If you were asked “What is a dream elephant?” what are you going to say?
The dream elephant is the appearance of the elephant to the dreaming

There are many analogies. This is the analogy of a dream elephant, and they
are the same. If you think about the elephant that is conjured by an
illusionist, the apparition of an elephant that is conjured by an
illusionist does exist but it is not an elephant. Then you have the analogy

The illusory elephant conjured by the illusionist exists but the illusory

It is none other than the appearance of the elephant to the eye

In explaining of how form is empty and what it means, just like a dream
elephant which is not an elephant, just like how a dream elephant is empty
of an elephant. But the very fact that the dream elephant is empty of an
elephant does not contradict a dream elephant from appearing. It does not
stop the appearance of the dream elephant. There is no contradiction.
Likewise, while form does not exist inherently, one can posit the form as a
mere appearance conventionally. That form as a mere appearance
conventionally is not contradicted, is not negated, by the fact that form
is empty of existing inherently.
If form is empty of existing inherently, does it mean that form ceases to
exist? No!
While form is empty of existing inherently, it still exists. It exists. To
address that point, then the next phrase “Emptiness is form”. While form is
empty of existing inherently, one still can posit form. That means one
still can account for form’s existence. That is the meaning of “Emptiness
is form”.
There may be a qualm raised at this point. The person may wonder: “A dream
elephant is empty of elephant, is form empty of form?” Of course the answer
is “No”.
A dream elephant is empty of elephant but that doesn’t mean, when you apply
to the meaning, “Therefore, if dream elephant is not elephant, then that
means form is also not form?”
That is the wrong way of applying the analogy to the meaning to what I have
just mentioned. If one thinks “Just as a dream elephant is empty of
elephant, likewise when you apply that to the meaning with regards to form,
then form is empty of form.” That would be a wrong way of applying the
analogy to the meaning. That is not so. The way to apply the analogy to the
meaning should be done like this; “Just like a dream elephant is empty of
elephant, form is empty of existing inherently.
The purpose of linking this analogy of dream elephant to the meaning is
just to help us to understand by way of example that is known to the world.
The analogy is to help us to understand that although form appears to exist
inherently, form does not exist inherently by using example that is well
known in the world, something that ordinary people who has not realized
emptiness can understand. Everybody and anybody should understand that a
dream elephant is not an elephant. When one wake up from the dream, one
understand that it is just a dream, and a dream elephant is not an
elephant. One does not need to have realized emptiness to realize that the
dream elephant is not an elephant. Any ordinary being can do that. This is
something that ordinary beings can relate to and understand. So the use of
such examples, something is that known to the world as an analogy, is to
try to help us understand how phenomena such as form are empty of existing
inherently.
Remember in our discussion of CMWS in the previous module (Tenets), there
was a division of conventionalities into real and unreal, and that division
is made from the perspective of a worldly consciousness. There is a
division of real and unreal conventionalities, from the perspective of a
worldly consciousness, of course in reality there is no such thing,
While an ordinary person who has not realized emptiness is able to realize
that a dream elephant is not an elephant, but it is a different matter when
it comes to form, because form is a real conventionality in the perspective
of a worldly consciousness. To ordinary beings who have not realized
emptiness, one apprehends form to be truly existent, to be inherently
existent. One believes that is how it exists, and it exists in the way it
appears. Form appears as inherently existent and one assent to that
appearance. One believes that is how form exists. So in the perspective of
a worldly being, form is real, conventionality is real, it exists in the
way it appears. For an ordinary being who has not realized emptiness, there
is not disparity between reality and appearance. For a person who has not
realized emptiness there is no way for that person to see that there is a
huge disparity between how things exist and how they appear. They don’t
realize the disparity. For example, form. We just take it everything and
anything that appear at face value. This is how it is. It exists in exactly
the way it appears, as truly existent. It is only through gradual process
of investigation using logic and reasoning, only then one can come to see
that there is a disparity between reality and appearance, especially after
realizing emptiness. Only then one can realize that form is not true, it is
a falsity.
Form is empty. What is form empty of? Form is empty of inherent existent.
Form is empty of existing inherently. While form is empty of existing
inherently, form exists. Form exists as a mere appearance conventionally.
While it is empty there is no contradiction, there is nothing that can stop
someone from positing form. Therefore, “emptiness is form”.
Think about this: “Form is empty. Emptiness is form.” By using the analogy
of a dream elephant, the whole point of the analogy is to help us to
understand, just as a dream elephant is not an elephant. The very fact that
the dream elephant is not an elephant doesn’t stop the existence of dream
elephant. You can account for dream elephant even though a dream elephant
is not an elephant. You can account for the dream elephant while the dream
elephant is not an elephant. While form is empty of existing inherently,
this does not necessarily entail that form does not exist. While form does
not exist inherently, form exists.
A qualm may arise about the relationship between form and its emptiness.
Form is compounded phenomena. The emptiness of form is uncompounded
phenomena.
Form is impermanent. The emptiness of form is permanent phenomena.
Form is a conventional truth. The emptiness of form is an ultimate truth.
Qualm is: Do these two have separate entities?
One is impermanent, one is permanent.  One is a conventional truth, one is
an ultimate truth.
The answer is “No”.
They are not different entities. A particular form and its emptiness are
one entity; not separate entities. Therefore, The Heart of the Perfection
of Wisdom Sutra says “Emptiness is not other than form”.
Here “not other than form” means the emptiness (of form) is not of a
different or separate entity from form.
Then the sutra continues: “form is also not other than emptiness”.
Here it says “Emptiness is form” but you cannot take it literally. Is
emptiness form?  Emptiness is not form.
“Emptiness is form” does not literally mean that emptiness is form. How can
emptiness be form? How can an uncompounded phenomenon be a compounded
phenomenon?
What it means is while form is empty of existing inherently, within the
emptiness of form, while it is empty you can account for form.  Form and
form’s emptiness are not different entities. They are one entity.
Going back to the analogy of a dream elephant, a dream elephant and it
being empty of elephant, these two are one entity. Just as a dream elephant
and its emptiness of elephant are one entity, we could likewise understand
that form and form’s emptiness (of inherent existence) are one entity.
“Emptiness is not other than form; form is also not other than emptiness” –
it is trying to show that form and form’s emptiness are one entity. The
entity of form is not separate from form’s emptiness of inherent existence.
A dream elephant is empty of elephant. Dream elephant is not elephant. So
there is a dream elephant and its emptiness of elephant. You cannot talk
about dream elephant separate from its emptiness of elephant. If you
separate the two, can you logically account for one and not the other?
Form and form’s emptiness of existing inherently are not the same; they are
different. Although they are not the same thing, they are one entity.
Although they are one entity, they are not the same.
“Form is empty” is trying to help us to realize that form does not exist
inherently.
“Emptiness is form” is trying to help us to understand that while form is
empty of existing inherently, form exists.
“Emptiness is not other than form” is trying to tell us that, in this
example of form’s emptiness, emptiness of form is not of different entity
from the basis of emptiness, which is form.
“Form is also not other than emptiness” is saying that one cannot account
for form that is of a different entity from its ultimate nature - its
emptiness of existing inherently.
In the same way one has to apply these four things to the rest of the
aggregates: feeling, discrimination, compositional factors and
consciousness. In the same way they are empty.  This paragraph shows that
the training in the perfection of wisdom on the Path of Accumulation and
Path of Preparation through reviewing or thinking about these four-fold
emptiness. As you remember our discussion on the Paths and Grounds, the
realization of emptiness on the Path of Accumulation and Path of
Preparation is a realization that is accompanied with meaning generality,

Shariputra, likewise, all phenomena are emptiness; without characteristic;
unproduced, unceased; stainless, not without stain; not deficient, not
fulfilled.
When all phenomena are elaborated into certain categories, we talk about
the twelve sources and the eighteen constituents. Essentially, all
phenomena are emptiness. Constituent sometimes is translated as element
There are the eighteen constituents. What are they made up of?
They are made up of the six objects, which are the objects of observation
of the six consciousnesses - form constituent, sound constituent, odor
constituent, taste constituent, tangible object constituent and phenomena
constituent.
Next there are the six inner constituents, basically the sense powers. The
sense powers are the basis for generating the respective consciousness -
the eye constituent, ear constituent, nose constituent, tongue constituent,
body constituent and mental constituent.
By depending on the six objects and the six sense powers then the six
consciousnesses are produced - eye consciousness, ear consciousness, nose
consciousness, tongue consciousness, body consciousness and mental
consciousness.
Altogether there are eighteen such phenomena, collectively are known as the
eighteen constituents

“All phenomena are emptiness” means the eighteen constituents are all
empties.
“Without characteristic” means that phenomena are not established by way of
their characteristics.
“Unproduced” - all phenomena such as form etc. are not produced inherently.
They are only produced conventionally.
“Unceased” - all produced phenomena necessarily cease, but this cessation
is not inherent cessation, it ceases conventionally.
Those phenomena that are produced from causes and conditions are not
produced inherently. There is no inherent production, therefore they are
unproduced. Any phenomenon that is produced from causes and conditions has
to cease, but their cessation is not inherent cessation. Therefore, they
are unceased.
“Stainless” – all defilements such as samsara and the causes of samsara do
not exist inherently, therefore they are stainless.
“Not without stain” - the state of abandonment, which is the abandonment of
the stain, nirvana also does not exist inherently. So it is not without
stain.
“Not deficient, not fulfilled” - these two are related to the class of the
thoroughly afflicted phenomena and the class of thoroughly pure phenomena.
The class of the thoroughly afflicted phenomena is the afflictions and so
forth decrease as one progress on the path, but their decrease, then
becoming lesser, is not something that happens inherently. Just as the
gaining of the different qualities which are included under the class of
thoroughly purified phenomena, those increment or improvement in the
qualities and realizations do not exist inherently. Therefore, the class of
the thoroughly afflicted phenomena is not deficient, and the class of
thoroughly pure phenomena is not fulfilled.
This short paragraph shows the training in the perfection of wisdom on the
Path of Seeing. On the Path of Seeing, emptiness is perceived directly.

of designation would have to be the collection of the limbs - the legs, the
hands, the torso and the head. If you remember Shantideva’s Engaging in the
Bodhisattva Deeds from the previous module, there is quite an extensive
discussion on, for example, what the hand is, that’s the body. If you say

There is quite a lot of discussion in the Engaging in the Bodhisattva
Deeds. If you go down to each level, from the body down to the individual
parts of the body and then when you look at the individual parts of the
body they themselves are also made up of their own parts. Going all the way
down to the atomic level, we talk about particles and so forth, even those
are not established inherently. Nothing is. Such a line of investigation
would reveal that things are dependent on their parts; the very concept of
whole is dependent on its parts and parts make up whole. Therefore it is
the uncommon feature of the CMWS that all phenomena are dependently
originated/ dependently arisen. There isn’t a single existent that is not
dependently arisen. Dependently arisen phenomena are necessarily not
inherently existent.
