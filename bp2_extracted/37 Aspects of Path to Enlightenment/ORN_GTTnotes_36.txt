Sutra says, “He does not abide in the involvement and subsequent involvement of the battle, because, due to abiding in dharmata in this way, he does not subsequently correctly see subsequent attachment or anger to any phenomenon,” and “He does not abide in the involvement and subsequent involvement of the talk of village. He does not abide in the involvement and subsequent involvement of the talk of city. He does not abide in the involvement and subsequent involvement of the talk of town. He does not abide in the involvement and subsequent involvement of the talk of self.”  Here [the top of p. 446] we have “the subject—a middling faculty bodhisattva abiding on the path of seeing—(9) possesses having individually ceased the abidance of involvement that initially adheres to true existence and subsequent involvement that subsequently adheres to true existence…” Remember that when we posit these signs of irreversibility, they are not posited on the occasions of meditative equipoise themselves, but on the occasion of subsequent attainment; since they are induced by meditative equipoise, meditative equipoise is presented [see Geshe-la’s explanation at the top of p. 44 in notes 35]. It says “abidance of involvement…and subsequent involvement…” You can understand these two parts as follows:   “Involvement that initially adheres to true existence” refers to adherence to true existence prior to the meditative equipoise of the path of seeing. Of course, during meditative equipoise itself, there is no manifest adherence to true existence, because meditative equipoise is supposed to be the antidote.   “Subsequent involvement that subsequently adheres to true existence” refers to adherence to true existence during subsequent attainment [of the path of seeing]. After the meditative equipoise of the path of seeing, there is subsequent attainment, so “subsequent involvement” refers to adherence to true existence that occurs during subsequent attainment [of the path of seeing].

The response begins by pointing out the reason for imputing the special conventions of body and speech with the names “forbearances” and “knowledges.” The text says [the bottom of p. 447], “There is a reason for imputing the special conventions of body and speech of a middling faculty bodhisattva abiding on the path of seeing in this manner, because these special conventions of body and speech are generated in dependence on the forbearances and knowledges.” Then there is a passage that talks about how meditative equipoise and subsequent attainment are concordant [the fourth line from the top on p. 448]: “when these instants of forbearances and knowledges that are meditative equipoise which are included within the sixteen aspects have been correctly attained during meditative equipoise, since the pure mundane subsequent attainment bearing the aspect of not strongly adhering to apprehendeds and apprehenders as truly existent even though apprehendeds and apprehenders appear dualistically is included within the mind of subsequent attainment…” This meditative equipoise and subsequent attainment are not discordant. In dependence on meditative equipoise, the conventions of body and speech are attained during subsequent attainment, and these conventions are really results concordant with meditative equipoise.   The person who has this doubt in his mind then thinks, “If these signs of irreversibility are the instants of forbearances and knowledges, they are meditative equipoise. How then can other persons infer irreversibility through these instants of forbearances and knowledges?” Our system responds through stating a reason, a purpose, and the damage to fact. The reason for imputing the special conventions of body and speech by the names “forbearances” and “knowledges” is as stated earlier: because these special conventions of body and speech arise in dependence on the forbearances and knowledges [the top of p. 448].  The purpose is stated in the second paragraph [p. 448]: “there is a purpose for imputing the special conventions of body and speech of one on the path of seeing with the name ‘meditative equipoise,’ because it is in order to make known that ‘the conventions of body and speech of yogis who have attained a ground are only concordant with these realizations of meditative equipoise at all times with the exception from the point of view of the purpose of taming sentient beings.’”   This last phrase—“with the exception from the point of view of the purpose of taming sentient beings”—is a bit difficult to understand. It is only for the purpose of taming sentient beings that the conventions of body and speech arise during subsequent attainment; if there is no need to tame sentient beings, then these conventions of body and speech would not be generated during subsequent attainment. If there is no need to tame sentient beings, what is the purpose of having the special conventions of body and speech? In other words, these special conventions of body and speech that occur during subsequent attainment are just for the purpose of taming sentient beings. If there were no need to tame sentient beings, then subsequent attainment would be concordant with meditative equipoise.   However, in the previous paragraph, we said that meditative equipoise and subsequent attainment are concordant. There are different modes of concordance; the mode of concordance here [in the context of explaining the purpose] is different from that mentioned above [in the context of explaining the reason]. Our system talks about collecting the various accumulations over three countless eons. The accumulation of the first countless eon is on the paths of accumulation and preparation; the accumulation of the second countless eon takes place on the seven impure grounds (the first seven grounds); the accumulation of the third countless eon takes place on the last three grounds, the three pure grounds. If you consider this, you can see that this collection of accumulations is really, really amazing. What is the purpose for gathering such extensive accumulations? A bodhisattva generates bodhicitta to benefit infinite sentient beings. After generating this, the bodhisattva engages in training in the deeds. The aim of a bodhisattva is to enact the welfare of sentient beings. If he/she does not have extensive accumulations, there is no way to enact the welfare of sentient beings; and one is not even able to fulfill one’s own welfare.   When we talk about accumulations, there are two types: accumulation of merit and accumulation of exalted wisdom. The accumulation of merit can include making prostrations, making offerings, doing circumambulations, practicing generosity, patience, etc. The accumulation of exalted wisdom involves a lot of time. For example, one needs to train in the dexterity of meditative stabilization. Let us look at how an uninterrupted path of the path of meditation is generated. One gathers accumulations and eventually, when the amount of accumulation is sufficient, one is able to generate the first uninterrupted path of the path of meditation. Once this uninterrupted path of the path of meditation is generated, there is no need to abide in it for a long time; it is the antidote to the coarse great-great conception of true existence. In the next instant, one arises into the liberated path, the state in which the objects of abandonment have been abandoned. There is no need to abide long in this liberated path, so the bodhisattva arises quickly into subsequent attainment. Subsequent attainment last a very, very long time, because during subsequent attainment, there are many activities of accumulating merit, training in meditative stabilization, etc. From time to time, the bodhisattva enters into meditative equipoise, and eventually, when there is enough accumulation to generate the next uninterrupted path, he/she will eventually generate the uninterrupted path that is the direct antidote to the next level of the objects of abandonment.
