THE ACHIEVING OF ACCUMULATION
- Having engaged in that manner diligently in the  previous  achieving,  the
achieving of engagement, the accumulations are generated.
The seventeen types of achieving of accumulations are:
   1. Achieving of accumulation of great compassion
   2. Achieving of accumulation of generosity
   3. Achieving of accumulation of ethics
   4. Achieving of accumulation of patience
   5. Achieving of accumulation of joyous effort
   6. Achieving of accumulation of concentration
   7. Achieving of accumulation of wisdom
   8. Achieving of accumulation of calm abiding
   9. Achieving of accumulation of special insight
  10. Achieving of accumulation of the union of those [union of calm abiding
      and special insight]
  11. Achieving of accumulation of skilful means
  12. Achieving of accumulation of exalted wisdom
  13. Achieving of accumulation of merit
  14. Achieving of accumulation of the paths of seeing and so forth
  15. Achieving of accumulation of the retentions (dharanis) of words and so
      forth
  16. Achieving of accumulation of the grounds, and
  17. Achieving of accumulation of the antidotes

The sequence of the achievings of accumulation is subsumed as:
1.  Behavior  together  with  support:  Support  is  great  compassion  (1).
Behavior is generosity and so forth(2-7).
2. Meditative equipoise includes calm abiding (8), special insight (9),  and
the  union  of  calm  abiding  and  special  insight  (10);  and  subsequent
attainment refers to skilful means (11)
3. The divisions of the  two  accumulations  include  the  accumulations  of
exalted wisdom and merit (12-13).
4. Achieving the result and holding the object of observation refers to  the
path of seeing and so forth (14) and the retention of  words  and  so  forth
(15).
5. Acting as a support for excellent qualities refers to the  grounds  (16).
Bearing  the  characteristics  of  abandoning  obscurations  refers  to  the
antidotes (17).
-  Although one has realized the  emptiness  of  true  existence  and  one’s
practices are conjoined  with  the  wisdom  realizing  emptiness,  when  one
engages in the practices, one does not pass beyond the  conventional  truth.
One will engage in these various practices without passing  beyond  the  two
truths.
- Sequence of explanation: achieving of armor (  explanation  of  the  first
ten (1st – 10th) achievings of accumulation ( explanation of  the  achieving
of engagement ( explanation of the next seven (11th -17th).

   1. Bhagavan, what is the great vehicle of a great bodhisattva?
   2. With just what is one considered to have correctly entered  the  great
      vehicle of a great bodhisattva?
   3. What gave rise to the vehicle?

entity of the Mahayana
  11 - Achievings of accumulation  of  skillful  means  -  the  training  in
wisdom and compassion
  12 & 13 - Achievings of accumulation of exalted wisdom  and  merit  -  the
two not being mutually different
  14 - Achievings of accumulation of the paths of seeing  and  the  path  of
meditation - transcending the  discordant  classes  (the  path  of  no-more-
learning is not included because we  are  accumulating  exalted  wisdom  and
merit in order to achieve Buddhahood i.e. we are talking about  the  process
before attaining the path of no-more-learning.
  15 - Achieving of accumulation of the retentions (dharanis) of  words  and
so forth -the occasion being definite
- Reply to the 2nd question: 16 - Achieving of accumulation of  the  grounds
– the realizer
- Reply to the 3rd, 4th & 5th questions: 17 - Achieving of  accumulation  of
the antidotes - pliancy in the application of the antidotal classes
- The 1st to 7th accumulations included within the accumulations of  perfect
attitude and application are as follows:
 -  a bodhisattva, who has donned the armor in the practice  possessing  the
six perfections, cultivates the great compassion which wishes  to  free  all
trainees from suffering at the beginning.
- Three kinds of compassion:
   1. Compassion observing sentient beings: that which,  by  observing  mere
      sentient beings, bears the aspect  of  wishing  them  to  be  free  of
      suffering.
   2. Compassion observing phenomena: that which  observes  sentient  beings
      qualified by the dawning of emptiness of coarse selflessness.
   3. Compassion of non-observation: that  which  observes  sentient  beings
      qualified by the dawning of freedom from elaboration.
- All three compassions are  similar  in  the  aspect  of  wishing  sentient
beings to be free of suffering
- The difference between these three compassions is in how  sentient  beings
are observed:
    - In the compassion observes mere sentient beings, without qualification
      by impermanence, selflessness, or emptiness. Merely observing sentient
      beings, one has the aspect of wishing them to be free of suffering.
    - In the compassion observing phenomena, one  observes  sentient  beings
      qualified by coarse  selflessness  and  wishes  them  to  be  free  of
      suffering.
    - In the compassion of non-observation,  one  observes  sentient  beings
      qualified by emptiness of true existence and wishes them to be free of
      suffering.
-  In the 2nd compassion, the compassion  observing  phenomena,  one  has  a
mind thinking, “sentient beings lack a self of persons,” and wishes them  to
be free of suffering.
- In the 3rd type of compassion, one thinks, “sentient beings are  empty  of
true existence.” This thought itself is not compassion; it is another  mind.
Having generated that thought and wishing sentient beings to  be  free  from
suffering is the compassion of non-observation.

In term of six perfections, how do those influenced by compassion take  care
of sentient beings?
- By way of generosity, one takes care of sentient beings in this life;
- through ethics, one accomplishes the happiness of high status of the  next
  rebirth
- By way of patience, joyous effort, and concentration,  one  sets  sentient
  beings in the happiness of meditative stabilization;
- By way of wisdom, one sets them in nirvana.
- Not only does one practice ethics, but one also causes others to  practice
  ethics and attain a good rebirth in the next life.
- Not only does one practice patience,  joyous  effort,  and  concentration,
  and attains the happiness  of  meditative  stabilization,  one  also  sets
  others in these practices.
- One practices wisdom and causes  others  to  practice  wisdom  and  attain
  everlasting happiness, by setting them in nirvana

Those included within meditative equipoise are:-
   - 8th:  Achieving of accumulation of calm abiding
   - 9th : Achieving of accumulation of special insight
   - 10th: Achieving of accumulation of the union of those  [union  of  calm
abiding and special insight]
Someone says: Based on a sutra  citation,  that  calm  abiding  and  special
insight are discordant.  Calm  abiding  is  abidance  without  movement  and
Special insight is awareness analyzing the meaning.  How  will  there  be  a
union?
Our system: They are not discordant. One is able to generate wisdom  through
analysis. This analysis by way of wisdom is  able  to  induce  a  meditative
stabilization. Such an exalted wisdom realizing the perfect meaning is  able
to abandon the obscurations, just as darkness is abandoned or  dispelled  by
illumination.
- In this situation the exalted wisdom and the meditative stabilization  are
  not different entities.
- The meditative stabilization is actually  induced  by  the  analysis  with
  wisdom. If the wisdom is not one entity with calm abiding,  or  meditative
  stabilization, one will not be able to abandon  the  manifest  objects  of
  abandonment.
- Example: calm abiding is  like  the  eye,  and  special  insight  is  like
  illumination. These  two,  calm  abiding  and  special  insight,  are  not
  contradictory. They abide  concordantly.  They  are  not  discordant  like
  illumination and darkness.
- Through possessing the accumulation of  the  union  of  calm  abiding  and
special insight, one will come to possess:-
   -11th: Achieving of accumulation of skilful  means  that  enacts  others’
welfare through attention to omniscience i.e. through  possessing  the  mind
of enlightenment.
- Through having familiarized with such paths of  meditative  equipoise  and
subsequent attainment, one accumulates:
   -12th: Achieving of accumulation of exalted wisdom
   -13th: Achieving of accumulation of merit
- Through having familiarized with the two accumulations, one will  come  to
possess
   - 14th: Achieving of accumulation of the paths of seeing and so forth
 By possessing the accumulation of paths, one will attain
   -15th: Achieving of accumulation of the retention of words and  so  forth
in which one is able to remember, without  limit,  words  and  meanings  for
many eons without forgetting them.
- The entity of retention is mindfulness and wisdom  concomitant  with  calm
abiding.
- In regards to secret mantra, this is a  case  of  the  name  of  a  result
imputed to its cause.
- There are three types of retention:  the  retention  of  forbearance,  the
retention of secret mantra, and the retention of words and meanings.
- The retention of forbearance is having analyzed the meaning of the  forty-
two syllables, A RA PA TSA NA and so forth. One meditates on the meaning  of
emptiness, the mode of abidance of the syllables, thinking,  “The  letter  A
is the door to all phenomena because  it  is  not  produced  from  the  very
beginning,” whereby one generates  a  meditative  stabilization  and  wisdom
which is not afraid of it. This  is  the  attainment  of  the  retention  of
forbearance.
- Boundary: exists from the forbearance level of the path of preparation.
- The retention of secret mantra is one  attains  the  retention  of  secret
mantra from the ability to compose secret mantras that are able  to  protect
others, such as those that pacify negativities through their  power.  Secret
mantra serves as both  a  cause  and  a  result.  Through  composing  secret
mantras, then one has secret mantras, and through the power of these  secret
mantras, one is able to protect others. Therefore, secret  mantra  seems  to
be both a cause and a result.
- Boundary: exists from the forbearance level of the  path  of  preparation.
There is an ordinary retention of secret mantra, from the forbearance  level
of the path of preparation, and a special  one,  from  having  attained  the
path of seeing.
- The retentions of words and meanings are the ability to retain  the  words
and meanings of phenomena for eons without forgetting them
- Boundary: exist from having attained the forbearance level of the path  of
preparation and above.
- By way of acting as the support of excellent qualities: 16th achieving  of
accumulation of the grounds
- For the sake of abandoning  the  discordant  classes:  17th  Achieving  of
accumulation of the antidotes
- The 17 accumulations are the accumulation of causes  issuing  forth  great
enlightenment, because these 17 include all the stages of the  profound  and
extensive paths that are objects to  be  practiced  by  bodhisattvas.  -  --
These 17 accumulations cause perfect quick achievement, so they are  posited
to be the causes issuing forth  great  enlightenment.  Since,  by  realizing
them without observing them to be truly  existent,  without  passing  beyond
ultimate truth, one achieves all the aims of  the  stages  of  the  Mahayana
path that is included within  the  extensive,  without  passing  beyond  the
conventional truth.
- The accumulation of exalted wisdom is difficult to realize, and if  it  is
realized, there will be great benefit. In order to realize it, one needs  to
realize emptiness.

- The 1st-16th emptinesses are said to  be  subsumed  under  the  last  four
emptinesses:
   1. Emptiness of things (17th)`
   2. Emptiness of non-things (18th)
   3. Emptiness of one’s own entity (19th)
   4. Emptiness of the entity of others (20th)
- Those in which [non-thusness] has been taken as the bases  for  emptiness,
the property-possessors, can be subsumed under 17th & 18th. Non-thusness  is
conventional truths.
- Those in which thusness has been taken as the bases of  emptiness  can  be
subsumed under 19th and 20th.
- For the 4th emptiness: The emptiness of true  existence  of  things  which
are empty of true existence is the emptiness of the just  empty.  Boundaries
of the fourth exalted wisdom: path of preparation.
Someone assert: The basis of emptiness is an exalted wisdom.
Our system: The bases of this emptiness are things that are  empty  of  true
existence, not an exalted wisdom. According to this, the bases of  emptiness
are things. The basis of the fourth emptiness  is  not  asserted  to  be  an
exalted wisdom in general. However, if it  is  asserted  to  be  an  exalted
wisdom, there are three ways in which thusness is taken  as  the  basis  for
emptiness.
-  The  accumulation  of  exalted  wisdom  included  within  the  levels  of
ordinary beings.
- The first three exalted wisdoms are posited to realize:
   1. the emptiness of the inner
   2. the emptiness of the outer, and
   3. the emptiness of both inner and outer
- These three exalted wisdoms stop adherence to true existence of  utilizers
(the six primary consciousnesses),  the  objects  of  utilization  (the  six
objects), and their supports (the six powers).
- Boundaries of the accumulation of exalted  wisdom  begin  from  the  small
level of the path of accumulation onwards, through the  buddha  ground.  The
accumulation of exalted wisdom thus exists on the paths of ordinary  beings.

- Boundaries of the achieving of accumulation begin from the great level  of
SMQ of the path of preparation  through  the  end  of  the  continuum  of  a
sentient being.
- The achieving of the accumulation of exalted wisdom does not exist on  the
buddha ground. Buddha ground is the result of the achieving of  accumulation
of exalted wisdom. So the achieving of the accumulation  of  exalted  wisdom
does not exist on the buddha ground.
-  The reason that the 18th, 19th & 20th exalted wisdoms are posited on  the
buddha ground because they are supports  that  have  exhaustively  abandoned
the two obscurations as well as their latencies. They  are  posited  on  the
buddha ground by way of the meaning of being  self-arisen  which  means  the
last three exalted wisdoms are posited to be exalted wisdoms of a buddha.
- The 1st to 17th exalted  wisdoms  have  abandoned  some  of  the  manifest
afflictions or seeds that are objects of abandonment and are posited on  the
10th ground and below.
- The 5th, the emptiness of the great, is posited on  the  1st  ground,  but
there is no contradiction in saying that it exists on all ten  grounds.  The
fact that it is said to exist on the 1st ground does not mean that  it  does
not exist on the 2nd ground, the 3rd ground, and so forth. So  there  is  no
contradiction in saying that it is posited on the first ground.
- It is alright to posit 5th to 17th exalted wisdoms on all ten grounds as
these exalted wisdoms realizing the thirteen emptinesses exist on all ten
grounds.
- 1st and 4th exalted wisdoms are posited on the paths of ordinary beings:
the path of accumulation and the path of preparation
- The ten grounds are the same in being exalted wisdoms  directly  realizing
emptiness.
- In terms of whether or not they have  attained  the  clear  appearance  of
emptiness, there is no difference between them. The path of seeing  and  the
path of meditation are the same in being direct  realizations  of  emptiness
and in having attained clear appearance of emptiness.
- We can differentiate among the ten grounds in terms of  the  accumulations
of merit and exalted wisdom.
- During subsequent attainment, there is a difference in terms  of  the  two
accumulations as well as the mode in which phenomena are seen  as  illusory.
Due to that, the grounds have their corresponding capabilities in  terms  of
being able to abandon their corresponding objects of abandonment.
- Dharmadhatu being partless means that  emptiness  does  not  appear  to  a
direct cognizer as individual parts, like blue, yellow, etc. However, it  is
appropriate to divide it into parts from the  point  of  view  of  isolates.
There are many types of phenomena, so we can talk about  many  dharmadhatus,
such as the emptiness of a flower, the emptiness of a  vase,  etc.  Although
the various emptinesses do not differ in terms of entity, we can talk  about
different emptinesses in terms of their isolates.
- The dharmata of a sentient being and the  dharmata  of  a  buddha  do  not
differ even slightly as to whether or not they are realized  through  direct
cognition by the meditative equipoise of a learner arya.
- Ascertainment  is  generated  progressively  mean  in  dependence  on  the

obscurations in a successive way.
- During subsequent attainment there are  differences:  there  is  great  or

-  The  appearances  of  wrong  classes   contradicting   dependent-arisings

- The Mind-Only school asserts: the very truly existent phenomenon  that  is
the emptiness of other-powered phenomena’s emptiness of  apprehended-objects
and their apprehender-subjects being different substances is the  object  of
comprehension of an arya’s meditative equipoise.
- The Middle Way asserts: an arya’s meditative equipoise realizes  that  all
phenomena are empty of true existence. If it is the object of  knowledge  of
any knower, it is pervaded by being empty of true existence; just  like  the
appearance of a dream horse or elephant. Stating  that  this  non-conceptual
exalted wisdom also “is a knower” is a nature sign.
- “The subject—a non-conceptual exalted wisdom realizing  emptiness—observes
its object that is empty of true existence, because it  is  a  knower;  just
like the appearance of a dream horse or elephant.”
- If it is a knower, it is pervaded by observing its object  that  is  empty
of true existence.
- However, if it is a knower, it is not pervaded by realizing the  emptiness
of true existence.
- If it observes its object, it is pervaded by realizing  its  object,  that
is empty of true existence, because its object is empty of true existence.
- Signs and Reasonings - the modes of the  forward  pervasion,  the  reverse
pervasion, and the property of the subject.
- The sign “it is a knower” is a correct nature sign. If  it  is  a  correct
sign, it is not a sign that is not established, nor  a  contradictory  sign,
nor an indeterminate sign.
- If the object that is the object of observation  has  not  been  observed,
then the object is not realized. Hence it is not that the  property  of  the
subject is not established; and
   - since the concordant class is seen, it is not  an  indeterminate  sign,
and
   - since the discordant class is not  seen,  it  is  not  a  contradictory
sign.
- The existence of truly existent objects of knowledge will  be  refuted  on
the occasion of “No basis, no progression, no production. It is also not  an
indeterminate sign.
- If a  non-conceptual  exalted  wisdom  does  not  observe  the  object  of
observation, then it does not realize the object.
- In the syllogism, the subject, the non-conceptual exalted wisdom,  is  the
same as an exalted wisdom directly realizing emptiness.
    - The subject observes its  object  that  is  empty  of  true  existence
because it is a knower.
    - This sign is a nature sign. It is not an unestablished  sign,  because
the property of the subject is established.
    - It is not an  indeterminate  sign  because  the  forward-pervasion  is
established.
    - It is not  a  contradictory  sign  because  the  reverse-pervasion  is
established.
- The reason why the property of the subject is established is because  that
a non-conceptual exalted wisdom is a knower  is  established  by  the  valid
cognition of the other party.
- The forward-pervasion is established because: that a  knower  is  pervaded
by observing its object that is empty of true existence is realized  by  the
other party.
- The reverse-pervasion is established because the other party does not  see
the discordant class.
- The discordant class is: does not observe its  object  that  is  empty  of
true existence.
- The concordant class is:  observes  its  object  that  is  empty  of  true
existence.

“If it is a knower, it is pervaded by realizing its object that is empty  of
true existence.”
Our system: all phenomena are empty of true existence.
Cittamatrins refute: You, Middle Way proponents, assert that  all  phenomena
are empty of  true  existence.  The  emptiness  of  true  existence  of  all
phenomena  does  not  exist  truly.  Therefore,  all  phenomena  are   truly
existent. (What Cittamatrins is saying - if the emptiness of true  existence
of all phenomena is not true, then all phenomena  must  exist  truly.  Since
the emptiness of true existence of all phenomena does not exist truly,  then
all phenomena should exist truly, because their  emptiness  does  not  exist
truly. To them, since the emptiness of true existence of  all  phenomena  is
itself a phenomenon, it is also empty of true existence)
Our system: The victim, Devadatta, is analogous to true existence.
The killer, Yajus, is analogous to the emptiness of true existence.
If Devadatta is killed by Yajus, having  died,  Devadatta  would  not  arise
again.
Just as Devadatta is killed by Yajus,  true  existence  is  negated  by  the
emptiness of true existence.
Likewise, having refuted  that  the  emptiness  of  true  existence  of  all
phenomena is truly  existent,  it  is  not  necessary  to  assert  that  all
phenomena are truly existent.
-  Cittamatrins  do  not  assert  that  all  phenomena  are  empty  of  true
existence. For them, some phenomena are truly existent, while some  are  not
truly  existent.  For  Cittamatra,  other-powered  natures  and   thoroughly
established natures are truly existent, while imputational natures  are  not
truly existent.
- Middle Way definition on ultimate truth:  that  which  is  realized  in  a
manner of vanished dualistic appearances by the direct valid  cognizer  that
realizes it directly. Emptiness and ultimate truth are mutually inclusive.
-  Refuting  the  Mind-Only  system:  If  you  assert  that  truly  existent
emptiness is the observed object, then it  follows  that:  a  non-conceptual
exalted  wisdom  and  the  emptiness  of   apprehended-objects   and   their
apprehender-subjects of being different substances would either be:
- one substance or
- different substances
- Since even emptiness exists truly, you  must  assert  emptiness  to  be  a
functioning thing.
If you accept that a non-conceptual exalted  wisdom  and  the  emptiness  of
apprehended  objects  and  their  apprehender   subjects   being   different
substances would either be different substances or one substance:
- If they are different substances, then  emptiness  would  be  a  different
substance from other-powered natures. Hence that exalted wisdom would  be  a
possessor of an object of utilization that is a stand-alone  emptiness  that
is unrelated to other-powered natures, whereby  that  exalted  wisdom  would
not eliminate erroneous superimpositions regarding other-powered natures.”
- If they were one substance,  then  it  follows  that  emptiness  would  be
compounded. Then by meditating  on  emptiness  through  observing  emptiness
would not be able to abandon the seeds of the obscurations.  [By  meditating
on a compounded emptiness, one would not be able to  abandon  the  seeds  of
the  obscurations  because  that  emptiness  is  a  functioning  thing.   By
observing  a  functioning  thing,  one  cannot  abandon  the  seeds  of  the
obscurations.]
- In the context of this  debate,  one  should  abandon  the  seeds  of  the
obscurations by observing the  emptiness  of  apprehendeds  and  apprehender
being different substances. By observing a  functioning  thing,  one  cannot
abandon the seeds because the emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders  is
not a functioning thing.  It  is  a  non-affirming  negation.  If  it  is  a
functioning thing, it cannot be  a  non-affirming  negative.  A  functioning
thing is  either  a  positive  phenomenon  or  an  affirming  negation.  The
emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders  being  different  substances  is
not a functioning thing.
-In the Autonomy school, we talk  about  different  types  of  uninterrupted
paths that are direct antidotes to the objects of abandonment. In  order  to
abandon the conception of a self of persons,  for  example,  one  needs  the
uninterrupted path realizing selflessness.
- To abandon the conception of external objects, one needs  to  realize  the
emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different  substances  i.e.
one needs to generate an uninterrupted path realizing non-duality.
- A bodhisattva who wants to abandon the conception of true  existence  must
generate the antidote, the uninterrupted path  realizing  the  emptiness  of
true existence. None of these – selflessness of  persons,  non-duality,  and
emptiness of true existence – are functioning  things.  They  are  all  non-
affirming negatives.
- In order to abandon the seeds of the knowledge obscurations, the seeds  of
the conceptions of true existence, one must realize the  emptiness  of  true
existence. By observing  an  object  that  is  not  the  emptiness  of  true
existence, how  can  one  abandon  the  seeds  of  the  conception  of  true
existence? The direct antidote has to be the realization  of  the  emptiness
of  true  existence.  Merit  has  to  serve  as  backing  support  for  this
realization. Without directly realizing the  emptiness  of  true  existence,
there is no way to abandon the seeds of the conception of true existence.
- By doing prostrations, making  mandala  offerings,  etc.,  we  can  purify
negativities with the four  opponent  powers.  Even  if  the  four  opponent
powers  are  complete,  however,  one  cannot  abandon  the  seeds  of   the
obscurations. They can only be abandoned by an uninterrupted  path  that  is
the direct antidote.
- For example, there are two types of motivation: the causal  motivation  of
ignorance, and the motivation at the time of an action such as killing.  The
motivation at the time is related to the karma of killing and  that  can  be
purified through proper confession and restraint.  However,  ignorance,  the
causal motivation, cannot be purified through the four opponent  powers.  To
abandon it, one must realize emptiness. By observing  a  functioning  thing,
the seeds of obscurations cannot be purified, but by  observing  functioning
things it is possible to purify negativities.
- When we consider the emptiness  of  apprehendeds  and  apprehenders  being
different substances, this is a non-affirming negative.
- It follows that it is not a  non-affirming  negative,  because  it  is  an
affirming negative. That emptiness is  an  affirming  negative  because,  by
negating that apprehendeds and apprehenders are different  substances,  that
they are one substance is implied.
Our Middle Way system say to the Cittamatrins: Since  you  assert  emptiness
to be truly existent, it would have to be an affirming negative.
-Following that: emptiness is an  affirming  negation  because  by  negating
apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances, that they are  one
substance is implied. It follows that emptiness would be  a  truly  existent
functioning thing.
- By negating apprehendeds  and  apprehenders  being  different  substances,
their being one substance  is  not  implied.  This  is  true  for  both  the
Svatantrikas and the Cittamatrins.
- In the Cittamatra system, if the emptiness of true  existence  is  refuted
as being  truly  existent,  then  there  is  true  existence.  Likewise,  if
apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances are  refuted,  then
they are one substance.
- Therefore, by refuting that apprehendeds and  apprehenders  are  different
substances, that they are  one  substance  is  established.  Therefore,  the
emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances  would
be a functioning thing; therefore, Cittamatrins must assert emptiness to  be
a truly existent functioning thing. However, in reality, they do not  assert
that. The Cittamatrins say that this emptiness is a non-affirming negation.
- The emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different  substances
is  a  non-affirming  negative.  When  apprehendeds  and  apprehenders   are
established to be empty of being different substances, their being the  same
substance is  established.  This  is  not  implied  implicitly,  but  it  is
established. This emptiness is not  an  affirming  negative.  [Note:  in  an
affirming negative, another positive phenomenon is implied in place  of  its
negation.]
- “If you accept this, then you must assert  that,  if  they  are  different
substances, then it is a different  substance  from  other-powered  natures.
Hence  that  exalted  wisdom  would  be  the  possessor  of  an  object   of
utilization that is a stand-alone emptiness.”
      - This exalted wisdom mentioned here is the exalted  wisdom  realizing
the emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances.
      - The object of utilization of this exalted wisdom  is  the  emptiness
of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances.
- This exalted wisdom would possess an emptiness  that  is  not  related  to
other-powered natures.
- This emptiness that is the  object  of  that  exalted  wisdom  and  other-
powered natures would become unrelated. In general, they are related.
- We fling this undesirable consequence to the Cittamatrins: If you were  to
make such an assertion, you would end up with this undesirable  consequence,
that the exalted wisdom  “would  not  eliminate  erroneous  superimpositions
regarding other-powered natures.”
- In general, we say that negativities are faults. However,  they  have  one
excellent quality as they can be  purified  by  way  of  the  complete  four
opponent powers. Therefore, in order  to  purify  negativities,  we  do  not
necessarily have to  meditate  on  emptiness.  These  practices  can  purify
negativities. However, they are unable to abandon the seeds of  obscurations
i.e. they are unable to abandon obscurations from their root.  In  order  to
abandon the seeds, one must meditate on emptiness.

 2. one knows that one is blessed by them
 3. one enters 100 meditative stabilizations
 4. one moves 100 world systems

Attributes of the objects of abandonment

- Objects of abandonment of the path of seeing are:
   - 112 acquired afflictive obscurations
   - 108 acquired knowledge obscurations
- Objects of abandonment of the path of meditation are:
      - 108 knowledge obscurations
- They have the same divisions. The only difference is that the ones  to  be
abandoned by the path of seeing are acquired, while those  to  be  abandoned
by the path of meditation are innate.
The 112 acquired afflictive obscurations
    - 10 acquired afflictive obscurations of desire realm
    - 9 acquired afflictive obscurations of form realm
    - 9 acquired afflictive obscurations of formless realm
    - In each of  the  four  noble  truths,  there  are  these  28  acquired
      afflictive obscurations.
    - Therefore there are total of 112 acquired afflictive obscurations.

The 108 knowledge obscurations
    - 36 knowledge obscurations included within the desire realm
    - 36 knowledge obscurations included within the form realm
    - 36 knowledge obscurations included within the formless realm.
The 36 knowledge obscurations included in the  level  of  the  desire  realm
are:
    ­ 9 conceptions of apprehendeds to be turned away from
    ­ 9 conceptions of apprehendeds to be engaged in
    ­ 9 conceptions of apprehenders of substantial existence
    ­ 9 conceptions of apprehenders of imputed existence
- These 108 knowledge obscurations to be abandoned by the path of seeing.
- There are also 108 knowledge obscurations to be abandoned by the  path  of
meditation. There are the same divisions:
    - 36 knowledge obscurations included within the desire realm
    - 36 knowledge obscurations included within the form realm
    - 36 knowledge obscurations included within the formless realm.
- Each of the three categories consisting of the conceptions listed above.

The 16 afflictions  that  are  objects  to  be  abandoned  by  the  path  of
meditation:
- To count the 16 afflictions to be abandoned by  the  path  of  meditation,
among the 5 non-views: attachment, anger, ignorance, pride,  and  doubt,  we
omit doubt, thus there are 4 non-views:
 ­ Attachment
 ­ Anger
 ­ Ignorance
 ­ Pride
  - Then, from among the 5 views: the view of the transitory collection, the
  view of holding to an extreme, the view holding a bad view as supreme, the
  view holding bad ethics and modes of conduct as supreme, and wrong  views,
  we omit the last three, thus there are 2 views:
 ­ View of the transitory collection
 ­ View holding to an extreme
  - Therefore the six desire realm afflictions to be six: 4 non-views and  2
  views.
  -  For the form realm, we throw out anger, leaving 5 non-views.
  -  For the formless realm, we throw out anger, leaving 5 non-views.
  - We have a total of 16 afflictions: 6 desire realm  afflictions,  5  form
  realm afflictions, and 5 formless realm afflictions.
  - If we divide these extensively we have 46:
­ 6 of the desire realm;
­ 20 of the form realm: 5 in relation to each of the four concentrations;
­ 20 of the formless realm: 5 for each of the four levels.
If divided more extensively, there are 414.
- Each of the 46 can be divided into 9, making 414. This  is  because  there
  are 9 uninterrupted paths for each of the 46.
- Among the 9 levels of the conception of true  existence,  the  first,  the
  great of the great, is divided into  coarse  and  subtle.  The  coarse  is
  abandoned by the 1st ground, the subtle by the 2nd ground, and so  on.  In
  that way we say that the 9 degrees of the conception of true existence are
  abandoned by the ten grounds.
- Upon attaining the uninterrupted path of the  path  of  seeing,  one  will
move on to attain the liberated path of the path of  seeing.  After  arising
from that liberated path,  there  is  a  period  of  subsequent  attainment,
belonging  to  the  path  of  seeing.  After  that,  one  enters  meditative
equipoise. When one attains  the  direct  antidote  to  the  objects  to  be
abandoned by the path of meditation,  one  attains  the  uninterrupted  path
included within the path of meditation.
- The uninterrupted path and liberated path are each one instant;  there  is
no need to abide in them for a long time.  After  the  liberated  path,  one
arises in subsequent attainment. The length  of  subsequent  attainment  can
vary; it could last a very long time
- Right after the path of preparation, the acquired afflictive obscurations
and the acquired knowledge obscurations together with their seeds are
removed by an uninterrupted path of the path of seeing. A liberated path of
the path of seeing and a true cessation of the acquired obstructions are
then actualized.
-  It is said that, through the nine steps of the path of meditation, the
seeds of the 16 afflictive emotions and the seeds of the 108 knowledge
obscurations, which are to be abandoned by the path of meditation, are
gradually abandoned. Finally, in dependence on the uninterrupted path at
the end of the continuum [of existence as a sentient being] the innate
afflictive emotions and the innate knowledge obscurations are
simultaneously abandoned. In the next moment, highest enlightenment is
attained.

      any position possible; in other words, be flexible.
- These 12 excellent qualities of training are  to  overcome  attachment  to
food, to clothing, and to one’s abode.  One  trains  in  accepting  whatever
food one receives, wearing whatever clothes one finds,  etc.,  and  in  this
way  one  is  able  to  overcome  attachment.  People  who  are  not  Dharma
practitioners  are  very  attached  to  food,   clothes,   and   reputation.
Practitioners of Dharma practice accept loss with regard to  food,  clothes,
and reputation.

“Having completely abandoned those ten,  “Familiarity”  and  so  forth,  the
fifth ground is perfectly attained,  because  its  excellent  qualities  are
completed without exception.”
- A bodhisattva ripens sentient beings in various ways  as  sentient  beings
are of  diverse  dispositions.  Some  hold  erroneous  views  and  are  very
difficult to subdue.
- The 5th ground is called “Difficult to Overcome” because a bodhisattva  on
this ground can subdue difficult sentient beings who could  not  be  subdued
on the previous grounds.
-  The 5th ground, Difficult to Overcome, is attained when the 10  qualities
are completely abandoned by the 10 types  of  complete  trainings  conjoined
with great compassion  and  the  wisdom  realizing  emptiness,  the  implied
opposite qualities, the antidotes.
 1. To associate with the ordained and so forth for the purpose of gain and
    so forth due to craving to gain and honor
 2. not reveal faithful laity to others out of craving to the laity
 3. staying in cities and so forth with  many  people  due  to  excessively
    desiring disquiet
 4. to praise oneself, for the sake of gain and honor
 5. to criticize others, for the sake of gain and honor
 6. especially increase the ten non-virtuous paths of actions in dependence
    on those
 7. to be conceited due to hearing and so forth, and not bowing to  others,
    oneself being puffed-up and scorning others
 8. due to erroneous adherence to what is to be adopted and what is  to  be
    discarded, having reversed virtue and non-virtue, to strongly adhere
 9. faulty intelligence, wrong view, the view of the transitory collection,
    and so forth
10. to turn towards all afflictions such as attachment

 - The wrong view of deprecating actions and their results as  well  as  its
   seeds have already been abandoned on the 1st ground. This wrong  view  is
   being included in the 112 afflictive obscurations to be abandoned by  the
   path of seeing.
 - For the 16 afflictive  obscurations  to  be  abandoned  by  the  path  of
   meditation,  there  are  6  desire  realm  afflictions,  5   form   realm
   afflictions, and 5 formless realm afflictions.
 - Among non-views, doubt is not included in these 16; and among views-  the
   view holding bad views as supreme, the view holding bad modes of  conduct
   as supreme, and wrong view are not  included.  Thus  wrong  view  is  not
   included in the 16 afflictive obscurations to be abandoned by the path of
   meditation.
 - Since wrong view is abandoned on the 1st ground, it  is  being  discussed
   here on the 5th ground in relation to the wrong views of the trainees  in
   this bodhisattva’s retinue.
 - An alternative is given: the view holding to an extreme is  not  a  wrong
   view, but is designated or labeled as a wrong view. When it is  explained
   definitively to be a wrong view, you should take  it  that  the  antidote
   that casts to a distance has been generated.
 - There are four types of antidotes  to  wrong  view:  (1)  antidotes  that
   abandon, (2) antidotes that are bases,  (3)  antidotes  that  cast  to  a
   distance, and (4) antidotes that repudiate.
 - In relation to the 4th and 5th trainings- to praise oneself and criticize
   others- those who do not practice Dharma become happy when  they  receive
   praise, and  become  upset  when  they  receive  criticism.  When  Dharma
   practitioners are praised, they do not find any  cause  to  be  happy  or
   excited, because when receiving praise, there is the risk that pride will
   increase. Likewise, when practitioners are criticized, they do  not  find
   any cause to  become  upset,  because  by  receiving  criticism  one  can
   recognize one’s own faults.

“The sixth ground-on which the six excellent qualities “generosity”  and  so
forth have  been  completed  and  the  six  faults  have  been  abandoned-is
perfectly attained, because it is  attained  by  means  of  eliminating  its
faults and completing the excellent qualities.”
- The 6th ground is known as  “Approaching”  because  one  contemplates  the
twelve links of dependent arising on the 6th ground.  By  contemplating  the
forward process of the twelve links of dependent  arising,  one  understands
that one approaches cyclic  existence,  and  by  contemplating  the  reverse
process of the twelve links of dependent arising, one understands  that  one
approaches nirvana.
- The bodhisattva abiding on the 6th ground should  perfectly  complete  the
six qualities by  the  12  complete  trainings,  having  completed  the  six
antidotes (the six perfections) and having  abandoned  the  six  objects  of
abandonment. Alternatively, due to the six perfections  being  conjoined  by
the mind of enlightenment, the first three  faults  are  abandoned;  due  to
having completed the perfection of generosity  the  last  three  faults  are
abandoned
(1-6) the attainment of the surpassing practice of the six perfections,  the
six objects of abandonment  are  abandoned  [the  surpassing  perfection  of
wisdom is attained but this does not mean that the perfection of  wisdom  is
attained], because,
(7-8)  by  completing  the  perfection  of  ethics  and  the  perfection  of
concentration,  desire  to  attain  the  states  of  hearers  and   solitary
realizers are abandoned;
(9)  by  completing  the  perfection  of  patience,  terror  regarding   not
observing entitiness of all phenomena is abandoned,
(10) by completing the perfection of generosity, dismay when begged from  by
mendicants is abandoned,
(11) by completing the perfection of joyous effort that is enthusiastic  and
joyful  regarding  generosity,  dislike  to  engage  in  giving   away   all
possessions voluntarily is abandoned
(12) by  completing  the  perfection  of  wisdom,  the  mind  of  abandoning
mendicants even though oneself is destitute is abandoned
- In dependence on having completed the perfection of concentration  on  the
5th ground, one completes the perfection of wisdom on this  6th  ground.  In
dependence on having attained a fully qualified common calm  abiding,  after
that  one  attains   special   insight.   Similarly,   the   perfection   of
concentration  comes  before  the  perfection  of  wisdom.  The   surpassing
practice of the perfection of concentration is attained on the  5th  ground,
and the surpassing practice of the perfection of concentration  is  attained
on the 6th ground. However, it is not that  there  is  no  practice  of  the
perfections of concentration and wisdom before those  grounds.  On  the  5th
and 6th grounds, they become surpassing practices, and their  order  on  the
5th and 6thgrounds is in the order of generating calm  abiding  and  special
insight.

“By severing the twenty faults such as  apprehending  a  self  and  sentient
beings, the seventh ground is attained, because it is attained by  means  of
completing twenty qualities of excellence.”
- The 7th ground is called “Gone Afar”  because  through  having  cultivated
the paths for a long time, the  bodhisattva  comes  to  the  end  of  coarse
exertion. Coarse exertion exists on the 7th  ground,  but  not  on  the  8th
ground. In that sense, the bodhisattva has “gone afar.”
- The bodhisattva abiding  on  the  7th  ground,  Gone  Afar,  should  fully
complete these 20 qualities by having separated from the 20  faults  through
full abandonment.
- The bodhisattva realized the 7th  ground  by  the  20  types  of  complete
trainings  conjoined  with  great  compassion  and  the   wisdom   realizing
emptiness
- [twenty abandonments] Strong adherence which views:—
 1. self,
 2. sentient beings,
 3. life,
 4. persons,
 5. annihilation
 6. permanence,
 7. signs,
 8. causes,
 9. aggregates,
10. constituents,
11. sources,
     12-13 abidance in and attachment to the three realms,
  14. discouraged mind,
15. Buddha,
16. Dharma,
17. sangha,
18. ethics
19. dispute about emptiness, holding emptiness as causing fear of things
20. holding to expressing the empty and the conventional as  contradictions
    to expressing contradictions

- [twenty antidotes] The bodhisattva on the 7th ground has cultivated
(1-3)  the  correct  knowing  by  direct   cognition   of   the   emptiness,
signlessness, and wishlessness doors of liberation, the  emptiness  of  true
existence of the three- entity, cause, and result- he respectively  abandons
the apprehension of the self of persons, sentient beings, and life.
(4) Regarding the ten virtuous paths of action such as  abandoning  killing,
by realizing with direct cognition  that  the  three  circles  such  as  the
object to be killed are not  observed  as  truly  existent,  abandoning  the
apprehension of a single independent person
(5) by possessing a merciful heart which observes all  beings  tormented  by
suffering in many rebirths, abandoning the apprehension of  sentient  beings
as annihilated,
(6) since it would be suitable to observe  truly  existent  things  if  they
were to exist, by possessing the lack of [such] observation, abandoning  the
apprehension of sentient beings as permanent,
(7) by realizing all phenomena of samsara  and  nirvana  as  just  equal  in
being empty of true existence, abandoning apprehension of the signs of  what
is to be adopted and what is to be abandoned as truly existent,
(8) by realizing all vehicles as one final Mahayana  (Great  Vehicle)  mode,
abandoning the apprehension of the final cause as three different  lineages,

(9)  by  fully  knowing  phenomena  of  name  and  form  are  not   produced
ultimately,  abandoning  the  apprehension  of  the  aggregates   as   truly
existent,
(10) by realizing the patience of definitely realizing the  profound  Dharma
that is emptiness, abandoning the apprehension of the constituents as  truly
existent,
(11) in dependence on strongly teaching all objects  of  knowledge  included
within the sources to be apprehended objects and apprehender  subjects  that
lack  true  existence  by  means  of   Mahayana   method,   abandoning   the
apprehension of the sources as truly existent,
(12)  in  dependence  on  cutting  all  conceptions  that   apprehend   true
existence, abandoning the apprehension of objects of abidance in  the  three
realms as truly existent,
(13) by the three-being  without  conceptions  of  improper  attention  that
apprehend signs of cleanliness,  happiness,  and  so  forth,  rejecting  the
five, view of  the  transitory  collection  and  so  forth,  and  abandoning
afflictions such  as  attachment-abandoning  attachment  to  objects  to  be
rejected in the three realms as truly existent,
(14) by cultivating calm-abiding that has attained  mastery  over  excellent
qualities due to special calm-abiding, abandoning a  discouraged  mind  that
thinks, “I cannot attain higher attributes”,
(15) by enacting the special wisdom included within special-insight and  the
skillful  means  that  knows  dependent-arisings  as  illusions,  abandoning
adherence to the buddha as truly existent,
(16) by being free from the conceit that views  the  object  of  observation
and finely pacifying the mind that directly realizes the mode  of  abidance,
abandoning apprehension of phenomena as truly existent,
(17) by possessing unimpeded exalted  wisdom  towards  forms  and  so  forth
included  within  objects  of  knowledge  that  are  modes  and   varieties,
abandoning adherence to the sangha as truly existent,
(18) by possessing the realization of the truth as not  being  an  abode  of
strong adherence anywhere, abandoning adherence to the true existence  which
views ethics,
(19) by possessing travel to all buddha lands  simultaneously  just  as  one
wishes  for  the  sake  of  finely  discriminating   phenomena,   abandoning
erroneous disputing about emptiness, and
(20) by exhibiting the attainment of dominion over  one's  body  everywhere,
in accordance with trainees for the sake of taking care of  sentient  beings
who adhere to objects of observation as truly existent,  abandoning  holding
to expressing the empty and dependent-arisings as contradictions.

“The eighth ground is completely trained by way of either of  the  two  sets
of four, knowing the mind of all sentient beings and so forth, because  they
eliminate its faults and fully complete the excellent qualities.”
- The  8th  ground  is  called  “Immovable”  because  on  this  ground,  the
bodhisattva has pacified  coarse  exertion,  and  is  not  moved  by  coarse
exertion.
- The 8th ground, Immovable, is experienced  through  8  types  of  complete
trainings  conjoined  with  great  compassion  and  the   wisdom   realizing
emptiness:

  1. To know just as it is with a single mind, the mental behavior  of  all
     sentient beings, being with attachment, being  free  from  attachment,
     and so forth
 2. to play in  the  world  systems  of  the  ten  directions  through  the
    clairvoyance of magical emanation of play
 3. to fully transform with extensive  roots  of  virtue  one’s  supporting
    buddha land into the entity of gold, vaidurya, and so forth
 4. through having fully investigated the words and  the  meanings  of  the
    Dharma in all ways, to please the Buddha with the three doors  for  the
    sake of that
 5. by principally generating  the  divine  eye  concordant  with  the  ten
    strengths, knowing faculties that are supreme and not supreme
 6. to completely purify the faults of the supported sentient beings of the
    buddha land where one will become enlightened
 7. to abide like an illusion in all arising from absorption  and  entering
    into absorption
 8. having seen the welfare of all sentient beings when having  apprehended
    where they will be reborn, to fully uphold a  premeditated  rebirth  by
    way of compassion and prayers

1. The 1st training has to do with knowing the mental behavior  of  sentient
   beings, those with attachment, those free of attachment, and so forth.
2. With the 2nd training, the bodhisattva has the  clairvoyance  of  magical
   emanation of play,  and  is  able  to  benefit  others  in  that  way.  A
   bodhisattva benefits sentient beings in various ways, so it is a bit like
   play. A bodhisattva takes different forms or manifestations, for  example
   teaching the Dharma in one way to some sentient beings, and  teaching  in
   another way to other sentient beings, and in that way, he takes  care  of
   them in diverse manners.
3. The 3rd point is about roots of virtue  transforming  one’s  buddha  land
   and so forth. On the 8th ground,  a  bodhisattva  accumulates  merit  and
   performs various activities, in order to establish the buddha land  where
   he will attain enlightenment in the future.
4.  A bodhisattva “through having  fully  investigated  the  words  and  the
   meanings of the Dharma in all ways, to please the Buddha with  the  three
   doors for the sake of that” upholds  the  Dharma  and  also  teaches  the
   Dharma to others. One tries to benefit  others  with  body,  speech,  and
   mind; for example, with a good motivation, speaking Dharma words  and  so
   forth.  In  this  way,  the  buddhas  are  pleased.  In  particular,  for
   Buddhists, we go for refuge to the Buddha, Dharma,  and  Sangha,  and  we
   need to make sure that our refuge is not simply words.  If our refuge  is
   just words, without being accompanied by right actions of  body,  speech,
   and mind, then this is not a pure refuge.  In  addition  to  knowing  the
   words of the Dharma, we also need to know the meanings of the Dharma  and
   to actualize those meanings; otherwise, if we just recite the words,  but
   engage in erroneous behavior with our body, speech, and mind, that is not
   pure refuge.
5. The 5th point is a bodhisattva on the 8th ground  has  not  attained  the
   ten strengths or powers of a buddha, but can have similitudes  of  these.
   With the 5th training, a bodhisattva generates the  divine  eye  that  is
   concordant with the ten strengths, and in that way he can know  faculties
   that are supreme and faculties that are not supreme.
6. The 6th point refers to ripening sentient beings of the buddha  land.  At
   this point, a bodhisattva is not yet  a  Buddha,  but  can  make  various
   prayers, such as saying: “Unless all the trainees in my pure land  attain
   the five clairvoyances in the future,  I  will  not  attain  buddhahood.”
   While on the learners’ path, bodhisattvas enact sentient beings’  welfare
   through explaining the dharma, but they also make prayers such  as  these
   to explain their aspirations to others.  In  that  way,  sentient  beings
   become ripened. One does not simply establish a  buddha  land.  A  buddha
   land is not empty. Sentient beings will be reborn in that buddha land.  A
   bodhisattva,  through  such  activities,  ripens  sentient   beings   and
   completely purifies the faults of the supported sentient  beings  of  the
   buddha land where he will become enlightened.
7. On the 7th point, “Arising” can be interpreted as subsequent  attainment,
   and “entering” as meditative equipoise. During  meditative  equipoise  on
   the 8th ground, a bodhisattva directly realizes  the  emptiness  of  true
   existence, and upon arising, during subsequent attainment, all phenomenon
   appear as empty of true existence, like illusions.
8. On the 8th point, a bodhisattva  is  able  to  see  the  dispositions  of
   sentient  beings  and  understand  where  they   will   be   reborn.   By
   understanding this, a bodhisattva will uphold a pre-meditated rebirth  by
   way of compassion and prayers in order to benefit sentient beings.

“The ninth ground is completely trained  by  way  of  the  twelve,  such  as
infinite  prayers  that  accomplish  whatever  wished  for,   because   they
eliminate its faults and fully complete the excellent qualities. “
- The 9th ground is called “Good Intelligence” because  the  bodhisattva  on
the 9th ground has the individual correct knowledges [as in the  2nd  &  3rd
complete trainings].
- the 9th ground: Good Intelligence, is actualized by 12 types  of  complete
trainings through the attributes which have completely trained by  means  of
being fully  conjoined  with  great  compassion  and  the  wisdom  realizing
emptiness

 1. By completing the six perfections, accomplishing infinite prayers
 2. by attaining the individual correct knowledge of definitive words which
    knows the various  conventions  for  propounding  Dharma,  knowing  the
    languages of all sentient beings,  gods  and  so  forth  [Through  this
    individual correct knowledge, the bodhisattva  teaches  the  Dharma  to
    sentient beings]
 3. by attaining the individual correct knowledge of  confidence  that  has
    mastery over profound  expressions,  inexhaustible  confidence  like  a
    river regarding teaching the Dharma
 4. to enter into the womb of a mother extolled by all beings due to  being
    free from all the faults of a woman
 5. to reside in an excellent caste, the king and Brahmin  castes,  and  so
    forth
 6. birth in an excellent clan [literally: bone (from the father)], such as
    the sun, sugarcane and so forth,
 7. maternal lineage, excellent well-related relatives, mother and so forth
    [talking about a continuous lineage of well-related relatives,  related
    to the mother’s lineage]
8. an excellent  independent  retinue  subdued  by  oneself  and  placed  in
   enlightenment [This  refers  to  the  bodhisattva  having  a  retinue  of
   trainees who have a Dharma relation  with  him.  They  are  the  intended
   trainees of this bodhisattva]
9. rebirths praised by Indra, lords of humans, and so forth [This refers  to
   rebirths praised by gods such as Indra, Brahma, kings, presidents,  prime
   ministers, and so forth]
10. having been urged by the buddhas, the sons of  the  gods  of  the  pure
    places, and so forth, to  renounce  being  a  householder  [“Renouncing
    being a householder”  can  mean  taking  ordination,  or  it  can  mean
    definitely emerging from the faults of a household]
11. A bodhi tree like a wish-granting jewel due to giving rise to all  that
    is needed and desired, sitting at the heart of enlightenment, Ashvadtha
    of this teacher, Nagavriksha of Maitreya, and so forth
12. to bring to full completion all excellent qualities that are the entity
    of the strengths of a buddha and the buddha-Dharma and so forth

- On the 4th point: A bodhisattva enters the womb of a woman.  The  womb  is
free of the faults of a womb. “Free from the faults of a  woman”  refers  to
the faults of the womb of the woman. For example, due to  the  faults  of  a
womb, a child may not be conceived properly, the child may become sick,  and
so on. This explanation can be found in the context of  the  explanation  on
carrying death, intermediate state,  and  rebirth  into  the  three  exalted
bodies of a buddha. In the context of rebirth, it discusses the faults of  a
woman as well as those of a man. For example,  a  woman’s  womb  can  be  of
various shapes and faults can arise on account of that, or due to  the  ovum
or sperm.
    - The point here is that the bodhisattva takes rebirth by  entering  the
      womb of a woman who is free of all the faults of a woman. For example,
      if the woman is sick, that is a fault. If the womb is  defective,  the
      child cannot be properly conceived. Also  the  ovum  has  to  be  non-
      defective. Such a woman, who  is  without  faults,  is  “extolled”  or
      praised by all beings; an  example  would  be  the  mother  of  Prince
      Siddharta/Shakyamuni Buddha.
- On 5th point, “To reside in an  excellent  caste,  the  king  and  brahmin
castes, and so forth” refer to at the time of Shakyamuni  Buddha,  the  king
caste was considered the  highest.  In  the  future,  when  Maitreya  Buddha
comes, he will be born into the brahmin caste as it will be  considered  the
highest caste at that time. “And so forth” allows for  other  possibilities,
because we do not know what the highest caste will be in the future.
- On 6th point, “Clan” literally means bone and this has to  do  with  one’s
lineage. In the past, clan was regarded as very important in Tibet. This  is
because if a woman from one family marries into another  family  of  similar
clans, the relationships are not so complicated. If  the  lineage  is  good,
then one’s conduct is also good. From this point of view,  it  is  important
to have a good lineage, or clan as those from a good lineage  seem  to  have
better conduct and better minds.
    - “Sun, sugarcane,” and so forth is related to the Shakya  clan.  A  man
      from the Shakya clan  gave  rise  to  some  semen,  and  two  Brahmins
      collected it. One portion of it was nourished by  sunlight,  and  gave
      rise to a human being, while the other part was placed in a sugarcane,
      from which a human being was born. These are examples of birth  in  an
      excellent clan.
- On 11th point, “This teacher” refers to Shakyamuni  Buddha.  This  teacher
meditated in the vajra posture under the bodhi tree. That tree  is  probably
called Ashvadtha. When Maitreya  Buddha  appears  in  the  future,  he  will
probably meditate under the Nagavriksha tree.
- The 10th ground is  called  “Cloud  of  Doctrine”  or  “Cloud  of  Dharma”
because when clouds gather in the sky, rain will fall and  cause  the  crops
to grow. Likewise,  the  excellent  qualities  of  a  bodhisattva  are  like
clouds. From these cloud-like  qualities  of  a  bodhisattva,  the  rain  of
Dharma will fall. This rain of Dharma causes the excellent qualities of  the
trainees to develop.
- For the 10th ground, the complete trainings of the  10th  ground  are  not
explained. The complete training for the 1st through  the  9th  grounds  are
explained while for the 10th ground,  instead  of  explaining  the  complete
trainings, the characteristics or entity of the 10th ground  are  explained.
Although they are not explained, one understands that the  10th  ground  has
its corresponding complete trainings because  the  complete  trainings  have
been explained for the previous nine grounds.
- The 1st through the 9th grounds are counted as one and known  collectively
as “the bodhisattva’s grounds.”
- The 1st nine grounds are to be passed through  initially  by  knowing  and
seeing them, and later they are passed  through  by  abiding.  After  having
abided on them, then one passes through them.
- Having passed through those, one move on to the 10th ground  and,  in  the
sutra, this 10th bodhisattva ground is called the buddha ground, but is  not
the buddha ground. The reason for calling  it  the  buddha  ground  is  that
during subsequent attainment on the 10th ground, bodhisattvas are  equal  to
buddhas in engaging in the exalted activities for sentient beings.

      - There are 9 grounds that are to be passed through. These consist  of
8 Hinayana grounds, and 1 is known as the “bodhisattva grounds.”
1st ground - The four levels of the path  of  preparation  of  the  hearers,
solitary realizers, etc. These are the grounds of lineage.
2nd ground - The realizations of serialist approachers to the first  result,
i.e. to stream-enterer. These are called the grounds of the eighth.
3rd ground - The realizations of serialist stream-enterers;  these  are  the
grounds of seeing.
4th ground - The realizations of once-returners; these are  the  grounds  of
diminishment.
5th  ground  -  The  realizations  of  non-returners  are  the  grounds   of
separation from attachment.
6th ground - The  realizations  of  hearer  foe-destroyers;  these  are  the
grounds of realizing completion.
7th ground - The realizations of the three approachers, i.e. approachers  to
once-returner, non-returner, and foe-destroyer; these  are  the  grounds  of
hearers.
8th ground - The clear realizations of solitary realizer  aryas;  these  are
the grounds of solitary realizers.
These are the eight Hinayana grounds.
9th ground - The nine bodhisattva grounds, counted as one:  the  grounds  of
bodhisattvas.
Thus altogether, there are nine grounds to be passed through.

1. The four  levels  of  the  path  of  preparation,  of  hearers,  solitary
   realizers,  etc.,  are  the  grounds  of  lineage,   because   they   are
   realizations of ordinary beings that are unmistaken regarding the lineage
   that have been correctly adopted by the Hinayana.
2. The second are “the grounds of the eighth” because  we  count  the  eight
   persons –
  i. Approacher to stream-enterer
 ii. Abider in the result of stream-enterer
iii. Approacher to once-returner
 iv. Abider in the result of once-returner
  v. Approacher to non-returner

viii. Abider in the result of foe-destroyer
-  if we count [in the reverse order],  starting  with  the  abider  in  the
result of foe-destroyer, the 8th would be the approacher to  stream-enterer.
So from that point of view, the serialist approacher to  stream  enterer  is
“the eighth.”
- In Gyeltsabje’s explanation, “serialist,” means the  serialist  approacher
to stream-enterer, and not just any kind of stream enterer.
-  If we talk about approachers and abiders in general,  without  specifying
“serialist,” they could be those who abandon simultaneously, in  which  case
the approacher to stream-enterer would not necessarily be the eighth.
-   The  term  “serialist”  is  significant.  If  we  do  not  consider  the
serialist, we could have a  person  who  abandons  simultaneously  and  this
person starts as an approacher to stream-enterer, becomes an abider  in  the
result of stream-enterer, then becomes an approacher to  foe-destroyer,  and
then an abider in the result of foe-destroyer. In this case there  are  only
four “items,” not eight, therefore “serialist” is significant.
3. “The realizations of the serialist stream-enterers  are  the  grounds  of
   seeing, because they are realizations distinguished by  having  abandoned
   the three entwinements.” The significance of the word “seeing” is that it
   refers to the path of seeing.
4. “The realizations of  once-returners—are  the  grounds  of  diminishment,
   because they are realizations of an abider in the result distinguished by
   having largely separated from attachment…” An abider  in  the  result  of
   once-returner has largely separated from attachment to the desire  realm;
   in  that  way,  the  attachment  has   “diminished,”   therefore,   their
   realizations are called “grounds of diminishment.”
5. “The realizations of non-returners are the  grounds  of  separation  from
   attachment because they are realizations  of  an  abider  in  the  result
   distinguished by having  abandoned  the  partial  concordances  with  the
   bottom.” This mean that they have separated from attachment to the desire
   realm.
6. “The realizations of the hearer foe-destroyers  themselves  because  they
   are realizations of the small vehicle distinguished by  having  abandoned
   the partial concordances with the upper.” They have realized the stage of
   non-learning.
7-8. The 7th and 8th refer to the realizations of the three  approachers  to
the results of hearers and to the realizations of solitary realizers.
9. The first through 9th bodhisattva grounds  are  collectively  called  the
   “grounds of bodhisattvas.” This is the 9th among the nine grounds  to  be
   passed through.

- Immediately after indicating having passed through  the  nine  grounds  in
that manner in sutra, that explained to be one abiding on the buddha  ground
who knows prayers with mastery  over  the  actions  of  great  clairvoyance,
immediately after which is the buddha ground, is  to  be  known  as  a  10th
-grounder bodhisattva, because in terms of a  bodhisattva  abiding  on  that
ground. A tenth-grounder “should be expressed as only  a  buddha  who  is  a
bodhisattva; he is not a perfect complete buddha” occurs in sutra.
- “‘Prayer’ refers to attaining the special ten  powers.  A  bodhisattva  of
this stage has attained a similitude of the buddha’s ten strengths. The  ten
powers are:
1. Power over life
2. Power over the mind
3. Power over necessities
4. Power over action (karma)
5. Power over rebirth
6. Power over belief
7. Power over prayer
8. Power over magical emanation
9. Power over exalted wisdom
10. Power over Dharma.
- Example, a bodhisattva being able to control  his  rebirth:  this  is  the
power over rebirth.
- A bodhisattva has the power over karma, and over the mind, whereas we  are
under the influence of afflictions and have no control over our minds.
- A bodhisattva also has power over life can control the time of death.  For
us, death is definite, but we don’t know when we will die; the time  of  our
death is uncertain.
- A bodhisattva enters the 10th ground by way  of  a  liberated  path.  This
lasts one instant and  immediately  after  which  he  arises  in  subsequent
attainment. During  the  subsequent  attainment  of  the  10th  ground,  the
bodhisattvas are expressed as being buddhas.
- From Maitreya’s  Uttaratantra:  “This  mode  of  the  bodhisattvas  during
subsequent attainment is seen in  the  world  to  be  just  equal  with  the
tathagatas with respect to correctly liberating sentient beings.”
- Having pacified coarse exertion in enacting the welfare  of  others,  they
are taught to be  equal  with  the  buddhas  in  just  engaging  in  exalted
activities for all sentient beings, whereby they are expressed as buddhas.
- This  explanation  should  be  understood  as  being  in  the  context  of
subsequent attainment of the 10th  ground.  Although  the  bodhisattvas  are
expressed as buddhas, they are not buddhas because these  bodhisattvas  have
not  exhaustively  abandoned  the  levels  of  imprints  of  ignorance,  the
uncontaminated actions arisen from the elaborations of signs,  a  body  that
is the nature of mind,  and  inconceivable  transformed  death-transference.
Hence, they have not attained the pure blissful truth  body  -  the  exalted
body of a Buddha -, an excellent  self,  and  permanent  perfection  thereby
they are not referred  to  as  “complete  buddhas  “–  i.e.  they  have  not
attained non-abiding nirvana.

- Accumulation of antidotes,  is  closely  related  to  the  four  types  of
conceptions in the context of the Mahayana path of preparation.
- The subject - regarding the Mahayana paths of seeing  and  familiarization
-eight types of antidotes are said to exist, because there are eight by  way
of enumeration of acting to thoroughly pacify the  eight  conceptualizations
of apprehendeds and apprehenders, the objects of abandonment.
-   The  eight  conceptualizations  of  apprehendeds  and  apprehenders  are
counted by looking at:
1. conceptions of apprehendeds of the thoroughly afflicted class
2. conceptions of apprehendeds of the completely pure class
3. conceptions of apprehenders of substantial existence
4. conceptions of apprehenders of imputed existence
- The eight conceptualizations of apprehendeds and apprehenders  are  a  set
of  four  acquired   conceptualizations   and   a   set   of   four   innate
conceptualizations.
- The antidotes to the objects of abandonment by  the  path  of  seeing  and
path of meditation are to be known in dependence on aspects of both the  two
truths. This is because:
    - (i) it has been  taught  that  asserting  the  locus  from  where  one
      definitely emerges  and  that  which  acts  to  definitely  emerge  as
      existing  conventionally  is  the  antidote  to  the  conceptions   of
      apprehendeds, the objects to be abandoned by the path  of  seeing,  in
      dependence on conventional truths and
            The locus [locus defined as a  particular  position,  point,  or
place] from where:
      - one definitely emerges is the thoroughly afflicted class and
      - that which acts to definitely emerge refer to the  completely  pure
        class.
      - These two classes exist conventionally.
      - The conceptions of apprehendeds, the objects to be abandoned by the
        path of seeing is the conceptions of apprehendeds that are only the
        acquired ones.
    - (ii) it has been taught that refuting them as existing  ultimately  is
      to be known as the antidote to the conceptions  of  apprehendeds,  the
      objects to be abandoned by the path of meditation,  in  dependence  on
      ultimate truths.
      - “Them” refers to the two things in the first point: the locus  from
        where one definitely emerges, and that  which  acts  to  definitely
        emerge. These are refuted  as  existing  ultimately.  That  is  the
        antidote to the conceptions of  apprehendeds,  the  objects  to  be
        abandoned by the path of meditation.  This  refers  to  the  innate
        conceptions. These are antidotes in dependence on ultimate truth.
- The acquired conceptions and their seeds are  abandoned  by  the  path  of
seeing, and the innate conceptions and their  seeds  are  abandoned  by  the
path of meditation.  The  conceptions  here  refer  to  the  four  types  of
conceptions of apprehendeds and conceptions of apprehenders.
- It is appropriate to divide the antidotes  into  eight  types  by  way  of
divisions of the discordant classes, since the seeds  of  the  acquired  and
the seeds of the innate of the two conceptions of apprehendeds  (called  the
conceptualizations of apprehendeds) and the two conceptions of  apprehenders
are  individually  abandoned  on  the  paths  of   seeing   and   meditation
respectively.

THE ACHIEVING OF DEFINITE EMERGENCE
- Having collected these accumulations as  explained  in  that  manner,  one
will definitely emerge. Therefore, the fourth is the achieving  of  definite
emergence.
- Know that  this  nature  of  the  eight  types  of  loci  from  where  one
definitely emerges is the achieving of definite emergence,  the  objects  to
be practiced, because the loci from where one definitely emerges are  to  be
known by means of the 8 generalities and particularities.
- The eight achievings of definite emergence are the  loci  from  where  one
definitely emerges.
- The objects to which one definitely  emerges  are  the  three  objects  of
intent.
- The 1st of the eight is the objects of intent but this does not  refer  to
the objects to which one definitely emerges. We are actually  talking  about
the achieving of definite emergence  of  the  objects  of  intent,  not  the
objects of intent. This is a locus from where one definitely emerges.
- achieving of definite emergence of the exalted knower of all  aspects:  it
is a locus from where one definitely emerges, while the  exalted  knower  of
all aspects is the object to which one definitely emerges.
- If we talk about objects  of  intent,  these  are  objects  to  which  one
definitely emerges.
- If we talk about the achieving of definite emergence  of  the  objects  of
intent, this is the practice that allows us to  get  there,  the  loci  from
where one definitely emerges.
- The welfare of sentient beings is  the  object  to  which  one  definitely
emerges, but the achieving of definite emergence of the welfare of  sentient
beings is what we definitely emerge from.
- Definition of achieving of  definite  emergence  -  a  yoga  of  the  pure
grounds that undoubtedly issues forth an exalted knower of all aspects.
- The objects to which one definitely  emerges  are  the  three  objects  of
intent. This means that without doubt, an  exalted  knower  of  all  aspects
will be issued forth. Definite emergence has the same  meaning  as  definite
issuance.
- There are eight types  of  achieving  of  definite  emergence  by  way  of
generalities and particularities.
- Among these eight, there is just one generality: the  objects  of  intent.
The other seven are particularities.
    (1) The objects of intent
    (2) the equality of all phenomena
    (3) to constantly enact the welfare of sentient beings
    (4)  through   just   performing   all   without   signs,   spontaneous
    accomplishment
    (5) attributes of the  occasions  of  the  entity  that  is  free  from
    permanence and annihilation
    (6) to attain all aims of the three vehicles
    (7) the exalted knower of all aspects itself
    (8) the path of distinction
- The eight divisions of the achieving of definite emergence  are  generated
on the three pure grounds.
1. The achieving of definite  emergence  of  the  objects  of  intent;  this
  refers to the achieving of the three objects of intent.
2. The achieving of definite emergence of the  equality  of  all  phenomena:
  this is an achieving in which all phenomena are seen  as  being  equal  in
  being empty of true existence, to constantly, that  is,  infinitely  enact
  the welfare of sentient beings
3. The achieving of definite emergence of constantly  enacting  the  welfare
  of sentient beings: one infinitely enacts the welfare of  sentient  beings
  by means of the great mind and application.
4.  The  achieving  of  definite  emergence  of  spontaneous  accomplishment
  through just performing all without  signs  of  conception  and  exertion,
  spontaneous accomplishment of others’ welfare.”
- These last three - achievings 2 through 4 - are attributes  of  the  great
mind.
The  2nd  achieving  is  the  attributes  to  the  great  mind  because  the
motivation for seeing the equality of all phenomena in being empty  of  true
existence is in order to enact the welfare of sentient beings.
The 3rd achieving has to do with enacting the  welfare  of  sentient  beings
and the 4th with spontaneous accomplishment of others’ welfare, so they  are
attributes of the great mind.
- Great compassion and the wisdom realizing emptiness in the continuum of  a
bodhisattva complement each other i.e.they assist and accompany each  other.
Through the force of great compassion, the  wisdom  realizing  emptiness  is
enhanced, and through the practice of the wisdom realizing emptiness,  great
compassion is enhanced.
5. Gyeltsab Je says, “Attaining exhaustion of the  objects  of  abandonment,
   attributes of the occasions of the entity that is  free  from  permanence
   and annihilation of the conception of extremes, due to  having  abandoned
   the extremes of cyclic existence and solitary peace”
    - Permanence and annihilation refer  to  cyclic  existence  and  nirvana
      respectively.
    - Free from permanence and  annihilation  is  non-abiding  nirvana.  The
      meaning of “non-abiding nirvana” is a nirvana that does not  abide  in
      the extremes of cyclic existence and solitary peace.
6. “Due to having exhausted the objects to be abandoned, attaining all  aims
   that are objects of realization of the three vehicles” Here we  have  the
   achieving of definite emergence  of  attaining  all  aims  of  the  three
   vehicles.
The 5th & 6th are attributes of the great abandonment.
7. The achieving  of  definite  emergence  of  the  exalted  knower  of  all
   aspects: this is explained from the point of view of the  exalted  knower
   of all aspects itself which directly knows the ten,  the  generated  mind
   and so forth
8. The achieving of definite emergence of the path of distinction  of  that:
   this refers to achieving of the  path  of  distinction  of  that  exalted
   knower  of  all  aspects,  included  within  the  vajra-like   meditative
   stabilization.
The 7th & 8th are attributes of great realization.
-Boundary of the achievings of definite emergence exist from the 8th  ground
and above - the three pure grounds.
-  The exalted knower of all aspects and the ten  topics  characterizing  it
have the relationship either of object and object-possessor or of cause  and
effect.
The object-possessor is the exalted knower  of  all  aspects,  and  the  ten
topics are the objects.
 - In terms of a cause and effect relationship, we can also see this in  the
   ten topics and the exalted knower of all aspects.
 - On the 1st topic, mind generation is a cause of the exalted knower of all
   aspects.
 - The lineage abiding in  the  nature  is  not  a  cause.  However  we  can
   interpret it to mean that by awakening the Mahayana lineage,  one  enters
   the Mahayana path. From that point of view, we can also  call  lineage  a
   cause.
 - The objects of observation of the Mahayana achievings are the cause of an
   exalted knower of all aspects because through observing  the  objects  of
   observation,  one   eliminates   the   superimpositions   and   generates
   realizations.
 - Gyeltsab Je says, “Due to this explanation of the first chapter, may  the
   precious teachings spread and flourish in all  directions  through  every
   means and remain for a long time - it means  “May  the  practice  of  the
   teachings remain.”
 - There are two types of  teachings:  teachings  that  are  scriptures  and
   teachings that are realizations.
 - The teachings that are realizations arise from those that are scriptures.
 - Uphold the scriptural teachings means remembering them, understand  their
   meanings, and cause those meanings to spread in the minds of oneself  and
   others. On  top  of  that,  we  engage  in  hearing,  contemplation,  and
   meditation, and in that way, generate realizations. We also cause  others
   to hear the teachings- we explain the teachings to them; we cause them to
   contemplate and meditate on the teachings, and to generate  realizations.
   This is the perfect way to uphold the teachings; in this way, one upholds
   both the teachings of scriptures and the teachings of realization.
