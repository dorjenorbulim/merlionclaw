In the third paragraph of the explanation, “The subject—the exalted wisdom of the rhinoceros-like—is clearly described as being more profound than a hearer’s…. it possesses profound realization by way of teaching Dharma with the body,” we are talking about the exalted wisdom of the rhinoceros-like solitary realizer. It is said that in general such solitary realizers teach Dharma through manifestations, or expressions, of the body, without sound or speech. When they do not use speech, the trainees are not able to understand what kind of excellent qualities or realizations they have; if they were to use speech to teach Dharma, the listeners would realize that they have excellent qualities or realizations within their continua. Because solitary realizer foe-destroyers do not use speech to teach Dharma, their realizations cannot be realized by the effect sign that is speech teaching the Dharma. In the first case [they are simultaneous], when a single mind directly realizes both [the sixteen aspects and emptiness] by way of direct cognition, the exalted knowers of modes and of varieties would exist as a single entity. That they differ in being an explicit or implicit realization is incorrect, because, otherwise, the same would follow on the occasion of the path of preparation.   In the latter case [they are sequential], if the path of seeing that is to be passed through is generated first, then a bodhisattva would need to sequentially abandon the seeds of the acquired afflictions that are the two obscurations; if they are generated in opposite order to that [the path of seeing that acts to pass through is generated first], then, after having already generated the path of seeing that acts to pass through, one would have to subsequently generate a path of seeing that newly realizes the selflessness of persons, whereby it would be endless.   Here, we are looking at two types of path of seeing: the path of seeing that acts to pass through, which directly realizes dharmata, and the path of seeing that is to be passed through, which directly realizes the sixteen aspects, impermanence and so forth. This person thinks that if you have both minds existing simultaneously and they are manifest, then the two are actually a single mind directly realizing sixteen aspects and emptiness by direct cognition. On the occasion of meditative equipoise, only one single mind can be manifest, so if you have both of these minds, then they must be a single mind directly realizing both the sixteen aspects and emptiness. Therefore, an exalted knower of modes and an exalted knower of varieties would exist as a single entity, which is problematic, because we say that on a learners’ path, an exalted knower of modes and an exalted knower of varieties cannot exist as a single entity. It is not that through proving emptiness by way of reasoning, true existence goes out of existence; in fact, it never existed from the beginning. It is not that when you prove that something is empty of true existence, true existence disappears; in fact, all phenomena are empty of true existence from the beginning. However, we need to apply reasonings to prove it so that we understand the emptiness of true existence.   Likewise, when we prove that something is impermanent, it is not that it becomes impermanent through our proving its impermanence; we are proving that something that is impermanent is impermanent. The purpose of applying various reasonings is in order to understand the nature of phenomena. If we do not understand the nature of phenomena correctly, we engage in various kinds of erroneous conceptions, through which we create karma; by creating karma, we circle in cyclic existence. We have these three words: praised, eulogized, and lauded. They mean roughly the same thing, but we correlate them in this way: 1. Praise is correlated with the path of meditation of belief of one’s welfare,

 52 2. Eulogy is correlated with the path of meditation of belief of both welfares, and  3. Laudation is correlated with the path of meditation of belief of others’ welfare.  There are three sets of nine: nine praises, nine eulogies, and nine laudations, making twenty-seven.   These three words—praise, eulogy, and laudation—are not different; they are posited from the point of view of the way of making praise: “praises” are made from the point of view of one’s own welfare, due to belief in one’s welfare; due to belief in both welfares being praised, there are eulogies; due to belief in others’ welfare being praised, there are laudations. These praises become superior as we move from our own welfare to both welfares, and then to others’ welfares. When the truth body is attained, this is really amazing, and is praised. When the complete enjoyment bodies are displayed, this is even more amazing; the welfare of the bodhisattva aryas is enacted, so this is even more amazing than the truth body. And when the emanation bodies are displayed, there is so much benefit; this is even more amazing than the previous two types, so this is praised even more.   Another way of explaining the distinctions among praise, eulogy, and laudation is as follows:  due to belief in the three Mothers, one practices the path of meditation of belief; this practice is something amazing and worthy of praise. In dependence on this practice of the path of meditation of belief, one places sentient beings in the states of stream-enterer, once-returner, non-returner, and foe-destroyer, and this is even more amazing. On top of that, one also places sentient beings in the state of unsurpassable enlightenment. So in this way there are three stages, and these three correspond to praises, eulogies, and laudations.
