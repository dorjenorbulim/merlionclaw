In the Autonomy school, we speak of three vehicles: the hearer vehicle, the solitary realizer vehicle, and the Mahayana vehicle. These are posited in terms of different objects of abandonment, as well as different main objects of cultivation.  1) For a hearer learner arya, the main object of cultivation is the selflessness of persons and the main object of abandonment is the conception of a self of persons.  2) For a solitary realizer learner arya, the main object of cultivation is the emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances and the main object of abandonment is the conception of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances. 3) For a Mahayana learner arya, the main object of cultivation is the emptiness of true existence and the main object of abandonment is the conception of true existence.  With this presentation, you might wonder, are there hearers and solitary realizers who realize the emptiness of true existence? There are hearers and solitary realizers who realize emptiness, but not all do. This is because in this school, to become free from samsara, there is no need to realize emptiness.   In this presentation, these three main objects of cultivation are presented in terms of the meditative equipoise that is either an uninterrupted path or a liberated path. There is also a third type of meditative equipoise: that which is neither an uninterrupted path nor a liberated path. These three different kinds of main objects of observation are not made in terms of this third kind of meditative equipoise, because during this third kind of meditative equipoise, all three objects can be taken as the object of observation. In other words, there are instances of the meditative equipoise that is neither an uninterrupted path nor a liberated path observing selflessness of persons, observing the emptiness of apprehenders and apprehendeds being empty of being different substances, and observing the emptiness of true existence. You need to understand this essential point; it is important.   Let’s focus now on the emptiness of apprehendeds and apprehenders being different substances: in terms of this emptiness, the Cittamatrins and the Autonomists hold the same view. If we take the apprehendeds as forms, then for this kind of emptiness, we are talking about the emptiness of form and its apprehender being different substances. For example, let us say that all of us in this gompa (there are about 60 of us) look at a flower, this red rose in front of me. We are looking at the same flower, but there are many different ways of looking at it. We can say that a flower appears commonly, but there is also an uncommon way of appearance of the flower: one person thinks it is beautiful, another sees that the color is too dark, etc. So although there is a common flower that is seen, there are many uncommon modes of seeing it.   The various individuals will have their individual objects of appearance. This particular object of appearance that is unique to the individual and the sense consciousness apprehending it are said to be of one substance, and this comes about because of a single imprint or latency being nourished. The uncommon object of appearance of a particular individual does not appear to someone else,, so this object of appearance is uncommon to the individual. This is because this object of appearance is of the nature of this individual’s consciousness.   This kind of presentation can have problems. To each of us there is an uncommonly appearing flower; this flower that appears uncommonly to one individual does not appear to the others, so there are 60 flowers! But in reality there is just one flower.   There are many such doubts that can come up with regard to this kind of presentation. Choden Rinpoche.]  Student: In the Svatantrika system, what is the meaning of substantial existence? What is the meaning of imputed existence? Is an apprehender observing substantial existence a correct awareness? Do substantially existent phenomena exist? For example? Geshe-la: An imputed existent is that which, in order to dawn as an object of awareness, relies on other phenomena dawning to the awareness. A substantial existent is that which, in order to dawn as an object of awareness, does not rely on other phenomena dawning to the awareness. Both substantial existents and imputed existents exist.   There are two types of substantial existence: substantial existence in the sense of self-sufficiency, and substantial existence in the sense of being able to perform a function. Between these two, substantial existence in the sense of being able to perform a function exists; the other type, substantial existence in the sense of self-sufficiency, does not necessarily exist. These two divisions are terminological divisions. Substantial existence in the sense of being able to perform a function is pervaded by existing, whereas the second type—self-sufficient substantial existence—does not necessarily exist.   Let us consider the apprehenders of these two types of substantial existence. The apprehender of a person as able to perform a function is not a wrong consciousness; however, an apprehender that apprehends a person as being self-sufficient, substantially existent is a wrong consciousness. An example of a substantially existent phenomenon is the aggregates. The aggregates that are the bases of imputation are substantially existent; the person is the phenomenon imputed. Student: Why should the meditative equipoise be both the object and object-possessor? Geshe-la: The reasoning is: if you say that suffering appears dualistically to an arya’s meditative equipoise, then we can say that a meditative equipoise appears dualistically to an arya’s meditative equipoise, in which case you would have to say that meditative equipoise is a different substance from meditative equipoise. But this is an absurd consequence; in our system, we say that suffering does not appear dualistically to an arya’s meditative equipoise. Suffering does not appear at all to an arya’s meditative equipoise that sees the emptiness of true existence. Our system says that it does not appear like water placed in water, and it does not appear dualistically, because it does not appear at all!   If it were to appear, would it appear in the manner of water placed in water, or would it appear dualistically? In the first case, if it appears like water placed in water, then the consequence is that suffering is an ultimate truth; this cannot not be countered. Therefore, similarly, a meditative equipoise must also appear dualistically to an arya’s meditative equipoise seeing emptiness, and therefore there is the consequence that meditative equipoise is a different substance from itself. This consequence also cannot be countered.  Basically, we are using parallel reasoning.
