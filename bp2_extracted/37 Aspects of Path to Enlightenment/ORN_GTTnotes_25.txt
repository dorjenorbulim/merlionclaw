Presenting our system It has already been explained that, after having asserted that they will attain buddhahood, not asserting that the attainment of buddhahood is certain contradicts reasoning, just as explained above. The second point is that “even though for the time being, sentient beings may not generate [a mind] seeking definite goodness, all sentient beings possess the two types of buddha lineage.” “Definite goodness” refers to liberation and omniscience. [The third point is] “since the [buddhas’] exalted compassion that wishes to place all sentient beings in buddhahood does not degenerate, [sentient beings] will generate [first] disillusionment regarding cyclic existence for the time being and [later an aspiration] seeking nirvana.” The buddhas take care of all sentient beings; it is not the case that they only take care of some sentient beings and neglect the rest. In fact, it is said that the buddhas enact the welfare of all sentient beings until cyclic existence becomes empty. For the time being, there are sentient beings who are not suitable vessels, but still the buddhas take care of all sentient beings. It is not that they only take care of Buddhists and those who take refuge in them, or those who have generated renunciation, bodhicitta, and right view. They take care of all sentient beings, life after life. It is said that in the vicinity of each sentient being, all the buddhas are present. When we do sadhanas (methods of accomplishment) in which we generate ourselves as a deity and so on, there is a common procedure of inviting the buddhas to come from their pure abodes to the space in front of us. One should not think that if they are invited and come, their abodes become empty, and that no one is home! For example, when I come to the gompa, the villetta (Geshe-la’s house) remains empty, but for the buddhas it is not like that.   At the end of this paragraph, it says “This is also established in dependence on a correct sign that is a scripture that is pure by way of the three analyses.” What are the three analyses? They are [analysis by]: 1. A direct valid cognizer, 2. Inferential valid cognizer by the power of fact, and 3. Inferential valid cognizer by way of belief.  To explain this a little bit, there are three categories of phenomena: 1. Manifest or obvious phenomena,

 4 2. Slightly hidden phenomena, and 3. Very hidden phenomena.  The first category, manifest phenomena, can be realized without relying upon a correct reason; they can be realized simply by a direct cognizer. The second category, slightly hidden phenomena, are such that when they are initially realized by ordinary beings, they are necessarily realized by way of a correct sign or reason. They cannot be directly perceived. The third category, very hidden phenomena, also cannot be directly perceived. On top of that, they also cannot be realized using a sign by way of fact. They can only be realized through a valid scripture.   Examples of the first category, manifest phenomena, are the objects of the five sense consciousnesses. Examples of the second category, slightly hidden phenomena, are impermanence, the selflessness of persons, and the selflessness of phenomena. An example of the third category, very hidden phenomena, is the presentation of actions and their results; for example, a certain result occurs due to a certain action at a certain time.   We mentioned valid scripture, but what is the criterion for a scripture to be valid? It is said that by depending on a valid scripture, we can realize very hidden phenomena. Is it one that arises from an unmistaken source? So what are the criteria for something to be a valid scripture such that in dependence on it, we can realize very hidden phenomena? How do we decide whether or not a scripture is valid?  1. First we need to check that the manifest phenomena indicated by the scripture are not damaged by a direct valid cognizer. This is something we can verify.  2. The second point, regarding slightly hidden phenomena that are indicated by the scripture, we should check that there is no damage by an inferential valid cognizer. This is something we can check using analysis and contemplation, such as checking the reasons for impermanence, selflessness, and so forth.  3. For very hidden phenomena, we need to check that within the scripture, the earlier words and later words [regarding very hidden phenomena] do not contradict [each other], and that in addition there is no reason for the composer of the scripture to lie, to say something that is false. Based on these points, for the time being we are able to assert that a certain scripture is a valid scripture. Then gradually we need to perform analysis and in this way, that it is a valid scripture will become clearer and clearer. This is the procedure for determining a scripture that is pure by way of the three analyses.   Student: What is the meaning of saying that the earlier words and later words do not contradict? Geshe-la: The earlier and later words do not refer to manifest phenomena and slightly hidden phenomena, they just mean words in general; the earlier and later words do not contradict [each other].  A valid scripture is necessarily one that is a scripture that is pure by way of the three analyses. We are able to check that the manifest phenomena that are taught in it are not damaged by a direct valid cognizer, and that the slightly hidden phenomena taught in it are not damaged by an inferential valid cognizer. Then we should check the very hidden phenomena taught in it using the criteria I explained: that there is no contradiction between the earlier and later words, and that there is no reason for the author to lie. Based on that, for the time being, we will assert that the scripture is a valid scripture.  Student: When it was said that this valid scripture must have an unmistaken source, what does this refer to? Geshe-la: To determine what a valid scripture is we have to apply the three analyses. If it is a scripture that is pure by way of the three analyses, it is a valid scripture. The second statement that “all sentient beings have attained buddhahood” means that they have attained buddhahood now. The disputant fails to make a difference between these two statements. In our own system, these two statements are very different.   The first statement, “all sentient beings’ having attained buddhahood exists,” means that a time when all sentient beings have attained buddhahood is possible. This statement is not a reason for proving that a time when there are no sentient beings is possible. What is the correct reason for proving that a time when there are no sentient beings is possible? If you examine these two statements, the main difference is that the first one has the word “exists,” whereas the second does not. So when we say “all sentient beings’ having attained buddhahood exists,” it is talking about the future, saying that a time when all sentient beings will have attained buddhahood is possible. The second statement, that all sentient beings have attained buddhahood, is a statement about the present.
