Prior to entering the actual leaping absorption, one has to engage in the preparation, the meditative stabilization of the loftily looking lion. With respect to the preparation for the absorptions in forward order and in reverse order, the text [see the first bullet point on p. 522] says, “the meditative stabilization of the loftily looking lion.” The second bullet point says, “an ascending process without inserting anything in-between.” For the first, the meditative stabilization of the loftily looking lion, one enters the nine serial absorptions, first in forward order, and then in reverse order. In other words, one enters: (1) the first concentration, followed by (2) the second concentration, (3) the third concentration, (4) the fourth concentration, then the four formless absorptions—(5) Infinite Space, (6) Infinite Consciousness, (7) Nothingness, and (8) Peak of Existence—then ending with (9) the absorption of cessation; this is the forward order. One then goes in reverse order: (10) the absorption of cessation, (11) Peak of existence, (12) Nothingness, (13) Infinite Consciousness, (14) Infinite Space, (15) the fourth concentration, (16) the third concentration, (17) the second concentration, and then (18) the first concentration. According to General Meaning, this is called the “long preparation.”

In the next outline [top of p. 523]—Explaining the nature of the leaping [absorption]—we look at the bases of alternation and the alternated property. If the four concentrations and the four formless absorptions are taken as the bases of alternation, then the alternated property is the absorption of cessation. Instead, if the nine serial absorptions are taken as the bases of alternation, then the alternated property is the desire realm mind. What is the purpose for engaging in leaping absorption in this manner? It is for the sake of completing the dexterity in meditative stabilization. This is why the text [p. 523, the last sentence of the first paragraph] says, “Therefore, when one is able to enter into absorption having performed alternation in that way, one will complete the dexterity in meditative stabilization.”   Saying, “Regarding the ascending process without inserting anything in-between, since merely the loci of the series is indicated, it is not an actual leaping [absorption],” means that this ascending process without inserting anything in-between is what is known as “the short preparation” indicated in General Meaning. When we use these terms—“long preparation” and “short preparation”—there are a few ways to interpret the words “long” and “short.” One reason for saying that the long preparation is long is because it involves eighteen steps—ascending from the first concentration all the way up to the absorption of cessation, and then descending from the absorption of cessation all the way down to the first concentration—whereas the short preparation involves only nine steps; for this reason, it is said to be short. There is another reason for calling them long or short: the long preparation is said to be long because it is far away from the actual leaping absorption, whereas the
