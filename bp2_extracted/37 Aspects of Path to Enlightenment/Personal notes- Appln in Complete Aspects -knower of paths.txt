The Application in Complete Aspects – Aspects of a knower of paths
- After those aspects of a knower of bases,  the  aspects  of  a  knower  of
paths:
- There are 36 aspects of a knower of paths:
      True origins           - 8
      True paths       - 7
      True sufferings  - 5
      True cessations  - 16
- True origins and true paths: the causes
- True sufferings and true cessations: the results
- The order of the four noble truths presented here is  different  from  the
usual order i.e. two causes are presented first, then the two results.  This
is for the sake of understanding the stages of  entering  and  turning  away
from cyclic existence.
- For the sake of understanding the stages of entering cyclic existence:
            - true origins are the causes, and
            - true sufferings are the  results  based  upon  the  thoroughly
afflicted class.
- For the sake of understanding the  stages  of  turning  away  from  cyclic
existence:
            - true paths are the causes, and
            - true cessations are the  results  based  upon  the  completely
pure class.
- Among true origins, the main origin of suffering  is  the  ignorance  i.e.
the conception of self. Based on this ignorance, afflictions arise  and  due
to which one accumulates karma. Due  to  karma,  one  is  reborn  in  cyclic
existence. Since the nature of cyclic existence does  not  pass  beyond  the
nature of suffering, as a result one experiences sufferings.
- The person is the agent who circles in cyclic existence through the  force
of karma and afflictions.
- As for the location (abode): we  are  at  Southern  Continent  (or  planet
Earth).
- Other abodes of cyclic existence: from the Peak of  Existence  through  to
the hell realm of Relentless Torment.
- Being liberated from cyclic existence means being  liberated  from  taking
rebirth through the force of karma and afflictions.  If  one  takes  rebirth
through the force of karma  and  afflictions,  one  is  not  liberated  from
cyclic existence. When one severs this cycle of rebirth  through  the  force
of karma and afflictions, one is then liberated from cyclic existence.
- We have afflictions in our continua. When we become liberated from  these,
we become liberated from cyclic existence. Afflictions are factors of  mind,
or  consciousnesses.  As  long  as  we  are  under  the  control  of   these
afflictions, we will not be happy. So we need  to  transform  our  minds  to
reduce the strength of these afflictions.
- If we are able to reduce the  strength  of  these  afflictions,  then  our
minds will become clearer. By having a clearer mind, we have the ability  to
realize  impermanence,  selflessness,  emptiness,  and  so  on.  This   also
benefits the attainment of calm abiding.
- If we do not try to work on our afflictions and  are  strongly  influenced
by them, then there  is  no  way  to  develop  meditative  stabilization  or
realize emptiness.
- Regarding the indication of sufferings first elsewhere,  the  four  truths
are indicated in accordance with the stages  of  the  object-possessors  (or
clear realizations) whereas here they are indicated in accordance  with  the
stages of generation.
-  In  accordance  with  the   stages   of   object-possessors   (or   clear
realizations), the order is sufferings, origins, cessations, and  paths.  So
in the order of clear  realization,  sufferings  should  be  known,  origins
should be abandoned, cessations are to be actualized, and paths  are  to  be
cultivated.

Objects of abandonment - Aspiration is the desire to view the object

                       - Craving that takes joy  in  rebirth  where  rebirth

Antidotes are:
1. The attributes of the desire realm are forms, sounds, odors, etc. If  you
have an exalted  wisdom  separated  from  the  attachment  of  craving  that
desires to obtain the attributes of the desire  realm,  this  serves  as  an
antidote to the aspiration that wants to view these objects.
2. The object of abandonment  is  the  attachment  that  craves  not  to  be
separated from the attributes of the desire realm.
    The antidote is the exalted wisdom of a bodhisattva that does not  abide
in the manner of adhering to not being separated from the attributes of  the
desire realm. In other words, as the antidote to abiding in  the  manner  of
adhering to not being separated from the attributes of the desire realm,  we
have the exalted wisdom that does not abide in the  manner  of  adhering  to
not being separated from the attributes of the desire realm.
3. The object of abandonment is craving that  takes  joy  in  rebirth  as  a
human or god.
    The antidote is the exalted wisdom of a bodhisattva  that  has  pacified
craving that takes joy in the body i.e. in rebirth as a human or god.
- For the antidotes, we  need  to  add  the  phrase  “exalted  wisdom  of  a
bodhisattva that….”
- The antidote is an exalted wisdom of  a  bodhisattva  with  these  various
qualities.
Aspect of origin
4. The antidote is a bodhisattva’s exalted  wisdom  directly  realizing  the
non-existence of the object  of  adherence  of  attachment  -  this  is  the
antidote to attachment.
5. A bodhisattva’s exalted wisdom directly realizing  the  non-existence  of
the object of adherence of hatred - this is the antidote to hatred.
6. A bodhisattva’s exalted wisdom directly realizing  the  non-existence  of

Aspect of strong production
7. A bodhisattva’s exalted wisdom directly realizing  the  non-existence  of
the thoroughly afflicted object of adherence of improper attention, this  is
the  antidote  to  the  indicative  conceptions  that  adhere  to  what  are
improper. The improper indicative conceptions are stated to be four types:
        a. The conception adhering to what is unclean as clean
        b. The conception adhering to what is suffering as happiness
        c. The conception adhering to what is impermanent as permanent

newly realizing with  direct  cognition  the  non-existence  of  independent
sentient beings, this is the antidote to  strong  adherence  to  independent
sentient beings.

Q&A: A true path of hearers is not conjoined with the motivation wishing  to
lead all sentient beings to liberation, therefore the true paths of  hearers
are not “paths that grant the opportunity  of  liberation  to  all  sentient
beings.” In this  context,  the  word  “liberation”  refers  to  non-abiding
nirvana or buddhahood. It grants the opportunity of liberation to  limitless
sentient beings because it grants the opportunity  of  non-abiding  nirvana,
which is liberation that is not associated with the two extremes of  samsara
and nirvana.

   - an exalted wisdom of ME of the PoS
   - an exalted wisdom of SA of the PoS, and
   - an exalted wisdom of the PoS that is neither ME nor SA.
- The exalted wisdom of ME of the PoS meditative is sub-divided into
      - an exalted wisdom of the UP of the PoS
      - an exalted wisdom of the LP of the PoS and
      - an exalted wisdom of ME of the PoS that is neither an UP nor a LP

In the AMWS, the attributes of true sufferings:
      - "empty" = empty of a permanent, unitary, independent self
      - "selfless" = empty  of  a  self-sufficient,  substantially  existent
self
- The meaning of “empty” as the empty of a permanent,  unitary,  independent
self is refuting certain tenets that posit the existence of a creator,  such
as Ishvara, who is permanent, unitary, and independent.
- A bodhisattva’s exalted wisdom that directly realizes those aspects is  an
aspect of the KP.
- These are the specific characteristics, because each of them has  its  own
"uncommon reason."
- There is one general characteristic: "the entitiness that is an aspect
lacking characteristics."
- The specific characteristics are the 1st four of these five  because  each
of these has their uncommon reasons. For example, for the 1st, it says  that
the five appropriated aggregates are impermanent because  they  disintegrate
from the point of view of continuity as well as from the point  of  view  of
an instant of time. So  there  is  this  specific  reason  for  their  being
impermanent.
- Disintegration from the point of view of continuity  does  not  mean  that
the continuity becomes  severed.  The  continuity  abides,  but  it  is  not
unchangeable. Although the continuity abides, it  transforms.  For  example,
feeling, there is a continuity but there can be a happy feeling,  a  neutral
feeling, etc. There is continuity but there is transformation, therefore  it
is impermanent.
- Disintegration from the point of view of  an  instant  of  time  can  mean
disintegration from one instant to the next, or within a single instant.  In
fact, production, abidance, and disintegration all occur at the  same  time.
All three occur within one instant.
- The 5th is a general characteristic because  if  we  look  at  the  reason
-“because they are an aspect that  is  empty  of  a  truly  existent  entity
ultimately.” This reason, the emptiness of true existence ultimately,  is  a
pervasive characteristic, and is thus a general characteristic.
- In fact, the first four  characteristics-  being  impermanent,  suffering,
empty, and the aspect of selfless- are all also empty of true existence.
-  “the  five  appropriated  aggregates   are   impermanent   because   they
disintegrate from the point of view of continuity as well as from the  point
of view of an instant of time”-  there  is  disintegration  from  moment  to
moment. This continuity of the aggregates is not constant; it is subject  to
change.
- “the five appropriated aggregates are suffering because they  are  objects
to be discarded that do not concord with the aryas.”- these  aggregates  are
under the influence of karma and afflictions therefore, they are objects  to
be discarded that do not concord with the aryas.

- By  taking  the  4  aspects  of  true  cessations  as  a  basis,  one  can
distinguish the 16 types of emptinesses, and one can establish that  the  16
emptinesses  are  aspects  of  cessations,  separations  from   adventitious
stains.
- According to this  system,  true  cessations  are  not  emptinesses.  True
cessations are explained from the point of view of benefit because when  one
meditates on the 16 emptinesses, one  becomes  free  from  the  adventitious
stains
- The emptiness of the inner is the emptiness of true existence of  the  eye
primary consciousness, ear  primary  consciousness,  etc  -the  six  primary
consciousnesses.
- The emptiness of the outer is the emptiness of true  existence  of  forms,
sounds, odors, and so forth.
- The emptiness of both is the emptiness of  true  existence  of  the  sense
powers, such as  the  eye  sense  power.  These  sense  powers  are  “inner”
relative to forms, etc., but “outer” in relation to the  eye  consciousness,
and so forth. Thus they are said to be “both inner and outer.”
-This aspect of cessation is of the 3 aspects of the 3  emptinesses  because
it is a separation that has ceased adherence to the things that are (1)  the
inner, (2) the outer, and (3) both as truly existent.  These  3  things  are
the bases of the 3 emptinesses. These 3 types of emptiness are indicated  as
aspects of true cessations.
-  True cessation here refers to the cessation of adherence  to  the  things
that are inner, outer, and both as truly existent.
-  Through  ceasing  adherence  to  these  things  as  truly  existent,  one
establishes the emptiness of the  inner,  the  outer,  and  both.  For  this
reason, these 3  types  of  emptiness  are  indicated  as  aspects  of  true
cessations.
- The adherence to things that are inner, outer, and both as truly  existent
are adventitious stains. By  ceasing  this  adherence,  one  eliminates  the
adventitious stains. By eliminating these  stains,  one  also  purifies  the
stains that are of the nature, or the natural stains.  Through  the  process
of eliminating the adventitious stains, the nature of the mind becomes  more
and more clear. The natural purity of the mind becomes more and more pure.
- For the adventitious stains, we posit the  conception  of  true  existence
and these obscure the natural purity of the mind. There are many  levels  of
adventitious stains, since there are many levels of the conception  of  true
existence - gross, subtle, and so  forth.  By  removing  these  adventitious
stains which obscure the seeing of the nature of the mind, one  is  able  to
see the nature of the mind more and more clearly, and in  that  way  we  say
that the purity of the mind becomes more and more pure.
- Of course, the stains have not entered the nature of  the  mind,  but  the
purity of the mind is obscured by stains. As much as we are able  to  remove
these stains, that much the obscurations obscuring the nature  of  the  mind
are eliminated.
- When one eliminates the  adventitious  stains,  the  obscurations  to  the
nature of the mind become eliminated. When we say the  nature  of  the  mind
becomes purer, this mean the obscurations to the nature of the  mind  become
eliminated.
- There are two types of purity: adventitious  purity  and  natural  purity.
When the adventitious stains are removed, the  obscurations  to  seeing  the
nature of the mind are removed. It is like peeling an onion;  you  can  peel
the layers off one by one. So when we remove the adventitious  obscurations,
the obscurations to seeing the nature of the mind are removed.
