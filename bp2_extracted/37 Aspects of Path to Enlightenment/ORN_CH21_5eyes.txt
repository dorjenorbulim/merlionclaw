Masters Program 2008: Ornament  – Chart 21 
The Five Eyes 
 
The definition of an eye  is: A knower that (i) arises in  dependence on the mind of an actual concentr ation that is its empow ering condition, and (ii) 
abides in a  class distinguished by di rectly seeing its object. (General Meaning) 
 
 Definition 
General Meaning  Description 
Ornament of Essence  Ability  
Eye of flesh One of the five eyes that is a knower that 
directly sees the subtle and coarse forms within 
100 yojanas in dependence on an eye of flesh1 
that is its uncommon empowering condition. An object-possessor that ascertains 
things individually, seeing subtle 
and coarse forms in the billionfold world systems within a hundred yojanas. Knows exactly where the spiritual 
guides teaching Dharma, your 
disciples, and so forth are abiding within the billionfold world systems. 
Divine eye One of the five eyes that is a knower which directly sees the death- transference and birth of 
all migrating beings in dependence on a divine eye that is its uncommon empowering condition. Sn object-possessor that knows 
exactly the death-transference and birth of all sentient beings, which arose from maturation of previous virtuous contaminated karma. Knows where your disciples die, 
transfer, and are reborn. 
Wisdom eye One of the five eyes that is observed as a non-conceptualizing exalted wisdom. An object-possessor that does not 
conceptualize true existence with respect to all phenomena, directly realizing the lack of true existence Directly knows the meaning of the 
mode of abidance, the method for subduing trainees. 
Dharma eye One of the five eyes that is an exalted knower which realizes exactly the levels of faculty, whether sharp or dull, of arya persons. An object-possessor that realizes all 
arya persons of sharp and dull faculties. Knows well the particularities of 
the faculties of the trainees who are objects to be assisted and taught the Dharma. 
Buddha eye One of the five eyes that is a culminated exalted wisdom directly real izing all phenomena. An object-possessor that is 
manifestly, completely enlightened in all aspects of all phenomena. Even having merely attained a 
similitude of the fifth, through knowing most of the varieties of objects of knowledge, you would be urged from within towards the achievings and would independently engage in the achievings without dependency upon others.
