COMPLETE ASPECTS APPLICATION

The first of the four is complete aspects application.
 •  Its  definition:  The  bodhisattva’s  yoga  conjoined  with  the  wisdom
   cultivating the compendium of the aspects of three exalted knowers.
 • Its boundary: It exists from the Mahayana path of accumulation  till  the
   end of the continuum (of the sentient being).
 • Simply, if it is a complete aspects application, it is necessarily a path
   in the continuum of a bodhisattva. If it is a path in the continuum of  a
   bodhisattva, it is necessarily a complete aspects application.

At the barest minimum, you  should  be  able  to  say  this,  “The  complete
aspects of application is a path in the continuum of a bodhisattva.”

If we looked at the term, complete aspects application,  “complete  aspects”
means all the aspects without missing out anything, i.e.,
 • the aspects of the exalted knower of all aspects
 • the aspects of the knower of paths
 • the aspects of the knower of bases
Complete aspects application is a mind that  meditates  on  the  aspects  of
these three knowers. What does the bodhisattva meditate on? He meditates  on
the aspects of the three knowers.

How does the bodhisattva meditate  on  the  aspects  of  the  three  exalted
knowers  completely?  The  bodhisattva   can   meditate   on   the   aspects
individually or he meditates on a compendium of all the aspects completely.

In the case of complete aspects application, as seen in the  definition,  it
is a mind that meditates on  a  compendium  of  the  aspects  of  the  three
exalted  knowers.  It  is  not  a  mind  that  meditates  on   the   aspects
individually. Rather, it meditates on a compendium of the aspects.

What does it mean to cultivate “a compendium of the  aspects  of  the  three
exalted knowers”? In  Tibetan,  literally  it  means  to  gather,  to  bring
together or to summarize all the aspects. It is not  so  straightforward  so
you need to investigate and to think about it.

According       to        Gyaltsab        Je’s        commentary,        the
Ornament of Essential Explanation, he says that the meaning of  “cultivating
a compendium  of  the  aspects  of  the  three  exalted  knowers”  means  to
cultivate these aspects with a union of calm abiding  and  special  insight.
With this as the  scriptural  authority,  we  say  that  a  bodhisattva  has
necessarily realized calm abiding and necessarily has special insight.  Why?
Because they cultivate a compendium of the  aspects  of  the  three  exalted
knowers and the meaning of that is to cultivate them with a  union  of  calm
abiding and special insight.

The meaning of “cultivating  a  compendium  of  the  aspects  of  the  three
exalted knowers” is not clearly stated in any text. This is why it is a  bit
challenging. I remembered my own teacher, Genla, used the example  of  going
to the very top of a  building  and  then  looking  at  the  view.  You  see
whatever is in front of you in its entirety. What  you  don’t  see  are  the
individual buildings here and there. This is the analogy and you  can  apply
this to the meaning as to how the bodhisattva  cultivates  a  compendium  of
the aspects of the three exalted  knowers.  So,  in  the  case  of  complete
aspects application, the bodhisattva is not looking at one  or  two  aspects
individually  but  he  engages  all  the  aspects  collectively,  in   their
entirety.

Although  complete  aspects  application  is  a  mind  that   cultivates   a
compendium of the aspects of the three exalted knowers, this does  not  mean
that complete aspects application is necessarily a mind that  realizes  each
and every single one of the 173 aspects. There  is  no  necessity  for  such
realization.

In summary,  the  complete  aspects  application  is  the  practice  of  the
bodhisattva who cultivates a compendium of the aspects of the three  exalted
knowers by meditating on all the 173 aspects of the three  exalted  knowers.
How do they do this? They meditate on all of them completely in a  condensed
way.

What does it mean when we say cultivate a compendium of the aspects  of  the
three exalted knowers? It means that the practice is done with  a  union  of
calm abiding and special insight. We have to think for ourselves  what  this
means.

Question: What does the term “bodhisattva yoga” mean?

Answer: What kind of yoga does the bodhisattva do? The yogi is the  one  who
does yoga. What is yoga? The  word  for  yoga  in  Tibetan  is  naljor,  two
syllables. Nal refers to the  natural  state.  In  its  ultimate  sense,  it
refers to the final nature of phenomena. Jor means to connect in  the  sense
to realize. So technically, a real yogi is someone who  realizes  the  final
nature of phenomena.

Here, nal can mean the natural state or the clear light nature of mind.  You
can also take it to mean that the  afflictions  have  never  abided  in  the
nature of the mind. So the yogi is somebody who realizes that. You can  also
explain it like that.  Such  a  person  is  a  yogi,  the  real  meaning  of
practitioner.

 • If you remember the terminological divisions of the perfection of wisdom,
   the first one is the natural perfection of wisdom. You can take  that  as
   the meaning of nal, the natural state, the final nature of all phenomena.


 • The mind that realizes that directly is the path perfection of wisdom.
 • The person who realizes that is the yogi.  He  possesses  this  yoga  and
   that’s why he is a yogi. He realizes the natural state.

What I have just said is probably the  ultimate,  i.e.,  final  or  highest,
meaning of the word ‘yoga’. Based on that is the highest  meaning  of  yogi,
the one who possesses the yoga. That is like the final sense.

The word ‘yoga’ can  mean  different  things.  For  example,  a  person  who
practises generosity  without  a  self-cherishing  attitude  whatsoever  and
completely  for  the  sake  of  others  can  be  called  a  yogi,   a   good
practitioner.

You can also say that a person who practises without any attraction  to  the
happiness of this life and  without  any  attraction  to  the  happiness  of
samsara is a yogi. You can call such a practitioner a yogi.

This is just my own idea. You can read nal to mean the state  of  liberation
and enlightenment or the highest level of happiness and jor to  mean  moving
towards or actualizing that state. So the person who  has  the  mind  moving
towards nal, the state of liberation and enlightenment, is the yogi.

In this context, the bodhisattva yoga in the definition of complete  aspects
application  refers  to  any  practice  that  is  in  the  continuum  of   a
bodhisattva. As such, a bodhisattva is necessarily a bodhisattva yogi.

In the first three chapters  of  the  Ornament  of  Clear  Realization,  the
aspects of the three knowers are presented individually:
 • Chapter One presents the exalted knower of all aspects.
 • Chapter Two presents the knower of paths.
 • Chapter Three presents the knower of bases.

Why complete aspects application is the first application
The aspects of the three knowers are presented but that is  not  sufficient.
One needs to meditate on and cultivate the aspects of these  three  knowers.
One’s meditation must be conjoined with these applications.

Chapter Seven  presents  the  momentary  application.  In  short,  momentary
application is the mind of the bodhisattva who has  attained  steadiness  or
stability with respect to all aspects of the exalted three knowers. It  only
exists for a very short time. Where does it exist? It  only  exists  at  the
end of the continuum of a sentient being.

In order for momentary application to come about, one  must  gain  stability
or steadiness with respect to all aspects of the three exalted  knowers.  As
such, before one can  achieve  momentary  application,  one  must  cultivate
serial application. As such, serial  application  is  presented  in  Chapter
Six.

The purpose of cultivating serial application is for somebody  who  has  not
gained stability or steadiness with respect to  all  aspects  of  the  three
exalted knowers. It is for that person to gain stability or steadiness  with
respect to all aspects of the three exalted knowers.

But before one can cultivate  serial  application,  one  must  achieve  peak
application, which is its basis. One gains peak application to gain  mastery
over the aspects of the three exalted knowers.

Before one can cultivate peak application, i.e., the cultivation of  gaining
mastery over the aspects of three exalted knowers, first, one must  meditate
on the aspects of the three exalted knowers. As such, one  begins  with  the
complete aspects application. This is the reason why  the  complete  aspects
application is the first of the four application.

Eleven topics
1. Knower-aspect of the antidote class indicated in this context
The knower-aspect of the antidote class indicated  in  this  context  is  an
exalted knower that is able to quell its discordant class.  This  refers  to
the paths, as a path is necessarily an exalted knower that is able to  quell
its discordant class.

So the knower-aspect of the antidote class indicated in this context  refers
to an exalted knower in the continuum of a bodhisattva. In order for  it  to
be a knower-aspect of an antidote class, it must be a wisdom  that  is  able
to quell its discordant class.

A path in the continuum of a bodhisattva that is  conjoined  with  a  wisdom
that cultivates or meditates on any aspect of the three exalted  knowers  is
an exalted knower that is able to quell its discordant class.

On top of the exalted knower-aspect of the antidote class indicated in  this
context, primarily, there are three:
   1. A knower-aspect that is a knower of bases
   2. A knower-aspect that is a knower of paths
   3. A knower-aspect that is an exalted knower of all aspects

 • When we say a knower-aspect that is  a  knower  of  bases,  you  have  to
   understand that is a knower of bases.
 • When we say a knower-aspect that is  a  knower  of  paths,  you  have  to
   understand that we are talking about knower of paths.
 • When we say a knower-aspect that is an exalted knower of all aspects, you
   have to understand that we are talking about an  exalted  knower  of  all
   aspects.

~ Object aspect and subject aspect
When we talk about aspect, there are object aspect and subject aspect.
Let’s use a mind realizing impermanence as an example.
 • The object of the mind is impermanence. This is the object aspect.
 • The mind that realizes impermanence is the subject aspect.

There are persons who advance the position that the object  aspect  is  that
which is to be meditated on, not the subject aspect. There are  also  others
who say that the subject aspect is that to  be  meditated  on  and  not  the
object aspect. There are these different views.

But in our system, we posit that both the object aspect and  subject  aspect
are to be meditated on. In our system, we say that when there  is  a  person
who is meditating on impermanence, there is a mind  that  is  meditating  on
impermanence. That mind meditating on impermanence is  not  the  only  thing
that needs to be cultivated. One is also meditating on the object,  in  this
case, impermanence. Impermanence here is the object  aspect  so  the  object
aspect is something to be meditated on.

When we meditate on  impermanence,  we  need  to  cultivate  the  mind  that
meditates on impermanence. If we were to take the position that  the  object
aspect is not to be cultivated and that only the subject  aspect  is  to  be
meditated on, it will result in  the  fallacy  of  emptiness  not  being  an
object of meditation, whereas emptiness is the main  object  of  cultivation
or the main object to be meditated on by the bodhisattva.

For example, the mind that meditates on great compassion. If  you  say  that
you meditate only on the subject aspect and  not  the  object  aspect,  what
about the mind realizing great compassion? Are you not meditating  on  great
compassion?

So you can meditate on both the subject aspect and the object  aspect.  Both
are objects to be cultivated. As for the manner  of  meditation,  there  are
different ways of meditating. Let’s take the example of the  mind  realizing
impermanence again.
 • Impermanence is the object to be meditated upon. We are meditating on the
   aspects   of   impermanence—thinking   about   what   they   are,   their
   characteristics and so forth.
 • For the mind that meditates on and realizes impermanence,  it  is  taking
   impermanence as the object and then meditating on it. It is not the  case
   that, when one  is  in  meditation,  one  is  trying  to  transform  that
   particular mind into the entity of impermanence.

But for the mind of great compassion—when one is trying to  cultivate  great
compassion—one is meditating on great compassion. At the  end  of  the  day,
the purpose of your meditation is to transform your mind into the entity  of
great compassion. So you see the manner of meditation is different from  the
mind meditating on and realizing impermanence.

 • The mind realizing impermanence is a meditation that  takes  impermanence
   as its  object  but  the  mind  doesn’t  transform  into  the  entity  of

 • However, in the case of great compassion, one is meditating on the  great

In general, both the object aspect and subject  aspect  are  objects  to  be
cultivated or to be meditated upon. In the context here, we are  looking  at
the knower-aspect of the antidote class. We are talking  about  the  knower-
aspect in that meditation where the mind is generated in the  actual  entity
of its object.

173 ASPECTS OF THE THREE KNOWERS

In Chapter Four in the Ornament of Clear Realization, when  the  aspects  of
the three knowers are enumerated, there are a total  of  173  aspects.  They
are divided into:
 • 27 aspects of a knower of bases
 • 36 aspects of a knower of paths
 • 110 aspects of an exalted knower of all aspects
In this context, we are looking mainly at the  knower-aspects  (or  subject-
aspects).

~ 27 knower-aspects of the knower of bases
There are 27 knower-aspects of the knower of bases. How do we get 27?  These
27 knower-aspects of a knower of bases are  discussed  in  relation  to  the
four noble truths.

Of the four noble truths, the first one is true suffering.  There  are  four
knower-aspects that realize the  four  characteristics  of  true  suffering,
which are:
1. Impermanent
2. Miserable
3. Empty
4. Selfless
These are the four knower-aspects of the knower of bases  that  realize  the
four characteristics of true suffering.

Next are the four knower-aspects of the knower of  bases  that  realize  the
four characteristics of true origins, which are:
   1. Cause
   2. Origin
   3. Strong producer
   4. Condition.

Next are the four knower-aspects of the knower of  bases  that  realize  the
four characteristics of true cessations, which are:
   1. Cessation
   2. Pacification
   3. Sublimity
   4. Definite emergence

Then there are the 15 knower-aspects of the knower of bases  that  know  the
true paths.
How do we end up with 15 knower-aspects when it comes to true paths?

First, divide the path into three paths:
1.  The  uncontaminated  paths  of  seeing,  which  are  antidotes  to   the
   afflictive obscurations.
2. The contaminated paths of meditation, which  are  the  antidotes  to  the
   conception or apprehension of external forms.
3. The uncontaminated paths of seeing,  which  are  also  antidotes  to  the
   conception or apprehension of external forms.

Of these three, let’s look at the  first  one—the  uncontaminated  paths  of
seeing, which  are  antidotes  to  the  afflictive  obscurations.  How  many
aspects are there to such a path? There are four aspects of true paths  that
is the uncontaminated paths of seeing, which are:
1. Path
2. Suitability
3. Achievement
4. Deliverance

How many aspects of true paths are  there  for  the  contaminated  paths  of
meditation, which are the antidotes to the apprehension of  external  forms?
There are five aspects.

How many aspects of true paths are there for  the  uncontaminated  paths  of
seeing, which are the antidotes  to  the  apprehension  of  external  forms?
There are six aspects.

So the total is 15.

The five aspects and the six  aspects  are  actually  indicated  by  certain
words in the Perfection of Wisdom Sutras. We will not be going into that.

Therefore, there are 27 knower-aspects of the knower of bases.

~ 36 knower-aspects of the knower of paths.
These 36 knower-aspects of a knower of paths are also explained in  relation
to the four noble truths. The numbers are eight, seven, five and 16 but  you
must know the order of the four noble truths here. There are:
 • 8 aspects of true origins
 • 7 aspects of true paths
 • 5 aspects of true suffering
 • 16 aspects of true cessations

~ 110 knower-aspects of the exalted knower of all aspects
There are 110 knower-aspects of an exalted  knower  of  all  aspects.  There
are:
 • 37 aspects of the exalted knower of all aspects that are concordant  with
   those existing in hearers
 • 34 aspects of the exalted knower of all aspects that are concordant  with
   those existing in bodhisattvas
 • 39 uncommon aspects of an exalted knower of all aspects

The first is the 37 aspects of an exalted knower of  all  aspects  that  are
concordant with those existing in hearers. What are they? These are  the  37
aspects that we will be talking about.

Khen Rinpoche: So finally, we have reached the topic! From the  next  class,
I will talk about the four mindfulnesses. This is  the  easy  part.  I  just
say, “You meditate!” Anyway, that topic will be easier to understand.

We need to know how these 37 aspects fit into the  whole  picture.  Now  you
have some very rough idea of how we have reached  this  point  here  in  the
text. The 37 aspects themselves are quite  straightforward  and  start  with
the four close placements of mindfulness and so forth. That is  much  easier
to understand.
