 2 so forth, the entitinesses that observe the objects to be turned away from as truly existent,) discarded by bodhisattvas.” In other words, here we are talking about the paths as well as the results of hearers that are bound by the conception of true existence. These are to be discarded; they are the objects to be turned away from [by bodhisattvas].   The subject here is the objects to be abandoned by the path of seeing that are conceptualizations with respect to these two types of apprehendeds. When we talk about conceptions of apprehendeds in general, there are two levels: innate conceptualizations and acquired conceptualizations. In this context, we are talking about the objects of abandonment of the path of seeing; in other words, we are talking about acquired conceptualizations with respect to apprehendeds. They are discordant classes, because they act to hinder the production of the path of seeing.   With regard to point 1, the first type of the apprehendeds, at the end of that description it mentions their being “adopted by bodhisattvas,” meaning we are talking about the objects to be engaged in by the bodhisattvas. In contrast, point 2 says “discarded by bodhisattvas,” i.e. they are objects to be turned away from by bodhisattvas. Note that these paths and results of hearers in general are not objects to be turned away from, but relative to bodhisattvas, they are objects to be turned away from. When we use the expression “conceptualizations of apprehendeds,” or “conceptualizations with respect to apprehendeds,” the apprehendeds are precisely the observed objects of the conceptualizations; for example, we can posit the paths and results of bodhisattvas; we can also posit the paths and results of hearers. These objects/apprehendeds are adhered to as truly existent objects of utilization by these conceptualizations.   The second paragraph says, “They are the nine types each that exist individually, because they exist in that way through divisions of the objects,” meaning that there are nine types of conceptualizations with respect to apprehendeds that are to be engaged in, and nine types of conceptualizations with respect to apprehendeds that are to be turned away from, and these divisions exist through divisions of the objects. Then it goes on to say, “They are to be known as the self-entities apprehending what are not exactly as the objects are, because their objects appear erroneously,” meaning these conceptualizations adhere to their objects in erroneous way, not in a way that accords with reality. The list of these nine types of conceptualizations each will come later.   Explanation: Ornament of the Essence, p. 488 2B2C-2A2B-2B1A-2. Let us look at the second point [top of p. 492]: “The subject—bodhisattvas—become definite in the Buddha lineage, due to definitely entering the immovable ground and so forth.” What does it mean to become definite in the Buddha lineage? This has the sense of the bodhisattvas definitely entering the Mahayana path; to become definite in the Buddha lineage means the bodhisattvas definitely enter the Mahayana path. The reason here is “due to definitely entering the immovable ground…” This “immovable ground” does not refer to the eighth bodhisattva ground/bhumi called “Immovable,” but it means definitely entering the Mahayana without moving to other grounds; it has the sense of not moving to other paths/levels, i.e. not moving to the grounds of hearers and solitary realizers.
