EXPLANATION According to [Vimuktisena’s] Illuminating the Twenty Thousand, having stated the objection, “The teachings on the result, countless merit and so forth, cannot bear up ultimately; hence they are incorrect,” [it states the reply that] it follows that there is no fault that they are incorrect conventionally, even though they are untenable ultimately, because the Subduer asserted those enumerations as indicated in the scriptures to be the result of the concordant cause that is the kindness of the buddhas conventionally, even though they are untenable ultimately. {171}   The meaning of this passage is: there is an objection saying, “Since countless merits and so forth are produced, the causes must be many; since there are many results, there must be many causes. Therefore, it is not correct to say that there are only nine paths of meditation. The division of the path of meditation as nine is [therefore] not definite.”   With respect to the reply, there are two points of view: the reply from the point of view of the ultimate, and the reply from the point of view of the conventional. From the point of view of the ultimate, the reply basically says “the reason is not established,” but relative to the conventional, it is tenable to have countless merit being produced from the nine divisions of the path of meditation. In short, relative to the conventional, it is tenable/feasible, but relative to the ultimate, it is untenable.   According to our system, it is necessary to accumulate countless, inestimable, and immeasurable merit. This is necessary in order to enact the welfare of others; since other sentient beings are countless in number, it is necessary to accumulate countless, inestimable, and immeasurable merit. For that reason, our system posits this huge amount of merit. This path of meditation being definite

 2 in number as nine is really not so relevant [in view of how much merit one needs to accumulate in order to enact the welfare of others].   To give an example, if we want to attain a human rebirth in a future life, we need to accumulate the causes, namely ethics, stainless prayers, and the practice of the six perfections. If we want to enact the welfare of others, it goes without saying that we need to accumulate more causes than these that are required for a human rebirth in our next life, because the enactment of others’ welfare is not simple at all. In fact, if you examine whether or not it is easy to enact one’s own welfare in this present life, you will find that it is quite difficult, so to enact others’ welfare is very complicated. Of course, when we talk about others’ welfare, we are not simply talking about others’ temporal welfare, but placing them in everlasting happiness; therefore, this is not easy. If we lack the backing support of a huge amount of merit, there is no way to enact others’ welfare. If we check our minds, sometimes it is difficult to be happy throughout a single day; there are times when we feel quite carefree, but deep down there is always the potential to become unhappy. If it is difficult for us to remain happy throughout a single day, what need is there to mention the difficulty of placing all sentient beings in everlasting happiness?  Basically, our system says that without countless, inestimable, and immeasurable merit, there is no way to enact others’ welfare; therefore, it is necessary to create such a great amount of merit.  It is important to make prayers for our studies to go well. Whatever merit we create, such as the merit from the recitation of prayers, our daily commitments, and so forth, should be dedicated well so that we do not encounter obstacles in our studies. Enlightenment of thusness that does not exist as an object to be qualified, the nature of exalted wisdom without elaboration, by the mere mastery of a buddha that is the nature of the truth body and so forth, enacts the desired aims by means of having generated the mind that appears as the special welfare concordant with the merit and exalted wisdom of the beings who are the trainees.   EXPLANATION Enlightenment with the characteristic of thusness that does not exist as an object to be qualified by increase or decrease, the nature of exalted wisdom without elaboration, enacts the desired aims of the trainees conventionally even though it does not exist ultimately, because it enacts the welfare conventionally even though it does not exist ultimately by means of having generated the mind that appears as the two form bodies, the special welfare concordant with the merit and exalted wisdom of the beings who are the trainees ranging from ordinary beings with pure karma through to bodhisattva aryas by the mere mastery of a buddha that is the nature of the realization exalted wisdom truth body and so forth. The expression “and so forth” subsumes the truth body acting as action of the truth body, the actual empowering condition of the complete enjoyment body, and the complete enjoyment body [acting as the action] the empowering condition of the emanation body.  Here we are looking at this analogy and meaning, under the outline “The pervasion is not established conventionally.” The analogy refers to enlightenment, and the meaning to be understood through the analogy refers to bodhisattvas on the path of meditation. This passage says that the desired aims of trainees are enacted conventionally; although enlightenment does not exist ultimately, it is able to enact the desired aims of trainees conventionally. There are different types of trainees: ordinary beings with pure karma through to bodhisattva aryas. With respect to ordinary beings with pure karma, the supreme emanation body enacts their welfare, and for bodhisattva aryas, the complete enjoyment body enacts their welfare. When we talk about the complete enjoyment body of a buddha, this is a coarse form body that can be perceived by bodhisattva aryas. Sentient beings who are not at the level of bodhisattva aryas cannot perceive enjoyment bodies. To benefit sentient beings of lower levels, there are supreme emanation bodies. This type of body can be perceived by ordinary beings with pure karma; it is a type of form body accessible to more sentient beings.   In order to benefit more sentient beings, the buddhas display supreme emanation bodies that are coarser than complete enjoyment bodies. Using the same reasoning, buddhas have to display an

In order to benefit sentient beings who do not have pure karma to perceive supreme emanation bodies, the buddhas display a form body that is even coarser, and there is a teaching that says that one’s guru is actually Vajradhara. Gurus are of various types; for example, the teacher who explains the Dharma can be taken as one’s guru. The buddhas need to display these coarser form bodies in the form of teachers of Dharma in order to benefit the trainees; if they show themselves in subtler forms, trainees may not have the karma to perceive them.  Although it is said that buddhas manifest as teachers, there is no pervasion that any teacher who explains the Dharma is a buddha! Through the conventional mind of enlightenment and the ultimate mind of enlightenment accompanying and assisting each other, one will attain enlightenment. An example of the actual preparation would be stabbing the person, i.e. you insert a knife into the person.  4. Do you have some understanding of the tenets of the Sutra Followers and of the Great Exposition school? The Sutra Followers and the Followers of the Great Exposition have very different ways of presenting the two truths. According to the Sautrantikas, ultimate truth and functioning thing are mutually inclusive. Furthermore, they say that conventional truth and permanent phenomenon are mutually inclusive. It is not the case that there is no water at that location, but due to karma, when the hungry ghost approaches, it cannot see the water.   Due to the influence of karma, different kinds of appearances can appear to beings, so we have to be careful, because if we move in the direction of non-virtue, our non-virtuous karma will cause us to have bad experiences. In Tibet, there was a student who had a very bad-tempered teacher, and thought the teacher was a very angry person. One day he told the teacher, “you are a very angry person; you are just like a snake!” One day, they were travelling together, and when they were halfway there, the student could not be seen. The teacher looked for the student, and saw that the student was transforming into a snake. Half of the student’s body had become the body of a snake, and he was wriggling like a snake, and eventually he completely transformed into a snake, due to the ripening of this karma. This is the case of karma that has been created ripening in that very life, so we have to be careful!
