The different systems have different ways of positing true existence, or existence by way of the object’s own character. For example, what is posited as true existence in the Mind-Only school, which coincides with what is posited as existence by way of its own character in the Mind-Only school, is an object of refutation/negation for the Middle Way schools. This true existence that is posited by the Mind-Only school is refuted/negated by the Autonomy school. In turn, what is accepted as existence by way of its own character according to the Autonomy school is negated by the Consequence school; according to the Autonomy school, phenomena do exist by way of their own character, but their measure of existence by way of their own character is in turn refuted/negated by the Consequence school.  5 EXPLANATION The subject—by the Mahayana path called “seeing”—what previously existing truly existent type of conceptualization that is a knowledge obscuration is extinguished, and what true cessation that is the aspect of non-production that is a severance of the continuation of production of a truly existent [type of conceptualization that is a knowledge obscuration] is attained? It follows that it is not attained, because [the stains] are of the nature that is without a cessation in which previously existing truly existent stains are newly stopped. That follows, because truly existent stains have never existed previously. You accepted the former consequence.  As it is said in this passage here, “what previously existing truly existent type of conceptualization that is a knowledge obscuration is extinguished”—there is no such conceptualization that is extinguished—“and what true cessation that is the aspect of non-production that is a severance of the continuation of production of a truly existent [type of conceptualization that is a knowledge obscuration] is attained?” There is also no such type of non-production. You should understand that when the emptiness, or the lack, of true existence is proven, it is not the case that something that was previously existent becomes non-existent. When one negates true existence, it is not that something that was previously existent becomes non-existent. If you think about it, why do we need to negate true existence, because true existence never existed? [In one sense] we don’t stop, or negate, it, because it never existed. Although true existence never existed, we have the conception of true existence, i.e., a mind that holds to the measure of true existence. In the process of negating true existence, what we are actually doing is proving that the object of adherence of the conception of true existence does not exist; this is not a process in which something that was previously existent becomes non-existent.  When we talk about different kinds of objects of negation, we say that there are two types: (1) an object of negation by way of reasoning, and (2) an object of negation by way of path. The objects of negation by way of reasoning do not exist; if they existed, there would be no way to negate them. The objects of negation by way of path do exist. If they did not exist, there would be no reason to negate them. An example of an object of negation by way of reasoning is true existence, and an example of an object of negation by way of path is the conception of true existence. We mentioned the desire to be separated from suffering and the desire to abandon the origins of suffering. If we examine our mind, we will see that there is some form of these desires, but if we do not cultivate them, they are probably quite feeble. In order to really have the desire to abandon the origins of suffering, one must have a strong desire to be free from suffering, and in order to have this genuine desire, it is necessary to think about the variety of sufferings and how they can happen to oneself. It is not sufficient just to think, “There is this suffering, etc.”; one must think, “It can happen to me,” etc.   If one can produce the desire to be free from suffering, one will ask, “What is the cause of suffering?” Through that one will go on to identify the origins of suffering and then generate the desire to abandon them. Without doing this contemplation, it is possible that our desire to be free from suffering and abandon the origins of suffering is very feeble. It is very likely that we have a strong wish to be free from suffering only intermittently; e.g. when we are unwell, sick, unhappy, etc., the wish to be free of suffering comes up strongly. But this wish is limited to temporal circumstances; we do not have the wish to be free in a long-term manner. This is definitely a fault. I am saying that if we simply have the wish to be free from temporal suffering and do not think about being free from suffering in the long term, this is not good. But this does not mean that when you feel sick, you should not seek treatment to be free from your temporal suffering! I am saying that on top of that, you should also try to generate the wish to be separated from suffering in the long-term way. If you contemplate like this on suffering and the origins of suffering on the basis of the twelve links, you will realize the uncertainty of cyclic existence in this life and future lives. You will realize how you are always vulnerable to suffering. If you contemplate in this way, you will develop the wish to be free from suffering in a long-term way, and the wish to be liberated from cyclic existence.
