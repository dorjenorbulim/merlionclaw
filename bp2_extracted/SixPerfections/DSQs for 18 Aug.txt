Heart Sutra & STAGES OF THE PATH (the Six Perfections)


Questions for discussion on Sunday, 18TH August 2013


Question 1:

The great Indian master Aryadeva says;
    Those of little merit would not even
    Entertain doubts that things are ultimately empty.
    Even suspicion that objects are empty
    Wrecks the seeds of cyclic existence.

      At the time of small merit, i.e., for  those  who  small  merit,  with
      regard to this Dharma, the teachings on  selflessness,  one  does  not
      generate profound doubt. However, even if one merely generates  doubt,
      that doubt will tear apart cyclic existence.


The above is a statement by the great Indian master Aryadeva.

How does entertaining mere doubt  of  the  nature  of  reality  “tear  apart
cyclic existence”?



Question 2:

It  is  said  that  the  view  of  emptiness  is   indispensable   if   full
enlightenment is to be achieved. It is indispensable even  if  one  is  only
seeking the enlightenments of the hearers  or  solitary  realisers.  Why  is
this so?
