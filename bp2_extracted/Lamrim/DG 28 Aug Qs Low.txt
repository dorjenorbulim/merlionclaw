MIDDLE LENGTH LAM-RIM

Questions for discussion on Sunday, 28th August 2011


1. What is faith and why is it so important?

A)  Definition from Lorig text:
Faith is a conviction, clarity, and wishing with respect to an existent
that is
endowed with excellent qualities and power. It has the function of acting
as a support for aspiration.

Faith is the foundation of all good qualities: from which all the mundane
and supramundane white dharmas are born. If one has faith in the qualified
Mahayana guru, one will be able to be guide by him to the state of full
enlightenment.

(Lorig is Mind and its cognition)


2. What is meditation and what is the purpose of meditation?

A) The object of meditation, whatever it may be, arrives through the
process of hearing and reflection. Through hearing and reflection, we
remove doubts or qualms that we may have pertaining to that object of
meditation. Once we have removed all the doubts and qualms pertaining to
that object of meditation, which means that after we have ascertained it,
we then meditate on the ascertainment that we had gained. That is what we
called meditation.  Meditation means familiarizing our mind with what we
have ascertained.

The purpose of meditation is to bring our mind under our control.

3. What is the most important thing to do in between the meditation
   sessions?

A) The most important thing to do in between the meditation sessions is one
should read the Dharma texts teaching that object and recall it again and
again.

During the periods between sessions, we should engage in those activities
that will benefit us when we re-enter the next meditation session. In
particular, during the in between sessions, we should read and consult the
texts that will deal specifically with the chosen object of meditation.

When we have to eat in between sessions, we eat with a proper motivation by
thinking: “I am going to eat in order to sustain this body so I can
continue with my practice.” It is important to eat without any afflictions
such as anger and attachment.
When it is time to sleep, sleep that occurs during the periods between
meditation sessions, it is important to fall asleep within the virtuous
state of mind. It is said that if we do fall asleep within the virtuous
state of mind, the entire sleep will become virtuous.


4. What is the difference between analytical and stabilisation meditation
   and is it correct for a single individual to engage in both?

A) Analytical meditations is sustained through analysis by means of
discriminating wisdom whereas  stabilization meditations is to place the
mind single-pointedly without analysis.

   1. Analytical meditation: by using the wisdom of discernment to analyze
      with what we have ascertained.
   2. Stabilizing meditation: we do not engage in analysis with respect to
      the object of meditation. When it comes to stabilizing meditation, it
      is done primarily to achieve calm abiding.

In the stabilizing meditation (also  translated  as  placement  meditation),
you must be familiar with the object of  meditation  and  you  have  already
ascertained it well. Placement meditation is that you  place  your  mind  on
the object of meditation without being distracted.

In order to do stabilizing meditation,  you  have  to  know  the  object  of
meditation well. In order to be clear of the object of meditation, you  must
have thought about it. Therefore before you do stabilizing  meditation,  you
already thought about things and that is analysis.

Although some who do not know this approach claim that, if you are a
scholar, you should only do analytical meditation while sadhus should only
do placement meditation this is not so, for it is necessary to do the two,
one by one - because the learned, too, need to achieve calm abiding and
sadhus (meditator who have abandoned external distraction and live in
isolation), too, need to achieve strong confidence in the spiritual teacher
and the like.

You can do both analytical meditation  and  stabilizing  meditation  in  one
session but they cannot be done at any one time. You need to alternate  them
i.e. you do analytical meditation and after having gained  an  ascertainment
then you switch to stabilizing meditation. You place your mind on  what  you
have ascertained. At that time you don’t do analytical meditation.  Also  at
the time when you do  analytical  meditation,  you  do  not  do  stabilizing
meditation. You alternate them. Both meditations do not occur  at  the  same
time.  By  alternating  between  analytical   meditation   and   stabilizing
meditation, they assist one another.


Yes, it is correct for a single individual to engage in both types of
meditation.
