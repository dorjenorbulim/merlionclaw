   1. The first greatness of the teachings on the  Stages  of  the  Path  to
      Enlightenment (Tibetan: Lamrim) is enabling one to know  that  all  of
      the teachings are free of contradiction.

 a. What is the meaning of the statement “all of the teachings are free  of
    contradiction?”


 b. The teachings consist of the scriptural teachings and the realisational
    teachings. How are they free of contradiction respectively?



 c. If you know that all of the teachings are free of contradiction, do you
    necessarily realise  that  all  the  scriptures  are  instructions  for
    practice?



 d. If you know that all the scriptures are instructions for  practice,  do
    you  necessarily  realise  that  all  of  the  teachings  are  free  of
    contradiction?



   2.  What  is  the  purpose  of   hearing,   reflection   and   meditation
      respectively? How are the wisdom that comes  from  study,  the  wisdom
      that comes from reflection and the wisdom that comes  from  meditation
      different from one another?

   3. Analytical  meditation  is  necessary  for  meditation  on  death  and
      impermanence because this meditation need an awareness that  is  long-
      lasting, very forceful and capable of changing the mind. Without this,
      you will not be able to stop the forces that  oppose  this  meditation
      such as grasping at the permanence of this life.



 a.  Why  is  it  necessary  to  achieve  the  realisation  of  death   and
    impermanence through  hearing,  reflection  and  meditation?  Can  this
    realisation be achieved if any of the three are missing?


 b. In particular, explain  why  analytical  meditation  is  necessary  for
    meditation on death and impermanence.



 c. Given your current lifestyle and commitments, what can you do to  start
    cultivating a mindfulness of death and impermanence on a daily basis?
