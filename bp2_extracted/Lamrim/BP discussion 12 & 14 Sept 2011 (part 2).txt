   1. Why is it necessary to train in  the  paths  of  the  three  types  of
      persons to achieve enlightenment? How does this understanding help you
      to realise that all the teachings are free of contradiction?

   2. The Stages of the Path to Enlightenment  (Tibetan:  Lamrim)  possesses
      the greatness of enabling one to understand that all of the scriptures
      are instructions for practice.

   a. How is our spiritual progress hindered if we do  not  understand  that
      all of the scriptures are instructions for practice?


   b. It says in the text that “the scriptural teachings determine  the  way
      that you take the teachings in hand, the procedures for practice;  the
      teachings as they are realised are your practice of  these  procedures
      in accordance with how you have already determined  them.  Thus  these
      two serve as cause and effect.” Explain this in relation  to  hearing,
      reflection and meditation. Does these three have a  cause  and  effect
      relationship as well?



   c. How does one realise that  the  classic  texts  are  instructions  for
      practice rather than  seeing  them  as  merely  promoting  superficial
      knowledge and eliminating others’ misconceptions?



   3. What is the meaning of “abandoning the dharma”? How can it be stopped?
