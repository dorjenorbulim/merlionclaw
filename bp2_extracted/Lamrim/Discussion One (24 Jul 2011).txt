   1. The first greatness of the teachings on the  Stages  of  the  Path  to
      Enlightenment is enabling one to know that all of  the  teachings  are
      free of contradiction.
a. What is the meaning of the statement “all of the teachings  are  free  of
 contradiction?”
 Understand that they are the path by which one to  achieve  enlightenment.
 Some are the main points of the path; some are the various branches of the
 path.
 By understanding the paths of the three types of person do assist one
 another, one realized that the realizational teachings are free of
 contradiction.
 E.g. the person of middling capacity: Main point of the path - to overcome
 the attraction to the future lives in samsara. So the person strives to
 develop the thought of renunciation and determine to be free from cyclic
 existence. Branches:  As a branch practice, he must meditate on death and
 impermanence or human life of freedom and endowment.
 E.g. the person of great capacity: Main point:  to develop bodhicitta
 based on the development of great compassion.
 Branches: In order to develop great compassion, he must first meditate on
 suffering individually in relation to his own existence.  On the basis of
 gaining the understanding of suffering of samsara, he switches the object
 to other sentient beings to enable him to develop great compassion for
 others through developing the feeling of unable to bear the suffering of
 others.
 In this context, main goal is enlightenment.  Preliminary practices - must
 first trains in the path that are shared with the person of small and
 middling capacities
 -to overcome the preoccupation of the happiness of this life by meditating
 on human life freedom and endowment, death and impermanence etc. and
 generate renunciation to be free from samsara.

b. The teachings consist of the scriptural teachings and  the  realizational
 teachings. How are they free of contradiction respectively?
c. If you know that all of the teachings are free of contradiction,  do  you
 necessarily realize that all the scriptures are instructions for practice?


d. If you know that all the scriptures are  instructions  for  practice,  do
 you  necessarily  realize  that  all  of  the  teachings   are   free   of
 contradiction?
The greatness of recognizing all teachings as non-contradictory - here the
teachings are mainly referred to the scriptural teachings which are the
three scriptural collections.
-The  scriptural  teachings  mean  those   scriptures   that   contain   the
information as to how or what we should put into practice.
-The realizational teachings refer to the teachings or methods that  we  are
supposed to put them into practice.
-The realizational teachings are the three higher trainings:
   (1) Higher training in ethics
   (2) Higher training in concentration
   (3) Higher training in wisdom
-To preserve and upholding the Buddhadharma means preserving  and  upholding
the scriptural teachings and the realizational teachings.
-How does one preserve and uphold the scriptural teachings?
One preserves and upholds the scriptural teachings  by  first  learning  the
scriptures so one understands and holds the  scriptures.  On  the  basis  of
understanding all the scriptures  which  come  from  learning  and  studying
them, then one  causes  others  to  gain  a  similar  understanding  of  the
scriptures. If one does this,  this  is  what  we  mean  by  preserving  and
upholding the scriptural teachings.
-The realizational teachings are basically the three  higher  trainings.  On
the  basis  of  possessing  the  three  higher  trainings  in  one’s  mental
continuum,  on  the  basis  of  one’s  realization  and  then  spread  those
teachings to others, this is what we mean by preserving  and  upholding  the
realizational teachings.

   2.  What  is  the  purpose  of   hearing,   reflection   and   meditation
      respectively? How are the wisdom that comes  from  study,  the  wisdom
      that comes from reflection and the wisdom that comes  from  meditation
      different from one another?
 From hearing the teaching, the wisdom arising from hearing is developed.
 The wisdom of hearing eliminates ignorance and doubt.
 From reflection, the wisdom arising from reflection is developed.
 Whatever object that you have ascertained through reflection is the object
 of meditation. Then familiarize your mind by meditating on the object of
 meditation until you achieve realization.
 From meditation, the wisdom arising from meditation is developed.

   3. Analytical  meditation  is  necessary  for  meditation  on  death  and
      impermanence because this meditation need an awareness that  is  long-
      lasting, very forceful and capable of changing the mind. Without this,
      you will not be able to stop the forces that  oppose  this  meditation
      such as grasping at the permanence of this life.
a. Why is it necessary to achieve the realization of death and  impermanence
  through hearing, reflection  and  meditation?  Can  this  realization  be
  achieved if any of the three are missing?
b. In  particular,  explain  why  analytical  meditation  is  necessary  for
  meditation on death and impermanence.
c. Given your current lifestyle and commitments, what can you  do  to  start
  cultivating a mindfulness of death and impermanence on a daily basis?
Impermanence- all products disintegrate because their very  existence  is  a
result of causes and conditions  and  the  collecting  together  of  various
parts which do not have the power to sustain themselves permanently.
Problem with us: Intellectually we know we will die eventually  but  we  are
so habituated to our misconceptions about impermanence and  death,  we  have
the feeling that we will not die today. As a result, we  absolutely  do  not
make effort to develop the thought that seek the benefit  of  future  lives.
Understanding death will make you come to accept it  as  inevitable  whereas
person denying death  and  unrealistically  clinging  to  permanence,  death
becomes frightening and something tragic.
Through the realization of death and impermanence
      -realize that we will not live forever and we could die this very day
      -thus inspire a sense of urgency in our dharma practice
      -will not fall under the control of the 8 worldly dharmas
      -able to direct out thoughts and actions towards the benefit of future
      lifetimes by practicing
Hearing the teaching on death and impermanence leads  you  to  practice  the
dharma.
