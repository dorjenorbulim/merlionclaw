The twelve factors Entity Boundary / Limits of pervasion Analogies Meanings
1. Ignorance  Not knowing the ultimate nature of reality, suchness Blind old man - never reaches intended  
destination, does not  know whether the  
food he eats is safe or not, unable to avoid  
dangerous places and fall over precipice  
and undergoes myriad sufferingsPrevented from reaching the higher realms  
due to not knowing about karma and its  
effects, falls and ends up in the lower  
realms
2. Compositional 
activity Compositional action / projecting karma Potter – determines the shape, size and  
quality of the pot he wishes to create Due to various karma accumulated, one  
takes good or bad rebirths, undergoing  
pleasant or miserable experiences
3. Consciousness Contaminated mental consciousness which is the  
basis of infusion of accumulated karmaEstablished when the imprint of action is left  
in this specific consciousnessMonkey – swinging from tree to tree and  
by nature very restlessConsciousness moving from one life to  
another, unable to focus on a chosen object  
and being distracted to all sorts of objects
4. Name & Form  Four mental (name) & one physical (form)  
aggregates. The four mental aggregates can only  
appear to the mind via a name/ term/ label, therefore  
not an object of the sense powersStarts from the time these five aggregates  
come into existence up to but before the six  
sources are developed, therefore do not refer  
to the general meaning of five aggregates.  
Also, the five aggregates of the intermediate  
state before rebirth is subsume here as wellPeople on boat - moving from one shore to  
another To enter into a birth state, one has to pass  
through the intermediate state. On the basis  
of possessing the five aggregates in the  
intermediate state, one enters into the birth  
state 
5. Six Sources Five physical & one mental sense powers, the  
empowering condition for the arising of the six  
consciousnessesStarts from the time the six sources come  
into existence up to but before the arisal of  
the six consciousnesses that depend on the  
six sources to come aboutEmpty house with 6 windows Symbolizing the establishment of the six  
sense powers without the establishment of  
the six consciousnesses
6. Contact The consciousness/ mental factor that arises upon  
the gathering of sense powers, sense objects and  
body. Distinguishes objects as pleasant, unpleasant  
or neutralStarts from the time this mental factor arises  
up to but before the arisal of the mental  
factor of feeling Couple in embrace When in first contact, feeling does not  
arise, only the gathering of sense powers,  
sense objects and body
7. FeelingMental factor of pleasant, unpleasant or neutral  
feelings arising from its corresponding contact  
distinguishing the three sense objects, the  
experiencing of birth or the fruition of karmaStarts from the time this mental factor  
manifests strongly up to but before cravingPerson whose eye is pierced by an arrow –  
the pain of having one's eye pierced by an  
arrow is so overwhelming that he is unable  
to have other thoughtsStrong feeling arising such that one is  
totally fixated on the experience and unable  
think of anything else
8. Craving Strong attachment of feeling that nourishes dormant  
karmic tendencies infused in the causal period  
consciousness, making it even stronger. Starts from the time this mental factor  
manifests up to but before graspingAlcoholic – one who drinks without  
measureThe more attachment arises, the more it  
cannot be satisfied, the stronger the  
attachment becomes
9. Grasping Even stronger form of craving, a yearning for  
somethingStarts from the time this mental factor  
manifests up to but before potential existenceMonkey plucking fruits – being  
dissatisfied with every fruit it plucks
10. Potential 
Existence The dormant karmic tendency that was nourished/  
nurtured by craving and grasping to the point that it  
becomes strong enough to almost ready to issue  
forth its effectStarts from the time this factor arises up to  
but before birthPregnant woman about to deliver
11. Birth First moment of consciousness at rebirth Newly born infant just emerging from the  
mother's womb
12. Aging & Death Second moment of consciousness up to the death  
state Person carrying a corpse on his back
