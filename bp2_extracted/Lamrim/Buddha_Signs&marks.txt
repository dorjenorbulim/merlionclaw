The 32 Signs of a Buddha’s Complete Enjoyment Bodyi
Definition of sign: an extremely positive quality depend ing upon an actual concentration which is its empowering condition, that 
signifies a tathagata as a holy being. ( Ocean of Sport ) 
Sign Causes practiced in previous lives 
1. PALMS OF HANDS AN D SOLES OF FEET ARE 
MARKED WITH THE SIGN OF WHEELS Seeing off and receiving the guru, offeri ng oneself to the guru as a servant, 
and so on  
2. FEET ARE AS LEVEL AS TORTOISE’S ABDOMEN Perfectly maintaining whichever of the three vows 
3. FINGERS OF HANDS AND TOES OF FEET ARE 
JOINED WITH WEBS Practicing the four means of gathering disciples such as generosity, speaking 
pleasantly, and so forth 
4. BOTH HANDS AND FEET ARE SMOOTH AND TENDER Offering abundant, fine food and drink to others  
5. SEVEN PARTS OF THE BODY ARE ELEVAT ED Offering an abundance of food th at can be licked (e.g. yogurt, honey) 
6. FINGERS AND TOES ARE LONG Freeing others from bondage and rescuing them from being killed 
7. HEELS ARE BROAD Protecting the lives of  others and benefiting their livelihood 
8. BODY IS LARGE AND STRAIGHT Completely abandoning killing 
9. KNEES AND ANKLES ARE NOT PROM INENT Correctly adopting virtuous dharmas such as generosity  
10. SEPARATE BODY HAIRS ARCH UPWARDS Increa sing the adoption of virtues by others 
11. CALVES ARE ROUNDED AND WELL-DEVELOPED LIKE AN ANTELOPE’S Being skilled in the knowledge of healing, science, arts, and so forth and teaching them to others 
12. ARMS ARE LONG AND BEAUTIFUL Not rejecting be ggars but rather responding with generosity 
13. SEXUAL ORGAN IS MODESTLY RETRACTED INSIDE Safeguarding secret words without divulging them to others 
14. SKIN IS PURE, CLEAR, AND GOLDEN-HUED Offering fine cushions, mattresses, and so forth to others 
15. SKIN IS DELICATE AND SMOOTH Offering fine ho uses, mansions, palaces and so forth to others 
16. HAIRS GROW SEPARATELY AND CURL  CLOCKWISE Abandoning multiple activities or distractions from Dharma practice  
17. FACE (AT THE FOREHEAD) IS ADORNED WITH A HAIR TREASURE Respecting and revering abbots, achary as, gurus, one’s parents and all those 
in a superior position 
18. UPPER BODY IS LIKE A LION’S Not slightin g others with haughty or denigrating words 
19. SHOULDER POINTS ARE ROUNDED Training in accord ance with the good and sound advice of others 
20. CHEST IS WELL DEVELOPED Providing medi cine, doctors, and so forth to others 
21. HAS KNOWLEDGE AND APPEARANCE OF ONLY GOOD TASTES Serving and helping sick beings 
22. BODY IS SYMMETRICAL LIKE A BANYAN TREE Constructing grov es, parks, gardens, shelters, and so forth for others’ use 
23. HEAD HAS CROWN PROTRUSION ( USHNISHA) Providing temples, monks’ quarters, and so forth 
24. TONGUE IS BEAUTIFUL AND LONG Speaking soft, plea sant, compassionate words to  others for three countless 
eons 
25. VOICE IS PLEASING AND MELODIOUS Giving Dharma to a ll sentient beings of the mundane realms with one speech 
that is understood by all in their own respective languages  
26. CHEEKS ARE ROUND AND FULL LIKE A LION’S Abandoning idle gossip 
27. TEETH ARE VERY WHITE Respecting and prai sing all beings and not denigrating them  
28. TEETH ARE EVEN Abandoning  the five wrong livelihoods 
29. TEETH ARE WELL SET Speaking only tr uthful words for three countless eons 
30. TEETH ARE COMPLETE BEING 40 IN NUMBER Abandoning divisive speech for three countless eons 
31. EYES ARE SAPPHIRE COLORED AND CLEAR Viewing all sentient beings equally and without bias as if one’s only child  
32. EYELASHES ARE LIKE A MAGNIFICENT CO W’S  Treating others without anger, attachment, ignorance, and so forth

Definition of mark: an extremely positive quality dependin g upon an actual concentration which is its empowering condition, enabling

universal king

5. FINGERS ARE WELL-DEVELOPED Having completed the collections of roots of virtue 
6. FINGERS ARE TAPERED Having gradually perfected the types of realizations of the three vehicles 
7. VEINS ARE NOT VISIBLE Having avoided the ten non-virtues and wrong livelihood
