Comment by Khen Rinpoche on selected questions

4. What is the difference between analytical and stabilising meditation  and
   is it correct for a single individual to engage in both?

There is a difference between stabilising meditation and  calm-abiding.  The
topic of calm-abiding comes towards the end  of  the  Lam-Rim  text  and  is
treated separately because there are a lot of details in it.

The two main obstacles for developing calm-abiding are both the  coarse  and
subtle forms of excitement and laxity. In  order  to  achieve  calm-abiding,
you have to go through the nine mental stages.

Calm-abiding refers to the ability to focus the mind on  any  chosen  object
without being influenced by laxity and excitement and being able to stay  on
the object as long as you want.  As  mentioned  in  the  text,  there  is  a
measure of this in terms of time, i.e., you are  able  to  do  this  for  at
least four hours. This is what calm-abiding is.

When you have achieved calm-abiding, you gain physical  and  mental  pliancy
where both the body and  mind  become  serviceable.  You  also  achieve  the
special blissful feeling that is called the bliss of  physical  pliancy  and
the bliss of mental pliancy.

When we engage in virtue, we get tired because  our  bodies  get  tired  and
exhausted. However, when you have achieved calm-abiding,  you  do  not  feel
that kind of tiredness and your body is very  serviceable  for  engaging  in
virtue.

In stabilising meditation (or placement meditation), you  must  be  familiar
with the object of  meditation  that  you  have  already  ascertained  well.
Placement  meditation  is  when  you  place  your  mind  on  the  object  of
meditation without being distracted.
Regarding  the  obstacles  to  meditation,   are   laxity   and   excitement
necessarily  obstacles  for  developing  calm-abiding  or  are   they   also
obstacles for other forms of meditation? You need to think about this.

When you are engaged in analytical meditation, if excitement creeps  in,  is
that helpful or  harmful  to  your  meditation?  Is  excitement  and  laxity
harmful to analytical meditation?

You say it is harmful? But when you are engaging in analysis,  doesn’t  that
bring about distraction as you need to think about  a  lot  of  things  from
various angles? At that time your mind is excited. Is the movement  of  your
mind during analysis helpful for the meditation? It is said that laxity  and
excitement are harmful for  meditation.  Are  they  harmful  for  analytical
meditation?

Does analytical meditation necessarily precede  stabilising  meditation?  In
order to  do  stabilising  meditation,  you  have  to  know  the  object  of
meditation well. In order to be clear about your object of  meditation,  you
must have thought about it. Therefore before you do stabilising  meditation,
you would already have thought about things and that is analysis.

We  are  not  saying  that  analytical   meditation   necessarily   precedes
stabilising meditation.

At times the answer  is  not  that  important.  What  is  important  is  the
thinking and reasoning process. You don’t have to worry  about  whether  the
answer is  correct  or  wrong  but  the  process  of  critical  analysis  is
important. There are always things  that  will  be  left  unsettled  or  are
inconclusive. Not every discussion ends with a conclusion.

Many  great  masters  and  scholars  also  left  many  issues  undecided  or
unsettled. So you don’t have to worry about that. That is the reason why  we
need  more  questions  and  doubts.  With  more  doubts,  then  there   will
improvement. That is the only way you can improve.

When you think it is all right not to have any  doubts  or  questions,  then
there is no benefit at all. It doesn’t mean that  when  you  don’t  ask  any
question and you  don’t  think  about  it  that  you  are  sharper  or  more
intelligent. That is not necessarily the case. The more you  ask,  the  more
you think, the more doubts you have, the greater will be your wisdom.

Question: In  any  one  meditation  session,  can  you  do  both  analytical
meditation and stabilising meditation?

Answer: You can do both analytical meditation and stabilising meditation  in
one session but they cannot be done at the same time. You need to  alternate
them, i.e.,  you  do  analytical  meditation  and  after  having  gained  an
ascertainment, then you switch to stabilising  meditation.  You  place  your
mind on what you have ascertained. At that  time  you  don’t  do  analytical
meditation.
Also at the time when you are doing analytical meditation,  you  do  not  do
stabilising meditation. You alternate them. Both meditations  do  not  occur
at  the  same  time.  By  alternating  between  analytical  meditation   and
stabilising meditation, they assist one another.
