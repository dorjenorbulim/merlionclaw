 Meditating on the suffering of animals  
Suffering from being 
eaten by each other.  
Suffering from being

Suffering from being

each other. Big fish eat smaller ones, 
tigers eat antelope, cat eat birds, birds 
eat worms, and some small parasitic 
animals eat larger one. Many anim als 
will spend their entire life killing and 
eating other animals. In this way they 
create negative karma continuously.  
Animals have very strong, deep 
ignorance. They have no way of 
recognising, a Buddha or Bodhisattva, 
even if directly in their presence.  It is 
impossible for them to receive advice or 
Dharma teachings because of their 
karma, which leave them strongly 
conditioned by ignorance.  
Human beings are able to alleviate the 
suffering of extreme temperatures. 
They use housing, heaters, coats and 
so on to protect them from cold. Light 
clothing, air conditioners etc, are used 
against heat. Animals, however, have 
no such options and must simply bear 
with the heat of a desert or the arctic 
cold.  
Animals often have to wait long periods 
to obtain food or water. Desert animals 
must wait for rain before they can drink. 
Animals living on high, snow -covered 
mountains often cannot find grass or 
other food, and the birds there 
frequently lack food and drink. Although 
there may be a twenty -four hour 
convenience store in the nearest town, 
because of their karma animals are 
unable to use it t o relieve their hunger 
and thirst.  Animals are exploited by humans for various 
purposes, and due to their ignorance are 
sometimes thought of in the same way as 
vegetables. They are used to carry luggage, do 
the farm work, or are killed for food and for the 
various parts of their bodies: fur, skim, horns, 
gut and so on. For the whole of their lives , 
animals have no control over their 
circumstances, and never have a chance to 
relax or experience any real peace.   
 
The causes  of being reborn in the animal 
realm include abandoning the Dharma, being 
disrespectful to the Dharma or criticising 
other spiritual practitioners.  
When we think of the suffering of animals, we 
will recognise that the perfect human rebirth 
of leisure, and fortune is extremely precious, 
and a wonderful opportunity to practise the 
Dharma and attain enlightenment.
