Lesson 62 				                                                          5 May 2018
Commentary on selected verses from The Foundation of All Good Qualities. Review of last lesson. How we should be doing our practice.
THE FOUNDATION OF ALL GOOD QUALITIES
Let us begin by reflecting on this stanza from The Foundation of All Good Qualities. The stanza says:
Having become a pure vessel by training in the general path, 
Please bless me to enter 
The holy gateway of the fortunate ones, 
The supreme vajra vehicle.
This stanza talks about becoming a pure vessel by training in the general or common path. Common path here means the three principles of the path—renunciation, bodhicitta and the correct view.  By studying this and reflecting on them, we can become suitable receptacles for entering “the supreme vajra vehicle.” 
Among the vehicles, the highest is the Vajra Vehicle, the Vajrayana. Among the four classes of tantra, the highest is the Highest Yoga Tantra. 
In order to be able to practise this, you have to enter the Vajra Vehicle. 
In order to enter the Vajra Vehicle, you have to receive an initiation or empowerment. 
In order to receive this initiation, you have to enter the mandala and request the vajra master to confer the initiation. 
If you want to practise the Vajrayana, you need to have some understanding of the three principles of the path, i.e., you need to reflect on renunciation, bodhicitta and the correct view so that your mind is moved by these three principles.
It is stated in this stanza that the Vajra Vehicle, the Vajrayana, is supreme among all vehicles. Why is it the highest or the best among all vehicles? In what way is it “supreme”?  You should be able to explain why the Vajrayana is the supreme vehicle. You have to cause yourself to understand the reasons.  I have provided the explanation before.   
Khen Rinpoche: My point is that if somebody were to ask you, “Why is it supreme among all the paths?” what are you going to say?  You have to be able to explain.  
You have to make yourself understand the reason why Vajrayana is the supreme path.  What is distinctive about the Tantra Vehicle? What distinguishes it from the Perfection Vehicle or the Sutra Vehicle?  Then among the four classes of tantra, why is Highest Yoga Tantra so distinctive?  Through your studies, you should be able to explain why this is so with confidence. You must know the reasons for the explanation that the Vajrayana is supreme and so on. 
The next stanza in The Foundation of All Good Qualities states:
At that time, the basis of accomplishing the two attainments 
Is keeping pure vows and samaya. 
As I have become firmly convinced of this, 
Please bless me to protect these vows and pledges like my life. 
After receiving an initiation, you have to keep all your vows and pledges properly.  The reason for receiving an initiation is that you understand that if you want to attain buddhahood or omniscience, you have to practise tantra.  When you make that kind of decision, it is because you see the reason for and the purpose of taking the initiation and practising tantra. You know that without doing this, there is no way you can obtain omniscience.  So, seeing the relevance of the practice of tantra, you take the initiation. Then you engage in the practice.
The “two attainments” that are mentioned in the stanza are the common attainment and the uncommon attainment, i.e., the attainment that is supreme or the attainment of buddhahood. Here, it is stated that “the basis of encompassing the two attainments” —the eight common attainments and the supreme attainment, the uncommon attainment of buddhahood—is keeping our vows and pledges properly.  This means that the foundation or the root of achieving these two attainments is keeping the vows and pledges properly. These are the vows and pledges to which you have committed during the initiation. 
You have to understand the importance of keeping your vows and pledges properly if you want to attain buddhahood.  You have to reflect on this point until you come to this ascertainment, “There is no way I can attain buddhahood and there is no way I can have these two attainments if I do not keep my vows and pledges properly.”  You have to generate an uncontrived awareness of this point. Once you have done that, then you will keep your vows and pledges at the cost of your life.
In terms of the vows that were conferred by the Buddha, there are many types. 
For example, in the Vinaya, the Buddha talked about the seven types of pratimoksha vows or vows of individual liberation that enable one to attain the high status of humans and gods and also liberation from samsara. 
If one is aspiring to not only attain one’s own liberation but also to be able to benefit all sentient beings, then there are the bodhisattva vows. 
If one wants to attain enlightenment quickly, then there are the vows of tantra.
The Buddha taught that if you want to have good rebirth in the realms of humans and gods in the next life, then you have to keep certain types of vows.  Similarly, if you aim to attain liberation from samsara, there are certain vows that you must keep.  Buddha also taught if you want to attain certain goals, there are certain vows that you have to keep.  This is something that you do because you strive to attain a certain goal.
If you are concerned about not just your own welfare but also the welfare of all sentient beings—all living beings pervading space—then you have to observe the bodhisattva vows. If you are the person who has a certain kind of courage and wants to enact the welfare of all sentient beings, then you have to guard the corresponding kind of ethics.  There are things you should do and there are things you shouldn’t do.
If you have the kind of mentality that cannot bear the fact that sentient beings suffer for a long time while you take three countless great eons to attain buddhahood and you want to attain buddhahood more swiftly so that you will be able to eliminate the suffering of sentient beings more quickly, there are the vows of tantra.
If you are someone who is genuinely interested in attaining buddhahood in this lifetime in the same body, then you would protect your vows and pledges properly.   This is because you have seen the reasons and purpose for keeping the vows and pledges. When you try to follow the rules, you don’t feel any hardship at all because you understand, “If I do this, if I stick to the rules, then I will be able to attain buddhahood quickly.”  As a result of that, there is no hardship at all. Instead you will experience joy.
It is important for us to see the reasons and the purpose for committing ourselves to these vows and pledges and making the promise to keep them.  If you are able to do that, there will be joy and delight when you take and keep these vows. Otherwise, you will feel quite miserable for having committed yourself to these vows and pledges. You will feel, “I am so pathetic. Poor me! I have all these vows to keep.” Indeed, the Buddha said in one sutra, “For some, vows are suffering. For some, vows are happiness.” 
It is very important for you to really realize the reasons and purpose for taking vows, for keeping vows and keeping your ethics well because if you are really interested in attaining buddhahood, then you realize the relevance of doing so. 
In the stanza from The Foundation of All Good Qualities, “Please bless me to protect these vows and pledges like my life,” you should be able to see that these vows and pledges are very precious and very valuable such that if you have to choose between giving up your life or giving up your vows and pledges, you should be prepared to give up your life! As to whether or not you are able to regard your vows and pledges as more precious than your life, then that is something that comes from your own mind.
The next stanza in the Foundation of All Good Qualities:
Then, having realized the importance of the two stages,
The essence of the Vajrayana,
By practicing with great energy, never giving up the four sessions, 
Please bless me to realize the teachings of the holy Guru.
It is not sufficient for you to simply keep your vows and pledges properly. If you do not meditate, then you are not going to get the result.  So, it is important for you to train in the path of tantra. When these paths are subsumed, we have the two stages of tantra— the generation stage and the completion stage.
You should also understand the reasons and the purpose for meditating on these two stages.  For example, you should understand:
why it is necessary for you to meditate on the generation stage
why it is not sufficient for you just to meditate on the generation stage without meditating on the completion stage
what are the reasons and purpose for meditating on the completion stage  
With the understanding of these reasons and purposes, then you will generate joyous effort when you cultivate these two stages. 
So, you should have the understanding of the relevance of the two stages.  You should understand that:
It is not alright if I do not meditate on the generation stage.  
It is not alright if I do not meditate on the completion stage.  
I have to meditate on the generation stage.
I have to meditate on the completion stage.  
In this way, you should generate enthusiasm and joy when you cultivate the two stages. As stated in these stanzas by Tsongkhapa, you should realize the importance of these two stages and you should practise with great enthusiasm. He also said that you should practise without giving up the four sessions.  In other words, we have to make effort to practice the yoga in the four sessions.
You need to practise the two stages. This means you need to understand the essential points of the generation stage:
What is the generation stage?
How do I practise the generation stage?
If I practise the generation stage, what are the benefits?
REVIEW 
How do you practise the generation stage?  After having received the initiation, you have to practise the sadhana. You have to recite the sadhana and do all the reflections and visualizations as described in the sadhana. You shouldn’t be just reciting, “Blah, blah, blah.” In your mind, you have to reflect on the meaning. In that way, you try to cultivate clear appearance and divine pride. This is why when an initiation is conferred, there is this commitment of doing the sadhana.
Similarly, having received the initiation, you make this commitment to recite the Six Session Guru Yoga on a daily basis. This is because you commit yourselves to many vows and pledges during the initiation.  By doing the Six Session Guru Yoga every day, you remind yourself of these vows and pledges.
As you recite the sadhana, you should meditate clearly on the supported and supporting mandala. As I have explained before, the main practice of the generation stage is the practice of bringing death, the intermediate state and rebirth through the path as the three buddha bodies. It is important that all the essential points related to bringing the death, the intermediate state and rebirth to the three buddha bodies are complete.  If the points of this practice are not complete, this means that you are not doing this practice in a complete way.  
During the generation stage practice, you recite the sadhana slowly and try to reflect as described in the sadhana. You should try to make all the points clear in your own mind.  During the generation stage, you have to make effort in making this happen. 
If you do the four sessions on a daily basis, then over time, due to the power of familiarization, you will be able to cause the entire supported and supporting mandala to appear clearly without using a lot of time.  Instantly, you will be able to cause this to appear clearly.  
When you are able to cause the supported and supporting mandala to appear instantly in a clear way to your mind, this is still not sufficient. At that point, you have already attained a certain degree of clear appearance and divine pride. You also have pacified a substantial degree of laxity and excitement. 
However, you still need to train in causing the subtle deities—the deities at the sense sources, your eyes and so forth—to appear clearly, instantly. So, you still need to make effort in achieving that. Slowly, you have to train in causing these subtle deities—the deities at the sense sources, your eyes and so forth—to appear clearly. 
You meditate again and again and through the force of familiarization, you will arrive at this point where not only the supported and supporting mandala appear clearly but also the subtle deities at your sense sources appear clearly.  This ability to cause the supported and supporting mandala and the subtle deities at the sense sources to appear clearly occur through the power of familiarization. This means that you will not need a long time to meditate on the sadhana once you achieve this ability to cause the mandala and all the subtle deities to appear instantly. Because of your familiarization, in the very short period of time, everything appears clearly.  There is no need to dwell on the sadhana for a long time.  
According to the text, even if you have reached this point, you have still not achieved calm abiding or samatha. This is because laxity and excitation can still arise sometimes.  In order to pacify laxity and excitation, you need to meditate on the subtle implement at the upper opening or the substance drop at the lower opening.  Through such meditation, you pacify laxity and excitement and then you return to your meditation on the mandala.  
Once you have completed the coarse generation stage, you move on to meditate on the subtle drop. Here, you are trying to achieve calm abiding.  For the purpose of achieving calm abiding, you want to cause your mind to be able to dwell on this very subtle minute drop in a stable way. Specifically, you focus on this subtle drop in a single-pointed way to achieve stability.
According to the text by Ngawang Palden that we are using, he describes how you should attain calm abiding: first, through focusing on this subtle drop. Then after you have attained calm abiding, you should train in special insight (vipassana). You do this by emitting and reabsorbing the subtle implements. 
This text by Ngawang Palden talks about the achievement of calm abiding and special insight in a serial way, i.e., first, you attain calm abiding. Then you attain special insight.  
In the context of the Sutra Vehicle, for example, in the explanation of the lam-rim, the stages of the path, you do not attain calm abiding and special insight simultaneously. The sequence is definite. First, you achieve calm abiding. Then you achieve special insight.
Some scholars have said that in tantra, the sequence described for calm abiding and special insight in the sutra is not definite because in tantra, it is possible to attain calm abiding and special insight simultaneously. Because of the special essential points present in tantra, it is possible to attain calm abiding and special insight at the same time.
According to many scholars, it is possible to attain calm abiding and special insight simultaneously due to the uncommon features of tantra. 
This is because the object is unusual as you are meditating on a subtle drop. 
In addition, you are meditating on the subtle drop at a very special location. You are meditating on the subtle drop inside the tip of the upper end of the central channel. 
Because of these extraordinary features, the winds are able to gather. This then gives you extraordinary power in attaining calm abiding and special insight. 
Because you are meditating on the subtle drop at a very special location, this special location within the body has the ability to cause the winds to gather. It has this extraordinary ability or power to cause the winds to gather. This means that when you are cultivating calm abiding, even though you do this meditation on emitting and reabsorbing the implements, it does not serve as a hindrance to your calm abiding. 
You have to understand this essential point. It is possible for you to attain calm abiding and special insight simultaneously in tantra. If it is possible to attain calm abiding and special insight simultaneously, what is the method for doing so? It is not clear in the text but maybe it is like this. As I mentioned in the previous class, when you arrive at the point of the seventh or eighth mental abiding during the cultivation of calm abiding, you meditate on this subtle implement inside the tip of the upper end of your central channel. You hold your mind there and you emit and reabsorb the subtle implement. At that point on the seventh or eighth mental abiding, you have yet to attain calm abiding. You do that practice on the seventh or eighth mental abiding and when you attain the completion of the ninth mental abiding, perhaps you will attain the union of calm abiding and special insight.
We can also recall this question and answer in the Medium Length Lam-rim that says, “In which system do you attain calm abiding and special insight serially?” There, Highest Yoga Tantra was omitted from the answer. From that, perhaps you can infer that in Highest Yoga Tantra, there is the simultaneous attainment of calm abiding and special insight. We need a source for saying that it is possible to attain calm abiding and special insight simultaneously. From the answer given by Lama Tsongkhapa in the Medium Length Lam-rim, can we conclude that Highest Yoga Tantra offers the possibility of the simultaneous attainment of calm abiding and special insight? This is something for us to think about. 
In the Sutra system, it is clear that when you are actually training to attain calm abiding, you should do only placement meditation. You do not do any analytical meditation because if you do any analytical meditation during this stage, it will be a hindrance that will prevent you from achieving calm abiding. After you have attained calm abiding and you want to achieve special insight, then you do analytical meditation. This is the Sutra system. 
However, in Highest Yoga Tantra, it is said that even before you attain calm abiding, there is a way of doing analytical meditation such that it does not hinder your achievement of calm abiding. So, this is an essential point that you should understand.

HOW WE SHOULD BE DOING OUR PRACTICE
In the Lama Chopa LC 110:
I seek your blessings that whatever appears may arise as the deity, 
Having cleansed all stains of ordinary appearance and grasping
With the first stage yoga of transforming birth,
Death, and the bardo into the three bodies of a conqueror. 
We train so that the environment appears as the deity’s celestial mansion or inestimable mansion and the inhabitants within the environment appear as the deities. This one stanza, LC 110 in the Lama Chopa, condenses the entire generation stage path. This means that when you read this one stanza, you are able to understand all the various parts that are involved in the generation stage through your understanding of the rough structure of the generation stage. Because of your understanding, when you read this stanza, you get a different feeling from before. It is like you are singing a song. Now you know the lyrics. If you understand the lyrics, you derive even greater enjoyment. If you don’t know the lyrics, you don’t understand what is being sung. Still when you hear the song, there is some enjoyment there. But the enjoyment is greater if you understand the lyrics. The same applies to when you recite your prayers, your sadhana and so on. If you understand what you are reciting, you will enjoy it more. If you know the benefits of doing the sadhana recitation and so on, you have greater enjoyment. If you don’t, then it is as if you are pushing yourself.
The three kayas
When we are studying the Dharma and take or receive initiations, we receive these commitments to recite the sadhana and so on. We have to know the significance of bringing death, the intermediate state, and rebirth into the path as the three buddha bodies.  That understanding is crucial. 
In your practice, it is also important for you to think about emptiness. If you are able to do that, then when you meditate on the three bodies, you will be able to purify ordinary death, the intermediate state and rebirth more effectively. 
We don’t want ordinary death. We don’t want the ordinary intermediate state and we don’t want ordinary rebirth. It is important for us to see they are in the nature of suffering. Therefore, in the beginning, we need to develop renunciation. Having recognized that they are in the nature of suffering, we want to eliminate suffering. That is the reason for meditating on bringing them to the path as the three buddha bodies. 
Of course, when we first meditate, we don’t actually have the three buddha bodies but we imagine having them as described in the sadhana. We are not able actually to purify ordinary death, the intermediate state and rebirth during the generation stage. But what we can purify is ordinary appearance and ordinary conception. During the generation stage, we are only trying to have this very stable clear appearance and divine pride. Then we cultivate calm abiding and special insight. When we are able to culminate both the coarse and subtle generation stages, then we are able to eliminate the appearance of ordinariness and adherence to ordinariness. 
Whether you are practising the short sadhana or the long sadhana, you have to ensure that the essential points of bringing death, the intermediate state and rebirth to the path as the three buddha bodies are complete. You have to try to the best of your ability to reflect on those points. 
Planting imprints
It is important for us to deposit imprints on our mind, to place these latencies on our mind. There are various ways to do this. For example, you can do so through meditation. You can do so through contemplation and you can do so through hearing. From hearing explanations, you will place some imprints on your mind. If you are able to contemplate on what you have heard, then the imprints will be even stronger. If you are able to meditate on what you have contemplated, then really you will place some extraordinary imprints on your mind. These very stable imprints will ripen life after life. If we believe in future lives, then we will understand the relevance of leaving powerful imprints on our mind. This is the time to do it.
When we are doing our recitations, we shouldn’t just be reciting. We need to think about what we are reciting. In that way, we will able to leave extraordinary imprints on our mind. To the very best of your ability, try to practise the sadhana and to develop familiarity with it so that you are able to understand, “At this point, this is what I am supposed to think. At that point, this is what I am supposed to think” and so on. In this way, you will be able to understand how to practise the generation stage. 
The purpose of teaching the generation stage in teaching tantra is so that we can make effort during the practice. When you are doing your recitation, if you are able to reflect on what you are reciting, then your practice will have power. If you want to do recitation, that is the way to do it. You will not feel any difficulty. You don’t feel hardship in doing so. However, if we don’t wish to do the recitation and we push ourselves to do it, then we will experience hardship.
The purpose of doing the sadhana is so that we can purify the appearance of ordinariness and adherence to ordinariness through cultivating clear appearance and the divine pride of being the deity. Also, we purify ordinary death, the intermediate state and rebirth through bringing them into the path as the three buddha bodies.
On the generation stage:
You meditate by cultivating clear appearance because that will counteract the appearance of ordinariness. 
You cultivate the divine pride of being the deity because that will counteract the adherence to ordinariness. 
If you have the view of the absence of inherent existence, then your meditation will counteract the conception of true existence. In that way, your meditation becomes very powerful. It becomes the best way to eliminate the conception of true existence. 
This is how you should meditate on the generation stage—with clear appearance, with divine pride and with the view of the absence of inherent or true existence.
During the sessions, you try to cultivate stable clear appearance and divine pride. 
Practising during the session break
After the session, during the session break, there will be a greater danger of there being the appearance of ordinariness and the adherence of ordinariness. Therefore, between the sessions during the break, you have to use mindfulness and introspection and try to counteract the appearance of ordinariness and the adherence to ordinariness. You need to practise in a continuous way during the session break also. If you are very careless after you get out of the session, then your practice will not be very powerful. 
During the break, you should think, “Everything that appears, everything that I see is the body of the Buddha.” You should not forget that. “Whatever sound I hear is the speech of the Buddha. Whatever I think is the mind of the Buddha.” You should use mindfulness and introspection to remember that everything is the body, speech and mind of the Buddha. If you are able to dwell in this awareness, then you will create very little misdeeds. There will be this special power in purifying your negativities. 
~Eating
You practise the different yogas of the deity. For example, when you eat, as I have explained previously, if you can, you should bless the food in the same way you bless the inner offering. You have to purify the substance, generate the substance and increase the substance and so on. Saying Om Ah Hum and visualizing that the food transforms into uncontaminated nectar will not take more than a minute. If you are very familiar with the procedure of blessing the inner offering, then when you say Om Ah Hum, the significance of the blessing will come to mind.
If you are already visualized as Vajrabhairava, there is a letter Hum at your heart. Then you think that this Hum encompasses all the gurus, yidams, buddhas, Dharma protectors and so on. All the objects of refuge are embodied in this letter Hum. You think, “I am offering this nectar to them. They accept the nectar and are satiated by the offering.”
In a very brief way, you can just think that this letter Hum at your heart is the nature of all the objects of refuge. Then you make the offering to this letter Hum.
Alternatively, you can think that inside this drop (the thigle), which is the little circle at the top of the letter Hum is the merit field. All the gurus, all the yidams, all the buddhas, all the Dharma protectors, daka, dakinis, heroes and so forth are inside this little circle. Visualize that they are all complete in that drop and make offerings to it. This practice of offering will not take more than a minute. Even if you don’t have more than a minute, you can do this practice! This is an easy way for you to accumulate merit and purify infinite negativities. When you enjoy this food, you won’t create any misdeeds. There are many reasons for and purpose to doing this practice. If you are a tantric practitioner, then you should try your best to do this practice even though you may not have more than a minute. 
~Sleeping
You can also practise the yoga of sleep. When you lay down to go to sleep, if you are able to reflect well, then your sleep becomes the nature of virtue. Sleep is one of the four changeable mental factors. Depending on the circumstance, it can be virtuous, non-virtuous or neutral. If you are able to reflect properly and cause the sleep to become virtuous, you will derive a lot of benefits. 
How to do this yoga of sleep? 
As you are falling asleep, you should think that with yourself visualized as Vajrabhairava Yamantaka, at your heart, is the letter, Hum.
Countless light rays radiate from this letter, Hum.  
When these light rays strike the environment, the environment transforms into the inestimable mansion of the deity. 
When these lights strike the inhabitants, they are transformed into deities. 
As you lie there, you should think that all these are absorbed back into you yourself visualized as Vajrabhairava.
You, yourself also start to absorb towards the centre, towards the Hum. 
The syllable Hum also absorbs upwards from the bottom towards the top. It absorbs into the crescent moon, the moon into the drop or circle (the thigle) and the circle into the nada, which is the little squiggle. 
Eventually, even the nada disappears and vanishes into unobservable emptiness.
If you are able to fall asleep within this state of clear light, there will be great benefit.
If you are a practitioner, then there are all these very profound detailed instructions for you. What you should do during the sessions, what you should do after the sessions, how you should eat, how you should sleep and so on. If you are able to put these instructions into practice in a consistent manner, then you will be able to accumulate infinite merit and purify infinite misdeeds and obscurations. In that way, you will easily achieve your goal. 
~Waking
From this state of the buddha dharma body, you have to arise in the state of the buddha form body or rupakaya. When you wake up from this clear light of sleep, you have to generate this thought, “I will enact the welfare of sentient beings. For that purpose, I will arise as the form body.” In that way, you visualize yourself arising in the form of the body of the deity. Then you go about doing all the activities for the day.
~Motivation
As long as you wake up, you must generate a pure motivation. You should think, “I will not cause any harm to sentient beings. I will only help them.” This is actually the essence of the bodhisattva vows. “I will not harm sentient beings with my body, speech and mind. I will only benefit them with my body, speech and mind.” You need to generate this kind of pure motivation.
This is what I want to tell you. You have to try your best to practise. When you do your recitation, you have to reflect on its meaning. You have to meditate. Whatever you have understood, please put it into practice. Try your best to do all these yogas of eating, sleeping, waking up and so on. 
THE FOUNDATION OF ALL GOOD QUALITIES (CONT’D)
We have cited some stanzas from The Foundation of All Good Qualities earlier on. The next stanza says:
Like that, may the gurus who show the noble path
And the spiritual friends who practice it have long lives.
Please bless me to pacify completely 
All outer and inner hindrances.
This stanzas talks about the guru who shows the noble path, the good path. This indicates the importance on relying on the spiritual guide. 
Prior to this stanza, the explanations on practising the small scope, the medium scope and the great scope of the stages of the path to enlightenment were given. But still that is not sufficient. One has to enter tantra. One has to practise the generation stage and the completion stage. Without doing that, there is no enlightenment. One has to practise all these stages. One has to practise taking death, the intermediate state and rebirth to the path as the three buddha bodies and so on. 
Through these practices, we are able to free ourselves from suffering. We understand that these have been shown to us by the spiritual guides. We then gain this appreciation of the value of our teachers. When we are really able to appreciate the value of the spiritual guides, then we will see that it is so important for them to have long lives. From the bottom of your heart, you will have this wish for them to live long. 
This is what I am trying to convey to you. You have to try your best. If you are able to connect these points to the short sadhana, that will be very good. I am saying the short sadhana rather than the long sadhana because the long sadhana is quite long.
