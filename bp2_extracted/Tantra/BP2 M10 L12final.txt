DIFFERENCE BETWEEN SUTRA AND TANTRA

Since I’ve talked about the differences between sutra and tantra, if  you’re
asked what the differences between sutra and tantra are, at the very  least,
you should be able to give an answer.

Khen Rinpoche: If someone were to ask you that question, would you  be  able
to give an answer? If you cannot do that after these few classes,  it  means
that nothing has gone in.  Anybody who knows the answer, come  up  and  give
your answer.

Student 1: The difference between sutra and tantra is the substantial  cause
for the form body of a buddha. Sutra asserts that  the  cause  of  the  form
body is  the  practice  of  the  five  perfections  from  generosity  up  to
concentration, whereas tantra asserts that  the  substantial  cause  of  the
form body is the extremely subtle wind.

Also, according to tantra, the wisdom realizing emptiness  asserted  by  the
sutric path is the  coarse  mind  that’s  unable  to  eliminate  the  subtle
knowledge obscurations.

Khen Rinpoche: What you’ve said is from  the  perspective  of  Highest  Yoga
Tantra. Only in Highest Yoga Tantra will you  find  the  discussion  of  the
extremely subtle winds and  mind.   Your  answer  does  not  illustrate  the
difference between sutra and tantra because the  answer  you  gave  is  only
applicable in the context of Highest Yoga Tantra.

The senior students must come up and say something!

Student 2:  I  think  it’s  mainly  due  to  the  fact  that  in  sutra  all
afflictions such as attachment and anger are  to  be  abandoned  whereas  in
tantra, we can actually use attachment to  generate  bliss  and  use  it  to
meditate on emptiness.   This  is  supposed  to  give  a  better  or  faster
understanding of emptiness. This is why it’s  the  quick  path  compared  to
sutra.

Khen Rinpoche:  Are you saying that you need to abandon attachment in  sutra
but when you practise tantra, you don’t have to abandon attachment? Are  you
saying that?

Student 2:  It’s not necessary to abandon attachment on the tantric path.

Khen Rinpoche:  Really?  I am hearing this for the first time.

Student 2: You don’t abandon attachment. You can use it as  a  path  whereas
in sutra you can't use it as a path.

Khen Rinpoche: What do you mean, “use it as a path”?

Student 2:  I cannot reveal too much!

Khen Rinpoche:  Good answer. It’s a secret.  Anyone else?

Student  3:  In  sutra,  we  practise  love  and  compassion  and  the   six
perfections.  In tantra, we have to  abandon  the  ‘I’  and  abandon  cyclic

Khen Rinpoche: Anyone else?

Student 4: In sutra, method and wisdom  are  practised  separately,  but  in
tantra, they are practised together.

Khen Rinpoche: What do you mean by separately?

Student 4: In sutra, you don’t practise the method and the wisdom together.

Khen Rinpoche: What does that mean, “not practised together”?

Student 4: First, you practise the method and then you practise the  wisdom.
 You don’t practise them together.

Khen Rinpoche: They are not practised together? They don’t come together?

Student 4: They come together but you don’t practise them at the  same  time

Student 5: I think sutra is  based  on  the  six  perfections  in  order  to

Khen Rinpoche: OK. That sounds better.  Can anybody else give more details?

I’ve already explained all of this. A lot of  information  has  been  given.
You should remember what I’ve said.
REVIEW

The four complete purities
We gave the two vehicles the names, the Causal Perfection  Vehicle  and  the
Resultant Vajra Vehicle.

The main point is that in tantra, you will find the perfect explanation  for
actualizing the form body.

In sutra, the factor of method is said to be the cause for  the  form  body.
If we investigate what constitute these methods in sutra,  it’s  essentially
the mind generation together with the practice of  the  perfections.   Other
than that, there isn’t any other explanation for  the  causes  of  the  form
body in sutra.

In tantra (or Secret Mantra), it’s posited as the  meditation  on  the  path
that’s concordant with the aspects of the  four  complete  purities  of  the
resultant stage of enlightenment.  There’s a presentation of  this  practice
that can act as the substantial cause of the form body.

Such a path is called tantra whereas a path that is merely  the  cultivation
of mind generation and the six perfections is called the  Causal  Perfection
Vehicle.

The  dividing  line  between  sutra  and  tantra  is  whether  there’s  this
meditation of the path that’s  concordant  with  the  aspects  of  the  four
complete purities of the resultant stage of enlightenment.

Khen Rinpoche: What are the four complete purities?

Student 6: The four complete purities are:
1. the complete pure body
2. the complete pure environment
3. the complete pure enjoyments
4. the complete pure activities

The difference between the sutra and tantra is based  on  whether  there  is
the meditation on the path that’s concordant with the aspects  of  the  four
complete purities of the resultant stage of  enlightenment.    You  must  be
able to say what the four complete purities are. The four complete  purities
are:
   1. The complete purity of body
   2. The complete purity of environment
   3. The complete purity of enjoyments
   4. The complete purity of enlightened activities
Being able to say that is one thing but you must be able to  say  how  these
are meditated on in tantra as well.

Khen Rinpoche: I think I have  explained  how  to  meditate  on  these  four
purities.

Student 6:
 •  The complete purity of body refers to  the  meditation  on  the  deity’s
   body.
 • The complete purity of the environment refers to the  meditation  on  the
   supporting and supported  mandalas,  in  particular,  meditating  on  the
   inestimable mansion.
 • The complete purity of  enjoyments  refers  to  the  practice  of  making
   offerings to the self-generation.
 • The complete purity of activities refers to the practice in Secret Mantra
   whereby on the basis of deity yoga, you visualize the seed  syllable  and
   mantra garland at your heart emitting light rays going out through  which
   you accomplish the purposes of oneself and others. This is  the  practice
   that is concordant in aspect to the complete purity  of  the  enlightened
   activities of the resultant stage.

Various terms for the tantric path
Sometimes, you hear terms like the Vajra Vehicle,  the  Method  Vehicle  and
the Mantra Vehicle being used.
 • The Vajra Vehicle (or Vajrayana)  refers  primarily  to  the  indivisible
   method and wisdom, i.e., both factors of method and wisdom are present in
   one consciousness.  This feature is not  found  in  sutra.  Although  the
   Causal Perfection Vehicle talks about the union  of  method  and  wisdom,
   that actually  means  the  practice  of  method  conjoined  with  wisdom.
   Likewise,  the  practice  of  wisdom  is  conjoined  with  method.   They
   complement and assist one another. Nevertheless, these  two  factors  are
   not practised together in one single consciousness.
 • It’s called the Resultant Vehicle because one practises the meditation on
   the four complete purities of the resultant  stage  while  one’s  on  the
   path.
 • It’s called the Method  Vehicle  because  it  possesses  a  far  superior
   explanation of achieving the form body than that presented in sutra.
 • It’s also called the Secret Mantra because this practice has to  be  kept
   hidden from unsuitable vessels and at the  time  of  cultivating  such  a
   practice, one has to do it privately and secretly.


   It is called mantra because it’s a mind protection. In this  context,  it
   primarily  means  protecting  the  mind  from  ordinary  appearance   and
   adherence to ordinariness. This is done with the practice of  deity  yoga
   where one elicits clear appearance and divine pride.  Through  that,  one
   overcomes ordinary appearance and adherence to  ordinariness.  These  are
   the methods for subduing and disciplining the mind. Whether it  is  mind,
   consciousness or knower, we are talking about the same thing.   Here,  we
   are protecting  the  mind  from  ordinary  appearance  and  adherence  to
   ordinariness.

Disciplining the body and mind
With regard to protecting the mind, it depends on the context. For  example,
in the practice of the persons of small  capacity,  when  you  look  at  the
scriptural collections of the Hinayana, you will find  teachings  mainly  on
impermanence and the four noble truths.  These are the  coarse  methods  for
disciplining the mind and subduing the coarser  consciousnesses.    This  is
why there’re lots of  explanations  on  the  training  of  the  precepts  in
relation to the pratimoksha vows, the vinaya and so forth. There’re lots  of
explanations on how to discipline even physical conduct and so forth.  These
are methods to subdue and discipline  the  body  and  mind  at  the  grosser
level.

In the Mahayana, on the basis of  pure  love  and  compassion,  there’s  the
practice  of  the  perfections.  These  are  also  methods  to  subdue   and
discipline the body and mind at a grosser level but these methods  are  more
elaborate than what’s found in the Hinayana teachings.

In general, in the Mahayana, there’s the explanation of  profound  emptiness
and  how  the  wisdom  realizing  emptiness  is  conjoined  with  the   mind
generation.   With  these  two,  there’s  an  explanation  of  how  the  two
obscurations are abandoned.  We dealt with  this  in  some  detail  when  we
looked at the grounds and paths.

On top of the Mahayana, there is tantra. There are four classes of tantra.

The first three classes of tantra are:
1. Action Tantra
2. Performance Tantra
3. Yoga Tantra

There are the practices of deity yoga and taking bliss as the path.  Through
that, one progresses towards enlightenment to achieve the  two  bodies.  The
methods of the three lower classes of  tantra  make  the  practitioner  come
even closer to enlightenment.

In Highest Yoga Tantra, one will find methods that make the  achievement  of
enlightenment even faster than that. In essence, there are the practices  of
the generation stage and the completion  stage.  Specifically,  by  striking
the vital points of the channel winds and drops, one comes  even  closer  to
achieving enlightenment.

As one moves from the Hinayana into the  sutric  Mahayana,  then  the  three
lower classes of tantra and finally, the Highest Yoga  Tantra,  there  is  a
progression on the profundity of the methods used  to  discipline  the  body
and mind. As the methods become increasingly more profound,  they  move  the
individual to achieving enlightenment more and more  quickly.  You  need  to
have a rough idea of this.

MIND GENERATION IN SUTRA VS. TANTRA

The mind generation that’s needed for the practice of tantra is said  to  be
much more powerful than the mind generation as  explained  in  the  Mahayana
sutra. Having said that, is there a difference between the bodhicitta  (mind
generation) of  the  Causal  Perfection  Vehicle  and  the  Resultant  Vajra
Vehicle? If you were to say that there’s a  difference—that  one’s  stronger
than the other—then you must be able to explain the reason why as well.

In  the  Mahayana  sutra,  one  develops  the  mind  generation  where   the
individual thinks, “No matter  how  many  eons  it’ll  take  me  to  achieve
enlightenment and no matter how difficult it is, I will engage in  the  path
to achieve enlightenment for the benefit of others.”

You’ll  also  find  the  special  bodhicitta  that  says,  “I  must  achieve
enlightenment quickly and more quickly.”

From one’s own perspective, one is willing to bear whatever hardships  there
may be for no matter how long it takes to finish the collections to  achieve
enlightenment. But  from  the  perspective  of  tantra,  although  the  mind
generation seeks  enlightenment,  one  also  considers  the  perspective  of
sentient beings. Although one can bear the hardships, one  also  understands
that sentient beings from their own side cannot wait so  long.  They  cannot
endure so much suffering while waiting for  one  to  achieve  enlightenment.
Therefore, one has that strong courage to achieve enlightenment  as  quickly
as possible for their benefit. Therefore, the courage underlying  bodhicitta
in tantra is far stronger than that found in the Mahayana sutra.

RECEIVING THE INITIATION

In the last lesson, I talked about  how  the  four  classes  of  tantra  are
differentiated by the way in which attachment is utilized.

Regardless of the class of tantra that one  wishes  to  enter  into,  first,
one’s  mental  continuum  must  be  ripened  by  receiving   an   initiation
(empowerment or wang).  After  receiving  the  initiation,  one  must  abide
purely in the vows and the samayas that one took during the initiation.

When one receives an initiation into Action Tantra, one  receives  only  two
initiations:
1. the water initiation
2. the crown initiation
Conferring these two is sufficient for Action Tantra.

In Performance Tantra, there are five initiations:
1. the water initiation
2. the crown initiation
3. the vajra initiation
4. the bell initiation
5. the name initiation

As for Yoga Tantra, on  top  of  these  five,  there  is  the  vajra  master
initiation.

In Action Tantra, although the  water  and  crown  initiations  are  usually
given, it is also alright to confer all the five initiations  (listed  under
Performance Tantra). That is not wrong.

Bodhisattva vows and tantric vows
In Action  Tantra,  the  main  vows  and  samayas  to  be  guarded  are  the
bodhisattva vows.
When one takes the bodhisattva vows, one has to guard against  the  18  root
downfalls and the 46 faults or infractions. Likewise, one has  to  take  the
bodhisattva vows in the case of taking a Performance Tantra initiation.

When one takes an initiation into  Action  Tantra  and  Performance  Tantra,
there is no taking of tantric vows and as such, there is  no  need  to  keep
the tantric vows.

With regard to the complete initiation into Yoga Tantra, on top of the  five
initiations, one has to take the vajra master  initiation.  Once  one  takes
the vajra master initiation, one has to take  the  tantric  vows.  One  will
have  to  keep  the  tantric  vows  that  involve  abandoning  the  14  root
downfalls, the eight heavy transgressions and so forth. In other  words,  if
an initiation has the vajra master initiation, then one will  have  to  take
and keep the tantric vows.
In Highest Yoga Tantra, there are the vase  initiation,  secret  initiation,
wisdom-knowledge initiation and  the  word  initiation.  One  has  to  take,
protect and keep both the bodhisattva vows and tantric vows.

Difference between vow and samaya
Sometimes, there is a question as to what is the difference  between  a  vow
and a samaya. Lama Tsongkhapa mentioned in his text, Clarifying the  Meaning
of Empowerment, that a vow  and  a  samaya  are  one  entity  but  different
isolates:
 • A samaya means not going beyond the prescribed boundary.
 • A vow means to restrain oneself from doing something that one should  not
   do. From that point of view, it is called a vow.
If you look at their meanings, both a samaya and  a  vow  are  the  same  in
meaning. It is just that they both have  different  names.  Therefore,  they
are considered to be different isolates.

Entering the mandala
There’s also a question as to whether an individual is allowed to enter  and
see the mandala if the  person  has  not  received  the  initiation  of  the
relevant deity. For example, there are  individuals  who  are  not  suitable
vessels for the practice of tantra.  They’re not ready  and  therefore  such
initiations are not given to them.

Nevertheless, it  is  possible  to  allow  such  individuals  to  enter  the
mandala, by revealing the mandala for them to see. It’s said that this  will
confer great benefit as  it  purifies  a  great  deal  of  negativities  and
establishes positive imprints for the practice of tantra in the future.

The procedure of the initiation
In  Action  Tantra,  one  receives  the  water  initiation  and  the   crown
initiation. Let’s take the water initiation as an  example.  In  this  case,
the initiation substance is the vase water. In  dependence  on  that—whether
the vase touches one’s head or whether one drinks the water—if one  were  to
actually generate the wisdom of great bliss, that’s the best initiation!

There’s a difference in the level of how one receives  an  initiation.  This
depends on the individual and his level of understanding of emptiness.  Does
he have any understanding of emptiness in the first place? Even if he  does,
is his level of understanding a doubt tending  to  the  factual,  a  correct
assumption or an inferential realization? Commensurate with one’s  level  of
understanding  of  the  view,  that  determines  the  extent  to  which   an
initiation has been received.

There are three different levels of receiving  the  initiation—the  supreme,
middling and lowest. These levels depend  on  the  individual’s  capacity—of
how much understanding of emptiness that individual has or doesn’t have.

The person who is conferring the initiation will give  an  introduction  and
say a lot of things during the initiation, “Now you visualize this. This  is
happening. Think that you have received this.” At the very least,  you  need
to  imagine  that  you  understood  what’s  being  said  and  what’s  to  be
visualized for you to actually receive the initiation. This is  the  minimal
way of receiving an initiation. If you don’t pay attention, then  you  don’t
receive the initiation at all.
We may say we’ve taken an initiation, but whether we have actually  received
it or not is a question mark and remains to be  seen  even  though  we  were
there physically and we felt we have taken it.

This is one reason for studying. At least now you  know  that  in  order  to
receive an initiation, you must pay full attention to what’s being said  and
to think, “This is happening. Now I have received  it.”  This  is  the  very
least you can do. You must have  this  single-pointed  devotion  and  belief
that you understood whatever is being said.

Initiations in Action Tantra
In the context of Action Tantra, you’ll receive  the  water  initiation  and
the crown initiation.

What is the purpose of receiving the water and crown initiations?
 • The five stains are purified.
 • The five aggregates are transformed.

 • The water initiation leaves  the  potential  (imprint)  in  the  mind  to
   achieve the resultant truth body, the dharmakaya, in the future. You  are
   empowered to meditate on the yoga without signs.
 • The crown initiation leaves the potential in  the  mind  to  achieve  the
   resultant form body in the future. You are empowered to meditate  on  the
   yoga with signs.

Initiations in Highest Yoga Tantra
~ Vase initiation
In Highest Yoga Tantra, among the four initiations, the first  is  the  vase
initiation. This purifies the bodily defilements and  leaves  the  potential
to achieve the resultant emanation body in the future. You are empowered  to
meditate on the generation stage.

In the beginning of the initiation, there is the flower  garland  initiation
that leaves the potential for you to achieve  enlightenment  in  a  specific
lineage of the five buddha lineages. It makes it certain for you to  achieve
enlightenment in that lineage.  It  also  establishes  a  special  dependent
arising to be taken care of by that deity.

During the vase initiation, we start off with the water initiation.
1.  The  water  initiation  leaves  an  imprint  to  achieve   the   perfect
   abandonment of the two stains.
2. By taking the crown initiation, it leaves a special potential for one  to
   achieve the form body of a buddha, in particular, some of the major signs
   such as the crown protrusion.
3. By taking the vajra initiation, one leaves the  imprint  to  achieve  the
   non-dual  wisdom  of  a  buddha  that  enables  one  to  achieve  perfect
   realizations.
4. The bell initiation leaves a potential  to  actualize  the  speech  of  a
   buddha.
5. The name initiation is said to leave the potential for you to attain  the
   particular buddha name that you will be called in the future.
6. The vajra master initiation empowers one to become a son of  the  lineage
   of Vajradhara.
These five initiations with the vajra master initiation constitute the  vase
initiation in the Highest Yoga Tantra.
~ Secret initiation
When you receive the secret initiation, your channel  winds  and  drops  are
blessed and an imprint is planted  to  achieve  the  illusory  body  in  the
future. On the basis of  achieving  the  illusory  body,  one  achieves  the
complete enjoyment body during enlightenment.

~ Knowledge-wisdom initiation
By  receiving  knowledge-wisdom   initiation,   you   purify   your   mental
obscurations. You are empowered to meditate on the path of clear light.  You
have the potential to achieve the resultant dharmakaya.

In short,
 • By receiving the vase initiation, the bodily defilements are purified and
   the potential is planted to achieve the resultant emanation body.
 • Through receiving the secret initiation on the  basis  of  blessing  your
   channel winds and drops, the defilements of speech are purified. You  are
   empowered to meditate on the illusory body and will achieve the  complete
   enjoyment body in the future.
 • Through receiving the knowledge-wisdom initiation, you purify the  mental
   defilements and you’re empowered to meditate on the path of clear  light.
   You’ll actualize the resultant dharmakaya in the future.
 • The word initiation means giving an introduction through  the  medium  of
   words  and  causing  the  recipient  of  the  initiation  to  develop  an
   understanding  of  the  meaning  of  unification.  The  word   initiation
   functions to leave an imprint to purify the defilements and  obscurations
   that are accumulated by the body, speech and mind. Through that,  one  is
   empowered to achieve the learners’ union and the non-learners’ union.

You have to know all these points. Whether you’ve received an initiation  or
not depends on  whether  you  know  what’s  happening.  If  you’re  able  to
think—“I have received this initiation. This is what was purified and  I  am
now  empowered  to  meditate  on  this.”—and  if  you’re  able  to  meditate
accordingly, it leaves a very special imprint in the mind.

This is one reason for studying. By  studying  this,  you’ll  know  what  to
think and you’ll know what’s happening when you take  an  initiation.  Also,
you’ll be able to think properly.  Naturally  this  will  leave  a  positive
imprint in your mind.

When an initiation is given, an explanation is given  on  what  to  meditate
and what to visualize. You need to think about  what  is  happening  and  so
forth and you have to follow accordingly. If  you  don’t  think  and  follow
accordingly, you’ll not receive the initiation at all.

Generally, there are many people  who  are  present  at  an  initiation  but
they’ve no idea what is happening and no idea what the person who is  giving
the initiation is saying. They’re unable to follow  what’s  being  said  nor
are they able to think. However, if these people have  some  real  faith  in
their guru and the deity, there are still  some  benefits.  At  least,  they
receive some imprints and make a connection with tantra. They  also  make  a
good connection with the guru and the deity.

Keeping the vows and commitments
It is emphasized that keeping the vows, commitments  and  samayas  that  are
taken during an initiation is very important.

One has to put effort into  guarding  whatever  vows  or  samayas  that  are
received during the initiation. If one neglects this, even if  one  were  to
practise, one will not get much benefit. Keeping pure vows  and  samayas  is
the very foundation for all the accomplishments  just  as  a  field  is  the
basis for crops to grow and be harvested.

When we take the bodhisattva vows during an initiation,  we  are  making  an
extremely heavy and huge promise. When we are taking the  bodhisattva  vows,
we are summoning all the buddhas and bodhisattvas to be  our  witnesses.  We
are also taking the vows in the presence of our gurus, calling  on  them  to
witness us making the promise to  work  for  the  welfare  of  every  single
sentient being. Since this is the case, when we go against  the  bodhisattva
vows, we incur a fault that is equal  to  the  number  of  sentient  beings,
i.e., numberless.

However, if we’re able to guard our training and keep our vows, the  benefit
is  likewise  commensurate  with  the  number  of  sentient  beings,   i.e.,
numberless.

For those who’ve developed bodhicitta,  even  if  they  engage  in  a  small
virtue, that virtue is so powerful because it’s multiplied by the number  of
sentient beings that’s numberless. This comes back  to  the  motivation  and
intention that focusses on the welfare of numberless sentient beings.

It’s said in the teachings that if you take the tantric vows and  you  don’t
incur a root downfall, even if you don’t practise the path,  you’ll  achieve
enlightenment within seven or 16 lifetimes.

It is strongly emphasized that it’s so important to keep the  vows  and  the
samayas purely. If you don’t keep the vows  and  the  samayas  purely,  then
whatever practices you try to do will not yield any results  whether  it  is
enlightenment or even a common attainment. You’ll achieve nothing!

If you keep  the  samayas  and  vows  purely,  even  if  you  don’t  do  any
spectacular practice, it is said that you’ll achieve enlightenment in  seven
or 16 lifetimes.

Student 1: After an initiation, as long as  one  does  not  incur  any  root
downfall, one’ll achieve enlightenment in seven lifetimes. Does  this  apply
to someone who has taken the initiation but hasn’t  entered  the  path  yet,
i.e., he has no renunciation, contrived or otherwise?  Or  does  this  apply
to both those who haven’t entered the path as well as those  who’ve  entered
the path?

Khen Rinpoche: I think it’s better to say that it  applies  to  someone  who
has not entered the path.

When we say that if you keep the vows and samayas purely, even if you  don’t
do any practice, you will achieve enlightenment in seven  or  16  lifetimes,
this doesn’t mean that you can just sit around and not  do  anything  except
keeping your vows and hoping to achieve enlightenment. This is not  what  it
means. You cannot take this literally. It refers to a person who is  keeping
the vows and samayas well  but  isn’t  putting  in  a  lot  of  effort.  But
definitely,  he  has  to  meditate  correctly.   Then,  even   without   any
spectacular practice, he’ll still be able to achieve enlightenment in  seven
or 16 lifetimes.

On the other hand, if you are not keeping  your  vows  and  samayas  purely,
then it doesn’t matter what you are meditating on. You may try very hard  to
meditate on this and that but you’ll not achieve much or anything at all.

Student 2:  Could you please elaborate further on the definition  of  samaya
and clarify the meaning of “not going beyond the boundary.”

Khen Rinpoche: Whether it is a vow or samaya,  essentially,  they  are  just
different isolates. They are the  same  entity  but  are  differentiated  by
these different names.
 • It is called a samaya (or commitment) because you do not  go  beyond  the
   prescribed boundary.
 • It is called a vow because you stop doing that which shouldn’t be done in
   the first place, i.e., you restrain yourself from doing those things that
   you shouldn’t be doing. From that point of view, it’s called a vow.
It’s just a different way of saying the same thing.

Student 2: In Lesson 8, you mentioned that in tantra, we  are  dealing  more
with enjoyment body and this is why we  don’t  beseech  them  to  remain.  I
thought we are dealing more with the nirmanakaya, the emanation body.

Khen Rinpoche: The emanation body is not the principal in tantra.

Student 2:  When we  receive  initiations  of  deities  such  as  Yamantaka,
Heruka, and Kalachakra, aren’t these  emanation  bodies  and  not  enjoyment
bodies?

Khen Rinpoche: If you looked at their  aspect,  most  of  them  are  in  the
aspect of being accompanied by attachment.

If you are looking at sutra, you are  dealing  with  the  supreme  emanation
body like Shakyamuni Buddha, who  is  in  the  aspect  of  being  devoid  of
attachment. This is why he is a monk.

When you look at tantra, however, most of them are in the  aspect  of  being
accompanied by attachment. Look at Chenrezig or  Tara.  From  head  to  toe,
they are wearing crowns, earrings,  bracelets,  anklets,  necklaces  and  so
forth. These are the aspects that show they are accompanied  by  attachment.
The Buddha is not adorned with these ornaments.

Student 2:  So  when  they  display  the  aspect  of  being  accompanied  by
attachment, they are enjoyment bodies?

Khen Rinpoche: You can say that.

Student 2: When you meditate, you generate  yourself  into  the  nirmanakaya
form first. I thought that that nirmanakaya  is  associated  with  emanation
bodies and not enjoyment bodies. I am wrong?

Khen Rinpoche: Before that, you are  a  sentient  being.  After  you  become
enlightened, you don’t die. (Pertaining to  the  Yamantaka  practice),  both
Manjushri and Yamantaka are enjoyment bodies.

Khen Rinpoche: You must read the root text first. I will read the root  text
in the next class.
