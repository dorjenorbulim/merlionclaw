THE VASE EMPOWERMENTS

As we were discussing last week, in order to practise tantra,  you  need  to
enter tantra. The way to enter tantra is by  receiving  an  empowerment  (or
initiation) and to do that, you have to enter the mandala.  The usual  types
of mandala are the painted cloth mandala and the coloured sand mandala.   By
entering either one of these mandalas, one receives the vase initiation.

As mentioned last week, there are  different  types  of  vase  empowerments.
The vase empowerment is made up of the empowerments of the  buddhas  of  the
five lineages plus the vajra master initiation.

The first initiation: the water initiation of Askhobhya
You enter the painted cloth mandala and  receive  the  water  initiation  of
Akshobhya. The initiation substance is the vase water.  Such  an  initiation
is called the water initiation of Akshobhya.

One begins by purifying oneself as well as  the  initiation  substance  into
emptiness. In this context, the initiation  substance  is  the  vase  water.
From the state of emptiness, the vase water is generated  as  Akshobhya  and
oneself is also generated as Akshobhya.
The vase water that has been generated as  Akshobhya  again  dissolves  into
the vase water. It is in the nature of Akshobhya  even  though  it  has  the
aspect of the vase water.
That is the essence of this ritual.

When you are  given  this  vase  water  that  is  obtained  from  dissolving
Akshobhya mother and father, you taste it in your mouth.  As the  master  is
giving you instructions during the  initiation,  you  must  try  to  do  the
visualization to the best of your ability.  So,  when  you  taste  the  vase
water, you have to try to visualize or imagine that you have  generated  the
exalted wisdom of bliss and emptiness. If you  are  able  to  do  this,  you
obtain the vase empowerment.

Through receiving this water initiation of Akshobhya:
• You purify the stain of hatred.
• Among the five aggregates, you are able to transform the aggregate of the
  consciousness. What that means is that your  aggregate  of  consciousness
  will become the nature of Akshobhya in the future.
• Also, you are able to manifest the wisdom of the dharmadhatu (the  sphere
  of reality) in the future. You are  made  to  deposit  the  potential  to
  achieve the attainment of the lineage of Akshobhya.
• Associated with  Akshobhya  are  the  seed  syllable  Hum  and  the  hand
  implement is the vajra

The second initiation: the crown initiation of Ratnasambhava
Here, the deity  is  Ratnasambhava.  The  substance,  i.e.,  the  crown,  is
generated as Ratnasambhava mother and father. They  dissolve  and  become  a
crown. When that crown is given to you, as soon as  it  touches  your  head,
you have to imagine you have generated the special exalted wisdom  of  bliss
and emptiness.

• The seed syllable of Ratnasambhava is Tram and the hand  implement  is  a
  jewel.
• The function of this crown empowerment is that it purifies the  stain  of
  miserliness.
• It also  transforms  your  aggregate  of  feeling  from  among  the  five
  aggregates.  What  that  means  is  that  you  will  be  able  to  become
  Ratnasambhava in the future.
• From among the five wisdoms, the wisdom of equality will be manifest.

The third initiation: the vajra initiation of Amitabha
As  before,  you  should  purify  yourself  as  well   as   the   initiation
substance—in this case, it is a vajra—into emptiness. It is  then  generated
as Amitabha father and  mother.   The  vajra  that  has  been  generated  as
Amitabha father and mother  will  dissolve  into  light  and  the  light  is
transformed into the vajra. When the vajra is given to you, as soon  as  you
hold it in your hand, you have to imagine that the  special  exalted  wisdom
of bliss and emptiness is  generated.   By  doing  that  visualization,  you
obtain the vajra initiation.

The function of this initiation is that:
• it purifies the stain of attachment
• it transforms  the  aggregate  of  discrimination  from  among  the  five
  aggregates
• it will manifest as the wisdom of the individual analysis from among  the
  five
  wisdoms
As for Amitabha, the seed syllable is Hrih and the implement (or symbol)  is
the lotus.

The fourth initiation: the bell initiation of Amoghasiddhi
As before, you do the visualization  to  purify  yourself  as  well  as  the
initiation substance—in this case, it is the bell—into emptiness.  From  the
state of emptiness, you are generated as  Amoghasiddhi  father  and  mother.
The bell is also generated as  Amoghasiddhi  father  and  mother.  Then  the
Amoghasiddhi father and mother generated from the bell dissolve  into  light
and arise as the bell. When the bell is given to you, as soon  as  you  hold
it in your hand, imagine that special exalted wisdom of bliss and  emptiness
is generated whereby you obtain the bell initiation.

The function of this bell initiation is that:
• it purifies the stain of jealousy
• it transforms the aggregate of compositional factors from among the  five
  aggregates
• it manifests the wisdom of accomplishing activities from among  the  five
  wisdoms

The seed syllable of Amoghasiddhi is Kham and the implement is a sword.

The fifth initiation: the name initiation of Vairochana.
In  this  case,  there  is  no  particular  initiation  substance.  In   the
beginning, you purify yourself into emptiness and then generate yourself  as
Vairochana.  As you have previously tossed the flower, you  have  determined
your connection with a particular lineage and based on that, you  are  given
a name. When that name is  expressed,  upon  hearing  that  name,  then  you
should generate the special exalted wisdom of the  same  emptiness.  Through
such a visualization, you obtain the name initiation of Vairochana.

The function of the name initiation is that:
 • it purifies the stains of ignorance
 • it transforms the aggregates of form among the five aggregates
 • it manifests the mirror-like wisdom from among the five wisdoms.

The seed syllabus of Vairochana is Om and the implement is the wheel.

The reason for explaining these procedures is so that you can receive  these
initiations properly.  You have many opportunities  to  receive  initiations
and many of you also perform self-initiation. In order to do that, you  have
to enter the mandala and receive the  initiation.  Therefore,  when  you  do
those practices, you should be thinking of the  meanings  I  have  explained
earlier.

These are the empowerments  of  the  buddhas  of  the  five  lineages.   The
significance is that the purified form of our five  aggregates  becomes  the
buddhas of the five lineages. For example:
• The purified aggregate of consciousness is Akshobhya.
• The aggregate of feeling is transformed and becomes Ratnasambhava,
• The aggregate of discrimination is transformed and becomes the nature  of
  Amitabha.
• The aggregate of compositional factor  is  transformed  and  becomes  the
  nature of Amoghasiddhi.
•  The  aggregate  of  form  is  transformed  and  becomes  the  nature  of
  Vairochana.
In that way, we have these five aggregates whose  purified  forms  arise  as
the buddhas of the five lineages.

The sixth initiation: the vajra master initiation
After the five initiations of the buddhas of the  five  lineages  are  done,
then we have the sixth initiation, which is  the  vajra  master  initiation.
In the vajra master initiation, the disciple  is  generated  as  Vajradhara,
placed on the throne and then given the three mudras.

What are these mudras? They are the three commitments:
 • the mudra—the commitment of the body
 • the bell—the commitment of speech
 • the vajra—the commitment of the mind
It is through the understanding of being conferred these three  mudras  that
one can engage in studies, later explaining these to others  and  conferring
initiations upon others.

So by receiving this empowerment, you are able to  cleanse  the  defilements
(or obscurations) preventing you from accomplishing  the  particular  buddha
lineage. For example:
• By receiving the initiation of Akshobhya, you are  able  to  cleanse  the
  defilements that prevent you from becoming enlightened in  the  Akshobhya
  lineage.
• The crown initiation of Ratnasambhava establishes the potential  for  you
  to be able to attain one of the signs  of  a  buddha's  body,  the  crown
  protrusion (or ushnisha).
• By receiving the vajra initiation of Amitabha, the potential is deposited
  for you to achieve the final wisdom of non-dual bliss and emptiness, that
  is, the mind of a buddha in your own continuum in the future.
• The fourth initiation, the bell initiation of Amoghasiddhi  deposits  the
  potential for you to actualize the speech of a buddha  and  to  turn  the
  Wheel of Dharma.
• The name initiation of Vairochana  deposits  the  potential  for  you  to
  achieve enlightenment and become a buddha with that particular lineage.
• The vajra master initiation deposits the potential  for  you  to  achieve
  dominance over the kingdom of Dharma.
So, these are the  six  vase  empowerments.  In  the  context  of  the  four
empowerments, the six initiations that we mentioned earlier are  counted  as
one called the vase empowerment.

COMMITMENTS OF THE FIVE BUDDHA LINEAGES

Having received the initiations of the buddhas of  the  five  lineages,  you
have to keep the commitments of the five buddha lineages.

So, first of all, in the lineage of Vairochana, there are six commitments:
 • 1—3: the commitment to practise the three kinds of ethics
 • 4—6: the commitment of going for refuge to the Three Jewels

The four commitments of Akshobhya are related to the  deity  Akshobhya.  The
four
commitments are:
1. the vajra
2. the bell
3. the mudra
4. the master

With the Buddha Ratnasambhava, there are the four commitments  of  the  four
types of giving.

For Amitabha, there are three commitments:
1. the outer commitment
2. the secret commitment
3. the commitment of the three vehicles
• The outer commitment refers to upholding the  Dharma  of  the  two  lower
  tantras, Action Tantra and Performance Tantra.
• The secret commitment refers to upholding the Dharma of Yoga  Tantra  and
  Highest Yoga Tantra.
• The two lower tantras emphasize the outer whereas the two higher  tantras
  emphasize the inner.
• The commitment of the three vehicles refers to upholding  the  Dharma  of
  the three vehicles—the Dharma of the Hearer, Solitary  Realizer  and  the
  Mahayana Vehicles. In other words, you have to engage in the hearing  and
  contemplation of all the Buddha’s teachings.

In the context of the Kalachakra tantra, His Holiness  the  Dalai  Lama  has
mentioned that:
• the outer refers to non-Buddhists. Then one has to  engage  in  the  non-
  Buddhist scriptures.
• the secret refers to the four classes of tantra
• the three vehicles are as before, i.e., the Vehicles of Hearers, Solitary
  Realizers and the Mahayana Vehicle.

Khen Rinpoche:  I heard something like that?  Did  you  hear  this?  At  the
Kalachakra initiation, he was saying something  like  that.  One  commentary
mentioned that was the meaning of ‘outer.’

There are two commitments of Amoghasiddhi:
1. To uphold all the vows. If you have the three types of vows,  you  should
  uphold them properly. If you have the two types of vows, then you  should
  uphold the two types of vows properly.
2. To make as many offerings as you can.
These are the 19 commitments of the buddhas of the five lineages.

THE SECRET EMPOWERMENT

From among the four empowerments,  the  second  empowerment  is  called  the
secret empowerment. The secret empowerment takes place  in  the  mandala  of
conventional bodhicitta. You are given a secret substance  to  taste.  As  a
result of tasting the secret substance, your  winds  are  made  serviceable.
So, then you should think you have generated the  exalted  wisdom  of  bliss
and emptiness. In that way, you receive the empowerment.

In dependence on the secret empowerment, in the future, your winds and  mind
will become indivisible and you will be able to  achieve  the  sambhogakaya,
complete enjoyment body. The sambhogakaya or  the  complete  enjoyment  body
mentioned here is not the same  as  the  enjoyment  body  mentioned  in  the
context of sutra. In  the  context  of  sutra,  we  say  that  the  complete
enjoyment body is the  final  body  having  the  five  certainties.  In  the
context of tantra, this is not what we  are  talking  about.  Here  we  talk
about the achievement of the wind and speech becoming indivisible. So,  that
is the attainment of the sambhogakaya, the complete enjoyment body.

Among the five certainties explained in the context of sutra,  one  of  them
is that of only teaching the Mahayana Dharma. That’s the quality of  speech.
So it’s reasonable that here in the context of  tantra,  the  attainment  of
speech is called the sambhogakaya, complete enjoyment body.

THE WISDOM EMPOWERMENT

In the third of the four  empowerments,  we  have  the  wisdom  empowerment.
Sometimes, this is called  the  knowledge  wisdom  empowerment.  This  takes
place in the bhaga mandala. Once inside the mandala, a  knowledge  woman  is
given. As a result of that, one generates the exalted wisdom  of  bliss  and
emptiness. As a result of
receiving the wisdom  empowerment  in  the  future,  one  will  achieve  the
dharmakaya.

THE WORD EMPOWERMENT

The fourth empowerment, the word empowerment, takes place in the mandala  of
ultimate bodhicitta. In this empowerment, the master introduces the  meaning
of union to you. He explains the meaning of union as the union of the  clear
light and illusory body. By developing a good understanding  of  what  union
means, you attain the word empowerment.

So, these are the four empowerments of Highest Yoga Tantra. By entering  the
mandala of Highest Yoga Tantra, one can  receive  these  four  empowerments.
You should try to remember their significance and in the  future,  when  you
are receiving an initiation or if you  are  taking  a  self-initiation,  you
should try to recall those various meanings to the best of your ability.  At
the appropriate juncture, you should think, “I have  generated  the  exalted
wisdom of bliss and emptiness and in the future, I am going  to  attain  the
state of this and that. I  have  deposited  the  latencies  to  be  able  to
achieve this and that.” If you are able to recall these  meanings  properly,
even if you do it for just one minute, you will receive great benefit.

     Reply: I think he means …

In the definition posited by Panchen Losang Chokyi Gyaltsen:
• He talks about the generation stage as something  that  “does  not  arise
  from causing the winds to enter,  abide,  and  dissolve  in  the  central
  channel due to meditation.”
• The first part of the definition mentions that it is “a yoga that matures
  the mental continuum for its resultant completion stage.” Therefore,  the
  generation stage matures or  acts  to  ripen  the  completion  stage.  It
  generates the completion stage.
• The last part of the definition says, “but newly investigates any  aspect
  related to the three stages of birth, death, and the intermediate state.”
  This means that the generation stage is a yoga that  newly  imagines  any
  aspect that is concordant with birth, death and the intermediate state.
This is the definition set forth by Panchen Losang Chokyi Gyaltsen.

However, the author Ngawang Palden does not agree. He  says  the  definition
applies to the main generation stage but it does not apply  to  all  of  the
generation stage because a generation stage does not necessarily fulfil  the
definition in that it does not
have to engage in meditation that has a concordant aspect with birth,  death
or the
intermediate state.

bringing birth, death and the intermediate state to the path  as  the  three
buddha bodies
What is most important in  the  context  of  the  generation  stage  is  the
practice of bringing birth, death and the intermediate state to the path  as
the three buddha bodies. Therefore, this meditation that accords  in  aspect
with the three stages of birth, death and the  intermediate  state  are  the
most important practices.

These meditations are very clear if you look at the  sadhana.  For  example,
you can see this in the long sadhana of Yamantaka or Vajrabhairava.

 • It begins by visualizing oneself as Vajrabhairava and at one’s  heart  is
   the syllable Hum.
 •  Light  radiates  from  this  syllable  Hum  pervading  the  world,   its
   environment and inhabitants, causing them to become the nature of  light.
   As a result  of  this  light  from  the  syllable  Hum,  the  world,  its
   environment and inhabitants become the nature of  boundless  purity.  The
   inhabitants themselves become the male and female deities.
 • Then one visualizes that they absorb  into  oneself  and  then  one  also
   dissolves into light.
 • When the  world,  its  environment  and  inhabitants  are  absorbed  into
   oneself, then one thinks that the earth element  is  absorbing  into  the
   water element. One receives the sign of the mirage-like appearance.
 • When we are doing this meditation, this is simply  imagined.  It  is  not
   actually happening. But it will actually happen at the time of death.  So
   one is simply visualizing and having this appearance.

Therefore,  it  is  beneficial  for  you  to  engage  in  this   series   of
visualizations.
• First, you are generated as the deity.
• Then light radiates from  the  Hum  at  your  heart,  pervading  all  the
  environment and its  inhabitants,  transforming  them  into  infinite  or
  boundless purity.
• Then they also dissolve into light and that light absorbs into you.
• That previous appearance of boundless purity vanishes.
• You have to visualize that if the earth element is absorbing into  water,
  the appearance you get is a mirage-like appearance. Besides  that,  there
  is no other appearance.
• At that point, you are alone as  Vajrabhairava.  You  are  alone  because
  everything else has already absorbed into you.
• Then you also start to melt into light, absorbing into the  Hum  at  your
  heart. So then only Hum is left.
• At that point you should think that the water element is  absorbing  into
  the fire element. The appearance that appears to your mind is that of the
  smoke-like appearance.
• Next is visualizing the shabkyu of the letter Hum. Shabkyu is  the  vowel
  sign at the bottom of the letter. That absorbs upwards  leaving  the  Ha.
  You should think the fire element is absorbing into the wind element  and
  you receive the appearance of fireflies.  You should think  that  besides
  the appearance of fireflies, nothing else appears. There is no appearance
  of the environment and its inhabitants  whatsoever.  There  is  only  the
  appearance of fireflies.
• In the next step, the letter Ha absorbs into its head. This letter Ha has
  a horizontal stroke right at the top.  This  means  that  the  letter  Ha
  absorbs upwards leaving only the horizontal stroke at the top. When  this
  is happening, the wind element is absorbing into the white appearance. At
  that time, you get the appearance of the butter lamp.
During these processes, the four  elements  dissolve  or  absorb.  When  you
meditate in this way, you  are  performing  a  meditation  that  accords  in
aspect with death. By doing such visualizations, you are meditating  on  the
aspect that accords with death.

The four elements serve as the support for the  consciousness  when  one  is
alive. But during the death process their ability  or  capacity  to  support
the consciousness becomes diminished. Due to the  karma  diminishing,  these
four elements become weaker  in  terms  of  their  ability  to  support  the
consciousness.  That  is  why   you   have   these   appearances.   As   the
consciousnesses become more  and  more  subtle,  then  such  appearances  as
described will arise. First, there is the  mirage-like  appearance  followed
by the smoke-like appearance, followed by the fireflies-like appearance and
the butter lamp appearance.

• At the next stage, the head of the Ha, the horizontal stroke that remains
  from the letter Ha, also absorbs into the crescent moon above it. At this
  point, there is only white appearance.
• Next, the crescent moon absorbs into the drop above it. This  drop  looks
  like a little circle. When this is visualized, you should think that  you
  receive this appearance of red appearance.
• Next, the drop, which is the little circle, absorbs into  the  nada.  The
  nada is the little squiggle having  three  curves.  At  that  point,  you
  should visualize the appearance called the black appearance. It  is  this
  thick darkness where nothing can be seen.
• The nada or the squiggle with the  three  curves  also  absorbs  upwards.
  Finally, it becomes invisible. It completely vanishes. You  should  think
  that now you receive this appearance of vacuity. There is  just  nothing.
  You generate the thought that the clear light of death has manifested. At
  this point, there is just  clear  vacuity.  You  should  think  there  is
  nothing except this clear voidness.

Some masters say that emptiness appears at this point.  Some  say  emptiness
does not appear  at  this  point.  It  seems  that  most  masters  say  that
emptiness does not appear during this clear light of death.

If you have studied about emptiness, then at this point,  you  should  think
about what this emptiness  is.  You  shouldn’t  think  that  there  is  just
voidness and nothingness. Rather, you should think  about  how  this  is  an
emptiness of the object of negation. So then  there  is  this  emptiness  of
true existence, the emptiness  of  inherent  existence.  Try  your  best  to
recall the meaning of the emptiness that you have studied.  Then  you  think
this is the dharmakaya, the truth body. Try  your  best  to  generate  clear
appearance and divine pride.

You have to think what the benefit of doing such a  practice  is.  You  have
placed yourself in meditative equipoise  on  the  dharmakaya.  What  is  the
benefit?

As I have mentioned earlier, the nada or squiggle with three curves  absorbs
upwards and then  it  completely  vanishes.  At  that  point,  you  have  to
meditate on emptiness using whatever understanding you  have  of  emptiness.
You have to place yourself in the meditative equipoise  on  emptiness.  Then
you can give rise to this thought, “The  mind  realizing  emptiness  is  the
actual truth body; the actual dharmakaya.” Then you try to  generate  divine
pride based on that.

You can cultivate divine pride after you have meditated on emptiness or  you
can do it even  while  you  are  meditating  on  emptiness.  While  you  are
meditating on emptiness, with a corner of your mind, you can  think,  “I  am
the actual dharmakaya.” This is one way to do it.

Another way to do this is to first meditate on emptiness  and  after  having
meditated well, then you cultivate divine  pride  by  thinking,  “I  am  the
actual dharmakaya.”

Try your best to place yourself  in  this  state  of  emptiness,  free  from
laxity and excitement; this vacuity, this emptiness that  you  set  yourself
in. That is the way to meditate on the dharmakaya.

If you are able to cultivate this meditation and become very  familiar  with
it so that you have a very fine quality meditation, then when death  arises,
you will be able to recognize it  and  you  will  be  able  to  meditate  on
emptiness. When this clear light of  death  occurs,  you  will  be  able  to
actually meditate on emptiness. By realizing emptiness  during  that  stage,
you will be able to stop ordinary death and actualize the special  potential
for realizing emptiness there.

When this very subtle mind manifests, you are able  to  use  it  to  realize
emptiness.
Then you will be able to make great  use  of  this  very  special  potential
because at that point, you will be free from dualistic appearances.  Through
this very subtle mind realizing emptiness, you will be able to  achieve  all
the various stages—the illusory body, clear  light  and  eventually,  union.
That is a very extraordinary way for you to attain enlightenment quickly.

However, if you are in such a state of meditative equipoise,  you  will  not
be able to enact the welfare of sentient  beings.  Therefore,  you  have  to
arise in the form body. This is  when  you  practise  the  bringing  of  the
intermediate state to the  path  as  the  sambhogakaya  (complete  enjoyment
body). In terms of the sadhana of Vajrabhairava, at  this  point,  you  will
generate the complete  celestial  mansion  or  inestimable  mansion  of  the
deity. In the centre of this mansion, in this palace, you will generate  the
youthful Manjushri. That is the way to practise  bringing  the  intermediate
state to the path as the complete enjoyment body.

With a mind that is free from laxity and excitement, you have  to  focus  on
the body and think, “I am the resultant time sambhogakaya. I am  the  actual
complete enjoyment body of the resultant time.”  At  that  point,  cultivate
clear appearance and divine pride of being the sambhogakaya.

Even after having arisen as the sambhogakaya, you are not able to enact  the
welfare of all sentient  beings  because  you  can  only  benefit  the  arya
bodhisattvas. You think, “I need to arise as the emanation body  so  that  I
can benefit all sentient beings.” According to  the  sadhana,  it  says,  “I
arise as the complete form of Vajrabhairava.” You see  all  the  details  as
described in the sadhana in a very extensive  way  such  as  the  number  of
faces, hands, legs and implements and so  on.  That  is  the  way  to  bring
rebirth into the path of the nirmanakaya (emanation body).  Then  you  focus
on this full complete body of Vajrabhairava. You have  to  cultivate  divine
pride and clear appearance. You should think, “I  am  the  actual  emanation
body of the resultant time”.

The bases, the paths and the results of purification
We talk about the three bodies that are the bases of purification. What  are
the (bases of purification)? They are  ordinary  death,  intermediate  state
and rebirth.

It is necessary for us  to  stop  ordinary  death  because  if  we  fail  to
eliminate ordinary death, an ordinary intermediate  state  will  follow.  If
that happens, then an ordinary rebirth in samsara will follow. In that  way,
we  will  experience  uninterrupted  samsara  by  being  reborn  in   cyclic
existence. This is why it is necessary for us to  put  a  stop  to  ordinary
death. In order to purify those three bases  of  purification,  we  need  to
rely on the purifying paths, i.e., the paths that are the  purifiers.  These
purifying paths are of two types. They occur on  the  generation  stage  and
they occur on the completion stage.

By relying on these paths that purify the bases of purification, we will  be
able to attain the results of purification. These  results  of  purification
are the resultant three bodies—the  dharmakaya  (truth  body),  sambhogakaya
(complete enjoyment body)
and nirmanakaya (emanation body).
This brings us back to the definition on page 100 of the root text,  “…newly
investigates any aspect related to the three stages  of  birth,  death,  and
the intermediate state.”

In  the  definition,  it  is  stated  that  the   generation   stage   newly
investigates any aspect related to or concordant with the  three  stages  of
birth, death and the intermediate state.   While  this  is  hard  to  do  in
reality, we imagine that we are cultivating those aspects that  accord  with
birth, death and the intermediate state. Death is  the  main  meditation  of
the generation stage.

So, to practise the generation stage, you have to try to meditate  on  those
aspects that are concordant with birth, death and  the  intermediate  state.
In other words, you have to try your best to  meditate  on  bringing  birth,
death and the intermediate state to the path as  the  three  buddha  bodies.
You have to look at the sadhana and then you have to do  these  meditations.
You should understand clearly at which point:
• you are meditating on bringing death to the path as the dharmakaya,
• you are bringing the intermediate state to the path as the sambhogakaya
• you are meditating on bringing rebirth to the path as the nirmanakaya

In this definition posited by Paṇchen Losang Chökyi Gyaltsen, he  is  saying
that the generation stage is the yoga that “newly  investigates  any  aspect
related to the three stages of the death, and the intermediate state.”

     Reply: I think he means only … or the intermediate state. (p.100)

Ngawang Palden replies,  “It  is  not  necessarily  so.”  So,  there  is  no
pervasion, i.e., it is not necessarily the case that a generation stage  has
to  newly  investigate  an  aspect  concordant  with  birth,  death  or  the
intermediate state. To  support  his  statement,  Ngawang  Palden  gave  the
following reason.

     For  instance,  yogas  following  union  with  a  knowledge  consort  …
     meditation on any aspect related to these three. (pp. 100—101)

At the beginning of the Vajrabhairava sadhana, there is  a  section  on  the
protection wheel. That is quite a  long  section.  It  does  not  accord  in
aspects with birth, death and the intermediate state.

If you were to ask, “Is this a generation stage practice?”  the  answer  is,
“Yes." “Is it included in the generation stage?” the answer is also,  “Yes.”


But it does not meditate on any aspect that accords  with  birth,  death  or
the intermediate state. Similarly, there is a section of  the  text  of  the
sadhana called  The  Concentration  of  The  Supreme  Conqueror  Activities.
During that section, you visualize performing the various  activities  of  a
fully enlightened Buddha. Those activities also
do not accord in aspects  with  birth,  death  or  the  intermediate  state.
However, it is
part of the generation stage of the Thirteen Deities of Vajrabhairava.

To substantiate the first reason, Ngawang Palden says:

     The first reason is established because … and so on. (p. 102)

Explanation of the Graded Presentation is a text by  Lama  Tsongkhapa.  This
means
that they do not accord in aspect with  birth,  death  or  the  intermediate
state.

To substantiate the second reason:

     The second reason is established …
     …
     … the stages of ordinary existence. (p. 102)

To summarize what has been explained, Ngawang Palden is  saying  that  there
is this one part of the definition by Panchen Losang  Chökyi  Gyaltsen  that
has no pervasion as in, “newly investigates any aspect related to the  three
stages of birth, death, and the intermediate state.” So, Ngawang  Palden  is
saying that it is not necessary  for  the  generation  stage  to  have  that
feature. This is because, for example,  the  yoga  following  union  with  a
knowledge consort  and  the  meditation  on  the  protection  wheel  do  not
meditate on any aspect related to those three.

     Further debate on the definition of the generation stage


     Someone says: The protection wheel and yogas following union …
     …
     …  must be a generation stage [path]. (p. 104)

The point here is that if someone were to say that any path  in  the  mental
continuum of someone on the generation stage is  a  generation  stage,  then
the opponent will  reply,  “No  pervasion!”  meaning  it  is  not  logically
necessary.

     Reply: That is not logically necessary.


     However, your reason is established …  [of someone  on  the  generation
     stage].

     Your first reason is unacceptable …
     …
     …  the extensive presentation in Mantric Stages. (p. 105)

The essence of the argument here is that in order to be a generation  stage,
it is not necessary to meditate on an aspect concordant  with  birth,  death
and the intermediate state.

Then this person gives his  objection.  He  replies,  “Your  explanation  is
unacceptable  because  you  said  that  certain  practices  are  not  actual
generation  stage  practices.  However  if  you  look  at  the  outlines  as
mentioned—the four branches  of  approximation,  the  four  yogas,  the  six
branches and the three  concentrations—all  these  belong  to  the  path  of
generation stage. You should not say that these practices do not  belong  to
the  generation  stage  and  that  they  are  not  actual  generation  stage
practices.”

Here, Ngawang Palden is saying that if you insist that  a  generation  stage
necessarily meditates on an aspect  concordant  with  birth,  death  or  the
intermediate state, then this is something that needs to investigate.

I guess he is saying it is not necessary that the generation  stage  has  to
meditate on an aspect concordant to birth, death or the intermediate  state.
He refutes this person who claims that  practices  such  as  the  protection
wheel and the union with a  knowledge  consort  are  not  actual  generation
stage practices. They do belong to the generation stage.
