Whether one is a Buddhist or not is determined by whether one has gone for refuge to the Three Jewels.
Buddhists are divided into the practitioners of the Lesser Vehicle and the Greater Vehicle. From view and method, they are differentiated by the latter.
 The Great Vehicle is also divided into the Causal Perfection Vehicle (Paramitayana) and the Resultant Vajra Vehicle (Vajrayana). They are also differentiated by method.
The Lesser and Greater Vehicles
The Four Kayas Of A Buddha
4. The Vajrayana is called the resultant vehicle because it speaks about the path being similar to the result in four ways. They are known as the four complete purities:
the complete purity of environment
the complete purity of body
the complete purity of resources
the complete purity of activity
5. Tantra is divided into 4 classes:
Action Tantra
Performance Tantra
Yoga Tantra
Highest Yoga Tantra
6. The four claases of tantra are differentiated in accordance with the four different ways in which desire is employed on the path by the trainees.
7. Quote from Lama TsongKhapa’s Great Exposition of Secret Mantra:
“Those who resort to a great many external activities in order to actualize these two yogas (emptiness and deity yogas) are trainees of Action Tantra. Those who balance their external activities and internal meditative stabilization without using very many activities are trainees of Performance Tantra. Those who mainly rely on meditative stabilization and resort to only few external activities are trainees of Yoga Tantra. Those who do no rely on external activities and are able to generate the yoga of which there is none higher are trainees of Highest Yoga Tantra.”
