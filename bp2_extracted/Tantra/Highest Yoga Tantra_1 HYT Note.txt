Highest Yoga Tantra  
  
 Introduction  
  
 A quote from Lama Tsongkhapa’s Great Exposition of Secret Mantra:  
 “The vehicles are divided through arranging practices into three types from the viewpoint of  
 the three types of trainees’ interest. Practices free from desire are taught to those interested in the  
 lowly, practices of the grounds and perfections to those interested in the vast; and practices of desire to  
 those specially interested in the profound.”  
  
 - Highest yoga tantra is divided into 2:  
  
 - Father tantra (e.g. Guhyasamaja)  
  
 - Mother tantra (e.g. Heruka Chakrasamvara)

 2. Maintaining the purity of the pledges and vows after becoming a suitable vessel  
  
 - Keeping the 18 root and 46 secondary bodhisattva vows  
  
 - Keeping the 14 root vows and avoiding the 8 gross transgressions  
  
 3. How to practice the path  
  
 a. Generation stage  
 b. Completion stage  
 1  
a. Generation stage  
  
 Definition :  
  
 A yoga that  
 (1) Does not arise from causing the winds to enter, abide and dissolve in the central channel                 
 through the power of meditation but is a ripener of the continuum for the completion stage,                 
 and  
 (2) Is a meditation newly and mentally imagining an aspect similar to any of the three: birth,                 
 death and intermediate state.  
  
 The generation stage is divided into 2:  
  
 - Coarse generation stage  
 - Subtle generation stage
