We were at the section of being conscientious in accomplishing virtue. In
this section, essentially we are looking at the practice of cultivating
conscientiousness with regards to cultivating virtue. If one is not
conscientious in accomplishing virtue, we are told that suffering will
ensue in this life and also in future lives.
In the earlier sections, there is a discussion about being conscientious in
not letting one’s bodhicitta degenerate. Then there is a discussion on
being conscientious in abandoning non-virtue. In that section, the
discussion is essentially about how it is important not to be stained by
the downfalls and faults.
The earlier section is being conscientious in protecting one’s bodhicitta
and not letting it degenerate.
Then there is being conscientious in the training in the precepts after one
has adopted them.
That discussion covers:
  - being conscientious in abandoning the faults,
  - being conscientious in cultivating virtue, and
  - being conscientious in abandoning the afflictions.
Being conscientious in the trainings covers those three points. Of the
three, the first one is being conscientious in abandoning the faults.
Essentially after having adopted the bodhisattva vow, one has to check
every now and then to see if one is stained by the downfalls and faults or
not.
Then the next section is being conscientious in cultivating/meditating on
virtue. If you were asked to give a summary of this particular section,
what would you say? What is the essence?
There are two main sections:
A. Being conscientious in not letting one’s bodhicitta degenerates
B. Being conscientious in the training.
Then being conscientious in the training has three points.
  1. Being conscientious in abandoning the faults.
  2. Being conscientious in cultivating virtue.
  3. Being conscientious about abandoning afflictions
What is the difference between these two - being conscientious in
abandoning the faults and being conscientious in cultivating virtue?

Khen Rinpoche (in English): What is the difference? You got my question? So
what is the answer?
The first section, being conscientious in abandoning faults, is essentially
talking about the need to not be stained by the downfalls and faults. If
one is stained by these faults, then suffering would follow.
The second section is being conscientious in meditating or cultivating
virtue.
Can anybody summarize what that whole section is driving at?
In the first section, being conscientious in abandoning the faults, the
emphasis is on not being stained by the faults and the downfalls.
But in the second section, being conscientious in cultivating virtue, it is
saying that if one is stained by the downfalls and faults, one should
confess and purify it. Otherwise, one will have to experience the
fruitional effects of having committed those downfalls and faults. This
particular section also states that even if one does manages to purify the
negativities that are accumulated through committing the downfalls and
faults, one’s realizations are postponed or are delayed.

I guess the point in the second section, being conscientious in cultivating
virtue, is that one has to engages in virtue and purify downfalls and
faults that are accumulated through applying four opponent powers. If one
does not purify after having committed the downfalls and faults, then
suffering will ensue. Therefore it is important to engage in virtue in
order to counteract what one has already accumulated.

Verse 4.25
If my body burns for a long time
In the fires of hell which are difficult to bear,
Inevitably my mind will be tormented

The fires of hell are difficult to bear. It is said that the fires of hell
is hundreds or thousands of times hotter than any fire that we humans are
aware of.
The verse is saying that if we do end up in the hell realm, it is said that
our mind is like being burned in the fire, metaphorically speaking burning
with the great fires of regret, on top of one’s body that is actually being
burned by the fire
[BP1 L21] Instead of practising virtue, we engage in non-virtue or
negativities all the time. In the future, we will be born in the unbearable
hells where we have to stay for a long time. Our bodies will be roasted in
the unbearable flames of hell and mental suffering will torment our minds.
Therefore, it is important to strive not to be stained by any downfalls or
negativities

Verse 4.27 a, b

This mind will be reduced to nothing.
“This beneficial state that is so hard to find” refers to the human life of
freedom and endowments that we now have. Such a human life of freedom and
endowments is extremely difficult to get. You can understand the difficulty
of getting such a rebirth through understanding that it is difficult by
nature to get such a rebirth, and the causes for achieving such a special
rebirth are difficult to create. One can also understand the difficulty of
getting such an opportunity through analogies.
This special basis, the human life of freedom and endowments that we have
now obtained, enables us to practice dharma readily. The reason why we have
such an opportunity now is due to the virtue and merit that we have
accumulated in the past and finding such an opportunity is something that
happens just about only once.
It is not as if we are not able to distinguish between what is harmful and
what is beneficial, we are able to tell the difference between what is
useful and what is harmful. It is not as if we are crazy now. It is not as
if we are not able to know what is useful or harmful. Despite all these, if
we are once again led into the fires of hell then that would be really
wrong.
If such a thing happens, have we lost our minds?  Has someone cast a spell
on us? It is like some people who are confused over their thoughts and are
unable to think when they received some black magic, is that happening to
us or it is just because we have completely lost our minds? Despite having
all the conditions, we have to go again to the lower realms or into the
hells. Then this would be same as having gone crazy and having lost our
minds.

What is it that led us back into the lower realms? Is it because we have
lost our minds? Are we unable to tell the difference between what is good
and what is good harmful? Or is it we are now confused by some magic spell?
The answer to that is in the following verse.
Verse 4.27 c, d
Even I do not know what confused me;
What is there inside me?

“Even I do not know what confused me; What is there inside me?” is just a
query asking ourselves what exactly is it? What is the very thing that is
leading me back to the hells?
We may have thoughts of wanting to learn the teachings, wanting to reflect
on them, wanting to internalize them, and wanting to meditate on them. We
may have thoughts of wanting to practice ethical discipline or, in general,
we have the thoughts of wanting to accomplish the dharma. While we have
those thoughts, yet at the same time, we don’t seem to have any
independence or any freedom in actually carrying out our intentions.
Whatever intentions we have about accomplishing the dharma and practicing
it, but yet at the same time, we don’t seem to be doing anything about it.
We don’t seem to have any freedom of choice in carrying out those
intentions. There must be something that is working in the background.
There must be something that is stopping us. What is it? We have to check
and look over and over again and then discover what exactly this source of
confusion is.
Well, it is not due to some black magic, it is not due to not knowing what
to do, and yet there is something that is stopping us. What is it? That is
what we have to find out by thinking over and over again. So this is the
meaning behind these two sentences “Even I do not know what confused me;
What is there inside me?”  This comes under the outline: It is appropriate
to contemplate the cause of ignorance.

The afflictions caused us suffering in that they take away our freedom.
They caused us to act without any freedom or any control. That goes with
the next verse.
Verse 4.28
Since enemies such as hatred and craving
Have neither arms nor legs,
And are neither courageous nor clever,
How have I been used like a slave by them?
If we link what we are going to look at here with the earlier question on
“what exactly is the cause of our confusion”, then we can link the
transition here. Essentially what we are going to discuss here is like the
answer to that question. In short, basically it is our afflictions that
stop us from carrying out our intentions, to practice the dharma and to
accomplish virtue.
Afflictions are the enemies of anger, craving encompasses of desire and
attachment and ignorance, which is always there. If we expand on the list
of afflictions, we have the six roots afflictions and twenty secondary
afflictions.
Here we are identifying the culprit which is the afflictions that cause us
to lose our freedom. The afflictions are like anger, craving, attachment
and so forth. The enemies of afflictions are unlike external enemies. Our
enemies, the afflictions, do not have any form, do not have legs, do not
have hands and do not carry weapons. Neither are they courageous. Neither
are they smart. Neither are they wise. Neither are they hardworking.
Although the afflictions are neither smart nor hardworking and nor
courageous, but they act as if they are hardworking. They are smart. The
afflictions harm everybody. They harm the rich, they harm the poor, they
harm the students, and the teachers are harmed. Everybody is harmed by the
afflictions.
Our afflictions such as attachment cause us to be emotionally glued and
attached to the objects of desire and the objects of enjoyment. We are kind
of helplessly led by the nose by these afflictions and day in and day out
running after all these objects of desire without any choice and then doing
everything to accomplish and to get those objects of desire. Likewise,
anger is also like that. We are controlled by our anger even to the point
that we do not even care about our lives. Anger can make us disregard our
lives. In essence, all of us, everybody without exception, have been made a
slave of their own afflictions.
We are slaves to our afflictions because we take all these without any
choice from the afflictions. We do exactly what our afflictions tell us. We
abide and carry out the orders of our afflictions without any choice. In
that sense, we have become the servants or slaves to our afflictions. As
the result of being a slave is suffering. So this verse is telling us that
our afflictions have basically made us their slaves and we have lost our
independence. As a result of becoming a slave to our afflictions, we are
led into boundless suffering.

[BP 1 L22] We have hatred and attachment/desire. Hatred and desire have
their roots in ignorance.
Ignorance, hatred and attachment are the three mental poisons. These three
negative emotions are called the enemies. These enemies, which are the
afflictions themselves, do not have any form (“arms or legs”), nor do they
wear any armour. They do not hold any weapons in their hands. They are
neither heroic nor courageous nor wise.
The afflictions are not hard working and do not possess joyous
perseverance. In fact, afflictions are concomitant with laziness. What is
the entity of laziness? Laziness is a state of mind that is not
enthusiastic about virtue whatsoever. Since the afflictions bear
similarities with laziness, therefore, the afflictions are not hard working
(literally, they do not possess joyous perseverance or effort from their
own side.) Therefore, the afflictions are also not courageous.
The afflictions are also not wise. We are not talking about intelligence
here but the factor of wisdom. The afflictions are not wise because they
bear similarities with ignorance. What is ignorance? Ignorance is a mind
that is unclear with regards to its object. Since the afflictions bear
similarities with ignorance, therefore, the afflictions themselves are not
wise.

Verse 4.29
While they dwell within my mind,
At their pleasure they cause me harm,
Yet I patiently endure them without any anger.
This is an inappropriate state and patience is a contemptible state.
Where this enemy of affliction does abides? Where is the temple that houses
this affliction? It is none other than our mind. This enemy of the
afflictions abides in this house or temple of our mind as a guest. While it
is there, it tells us what we should do and gives us orders. We happily
follow what it tells us to do and in return, we get harm. The afflictions
harm us readily without holding anything back and without hesitation. This
is what we get.
Although we listen to the afflictions, we follow the advice, the orders and
the commands of the afflictions abiding in our minds, what it does in
return is to harm us without holding anything back. They cause us harm
happily and joyously. This is what the afflictions do.
If this is what the afflictions do to us, then it would be wrong of us to
practice patience with the afflictions and just accept it. In this sense if
we practice patience with our afflictions and letting it do whatever it
wants to, then this will be a contemptible state. It is something that the
wise and the holy ones would criticize us for.
Essentially this verse is telling us that we should look at the afflictions
as an enemy and then strive to overcome them.
An affliction is defined as that which disturbs the mind greatly. That is
what is posited as an affliction.
It is extremely important to contemplate or to realize the faults of our
afflictions. For the moment they arise, our minds are greatly disturbed.
Our afflictions cause our minds to look at things in a very distorted way.
Whenever the afflictions manifest, they further strengthen those non-
virtuous imprints in our minds.
When an affliction arises, it causes those afflictions that of similar type
to maintain their continuity in the mind. Our afflictions harm us and they
harm others. Our afflictions will harm us in this life and they will also
harm us in our future lives.
Afflictions greatly disturb our minds and cause us great suffering and
anguish.
Our affliction is that which brings about all the problems of samsara.
Afflictions separate us and lead us further away from permanent happiness –
liberation.
Our afflictions cause our virtue to degenerate.
The point of understanding the fault of affliction is so that we can regard
it as an enemy. On that basis we will then be able to strive to defeat the
affliction.
Verse 4.30

Verse 4.31
But this foe, the powerful afflictions,
In an instant can cast me there,
Which when met will cause not even the ashes
Of Mount Meru to remain.
The Sanskrit word is translated as demigods but sometime it can also be
translated as non-humans. Whatever it is, whether it is demigods or the non-
humans, we are talking about interferers i.e. beings that can cause us
harm.
The point here is to think about the great harm that our afflictions do to
us. The verse is telling us that even if all the gods such as those very
powerful like Indra, demigods and non-human beings were to rise up as our
enemies, they can’t do much harm to us. The harm that they can do to us is
only limited to destroying our possessions, our enjoyments and at the most
causing us to die by taking our lives. No matter how powerful they maybe,
at the very most this is what they can do to us.
This verse is saying that no matter how powerful these interferers are,
they cause us problems and at the very most they can only kill us. This is
about the maximum damage they can do to us. They on their own can never put
us in the lowest of the hell realms - the Avici hell.
The inner enemy of afflictions is so much more powerful than the external
enemy. For this inner enemy of afflictions in very short moment of time can
throws us into the lower realms and even cause us to be born in the Avici
hell. This causes us many other problems and suffering.  For that reason,
therefore, we have to destroy this real enemy – the enemy of afflictions.
This verse is saying that our enemy of afflictions is so much more
powerful. Our afflictions not only caused us problems, suffering, but also
harm us over very long period of time.

Verse 4.32
All other enemies are incapable
Of such a length of time
As can my afflictions,
The long-time enemy with neither beginning nor end.
Our afflictions have harmed us in the past. If we do not apply antidotes
now and destroy the afflictions they would harm us in the way they have
already done so in the past.
Our inner enemy of the afflictions harmed us in the past and would continue
harm us in the future if we do not do anything about it. Our external
enemies are not like that. Our external enemies do not have that capacity
to harm us in the way our inner enemy of the afflictions can do.
The person that we don’t like and whom we consider as enemy harms us and
causes us problem. It is nothing compared to our afflictions. Our
afflictions have harmed us since beginningless lifetimes, with no
beginning. If we don’t do anything about it, it will have no end as well in
harming us. Our external enemies can never harm us in such way. In order to
destroy this long existent enemy of the afflictions, it is not sufficient
just to apply the antidote for a short period of time or just apply the
antidote once or twice. There is no way the afflictions can be defeated by
just this one or two time application of the antidote. But rather we have
to continuously apply the antidote to the afflictions just like the flow of
a river.
The teaching is telling us to deal with this very powerful enemy of the
afflictions. If the enemy is very powerful and it has been there for a very
long time, and if we don’t apply persistent effort into dealing with this
enemy, there is nothing we can do about it.

Verse 4.33
If I agreeably honor and rely upon [others],
All will enact benefit and happiness,
But if I rely upon these afflictions
In return I will be harmed by suffering.
If we give presents and things to the external enemies in order to befriend
them, at least we will get something in return. We maybe can placate and
pacify that external enemy. We may even get some happiness and some
benefits in return from that external enemy. But if we befriend the inner
enemy of the afflictions, we give what it wants, we do what it wants us to
do, not only it will not return the favor, it will not give us any
happiness and benefit in return. On top of that, they will cause us further
harm and more damage.
The more we befriend the afflictions, the more we do what they like, the
more problem and more harm we receive in return. Therefore it is wrong to
regard them as friend. But rather we have to regard and look at them as
enemy and strive hard to destroy them. Destroying the inner enemy of
afflictions is the method to achieve peace and happiness.
Actually the two verses here are saying that while it is appropriate to
make friends with the external enemy, it is inappropriate and wrong to make
friends with the inner enemy of the afflictions. The external enemy is not
somebody we hang around with all the time. It is not there and is not
something that we have to be around or mixed with all the time. But the
afflictions are there all the time. They accompany us all the time.
What most people usually do is to fight the external enemy and make friends
with the afflictions. What we have to do is the opposite, like what the
Engaging in the Bodhisattva Deeds say, we should make friends with the
external enemy and make enemies with the afflictions.
This verse is saying that don’t be friends with the afflictions.

Verse 4.34
While in cyclic existence how can I be joyful and unafraid
If in my heart I certainly prepare a place
For this incessant enemy of long duration,
The sole cause for the increase of collections of harms?
Like what was discussed earlier, the afflictions have harmed us for a very
long time in the past. So the moment an afflictions manifests in our mind,
we are immediately disturbed and we are harmed. They bring about all the
problems of this life and they will cause us harm in our future lives. They
will harm our future lives. This enemy of the affliction has been around
for a long time abiding in the center of our heart.
If we hold in high regard in whatever the afflictions tell us to do then we
will harm in a great way.
If we only hold in middling regard for what the afflictions tell us then we
will be harmed in the middling way.
If we do not really hold in much regard what the afflictions tell us then
we will only be harmed in a small way.
If we completely disobey the commands of our afflictions then we will never
be harmed.
As long as the afflictions are abiding in the center of our heart, there is
no way to be really happy in samsara, there is no way to be freed of
worries and there is no way to be freed of fears in samsara.

Verse 4.35
How shall I ever have happiness
If in a net of attachment within my mind
There dwell the guardians of the prison of cyclic existence
Who become the slayers who kill me in hell and so forth?
Our afflictions are the guardian of our prison of three realms. When we are
hacked and killed by the hell guardians while in hell, these hell guardians
are the products of our afflictions. There is no room for happiness in this
life and in future lives if our minds are snatched in this net of the
afflictions such as attachment. If we are snatched in the net of the
afflictions, it is like being captured. Then there is no room for
happiness.

This is the discussion of how the afflictions harm the mind and the body.
