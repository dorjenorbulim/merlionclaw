Verse 3.10
May I become an inexhaustible treasure
For those who are poor and destitute.
May I turn into various articles of all necessities
And abide close before them.
Prior to dedicating the merit that we have accumulated, there were earlier
practices of urging the buddhas to turn the wheel of dharma and requesting
them not to pass away into nirvana and so forth.
By using the verses here, we are dedicating the virtues that we have
accumulated.
One dedicates the merit that one has accumulated such that :
- it becomes food for those who are hungry,
- it becomes water for those who are thirsty and
- it becomes objects of enjoyment and wealth for those who are poor
So one dedicates the merit that one becomes an inexhaustible treasure for
the sentient beings.
The context of our discussion here is the generation of bodhicitta, and in
this chapter is on adopting bodhicitta.
Prior to adopting bodhicitta:
- we have to purify the negativities; which are the unfavorable conditions
and
- we have to accumulate the collections of merit; which is the favorable
conditions.
After having done these, then one needs to train the mind before adopting
bodhicitta. Initially, one trains in the attitude or the thought of
dedicating and giving away one’s body, possessions and roots of virtue to
others.
Before one can generate bodhicitta, one first has to eliminate the thoughts
of self-cherishing and develop the thought of wanting to benefit others.
Prior to developing bodhicitta, one has to trains the mind in different
attitudes. The objects of bodhicitta are all the limitless and numberless
sentient beings. Of course, at the present moment one does not have the
ability to actually benefit all the sentient beings but still one has to
trains in that thought from the beginning.
Although one is incapable of actually giving or dedicating oneself and
everything to sentient beings right now, nevertheless one has to start by
training and developing these thoughts in one’s mind. The teachings tell us
that on the basis of training these thoughts then gradually it will come a
time when one will actually be able to dedicate and give away one’s body,
possession and so forth to others.
The objects of giving here is primarily one’s body, one’s possessions and
one’s merit or roots of virtue. Of the three, it is said that dedicating
one’s body is the most important one because our body is the very thing
that we cherish, hold on to and grasp at the most.  We hold onto this body
and identifying it as “my body”.  On this basis, in order to bring about
happiness and pleasure to this body, everybody then goes about accumulating
wealth, possessions, merit and even virtue in order to take care of the
body.

Verse 3.11
Without any sense of loss
I shall give my body and resources,
As well as all my virtues of the three times,
For the sake of accomplishing the welfare of all sentient beings.
As read out by Venerable Gyurme
[Also my body and possessions and
all virtues of the three times,
I will give away without regret
 To achieve the purpose of all sentient beings]
The verse is talking about dedicating and giving away one’s body, one’s
possessions, and one’s merit accumulated in the past, present and future
for the accomplishment of the happiness and welfare of sentient beings.
This dedication of one’s body, possessions and roots of virtue has to be
done without a sense of loss.
You train your minds in developing this thought of dedicating your body,
possessions and roots of virtue of the three times to all sentient beings
mainly by through reciting this verse, and at the same time to allow that
thought to arise from the heart.
You train the mind in developing the thought of really dedicating the basis
of designation, possessions and the roots of virtue of the past, present
and future to others. Your mind has to be familiarized with it, and in
particular in those situations when you feel your life is being threatened
or you feel that you are experiencing some harm when there are usually
worry and fear. At those times, it is more important to train the mind
dedicating one’s body away. If you are familiar with the practice, once you
have dedicated it away, and during those times if you were to think “I have
already dedicated my body away to others and it has belongs to others”,
perhaps there will be less fear or no fear because you have already given
it away to others.  Likewise, when your possessions are threatened, if you
have trained the mind, you can remind yourself that “I have given this to
others, and I have already completely dedicated it to others.” For if you
have dedicated it to others from the heart then there is no basis to
conceive of the body and having the idea “This is mine.” As such, since you
are not holding onto it as “mines”, the mind will be very peaceful and
relax.
When you lose a particular object belonging to yourself, at those times if
your mind is familiarized through such way of thinking, you just have to
bring to mind the thought “I have already given this to others”. If that
idea of holding on to that object as being “mine” is not there, then even
if you lose that thing, it doesn’t cause you a great deal of worry. You
will not get uptight as your mind is very peaceful.
Whether something belongs to you or not basically depends on whether you
have this idea of ownership. If you have this idea that this is yours and
you hold on to it as yours, then it is yours. But if you don’t have the
idea and feeling that is yours because you have given it away to others,
even if something happens to it or maybe you lose something technically
belonging to you, then there is no real basis for the mind to be so uptight
and disturb. Therefore, it is says that it is good and important to train
in this thought of giving and dedicating it to others.
When things happen to your body, sometimes you get harmed physically or get
disturbances from non-human beings or your possessions are on the decline
or your possessions are harmed or stolen, at those times if you are
familiar with the thought of dedicating all these away to others, the mind
will not get very uptight and will not be disturbed.
We will talk about the practice of ‘cutting’ or ‘chod’ in Tibetan.
Essentially, ‘chod’ is about cutting the ego grasping. By cutting the ego
grasping, you don’t have the sense of self-cherishing, then you will be
able to dedicate and give away the body without any sense of loss.  The
real meaning of ‘chod’ is cutting the self-grasping.
The practice of ‘chod’ is a practice of dedicating and giving one’s body
away to others.
For the inner ‘chod’ imagining cutting your body, dedicating and giving it
away it from your heart to those different kinds of non-human entities.
For the outer ‘chod’, it is cut away your ties with others so you  live
alone in isolation.
(BP1 L16) When we say we have given up all these things, sincerely from our
hearts, for others, the question may arise: How are we going to use these
things that we still have? The answer is that we should utilize our bodies,
wealth and possessions for the benefit of others. We dedicate them to
others and use them for their benefit. Once we have dedicated them to
others, then we should use them sincerely and purely for others.

There are reasons and the reasons are valid for doing the practice of
giving away one’s body, possessions and roots of virtue. It is an
appropriate thing to do. Why?

Verse 3.12 c,d
All will be given similarly;
It is best that I give to all sentient beings.
By dedicating one’s body, possessions  and roots of virtue accumulated in
the three times and giving them away to others, then what one get in return
is the sorrowless state.
What we want is to reach a state of existence whereby there is no suffering
whatsoever.
The sorrowless state is essentially what we get from giving.
The sorrowless state is essentially the very thing we want.
So by putting these two together, therefore it is highly appropriate to
give it away.
The teachings state that we dedicate all these things to all sentient
beings.
-  Whether we give our body and dedicate it to others or not, we will have
to leave it at the time of death.
-  Whether we give away and dedicate our possessions to others or not it
will be lost one day and we have to part with it.
-  Whether we dedicate our roots of virtue to others or not, it will be
destroyed by anger anyway. Even if it is not destroyed by anger, once the
merit ripens i.e. once one experience the fruitional effect of that
particular virtue, it ceases anyway.
So for those reasons, it is also highly appropriate to dedicate it to
others.
Since now we have the opportunity to be able to dedicate it to others, so
while the opportunity is there, before the opportunity is gone, before all
these things are lost, then it becomes the best thing to do to dedicate it
for others.
(BP1 L16) Once we have given away and dedicated everything to others, we
need to use these things for the benefit of others. Whenever we enjoy food,
clothes or our other possessions, we should conjoin our enjoyment with the
attitude of benefiting others. In order to sustain our bodies for the
benefit of others, we should think: ’I am going to eat this or use this for
the benefit of others.’ We reduce our attachment to the things mentioned
here by mentally giving them away. We will attain nirvana once we reduce
our attachment to them. By giving up attachment, we will achieve
liberation. Once we have this mind that wishes to achieve the sorrowless
state of liberation, we become Buddhists and take refuge with the purpose
of achieving such liberation.

Verse 3.13
Having already given this body as what makes them happy
For the pleasure of all embodied beings,
Depending on what pleases them,
Constantly killing, abusing, or beating it,
After one has completely dedicated the body to others from the heart to all
sentient beings, then one has to develop or trains in the thought that
since one has already dedicated to others, they can do whatever they want
with it. However they want to use the body, they can do with it - they can
bash it up, they can kill you or they can criticize it. The idea is to
train the mind that they can do whatever they want with it. Since one has
dedicated it completely from the heart to others, then one need to project
this intention and the will that allows them to do whatever they want with
it.

Verse 3.14
Although they may play with my body
And make it a basis of jest and blame,
Because I have already given this body of mine to them
Why do I act with concern?
Whether they use the body for play or amusement or use it as a basis for
ridicule or make fun in the presence of many people, and since one has
given it completely to others, one should not have the idea that “Oh, what
they are doing is bad, they should not be doing this”. The point here is to
cut the idea thinking that the body is “mine”. This way of thinking is to
cut this sense of ownership or possession of the body.
We do get emotionally very excited when we are praised. But when we are
criticized or somebody points out our fault, we get emotionally very
disturbed. When somebody points to some nice feature that a body possess,
again one gets very excited. But when one points out certain faults of the
body, one gets very upset. There are these emotional rollercoaster of up
and down. All these turmoil comes because of very strong self-cherishing.
If one doesn’t have self-cherishing or one’s self-cherishing is less, then
the corresponding emotional turmoil of ups and downs will be lesser. If one
can think along these lines, I guess you will not experience so much mental
upheavals and turmoil of ups and downs. Also one can develop thoughts
thinking about others more and concentrating on others more.

Verse 3.16
If in those who encounter me
A faithful or an angry mind arises,
May that eternally become the cause
For fulfilling all their aims.
The verse is saying that be it a person who is angry at you or be it a
person who has faith and devotion in you or even wanting to help you or
wanting to harm you, it doesn’t matter just by that connection. May it
become meaningful for them through making connection with you, may all
their wishes be fulfilled and whatever desires they have.
The point here is that regardless of the intentions or the attitudes of the
person who focuses their attention on you  or who encounters you, from your
side, you dedicate the merit and pray that may the connection becomes a
cause to fulfill all their wishes.

the moment that they come into contact with one and another, they get upset
with one and another and become extremely violent. Whatever they pick up
will transform into the weapon and they start hacking onto one and another.
That is a lot of suffering.
The main cause for not being born during the eon of weapons is to abandon
killing and to cultivate love and compassion. So, by keeping this in mind,
as much as possible we should guard our ethical discipline and then
cultivate love and compassion.
