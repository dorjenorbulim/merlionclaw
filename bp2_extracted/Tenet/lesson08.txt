Let’s look at the three vehicles (yanas):
The three vehicles are:
   1. The Hearers vehicles
   2. The Solitary Realizers vehicles
   3. The Bodhisattvas vehicles
We have to know that this is different from the four Buddhist tenets. We
have talked about the four types of proponents of the Buddhist tenets.
These two are different things.
If we look at the history of the presentation of the tenets and the three
vehicles, there are some commentaries which said that:
    ➢ the GES and the SS belong to the Hearers Vehicle
    ➢ the MOS belong to the Solitary Realizers Vehicle
    ➢ the MWS belong to the Great Vehicle (Mahayana)
However, you have to know that this is incorrect.
A proponent of a Buddhist tenet is not necessary someone who has entered
the path; whether it is a Hearers path, Solitary Realizers path or Mahayana
path. We only talk about the three vehicles in the context of someone who
has already entered the path; be it the path of the Hearers, Solitary
Realizers or Mahayana.
In the case of someone who is a proponent of the Buddhist tenets; be it of
any of the four tenets, that proponent need not necessary have already
generated a path in his mind. As such it is not necessary of someone who
has entered the Hearers path, Solitary Realizers path or Mahayana path.
I am trying to help you to understand that there is a difference between
the proponent of the four Buddhist tenets and the three vehicles. These two
are not necessary the same.
There is a difference between:
    ➢ the proponent of any of the Buddhist tenets and
    ➢ for someone who is belongs to one of the three vehicles (yanas)
There is a difference because a proponent of any of the Buddhist tenets
need not be someone who has already entered the path; be it Hearers path,
Solitary Realizers path or Mahayana path.
Basically, there are proponents of the Buddhist tenets who have not
generated any path in their mind.
For example:  A Hinayanist is someone who has entered the Hinayana path,
i.e. has generated a path in his continuum. Such a Hinayanist could be a
proponent of Great Exposition, could be a proponent of Sutra, could be a
proponent of Mind Only or he could be a proponent of Middle Way.
Similarly, someone who has entered the Solitary Realizer path could be a
proponent of any of the four Buddhist tenets; he could be a proponent of
Great Exposition, could be a proponent of Sutra, could be a proponent of
Mind Only or could be a proponent of Middle Way.
Likewise, someone who has entered the Mahayana path, he is a Mahayanist, he
could be a proponent of Great Exposition, he could be a proponent of Sutra,
he could be a proponent of Mind Only or he could be a proponent of Middle
Way.
Therefore, the proponent of Hinayana tenets is not the same as someone who
has entered the Hinayana path. They are not the same.
It is wrong to think that the proponent of Mahayana tenets is the same as
the Mahayanist (someone who has entered the Mahayana path). It is wrong to
think they are the same.
Why a proponent of Mahayana tenets is not necessarily a Mahayanist?
What kind of fallacy is incurred if one posit that the proponent a Mahayana
tenets is necessary a Mahayanist?
We talk about the three vehicles:
   1. The Hearers vehicles
   2. The Solitary Realizers vehicles
   3. The Bodhisattvas vehicles (Mahayana vehicles or the Great vehicles)
In this context, vehicles, paths and clear realizations are synonymous.
When we talk path, what exactly is a path?
What is the boundary between a path and non-path?
What is the boundary of the generation of the path and the absence of the
path?
The moment a fully qualified renunciation is developed in the mental
continuum, it is said the path has been generated. Renunciation is the
determination to be freed. A proponent of any of the four Buddhist tenets
need not be someone who has generated a fully qualified determination to be
freed/ renunciation in his mind.
Each of the three vehicles (the Hearer vehicles, Solitary Realizer vehicles

   2. The path of preparation
   3. The path of seeing

You have to know about these paths.
The first path is the path of accumulation. For example, we take the
Hearers’ path of accumulation.
Why is it called the path of accumulation? The path of accumulation refers
to the time in which that individual is accumulating the collection
primarily focus on hearing and reflection.
We have talked about the threefold activity of hearing, reflection and
meditation.
The path of accumulation refers to the times when the individual, the one
who has entered the respective path, is primarily focusing on accumulating
the collection mainly through the activities of hearing and reflection.
When the path of accumulation is divided, then there are three divisions:
   1. Small path of accumulation
   2. Middling path of accumulation
   3. Great path of accumulation
That is roughly what a path of accumulation is.
Then next is the path of preparation. In Tibetan, path of preparation is
sbyor lam; sbyor literally means connecting or linking to, and is usually
translated as path of preparation.
As opposed to the path of accumulation which primary focus on hearing and
reflection, on the path of preparation, the focus is on cultivating the
wisdom arisen from meditation. It is meditating or familiarizing the mind
with the meaning of the scriptures, and in particular the meaning of
selflessness that has been ascertained through the earlier activities of
hearing and reflection.
This meditation on the meaning of the scriptures that has been ascertained
earlier and in particular meditating on the meaning of selflessness is done
through the medium of mental image or meaning generality. This stage of
spiritual development prepares the person to actualize the path of seeing.
This practice will connect/link this person to the path of seeing.
So on the path of preparation, essentially the individual is primarily
meditating on selflessness; and this meditation is done via mental image or
meaning generality. This meditation will eventually connect/link this
individual to seeing the selflessness directly as opposed to the seeing or
realizing via meaning generality.
When the path of preparation is divided, there are four divisions:
   1. Heat stage of the path of preparation
   2. Peak stage of the path of preparation
   3. Forbearance stage of the path of preparation
   4. Supreme mundane qualities stage of the path of preparation
The third path is the path of seeing. Why is it being called path of
seeing? This is because the individual is seeing the truth directly for the
first time.

There are different ways for dividing the path of seeing. One way is

The fourth path is path of meditation. On the path of seeing, the
individual has seen the truth directly for the first time. Once one has
seen the object directly then with respect to that object, there is nothing
higher mode than that.
What happens on the path of meditation? On the path of meditation, the
individual is familiarizing his mind with what he saw in the path of
seeing; which is the truth. So he is familiarizing the realization over and
over again.
The fifth path is the path of no-more learning, which refers to the time
that there is no need to train anymore on one’s respective path; whichever
vehicle one has entered. There is nothing to train or learn anymore.
So when we talked about the five Hearer’s path, there are:
        1. The Hearers’ path of accumulation
        2. The Hearers’ path of preparation
        3. The Hearers’ path of seeing
        4. The Hearers’ path of meditation
        5. The Hearers’ path of no more learning
On the path of accumulation, the individual is focused on accumulating
merit. In the threefold activity of hearing, reflection and meditation, the
individual on the path of accumulation focuses primarily on hearing and
reflection on the meaning of the scriptures and particularly on the meaning
of selflessness in order to gain correct understanding of selflessness.
On the path of preparation that individual primarily meditating on the
meaning of selflessness that has been settled in his own mind through the
earlier hearing and reflection.  He is meditating on selflessness through
the medium of the meaning generality or mental image. This meditation will
make him closer and prepares him to realize selflessness directly i.e. to
see the truth directly. He is primarily meditating on the wisdom arisen
from meditation on the path of preparation. This meditation can counter or
stop many manifest afflictions such as manifest anger, manifest attachment
and so forth. Through meditating over and over again on selflessness via
mental image or meaning generality, his mind is prepared for the eventual
direct perception of selflessness.
When the individual see the truth directly for the first time then that
individual, for the first time, come to possess in his hand the actual
antidote to the afflictions.
In the previous class we talked about the two types of obscurations:
    ➢ Afflictive obscurations
    ➢ Non-afflictive obscurations
This wisdom directly perceiving selflessness becomes the actual antidote to
the afflictive obscurations.
An afflictive obscuration can be seen as in term of:
    ➢ path of seeing abandonment and
    ➢ path of meditation abandonment
There are some afflictive obscurations that have to be abandoned by the
path of seeing, while there are other afflictive obscurations that are
abandoned by the path of meditation.
What is the path of seeing abandonment? The afflictive obscurations that
are abandoned by the path of seeing are the intellectually acquired
afflictions. So when an individual directly perceives the truth for the
first time that is when he entered and achieved the path of seeing. At that
time, the intellectually acquired afflictive obscurations are abandoned.
Then the innate afflictive obscurations are to be abandoned by the path of
meditation.
Because it is at the path of meditation one is familiarizing one’s mind and
meditating over and over again on what has been realized earlier. After
abandoning all the innate afflictive obscuration by the path of meditation,
then this Hearer (who is on the Hearers’ path) achieves the Hearers’ path
of no-more learning and he achieves the respective enlightenment of his own
path. In this case, he achieved the result of the foe destroyer; Hearer
arhat.
If we look at the presentation of the path, according to the GES, this is
the general type of explanation of the five paths. Now, we look at what the
GES say about the three vehicles and the five paths.

7B Actual presentation of the grounds and paths
It is asserted that there are differences in the way in which the persons
of the three vehicles traverse the path.
Those of the hearer lineage conjoin the view realizing the person to be
empty of being self-sufficient substantially existent with a small
collection of merit and, having cultivated that for at least three
lifetimes, attain the small enlightenment.
Those of the solitary realizer lineage conjoin the view realizing the
person to be empty of being self-sufficient substantially existent with a
middling collection of merit and, having cultivated that for at least one
hundred eons, attain the middling enlightenment.
Bodhisattvas conjoin the view realizing the person to be empty of being
self-sufficient substantially existent with a great collection of merit
and, having cultivated that for at least three countless great eons, attain
the great enlightenment.
There are differences in the way in which they accumulate merit.
Bodhisattvas, having accumulated merit for at least three countless great
eons on the great stage of the path of accumulation and below, actualize
the heat stage of the path of preparation through to the path of no-more-
learning on one seat.
Those of the solitary realizer lineage, having accumulated merit for at
least one hundred great eons on the great stage of the path of accumulation
and below, actualize the heat stage of the path of preparation through to
the path of no-more-learning on one seat.
Those of the hearer lineage accumulate merit on all four learning paths
and, even after having attained a superior’s path, must also train on the
learning paths for up to fourteen lifetimes.
According to GES, the object of meditation of the three vehicles (the
Hearer, the Solitary Realizer and the Bodhisattva) is the same; which is
the emptiness of a self-sufficient substantially existent person.
In term of the object of negation, the apprehension of the self-sufficient
substantially existent person, is asserted to be the same.
But the time that they spent in accumulating the collection of merit
differs:
 ➢ For the Hearer, with a small collection of merit and, having cultivated
   that for at least three lifetimes, attain the Hearer enlightenment.
 ➢ For the Solitary Realizer, with a middling collection of merit and,
   having cultivated that for at least one hundred eons, attain the
   Solitary Realizer enlightenment.
 ➢ For the Bodhisattva, with a great collection of merit and, having
   cultivated that for at least three countless great eons, attain the full
   enlightenment
According to the GES, the Bodhisattva accumulate merit over three countless
great eons, but these three countless great eons happen on which path of
the five paths? It happens on the great stage of the path of accumulation
and below. If you think about it, according to the GES, the collection of
merit happen primarily on the path of accumulation. Based on that, there is
some meaning behind the term the path of accumulation.
We are talking about the Bodhisattva from the perspective of the GES. The
Bodhisattva accumulate merit for at least three countless great eons. These
three countless great eons happen on the path of accumulation. After having
accumulated this huge amount of merit, within the same meditative
equipoise:
    • they actualize the heat stage of the path of preparation
    • they manifest the peak stage of the path of preparation,
    • then follow-by forbearance stage of the path of preparation
    • then follow-by supreme mundane qualities stage of the path of
      preparation
    • then the path of seeing manifest
    • then the path of meditation manifest
    • finally, the path of no-more learning manifest.
All of these occur in one meditative equipoise in one seating
According to the GES, this is how a Bodhisattva achieves enlightenment.
From the very start of the path of preparation, which is the heat stage of
path of preparation, and all the way up to the path of no-more learning is
achieved in one meditative equipoise in one seating.

They assert that a buddha’s form aggregate is not a buddha because it is an
object to be abandoned. This is so because it is included in the same
lifetime as the bodily support of the earlier bodhisattva on the path of
preparation. This is necessarily so because the bodily support of the
bodhisattva on the path of preparation is an aggregate projected by
previous karma and afflictions.
A complete enjoyment body (Skt. sambhogakaya) is not accepted, and it is
asserted that, at the time of a nirvana without remainder of a supreme
emanation body, the continuum of awareness is severed.
Although a buddha superior has abandoned all sufferings and their origins
without exception, it is not contradictory that he still has true
sufferings in his continuum. This is because abandoning every single
affliction that observes true sufferings is posited as abandoning true
sufferings.
From the point of attaining the state of foe destroyer until they give up
their compositional factor of life [i.e., until they die], hearer and
solitary realizer foe destroyers are posited as being with remainder,
whereas from the point of giving up their compositional factor of life
[i.e., having died] they are posited as having passed beyond sorrow without
remainder [i.e., having attained a nirvana without remainder].
Although at the time of a nirvana with remainder they have abandoned the
afflictive obscurations without exception, they have not abandoned the non-
afflictive obscurations. Although at the time of a nirvana without
remainder the non-afflictive obscurations are not destroyed by the power of
an antidote, they do not exist because at that time, their support –the
continuum of awareness – is severed.
You may have heard about the twelve deeds of the Buddha, starting from the
deed of birth, and all the way up to the deed of passing way.
According to the GES, starting from the first deed of being born up to the
deed of subduing the mara, they are considered to be the deeds of ordinary
person. The deed of achieving enlightenment, the deed of turning the wheel
of dharma, and the deed of passing away into parinirvana are considered to
be the deeds of the Buddha.
According to what is being commonly accepted in the case of the life of the
Buddha, he finished the accumulation of merit, then he sat under the Bodhi
tree, and after subduing the hosts of mara under the Bodhi tree, he entered
into meditative equipoise, and within that meditative equipoise of one
seating he achieved enlightenment.
At that moment of entering into meditative equipoise, and of the five
paths, that is the heat stage of the path of preparation. At that one
seating, he progressed from the heat stage of the path of preparation all
the way to the path of no-more learning. This is the Hinayana explanation
of how the Buddha has achieved enlightenment in one seating under the Bodhi
tree; after having subduing the mara, he entered into meditative equipoise
and then he actualized enlightenment. From the time he entered the
meditative equipoise is where the heat stage of the path of preparation
starts, and in one seating all the way up to the path of no-more learning.
So this is the commonly accepted version of how the Buddha has achieved
enlightenment, primarily from the perspective of the Hinayana.
If we think according to this perspective, who is that person who entered
into meditative equipoise under the Bodhi tree after having subduing mara?
Isn’t that person an ordinary being?
A person who is in the path of preparation is an ordinary being, not a
superior being. An ordinary being has a contaminated body and mind. On the
basis of the same body, in one meditation session and in one seating, he
progresses all the up to enlightenment. After having achieved
enlightenment, according to the Hinayana perspective, the body is still
there. This is the body that the Buddha had when he was an ordinary being
on the path of preparation. Therefore, according to the Hinayana
perspective, the Buddha body is not the Buddha Jewel because it is true
suffering.
According to the Hinayana perspective, after having enlightenment, that
body is the same body that the Buddha had when he entered into meditative
equipoise under the Bodhi tree. At that time of entering into meditative
equipoise, he was not a Buddha. He was an ordinary being. But an ordinary
being means that having a contaminated body and that body of the person
entering into meditative equipoise is the result of karma and afflictions.
When in one seating he achieved enlightenment but after having achieved
enlightenment, the body is still there. From the Hinayana perspective, that
body is contaminated, it is true suffering. It is not Buddha but is an
object of abandonment.
According to the Hinayana perspective, they do assert a supreme emanation
body. The supreme emanation body is the form body of the Buddha. After
having enlightenment up to the point before manifesting the deed of passing
in parinirvana, that form body during that period is a supreme emanation
body. After passing in parinirvana, the continuum of the matter, which is
the body, is severed. So is the continuum of the Buddha mind which also
severed; just like the flame of the butter lamp that goes out of existent.
This is the Hinayana explanation.
In the Mahayana, there is a presentation of the complete enjoyment body;
one of the form body of Buddha. However, according to the Hinayana, this is
not accepted because, according to the Hinayana perspective, once the
Buddha has passed in parinirvana, the continuum of the Buddha’s
consciousness and body is severed.
You may have heard about the term nirvana with remainder. According to the
Hinayana perspective, this refers to the time when the Buddha was having
his contaminated body up to just before he passed in parinirvana. At that
time the Buddha has the contaminated body from before. During that period,
it is called nirvana with remainder. After passing in parinirvana (passing
beyond the sorrowless stage) the continuum of both matter and consciousness
is severed.  We have to think about this particular perspective about the
Buddha going out of existent; because both continuum of both matter and
consciousness is severed. Is there something that remains? It is the non-
compositional factor that remains. We have to think about what it really
mean when it says that the Buddha goes out of existent.
