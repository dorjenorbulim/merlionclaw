The definition of a person who propounds Buddhist tenets  is: a person propounding tene ts who accepts the Three Jewels  to be the final objects of 
refuge and who does not assert any ob jects of refuge other than these. 
TENET SCHOOL SUB-SCHOOLS  
CONSEQUENCE   (Prasangika) 
A proponent of the Middle Way who, by way of accepting just other-approved consequences, does not assert true 
existence even conventionally. 
SUTRA AUTONOMY   (Sautrantika-Svatantrika) 
A proponent of the Middle Way who propounds a presentation of conventionalities through mostly conforming with the Proponents of Sutra. MIDDLE WAY  (Madhyamika) 
A person propounding Great Vehicle tenets 
who does not assert true existence even conventionally. AUTONOMY  (Svatantrika) 
A proponent of the Middle Way who, by way of accepting autonomous 
reasons, does not assert true 
existence even conventionally. YOGIC AUTONOMY   (Yogacara-Svatantrika) 
A proponent of the Middle Way who propounds a presentation of conventionalities through mostly conforming with the Proponents of Mind Only. 
FALSE ASPECTARIANS (Alikakaravadin)  
A Proponent of Mind Only who asserts that a perception appr ehending form in the continuum of an ordinary being does 
engage, polluted by the latencies of ignorance, in  the factor of its appearance as a gross form. 
PROPONENTS OF AN EQUAL NUMBER 
OF OBJECTS AND SUBJECTS 
HALF-EGGISTS MIND ONLY  (Cittamatra)

who does not accept external objects but asserts truly existent self-cognition. TRUE ASPECTARIANS ( Satyakaravadin)  
A Proponent of Mind Only who asserts that a perception apprehending form in the continuum of an ordina ry being does not engage, polluted by 
the latencies of ignorance, in the fact or of its appearance as a gross form. 
NON-PLURALISTS 
FOLLOWERS OF REASONING 
A proponent of Sutra who follows [Dharmakir ti’s] Seven Treatises on Valid Cognition. SUTRA  (Sautrantika)
