Question: In the note it is said that there are three types of dualistic
appearance:
   1. appearance of external object
   2. appearance of subject and object being different entities
   3. appearance of conventional phenomena
When the dualistic appearance arises, is any one of these three appearances
or all the three appearances arise simultaneously to the mind?
Answer: Any of the three in the examples given by you can be posited to be
dualistic appearance. Dualistic appearance need not be all three.
Khen Rinpoche’s Question: Why do you think that in order to be dualistic
appearance, it must be all the three appearances?
I have just said that dualistic appearance can be any of the three in the
examples given by you and you all are satisfied with that. It needs not to
be all three of them. Do you all understand the reason for this? Why you
don’t need all the three in order for it to be dualistic appearance?
This is not straightforward and is not easy. The way to learn is not to be
just satisfied with it and accept it.  This is not going to work. The way
to study is to ask why this and why that. The only way to learn is to have
a continuous succession of questions; Why? Why? Why? Only then you will get
the point.
What fault would there be if one assert that all three must be present in
order for it to be dualistic appearance?
(Student presented her proposal)
Khen Rinpoche: Appearance of external object is always there. As it is
being stated very clearly, all sense consciousnesses are necessarily
mistaken. So definitely it is there.
You mentioned that all three have to be present because there is an
appearance of external object therefore there is an appearance of subject
and object being different entities, and …..
What are the problems if all three appearances have to be there in order
for it to be dualistic appearance?

We have started with the MWS and there are two divisions:
   1. AMWS
   2. CMWS
Both AMWS and CMWS are the proponents of the Middle Way, which means both
refute true existence even conventionally.
The difference between AMWS and CMWS:
    ➢ AMWS assert existence by way of its own character
    ➢ CMWS do not assert existence by way of its own character

In order to know the difference between these two proponents of the Middle
Way, one must know:
    ➢ what is true existence
    ➢ what is the meaning of existence by way of its own character
Without which and just by saying there is a difference between these two,
it is nothing.
What is establishment by way of its own character?
Establishment by way of its own character means that the imputed object
when sought is findable.
So when the imputed object when sought is findable, it shows that there is
establishment by way of its own character.
When the imputed object when sought is NOT findable, it shows that there
isn’t establishment by way of its own character.
The AMWS believe that object exist from its own side in that when you point
your finger at it, at the end of the day, there is something that you can
call as the object. That is what you are looking for. So there is
appearance of such an object. This appearance is coming from the side of
the object. The AMWS assert that this is how things and events are in
reality; when you look for the imputed object you will be able to find it.
You can definitely point to it.
For the tenets of AMWS and below, they believe that thing exist from its
own side and when the imputed object when sought is findable. Everything is
like that which includes the ‘I’, the self, the person.
For the ‘I’, the self, the person, when you look for it, you should be able
to point to something that it is the person. According to the AMWS, the
illustration of a person would be the mental consciousness.
Therefore, the person can be found. They do think about this to account for
how a person that moves from life to life, and yet the person is findable.
At the end of the day, they conclude that it is not this and it is not
that, and it has to be the mind. Within the mind, it has to be the mental
consciousness. Not just consciousness, it has to be the mental
consciousness. Many other tenets also assert that the mental consciousness
is the person.
It is stated very clearly in the writing of the great master Bhavaviveka,
the trailblazer of AMWS. He clearly said that the mental consciousness is
the ‘I’, the self.
Now we are talking about the assertion of the AMWS. AMWS say that the
mental consciousness is the ‘I’. However, bear in mind that at the end of
the day, in actual reality, the mental consciousness is not the ‘I’ when we
look at it from the viewpoint of CMWS. It is wrong to posit the mental
consciousness to be the illustration of ‘I’, but this is AMWS’ view. This
is because of their fundamental assertion that thing exist by way of its
own character, and you must be able to point to something that is the
phenomenon in question. Since you believe that the phenomenon can be
pointed to and can be found when sought, then with regards to the ‘I’, the
best illustration that can be pointed to is the mental consciousness.
Of course it is an extremely difficult task to realize what exactly the ‘I’
or the self or the person is. There is the ‘I’ that want to be happy and
there is an ‘I’ that do not want to suffer. What exactly is this ‘I’? It is
a big question. This is the main object of enquiry of all Buddhist tenets.
Everybody is trying to find out what exactly is this ‘I’ or the person. All
the tenets have their own explanation for this. Because of not
understanding what exactly is the ‘I’ or the person, all problems arises.
Every single problems, difficulties and hardships comes from that. With a
correct and proper understanding of what exactly the ‘I’ or the person is,
these problems will go away.
The AMWS do not assert true existence because they are the Proponent of
Middle Way. What exactly is true existence? As in the previous lesson, with
the quotation from “The Descent into Lanka Sutra”* as the source, the AMWS
states that something is an existence because it appears to the mind/
awareness.
That is why it exists. Without it appearing, it cannot be posited as an
existent.
So for the AMWS, this is the meaning of existence.
The opposite of that would be if there is something that can exist without
appearing, that would make the object truly existent. In order for
something to be an existent, it has to appear to a mind/ awareness.
This is AMWS view. They explain that anything that exist has to be posited
through it appearing to an awareness; not just any awareness, it has to
appear to a non-defective awareness.
* The Descent into Lanka Sutra:
  All phenomena exist through appearing to consciousness,
  All phenomena exist conventionally and
  All phenomena do not exist ultimately
What determine whether something exists or not? If  it  is  posited  through
the force of appearing to a non-defective awareness, then it is said  to  be
an existent. It has to appear to a non-defective awareness.
What is the meaning of non-defective awareness?
Non-defective awareness has two features:
1.  It is a mind that is not affected by adventitious causes of error.
2.  It is not mistaken with object appearing as existing by way of  its  own
character.
In short, if it is an existent it has to be posited  through  the  force  of
appearing to a non-defective awareness.
According to AMWS, true existence means establishment  by  way  of  its  own
uncommon mode of existence  without  being  posited  through  the  force  of
appearing to a non-defective awareness.

The YMWA assert that:
    ➢ A form and a cognizer apprehending that form being empty of different

    ➢ All phenomena being empty of true existence is asserted to be the

Therefore YMWA divide the selflessness of phenomena into coarse and subtle.
The YMWA distinguishes between:
    ➢ coarse and subtle selflessness of person
    ➢ coarse and subtle selflessness of phenomena
The difference is not made on the basis of emptiness. The difference is
made by way of the object of negation.
Having said that you should be able to explain why, according to the AMWS,

- the refutation of the object of negation - true existence – upon the

7 Presentation of the grounds and paths
According to Yogic Middle Way Autonomists the differences between the
persons of the three vehicles are posited in terms of three different
obscurations that are the main objects of abandonment and three different
views that are the main objects of meditation.
In order to clarify what I have just said, the next three paragraphs
explain how the differences between the persons of three vehicles are
posited in term of the three obscurations.
[Root text: page 21]
Those of the hearer lineage, having taken the conception apprehending [the
person] to be self- sufficient substantially existent together with its
retinue as their main object of abandonment, through taking, as the
antidote to that, the view realizing the person to be empty of being self-
sufficient substantially existent as their main object of meditation,
attain the small enlightenment.
The three vehicles are the Hearer vehicle, the Solitary Realizer vehicle
and the Bodhisattva vehicle.
For those of the Hearer lineage:
➢ The main object of meditation is the emptiness of a self-sufficient
  substantially existent person.
➢ The main object of abandonment is the conception apprehending a self-
  sufficient substantially existent person
➢ Through meditating on the emptiness of a self-sufficient substantially
  existent person they abandon the conception apprehending a self-
  sufficient substantially existent person.
➢ Through that they achieve small enlightenment, which is the Hearer’s
  enlightenment
Those of the solitary realizer lineage, having taken the conception
apprehending a form and the valid cognizer apprehending that form to be
different substances as their main object of abandonment, through taking,
as the antidote to that, the view realizing apprehenders and apprehendeds
to be empty of being different substances as their main object of
meditation, attain the middling enlightenment.
For those of the Solitary Realizer lineage:
➢ The main of object meditation is the emptiness of object and subject
  being different substances.
➢ The main object of abandonment is the conception apprehending form and
  the valid cognizer apprehending that form to be of different substances.
➢ Through meditating on the emptiness, they abandon these objects of
  abandonment.
➢ Through that they achieve middling enlightenment, which is the Solitary
  Realizer’s enlightenment
Bodhisattvas, having taken the conception of true existence together with
its latencies as their main object of abandonment, through taking, as the
antidote to that, the view realizing all phenomena to be lacking true
existence as their main object of meditation, attain the great
enlightenment.
For those of the Bodhisattva lineage:
➢ The main object of meditation is the emptiness of true existence.
➢ The main object of abandonment is the conception of true existence
➢ Through meditating on the emptiness they abandon these objects of
  abandonment
➢ Through that they achieve great enlightenment, which is the full
  enlightenment
This is the explanation of YMWA. YMWA assert that there are differences
between the person of the three vehicles and the differences are posited in
term of them having different obscurations as the main object of
abandonment. Therefore, there is a difference in their main object of
meditation.

According to Sutra Middle Way Autonomists there is no difference between
hearers and solitary realizers regarding their main objects of abandonment
and main objects of meditation because they are similar in taking the
afflictive obscurations as their main objects of abandonment, and they are
also similar in taking the selflessness of persons as their main object of
meditation. However, there is a reason why there is a difference in the
results of those two in terms of inferiority and superiority. It is because
there is a difference in terms of the amount of merit accumulated and the
length of time [spent accumulating that merit].
SMWA are similar to the Sutra School in asserting external object. Because
they assert external object, they don’t talk about the emptiness of object
and subject being different substances.
The SMWA assert that:
➢ The main object of meditation of both Hearer and Solitary Realizer is
  the selflessness of persons
➢ The main object of abandonment of for both Hearer and Solitary Realizer
  is the afflictive obscurations.
This is according to the SMWA.
Having said that, if you were to be asked a question: According to the
SMWA, is there any difference between the Hearer and the Solitary Realizer?

The answer is: Yes, there is a difference. The difference comes in terms of
the amount of merit that Hearer and Solitary Realizer accumulate.
The Autonomists are similar to the Proponents of Mind Only in asserting two
types of sutras – Great Vehicle sutras and Lesser Vehicle sutras – and in
asserting two parts to sutras – the definitive and interpretive.
However, they are not similar in the illustrations of these because the
Proponents of Mind Only assert the first two turnings of the wheel [of
Dharma] to be sutras of interpretive meaning and the third to be sutras of
definitive meaning.
The Buddha words can be divided into the Hinayana sutra and Mahayana sutra.

How are the Hinayana and Mahayana differentiated?
There are three turnings the wheel of dharma.
The sutra of the first turning the wheel of dharma are Hinayana sutras and
the uncommon Hinayana sutras.
The second turning the wheel of dharma consists of the Mahayana sutras and
the uncommon Mahayana sutra.
The third turning the wheel of dharma consist of sutras that are common/
shared with both the Hinayana and Mahayana sutras.
According the YMWA:
    ➢ the first and third turnings the wheel of dharma are the sutras of
      interpretive meaning
    ➢ the second turning the wheel of dharma are the sutras of both
      interpretive meaning and definitive meaning
According the AMWS:
    ➢ The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom Sutra, which we recite all the
      times, it is a sutra that belongs to the second turning the wheel of
      dharma and it is a sutra of interpretive meaning.
    ➢ The Short, Middling and Extensive Perfection of Wisdom Sutra are the
      sutras that belong to the second turning the wheel of dharma and it is
      a sutra of definitive meaning
The Heart of the Perfection of the Wisdom Sutra, it is sutra belong to the
second turning the wheel of dharma and it is a sutra of interpretive
meaning but not the definitive meaning. Why? This is because, according to
the AMWS, the Heart of the Perfection of the Wisdom Sutra cannot be read or
taken literally. According to them, if you take or read it literally there
will be many problems.
Why, according to the AMWS, the Heart of the Perfection of the Wisdom sutra
is not suitable to be read or taken literally?
This is because the Heart of the Perfection of the Wisdom Sutra states
clearly that phenomena are empty of inherent existent. But the AMWS assert
that phenomena exist inherently. The AMWS assert that if it exists it is
necessarily exist inherently. If you take the Heart of the Perfection of
the Wisdom Sutra literally that means you have to accept that things do not
exist inherently.
The AMWS do accept that the Short, Middling and Extensive Perfection of
Wisdom Sutra are sutras of definitive meaning. Why? Because in those sutras
the qualification of ‘ultimately’ is added. So whatever things are said to
be empty of existing inherently the qualification of ‘ultimately’ is added.
Ultimately thing are not inherently existent.
In this context, the first and third turnings of the wheel [of Dharma] are
sutras of interpretive meaning, while the middle [turning of the wheel of
Dharma] has both parts – interpretive and definitive – because those in
which the qualification of ‘ultimately’ is applied to the object of
negation are asserted to be sutras of definitive meaning, while those of
the middle turning of the wheel, in which it is not applied, are asserted
to be sutras of interpretive meaning.
