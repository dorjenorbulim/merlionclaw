Sutra School
The explanation of the Sutra School (Skt. Sautrantika) has seven outlines:
   1. definition,
   2. divisions,
   3. etymology,
   4. way of asserting objects,
   5. way of asserting object-possessors,
   6. way of asserting selflessness, and
   7. presentation of the grounds and paths.

1 Definition
The definition of a Proponent of Sutra is: a person propounding Lesser
Vehicle tenets who accepts both self-cognizers and external objects.
Proponent of Sutra (Skt. Sautrantika) and Exemplifier (Skt. Darstantika)
are equivalent.
A Proponent of Sutra is a proponent of Hinayana tenets.
The difference between a Proponent of Sutra and a Proponent of Great
Exposition lies in whether one asserts self-knower or not.
A Proponent of Sutra is a proponent of Hinayana tenets who accepts self-
knower; whereas the Proponent of Great Exposition does not accept self-
knower
We did talk about the self-knower in the previous module (Lorig).
Let’s use the illustration of an eye consciousness apprehending blue:
The eye consciousness apprehending blue itself has:
    - Blue as its object
    - Blue is that which is apprehended
So the eye consciousness apprehending blue has the aspect of the
apprehended
According to the Proponent of Sutra, this eye consciousness apprehending
blue has two aspects:
    - There is an aspect of it that apprehends an object other than itself -
      in this case is blue.
      This has an aspect of the apprehended
    - There is another aspect of it that has the potential/ power to which
      is mere experience of itself. It has the entity of clarity and
      knowing. This aspect can experience itself.
In short, within the collection of an eye consciousness apprehending blue,
there are two parts:
    - One part is an aspect of apprehended - its ability to experience the
      object blue
    - Another part of it is being able to experience itself.
 ➢ Within this eye consciousness apprehending blue, there is part of it
   apprehending blue; which is the apprehended. So it has the aspect of the
   apprehended.
 ➢ There is another part of this consciousness being able to experience
   itself.
   This is what we call a self-knower, which is the aspect of the
   apprehender.
So the Proponent of Sutra and the other proponents who also assert self-
knower have their reasons for asserting self-knower. The main reason is
memory. People who assert self-knower assert that there must be within the
consciousness itself that can experience itself.
In the case of an eye consciousness apprehending blue, there must be a part
of this eye consciousness apprehending blue that experiences itself. After
having seeing blue, subsequently at a later time, it is possible for one to
remember having seeing blue.
Why do you remember seeing blue?
According to those who assert self-knower, the only reason of remembering
seeing blue is because of having experienced it in the past before.  In the
past our eye consciousness apprehended blue. Because the blue was
experienced at the earlier time, then in the later time, it is possible to
remember having seeing blue.
Without a consciousness having actually experience the object, we cannot
remember the object later on. Just as we can remember blue because of our
eye consciousness have apprehended blue earlier, similarly there is a
reason why we can remember at the later time the experience of having
seeing blue itself. We can actually remember at later time; “There was an
eye consciousness of mine that apprehended blue.”
Let’s say you do remember; “In the past I have the experience of blue.” You
remember you were aware of blue. We are not talking about blue; we are
talking about the subject.
Earlier on there was a consciousness of yours, in this case an eye
consciousness that apprehended blue , you do remember. Why you are able to
remember that there was an eye consciousness earlier on that apprehended
blue? The reason is being that there must have been something that
experienced that eye consciousness that apprehended blue. Because of this,
they assert the self-knower.
Based on this line of reasoning therefore they assert that in the case of
the eye consciousness apprehending blue, within the collection of the eye
consciousness apprehending blue, there must be part of it that is able to
experience itself.  Without which, it is not possible that at the later
time to remember that there was an eye consciousness apprehending blue
being generated earlier.
The point is get is the line of reasoning. If you understand the line of
reasoning, then it is not difficult at all. What we need to do is to
understand the line of reasoning.
In the text, how is it being phrased in the logical form? This is how it is
formed in the logical sentence.
The subject, the eye consciousness apprehending blue, [thesis] have an
experiencer, because [the reason/sign] of being at the later time it is
possible to have a memory of that eye consciousness apprehending blue.
This is how the logical way of presenting an argument is being formulated.
We have to see the line of reasoning.
Let’s start with ‘blue’.
Do we remember ‘blue’? Yes, because you have seen blue before.
If you have not seen blue before, and have not experienced blue before, is
it possible to remember ‘blue’ later on? No. It is 100% sure that if you
have not experienced blue before, you have not seen blue before, then it is
not possible for you to remember something that you did not see. We do not
remember blue.
Now we talk about remembering the eye consciousness that apprehended blue.
Is it possible to remember that you have an eye consciousness apprehending
blue if you do not experience that eye consciousness apprehending blue
earlier on? Can you remember something that you do not experience earlier?
If that is the case, therefore it follow that there must be an experiencer
of that eye consciousness apprehending blue. That experiencer is called a
self-knower.
This is the mother of all the reasons for proving self-knower, but it
doesn’t mean that if it is the mother of all the reasons, that means it is
a reason of reality. If you think about reality, there is no self-knower.
The Proponent of Sutra asserts self-knower. The Proponent of the Mind Only
and the proponent of the Autonomy Middle Ways also assert self-knower. It
is only the GES and CMWS do not assert self-knower.

2 Divisions
There are two divisions:
1. Followers of Scripture and
2. Followers of Reasoning.
An example of a Follower of Scripture is a Proponent of Sutra who follows
[Vasubandhu’s] Treasury of Manifest Knowledge.
An example of a Follower of Reasoning is a Proponent of Sutra who follows
[Dharmakirti’s] Seven Treatises on Valid Cognition.
A Follower of Scripture is a Proponent of Sutra who follows Vasubandhu’s
Treasury of Manifest Knowledge (Abhidharmakosa). They are in almost every
way similar to the Proponents of the Great Exposition.
A Follower of Reasoning is a Proponent of Sutra who follows Dharmakirti’s
Seven Treatises on Valid Cognition.
Dharmakirti was a great logician who composed this Seven Treatises on Valid
Cognition and one of this is called
Pramanavartika; which has four chapters. It is a difficult text, but the
study of it still exists as today it is done in the monastery.
Pramanavartika, perhaps, is the most extensive text on valid cognition.
Why they are the proponent of Sutra?
3 Etymology
Why are they called ‘Proponents of Sutra’?
They are called Proponents of Sutra due to propounding tenets through
following the sutras of the Bhagavan, and they are called Exemplifiers due
to desiring to teach all phenomena by way of examples.
4 Way of asserting objects
The definition of existent is: that observed by a valid cognizer.
There are two types of existents:
  1. conventional truths and
  2. ultimate truths.
The definition of an ultimate truth* is: a phenomenon that is ultimately
able to perform a function.
Ultimate truth, truly existent, thing, product, impermanent phenomenon,
compounded phenomenon, substance, and specifically characterized phenomenon
are equivalent.
(*This is according to the Followers of Reasoning. The Followers of
Scripture assert the two truths in the same way as the Proponents of the
Great Exposition.)

The definition of a conventional truth is: a phenomenon that is not
ultimately able to perform a function.
Conventional truth, falsely existent, permanent phenomenon, and generally
characterized phenomenon are equivalent.
According to this school, you have to remember:
-Ultimate truth, truly existent, thing, product, impermanent phenomenon,
compounded phenomenon, substance, and specifically characterized phenomenon
are mutually inclusive
-Conventional truth, falsely existent, permanent phenomenon, and generally
characterized phenomenon are mutually inclusive

There are facsimiles of a direct perceiver that are conceptual and non-

In this context, an ultimate awareness is a direct perceiver. It is not a

context, direct valid cognizer is an ultimate awareness.
Why is it an ultimate awareness?
Because it is non-mistaken, therefore it is an ultimate mind. It is correct
and a good mind. Therefore it is called ultimate.
What is the meaning of ultimate? Ultimate means correct.
In this case, a direct valid cognizer is an ultimate awareness because it
is non-mistaken. It is non-mistaken therefore it is ultimate.
An illustration of ultimate awareness: An eye consciousness apprehending
blue
An eye consciousness apprehending blue is a direct valid cognizer, it is an
ultimate awareness, and it is non-mistaken.
The object, blue, exists in the way it appears to the eye consciousness
apprehending blue.
There is an accord between what it appears and what exist. Blue exists in
the way it appears to the eye consciousness apprehending it.
An eye consciousness apprehending blue is a direct valid cognizer;
therefore it is an ultimate awareness. Why is it an ultimate awareness?
Because it is not mistaken.
Blue is an ultimate truth because blue is true in the perspective of this
ultimate awareness.
Now we are looking at ultimate truth and looking at the etymology of the
phrase “ultimate truth”.
In order to understand that, you first have to understand what is an
ultimate awareness. Once you have understood what an ultimate awareness is,

In this case, the blue is the ultimate truth. Blue is true in the
perspective of the direct valid cognizer apprehending it because it exist

awareness.
In this context, conventional awareness is a mistaken mind. In this
context, we can understand it to be a mind that obscures one from seeing
the truth. Therefore, the conventional awareness is a mistaken mind and
also a conceptual consciousness.
Conceptual consciousness is necessarily mistaken. Why? Because all
conceptual consciousness are necessarily cannot directly see the
specifically characterized phenomenon.
The conceptual consciousness is the obscurer which obscures from directly
perceiving the specifically characterized phenomenon.
No matter how a conceptual consciousness operates, in order for a
phenomenon to appear to a conceptual consciousness, the phenomenon always
appears to be mixed with the meaning generality of that object that it
appears. The phenomenon cannot appear without being mixed with the meaning
generality of the object.
Conceptual consciousness engages its object through the object and the
meaning generality appearing to be mixed.
So, all conceptual consciousnesses are necessarily mistaken because the
conceptual consciousness engages its object through mixing up the actual
object with the meaning generality. Although what is appearing to the
conceptual consciousness is not the actual object, it appears as if it is
the actual object. This is how it engages any object through that
appearance.
What we have to sort out from the beginning are these two kinds of

The definition of the ultimate truth given here is: a phenomenon that is

Blue is an example of an ultimate truth in this system.
Blue is a cause (of something). The fact that it is a cause means that, by

If it is a cause, from the moment it come to existence, by nature from its

characteristic would make it an ultimate truth.
Conventional truth refers to a phenomenon which by nature cannot produce an
effect.
Therefore, the definition of a conventional truth is: a phenomenon that is
not ultimately able to perform a function.

- Impermanent phenomena are mutually inclusive with ultimate truth

7 Presentation of the grounds and paths
Since for those of all three lineages the accumulation of merit is
accumulated on all four learning paths, a buddha’s form aggregate is
accepted to be a buddha.
The presentation of the obscurations and the way of traversing the grounds
and paths and so forth are similar to [the assertions of] the Proponents of
the Great Exposition.

Student: Are true cessations ultimate truth?
Khen Rinpoche: I guess, in this school, true cessation is posited to be
permanent, therefore it is conventional truth.  Don’t worry about this as

Student: For the GES and SS, can we say the innate apprehension of a self
of person is a non-conceptual consciousness and an intellectually acquired
apprehension of a self of person is a conceptual consciousness?
Khen Rinpoche: If you say the innate apprehension of a self is non-
conceptual then is it a sense consciousness or mental consciousness?
Definitely it cannot be sense consciousness. So it has to be mental
consciousness.
Within the mental consciousness, there are conceptual and non-conceptual.
How many type of mental direct perceiver are there? There are three mental
direct perceivers:
   1. Awareness to which an object appears and is not ascertained,
   2. Valid cognizer
   3. Subsequent cognizer
An innate apprehension of self-sufficient substantially existent person
cannot be one of these three mental direct perceivers.
Then what is left is a facsimile of a mental direct perceiver. If we say
that it is non-conceptual, then it has to be a facsimile of a mental direct
perceiver which is non-conceptual.
So what is left is a facsimile of a mental direct perceiver of which they
are conceptual or non-conceptual. How about dream consciousness? It cannot
be just dreaming. When you are awake you have to account for consciousness
which is non-conceptual. If you think about in this way, you start to
eliminate the possibilities. Probably you have to say that the innate
apprehension of a self-sufficient substantially existent person is a
conceptual mind. So an apprehension of a permanent, unitary and independent
self is also a conceptual. Both of these apprehensions are wrong
consciousness.
Within the division of wrong consciousness, there are conceptual wrong
consciousness and non-conceptual wrong consciousnesses. These two
apprehensions of a self of person are conceptual wrong consciousness. So
between conceptual and non-conceptual, these two apprehensions would be
conceptual.
