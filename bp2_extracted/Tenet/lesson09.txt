Student: What is the meaning of one seating in term of travelling through
the five paths?
Khen Rinpoche: One seating, literally it means one seating. For the case of
someone who is a bodhisattva, he achieves enlightenment by progressing from
the heat stage of the path of preparation all the way to the path of no-
more learning in one seating, that means on the heat stage of the path of
preparation, he enters into the meditative equipoise, and within that
meditative equipoise, he achieves all the paths and then manifests
enlightenment without arising into the subsequent attainment; i.e. without
getting into the post-meditative session.

Khen Rinpoche (In English): Getting enlighten overnight, not one year, not

has already engaged in many of the practices of austerity. Then he seated
under the Bodhi tree. There were host of maras came to disturb him. After
having overcome them, he entered into meditative equipoise. In that one
meditative equipoise, he progressed from the heat stage of the path of
preparation all the ways to the path of no-more learning within a couple of
hours, which was an overnight thing.
Student: What is the boundary for saying someone is a proponent of a
particular tenet?
Khen Rinpoche: Now we are studying tenets, does it make us a proponent of
tenets? In order for us to be a proponent of Buddhist tenets, obviously we
need to be a Buddhist and must have accepted the Three Jewels as the object
of refuge. At the beginning of this module, we have explained the meaning
of tenets, which it is an established conclusion. As long as in your mind
you accept any one of the four schools and think; “It is definitely like
that” and you are also able to propound to others whatever you have
accepted by using logic and reasoning, perhaps then could be said that to
be a proponent of that Buddhist tenets.
If you remembered in our earlier discussion, a proponent of Buddhist tenets
is someone who necessarily has accepted the Four Seals, which means, you
have thought about the four seals and accepted it; “It is definitely like
that”.

Student: For the Hinayana hearers and solitary realizers, the factor of non-
knowingness is achieved not by antidotes but due to the continuum of body
and mind being severed. They have abandoned the factor of non-knowingness
unwittingly; i.e. no need to go through the three countless great eons to
abandon the non-knowingness like a bodhisattva.  For the bodhisattvas who
achieve the same nirvana without remainder, he has no more continuums, no
more body and mind. How do the GES assert that a Buddha is able to benefit
sentient beings limitlessly and in a countless way?
Khen Rinpoche: When the arhats achieve the nirvana with remainder, they
also abandon the non-afflictive obscurations, but the abandonment comes
about not through the force of an antidote but through the basis of going
out of existent. The GES assert that the Buddha’s form aggregate ceases to
exist after passing into nirvana without remainder, so they do not assert
that the Buddha manifesting here and there and giving teachings and doing
works for sentient beings. According to this school, after Buddha has
passed into nirvana without remainder and probably you have to say the
something that is left over is a non-associated compositional factor.
Unlike the Mahayana, the GES do not assert that after the Buddha have
passed into parinirvana without remainder the Buddha still remain emanating
and doing works for sentient beings.
Student: Is there any difference between substantially existent and
substantially established? If there is a difference, what is the
difference?
Khen Rinpoche: According to this school, substantial existent is mutually
inclusive with ultimate truth.  In this school, everything that exists is
necessary substantial established. All phenomena are substantially
established/ truly established/ existing by way of their own character.
Substantial existent is mutually inclusive with ultimate truth. So, in
order to understand what substantially existent is, you just have to think
about the definition of ultimate truth.  According to definition, as long
as the phenomena such that an awareness apprehending it is not cancelled if
it is broken up or mentally separated into its individual parts, that would
be an ultimate truth, then it would be substantially existent.
Student: How do we know that we are not over negating the ‘I’ when we are
doing the meditation on the self-sufficient substantially existent I
according to this school?
Khen Rinpoche: Here we are talking about this from the perspective of this
school. In any case, whenever you want to negate the self that doesn’t
exist, you first must identify correctly an experience of what exactly is
the thing that you are negating.
First, the most important step, always identify correctly the object of
negation/object of refutation. After having identify correctly the target,
one has to understand that the I definitely exist.
The question is not whether the I exist or not, as the I does exist. The
question is how does the I exists?
I exists but the object of negation here -a self-sufficient substantially
existent person- doesn’t exist.
That is what we are trying to understand - why such a self-sufficient
substantially existent person doesn’t exist? According to this school, we
must differentiate between:
- the I that exists; which is the substantially existent I, and
- the I that doesn’t exist, the self-sufficient substantially existent
person.

Student: Are the mode of apprehension of these two minds the same?
   1. The mind apprehending a permanent, unitary and independent person
   2. The mind apprehending the self-sufficient, substantially existent
      person that is intellectually acquired
Why if you realize the selflessness of the self-sufficient substantially
existent person, you abandon the conception of the permanent, unitary and
independent person? Why the other way can work if you say apprehension is
different?
Khen Rinpoche: As I mentioned before, first you have to be clear about the
mode of apprehension of these two minds.

For the apprehension of a permanent, unitary and independent self, what is
the particular way that this mind apprehending the self?
For the apprehension of a self-sufficient substantially existent person,
this mind also has a particular way of apprehending the self. What is its
manner of apprehending the self?
So, first you have to be clear about the two modes of apprehensions.
Many texts say that the apprehension of the permanent, unitary and
independent self is a coarse apprehension of a self and it is
intellectually acquired.
For the apprehension of a self-sufficient substantially existent person,
this mind can be an intellectually acquired form and it can be an innate
form.
One of the sub-schools of the GES, the followers of Vatsiputra, the
Vatsiputriyans, asserts a self-sufficient substantially existent person.
Their assertion is an intellectually acquired one because it is a result of
their own thinking; basically it is their tenet, their belief, their own
conclusion, but nevertheless it is something that comes about through
thinking or intellectual. So the Vatsiputriyans’ mode of apprehension of
the self-sufficient substantially existent person is an intellectually
acquired apprehension.
The mode of apprehension of this mind (self-sufficient substantially
existent person) is not exactly the same like the mode of apprehension of a
permanent, unitary and independent self.
Student: If the apprehension is different, then why is it that when the
person realizes that the self-sufficient substantially existent person does
not exist, he necessarily realizes that the self is not permanent, unitary
and independent?
Khen Rinpoche: When you realize the emptiness of self-sufficient
substantially existent person, you also realize the emptiness of permanent,
unitary and independent self.
The intellectually acquired afflictions are the abandonment of the path of
seeing; specifically they are abandoned by the uninterrupted path of the
path of seeing. So it is at the uninterrupted path of the path of seeing
where you realize the emptiness of the self-sufficient substantially
existent person. That uninterrupted path of the path of seeing does abandon
the intellectually acquired afflictions.
According to the GES, there is a way of looking at the path of seeing in
terms of the 8 moments of knowledge and 8 moments of forbearance and one
can apply that to the afflictions of the three realms -the desire, form and
the formless realms. The point is that these afflictions of the three
realms have to be abandoned serially, not simultaneously on the path of
seeing.
As in the path of seeing there are the 8 moments of forbearance and 8
moments of knowledge, that means there are 8 uninterrupted paths and 8
paths of released. I didn’t explain this because there is too much
information and there is no time. It is in the reference text (Cutting
Through Appearances). For those of you who are interested you can read this
on your own, and if there is anything you don’t understand you can bring it
up in class.

Then if you look at what is an intellectually acquired affliction and

In the case of an apprehension of a self-sufficient substantially existent

The difference between these two lies in:
- whether that affliction is a result of a process of thinking, which means
intellectually acquired or
- whether the affliction or the way of seeing the self is innate or
natural.

Student: Khen Rinpoche explained about the three vehicles and the three
paths and how they accumulate merits and the different levels of
enlightenment achieved by the hearer, solitary realize and bodhisattva. It
seems that at the end of that path, the consciousness is severed in all
three instances. Nirvana without remainder means that the consciousness is
severed. I am trying to understand what is the point of engaging in any of
the path if at the end everything is severed?
Khen Rinpoche: We must always keep in mind that whatever discussion we are
doing here is from the perspective of the GES. There is a difference
between a Mahayana arhat and the Hinayana arhat. Buddha is called the
Mahayana Arhat, which is a result of having accumulated merits over the
period of three great countless eons. What does it mean to be a Mahayana
Arhat? The all-knowing i.e. know everything because of having abandoned
even the non-afflictive obscuration. This is very different from the
Hinayana Arhat. When one doesn’t have any non-afflictive obscuration, that
means one know everything. When one knows everything, then working for
others is an easier job because one can see exactly what needs to be done
to accomplish the benefits of sentient beings. Then it doesn’t matter how
long one live, as long as one is around, one will just do one best to
benefit others. When it is time to die, then it is finished and that is the
real nirvana, which is peace, isn’t it.
Regarding the consciousness/mind going out of existence, this is a Hinayana
perspective, not a Mahayana perspective. Regarding the continuum of
consciousness is severed at the time of achieving nirvana without
remainder; this is a tenet and not necessary what the reality is. If you
think about it, explain by using logic it is difficult to explain how the
continuum of the consciousness can ever be severed? If you apply logic and
reasoning, definitely you will see the internal contradiction. On one hand,
they assert that there are past and future lives, and then suddenly…. If
you apply logic then it is difficult to hold on to that position. Because
you can find faults in this assertion, therefore this is not the final
view.

Student: Is compounded space permanent or impermanent? What is the
difference between compounded space and uncompounded space?
Khen Rinpoche: You can take space to be that vacuity that you can see the
absence of something there. That is compounded space.  There is this
vacuity that you can see if there is nothing that is obstructing. But if
there is something that is obstructing then you can’t see. So that vacuity
that you can see is space. So that vacuity that you see is compounded
space.
Whereas uncompounded space is a phenomenon that does not arise from causes
and conditions, because it is permanent. Permanent phenomena do not arise
from causes and conditions.
So what exactly is uncompounded space? It is that mere negation of
obstructive contact.
When we talk about space, the compounded space, the space that we can see
is the impermanent one.
When we talk about uncompounded space, that is permanent.
