Object of engagement primarily refers to the principal object that is
engaged in or realized or gotten at by a valid cognizer.
A valid cognizer gets its object of engagement; its principal object.
A wrong consciousness is the opposite of a valid cognizer because a wrong
consciousness does not get at the principal object. For that reason, we do
not posit object of engagement for wrong consciousness.
Having said that, this doesn’t mean that consciousnesses that have object
of engagement are valid cognizers.
This is the reason why we do not posit object of engagement for all wrong
consciousnesses. This is the position of Sera Je College. There are some
different opinions on this as some other colleges have different position
and they have different definition for wrong consciousness.
Our definition: Wrong consciousness is a knower that engages its object
erroneously.
Other colleges’ definition: Wrong consciousness is a knower that is
mistaken with regards to its object of engagement.

There are two types of valid cognizers:
   1. Direct valid cognizer
   2. Inferential valid cognizer.
When we talked about direct perceivers, there are:
   1. Sense direct perceiver
   2. Mental direct perceiver
A sense direct perceiver is generated in dependence upon three conditions:
    - Its uncommon empowering condition : a physical sense power
    - Its observed object condition
    - Immediately preceding condition
So in dependence on these three conditions, a sense direct valid cognizer
is produced. Similarly, the sense direct perceiver is produced as well.

Definition of an inferential valid cognizer is: a new incontrovertible
determinative knower that is directly produced in dependence on a correct
sign which is its basis.
How an inferential valid cognizer is produced?
Inferential valid cognizer is generated in dependence upon a correct sign
which is its basis.
[pic]
Inferential valid cognizer has three divisions:
1. Inferential cognizer by power of the fact
    e.g. an inferential cognizer which realizes that sound is impermanent
through the sign of being a product.
2. Inferential cognizer through renown
  e.g an inferential cognizer which realizes that it is suitable to express
  the rabbit-possessor by the term moon from the sign of its existing among
  objects of thought.
3. Inferential cognizer through belief
  e.g an inferential cognizer which realizes that the scripture, “From
  giving, resources, from ethics, a happy migration,” is incontrovertible
  with respect to the meaning indicated by it by the sign of its being a
  scripture free from the three contradictions.
Since an inferential valid cognizer is generated in dependence on a correct
sign which is its basis, therefore it is very important to know what a
correct sign is.
Refer to page 3 of handout dated 24 July: There is a brief introduction of
what a correct sign is.
When stating a correct sign:
    - First there must first be a subject. The subject is that which you are
      trying to analyze
    - There must be a predicate to be proven
    - Then comes the correct sign
When you state a reason for proving something, the sign/reason could be
correct and it could be incorrect as well.
It is important to identify what constitute a correct sign because it is in
dependence upon a correct sign that an inferential valid cognizer is
generated.
Example, a syllogism is stated:
Sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of being a product.









Here product is stated as the sign to proof that sound is an impermanent
phenomena.
When you put forth a syllogism, you can either try to prove that:
- the subject is something or
- the subject has something such as some properties or some
characteristics; existential is or it being something or it having
something.
So, basically, you can establish that the subject is something or the
subject has something.
Example: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon. In this case,
you are trying to prove the subject is something.
Likewise, you can also try to establish that the subject is not something
or the subject does not have something.
Example: There is fire in the mountain pass because there is smoke.
Here you are trying to establish there is something.
There is fire. Fire exists in the mountain pass by the sign, smoke.
Here you are trying to establish that the subject is something or the
subject has something.
Similarly, on the basis of the subject, you are trying to establish that
the subject is not something or the subject does not have something.
Example:
The subject, the consciousness to which one moon appears as two moons, is a
wrong consciousness because it is a knower that engages its object
erroneously.
Here we are trying to establish that the sense consciousness to which one
moon appears as two moons is a wrong consciousness by the reason that of
that consciousness being a knower that engages its object erroneously.

Correct sign
The definition of a correct sign is that which is the three modes.
So if that which is the three modes then it is a correct sign. If that
which is not the three modes then it is not a correct sign.
What are the three modes? The three modes are three criteria that a correct
sign must satisfy.
These are its being:
   1. The property of the subject
   2. The forward pervasion
   3. The counter pervasion
Example: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of being
a product.
This is a correct sign.
So what is a correct sign? A correct sign is that which is the three modes.
So you have to know what the three modes are.
1. The property of the subject
[Handout page 4: The property of the subject is reckoned between the sign
and its basis of relation, the subject.]
First, literally, you need a subject that is faultless.
Property of the subject means that the sign/ reason that is stated must
exist on the subject.
Depending on the statement, if it is proving that the subject is something,
for the sign to be the property of the subject, then the subject is the
sign/ reason.
So in order for it to be a correct sign, the sign must exist on the subject
In the illustration: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon
because of being a product.
Subject - sound
Sign/ reason: product
If you are trying to prove that subject is something, then the subject is
the sign.
Here, for the sign to be the property of the subject, the subject [sound]
is the sign [product] i.e. sound is a product.
Therefore the sign fulfilled the criteria of being a property of the
subject.
That is the first criteria.
If sound happens to be not a product, then the property of the subject does
not exist.
If that is case, then it does not fulfill the criteria of being a correct
sign.
The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of being a product
In this case, sound is a faultless subject.
Sound can only be a faultless subject in relation to someone who has
already realized that sound is a product.
What that person has not realized is whether sound is an impermanent
phenomenon or not.
You are trying to proof to him that sound is an impermanent phenomenon by
the sign product.
So, in the first place, that person that you are trying to prove that sound
is an impermanent phenomenon must have already realized that sound is a
product.
If the person has already realized that sound is a product but is not sure
whether sound is impermanent phenomenon, then for this person, the sound
become a faultless subject.
The subject and the sign that is stated must be different
Illustration: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of
being a product
This is a correct sign because it is the three modes.
First, the property of the subject must be established.
Here, the property of the subject is established in this syllogism.
Why the property of the subject is established in this case?  This is
because:
1. There is a faultless subject. The person whom you are trying to prove
  that sound is an impermanent phenomenon has not realized that sound is an
  impermanent phenomenon.
2. That person has already realized that sound is product
3. The subject [sound] and sign [product] are different.
So these three things are satisfied.

2. Forward pervasion
[Handout page 4: The forward pervasion is a relationship between the sign
and the similar class. The similar class is the basis of relation of the
forward pervasion.]
If the sign is the predicate to be proven, that is the forward pervasion.
Example: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of being
a product.
Subject - sound
Sign/ reason - product
Predicate to be proven - impermanent phenomenon.
The sign is the predicate to be proven - product is an impermanent
phenomenon.
The forward pervasion is established because whatever is a product is
necessarily impermanent phenomenon.
One thing to check is whether the forward pervasion is established or not
is to see whether the sign is the predicate to be proven.
Example: The sense consciousness to which one moon appears as two moons is
a direct perceiver because of being a sense consciousness.
Here I am not saying this is a correct sign. It is a counterfeit sign.
Subject: sense consciousness to which one moon appears as two moons
Sign: sense consciousness
Predicate to be proven: a direct perceiver
Is the forward pervasion established?
Is the property of the subject is established?  Yes, the property of the
subject is established.
The sign [sense consciousness] is the subject [sense consciousness to which
one moon appears as two moons]. The sign exists with the subject.
The property of the subject is established because the sense consciousness
to which one moon appears as two moons is a sense consciousness.
In order for it to be a property of the subject there are three things we
need to look at.
 ➢ The criterion, sign exists with the subject, is fulfilled.
 ➢ The second criteria, the subject and the sign are different. In this
   case, these two are different.
 ➢ Is the subject, the sense consciousness to which one moon appears as
   two moons, a faultless subject?
   Yes, it is a faultless subject because there can be a person who has
   realized that the sense consciousness to which one moon appears as two
   moons is a sense consciousness but is not sure whether it is a direct
   perceiver or not.
So, for these reasons, in this syllogism, the property of the subject is
established.
In this case, although the property of the subject is established, it
doesn’t necessary mean that is a correct sign.
In order for it to be a correct sign, it must also satisfy the criteria of
being a forward pervasion.
In this case there is no forward pervasion because whatever is a sense
consciousness is not necessary a direct perceiver.

3. Counter Pervasion
[Handout page 6: The main requirement of the counter-pervasion is that the
sign must be universally absent in the dissimilar class.]
Example: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of being
a product.
Subject - sound
Sign/ reason - product
Predicate to be proven - impermanent phenomenon.
What is the opposite of impermanent phenomenon?
The opposite of [predicate to be proven] impermanent phenomena is non-
impermanent phenomenon (i.e. not impermanent phenomenon)
The opposite of [sign] product is non-product.
If it is a non-impermanent phenomenon, it is also a non-product.
So what is the counter pervasion? The opposite of the predicate to be
proven is the opposite of the sign.

How do we determine the forward pervasion is established?
In order for the sign to be a forward pervasion, the sign must exist
exclusively in the similar class.
The similar class is the predicate to be proven.
Example: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because of being
a product.
Subject - sound
Sign/ reason - product
Predicate to be proven - impermanent phenomenon.
So the product [sign] must exist exclusively with impermanent and that
which is concordant with impermanent phenomenon.
Here, similar class is impermanent phenomenon.
In order for it to be the forward pervasion the sign must exist exclusively
in this similar class; that means the product [sign] must exist exclusively
with impermanent phenomenon [similar class].
If the similar class is impermanent phenomenon, the dissimilar class is non-
impermanent phenomenon.
In order for the counter pervasion to be established, the sign must not
exist in the dissimilar class.
That means product [sign] cannot be a non-impermanent phenomenon
[dissimilar class].
In the syllogism: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon because
of being a product.
The forward pervasion is established because product exists necessary only
in the class of impermanent phenomenon.
The counter pervasion is also established because the product [sign] does
not exist whatsoever in non-impermanent phenomenon [the dissimilar class].
The product does not exist whatsoever within the category of non-
impermanent phenomenon.
The product is a non-existent in the dissimilar class.
In order for it to be a correct sign:
    - You must have already realized sound
    - You must know what an impermanent phenomenon is
    - You must know what a product is
On top of that you need to know that the subject is a sign; i.e. sound
[subject] is product [sign]
In order for it to be a correct sign for you:
    - You must have realized that product is necessarily impermanent

    - You must realized that if it is a non-impermanent phenomenon it is

So you must have ascertained these three.

When you state the correct sign, he understands immediately and realizes
