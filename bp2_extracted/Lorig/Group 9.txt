What is Awareness and Knowledge? This subject is the study of mind.
Why study this subject?  As Lati Rinpoche says:
  “Understanding mind is essential to understanding Buddhism in both its
  theoretical and practical aspects, for the process of achieving
  enlightenment is one of systematically purifying and enhancing the mind”.
There are four Buddhist tenet systems in ascending order:
   1. The Great Exposition School / Vaibhashika
   2. The Sutra School / Sauntrantika (divided into those following
      scripture and those following reasoning)
   3. The Mind-only School / Cittamatra
   4. The Middle Way School / Madhyamika (divided into the Autonomy Middle
      Way School and the Consequence Middle Way School)
[pic]
The study of Lorig is from the viewpoint of Proponents of Sutra Following
Reasoning.









All Buddhist tenet systems assert selflessness as the main principle of
their philosophies.
What is selfless? Selfless means is empty of or lacks a certain type of
self.
A fundamental assertion in Buddhism is that only through understanding
selflessness, or emptiness, can one gain liberation and thereby be relieved
of powerless rebirth.
In the broadest possible category, selfless may be divided into the
existent and non-existent.
So what is Existent and Non-existent?
-Non-existent:  Phenomenon that does not exist at all. It cannot be
realized by any valid cognizer. E.g. horns of a rabbit
-Existent: All existents are existent bases or established base (EB)
because they are established as existing by valid cognizers. So whatever
exists is realized as existing by a valid cognizer.  E.g. a table
So the difference between existent and non-existent is that an existent can
be realized by a valid cognizer.
Can uncompounded space be an object of the mode of apprehension of a non-
conceptual consciousness?
Since a valid cognizer can directly realize uncomposed space, then an
uncomposed space would be the object of the mode of apprehension of a non-
conceptual consciousness.
So, with no choice, you have to memorize those phenomena that are mutually
inclusive with EB.

Division of Object of Knowledge









Object of knowledge may be divided exhaustively into two:
   1. Singular phenomenon
   2. Different Phenomenon
Same meaning and same name - The only thing that is one with sound is
sound. All other existents are different from sound.
Same meaning but different name - product and impermanent phenomena are
mutually inclusive have same meaning but different name
Different names or different meanings - a gold pot and a copper pot which
are separate and distinct phenomena.

Impermanent phenomenon has three divisions:
   1. Matter
   2. Consciousness
   3. Non-associated compositional factor (NACF)











These three – matter, consciousness & NACF are mutually exclusive.
Note: Mutually exclusive means that you will not be able to point to any
object that is all three of these divisions.
Matter
What is matter? Matter is that which is atomically established.
Matter and form are mutually inclusive.
Matter or form comes into existence by depending on concordant cause. It is
established by former moment of form or matter
Matter is divided into:
   A. External Matter
   B. Internal Matter
On what basis they are different? The difference depends on whether that
matter is included within the continuum of the person or not.














Consciousness
What is consciousness? Consciousness is that:
 - which is clear and knowing
 - is non-obstructive, i.e., it does not have color and shape
 - is an impermanent phenomenon and is not matter
 - is established in dependence on concordant cause
What does “clear and knowing” means?
The factor of 'clarity' can be explained in several ways:
1) It has the entity of clarity, being clear, in the sense of not being
atomically established.
2) Any objects, when being focused upon, can appear clearly to it.
3) It has the nature of clear light, in the sense that afflictions have not
entered into its deepest recess. Therefore, it is by nature, not
afflictive.
Its factor of 'knowing' - realizing its object or that objects can appear
to it.
Consciousness, awareness, and knower are mutually inclusive

Consciousness has two divisions:
   A. Sense consciousnesses
   B. Mental consciousness


Consciousness can also be divided into
   A. Mind
   B. Mental Factor



How a consciousness is generated?
A consciousness is generated with the presence of three conditions:
1. the observed object condition
2. the uncommon empowering condition
3. the immediately preceding condition
Example: For an eye consciousness apprehending form to be generated,
1. the observed object condition           - form
2. the uncommon empowering condition   - eye sense power
3. the immediately preceding condition     - any of the six consciousnesses

Non-associated compositional factors
What is non-associated compositional factor?
It is a composed phenomenon which is neither matter nor consciousness
Non-associated compositional factor has two divisions:
A. Non-associated compositional factors which are persons (or more exactly,
all living beings)
B. Non-associated compositional factors which are not persons (e.g. time)
A person is a NACF because of being designated in dependence upon a
collection of form and consciousness

Object and object possessor
Object-possessor (or subject): a thing that possesses its respective object
Object: that which is known by an awareness. Generally, if it is a
phenomenon or an existent, it is necessarily an object.
   1. All consciousnesses are object possessor e.g. eye consciousness
   2. All other phenomena that are not consciousnesses are object e.g. book
There are three main types of object posited for consciousness
   1. Object of engagement (jug.yul)
   2. Determined object (zhen.yul)
   3. Appearing object (nang.yul)

Terminologies for other object
Apprehended object (zung.yul) is what is actually apprehended by the
consciousness. It is mutually inclusive with appearing object
E.g. An eye consciousness is perceiving a book , the book is the
apprehended object.
Object of the mode of apprehension (dzin.tang.kyi.yul): the object that is
understood by the cognition
Object of observation (migs.yul): the object that is observed.
Example of conceptual consciousness: Thought thinking about rose
   Object of engagement            = rose
   Determined object               = rose
   Object of the mode of apprehension = rose
   Appearing object         = mental image of rose
   Apprehended object  = mental image of rose
Mental image of rose is also called meaning generality and is a permanent
phenomenon that does not disintegrate moment by moment. It is a negative
phenomenon.
Object of engagement & determined object of thought are the actual object
the consciousness understand = rose



Example of direct perceiver: Eye consciousness apprehending rose:
Object of engagement              = rose
Object of the mode of apprehension      = rose
Appearing object                  = rose
Apprehended object                = rose
The object ‘rose’ appears to the direct perceiver and is what it realizes.

From the above example, you can see that:
For direct perceivers:
– appearing object, apprehended object, and functioning thing are mutually
inclusive
– object of engagement and object of the mode of apprehension are mutually
inclusive

For conceptual consciousnesses:
– appearing object, apprehended object, and permanent phenomenon are
mutually inclusive
– determined object, object of engagement, and the object of the mode of
apprehension are mutually inclusive

For the eye consciousness apprehending rose is a direct perceiver
 - Rose is the object of observation
 - Appearing object & apprehended object (that are mutually inclusive) is
   rose which is a functioning thing
 - Object of engagement & object of the mode of apprehension (which are
   mutually inclusive) is rose
For thought thinking about rose is a conceptual consciousness:
 - Rose is the object of observation
 - Appearing object & apprehended object (that are mutually inclusive) is
   mental image of rose which is permanent
 - Determined object, object of engagement, & the object of the mode of
   apprehension (that are mutually inclusive) is rose

*1: The sound and meaning generalities which a determinative knower
apprehends as suitable to be mixed can be explained in two ways.
1st explanation:
➢ Sound generality – if someone describes an object that you have never
  seen, such as an ocean, the image that appears to your mind based on the
  description is a sound generality.
➢ Meaning generality - When you see an ocean for the first time, without
  having been told beforehand what it is, the image appearing to your mind
  is just a meaning generality.
➢ Later when someone tells you, ‘This is an ocean’, the sound and meaning
  generalities then appear to your mind as if associated – that is to say
  mixed.
2nd explanation
➢ Sound generality – if someone says the word ‘ocean’ to you without your
  knowing its meaning, the image generated at that time is a sound
  generality, a mere reverberation of the sound ‘ocean’.”
➢ Meaning generality –“when someone says the word ‘ocean’ and you know its
  meaning, the image that appears to your mind is a meaning generality.
  Although not the actual object, the image appears to be that object.
The reason that the definition specifies that sound and meaning
generalities are apprehended as suitable to be mixed is that it is possible
to have a thought consciousness apprehending only a sound generality, one
apprehending only a meaning generality, or one apprehending the two as if
mixed

Example: Conceptual consciousness apprehending blue
-Does blue appear to the conceptual consciousness apprehending blue? Yes it
appears but blue is not its appearing object.
-Why is blue not the appearing object of a conceptual consciousness
apprehending blue although blue appears to it? To a conceptual
consciousness apprehending blue, the actual object blue does not appear
with all its complete characteristics to the conceptual consciousness
apprehending blue. This is because SCP can only cast an aspect of itself
fully with all its characteristics only to a direct perceiver, not to a
conceptual consciousness.
-Why do we say blue appears to that conceptual consciousness apprehending
blue? Because the conceptual consciousness apprehending blue explicitly
realizes blue. The conceptual consciousness apprehending blue can only
explicitly realize blue through blue appearing to it

Threefold divisions of Awareness
   1. Person
   2. Awareness

A person is a being imputed in dependence upon any of its five aggregates.
Self, I, person, being are mutually inclusive
Illustration: A being who possesses a basis of one of the three realms.
Why is a person not a being imputed in dependence upon the five aggregates?
For beings of the formless realm, you cannot posit a form aggregate because
in the formless realms, there is no form. Therefore a person of a formless
realm can only be a being that is imputed in dependence upon the remaining
four aggregates.

Awareness can be divided into
   1) Valid cognizer
   2) Non-valid awareness.
A valid cognizer is a new incontrovertible knower
New refers to only the first moment of a continuum of consciousness
apprehending an object.  Eliminates that subsequent cognizers are valid
cognizers.
Incontrovertible means knows the object as it is.
Eliminates that correctly assuming consciousnesses are valid cognizers.
Knower eliminates that physical sense powers are valid cognizers

There are two valid cognizer:
   1. Direct valid cognizer
   2. Inferential valid cognizer
Free of conceptuality means it is necessarily a non-conceptual
consciousness. Such consciousness deals with its object directly without
making use of an internal image.
Determinative knower means a cognition that is conceptual in nature
Non-mistaken means that there is no erroneous element involved in that
which is appearing to the consciousness.

We need to know the consciousnesses whether they are:
- valid cognizers or non-valid awarenesses,
- knowers who realize their object or knowers that do not realize their
  objects
- factually concordant consciousnesses or factually discordant
  consciousnesses
- conceptual or non-conceptual consciousnesses
- mistaken or non-mistaken consciousness
Valid cognizer is a new incontrovertible knower
Non-valid awareness is a not new and incontrovertible knower
Knower that realizes its object is necessarily an awareness that knows and
understands its object
Knower that do not realizes its object is an awareness that do not knows
and understands its object
Factually concordant consciousnesses is a knower that accords with the fact
(a consciousness that apprehends the object in the way the object exists in
reality)
Factually discordant consciousness is a knower that does not accord with
the fact such as apprehending non-existent phenomena. (a consciousness that
apprehends the object in the way the object does not exists in reality)

Difference between Buddhist and non-Buddhist on term and conceptual
consciousness
The term (or expressive sound) and conceptual consciousnesses are:
    - eliminative engagers for Buddhist
    - collective engagers for some non-Buddhist
Accordingly to Buddhist epistemology and the texts on valid cognition, what
allows you to understand something that is similar to what you have seen or
known earlier is due to the generality existing on all instances of that
object; such as innately understand the copper flat-based bulbous thing as
vase after having seen a golden vase.

Appearance of opposite from non-vase appear to a conceptual consciousness
apprehending a vase
The two factors appear to a conceptual consciousness:
   1. the factor of 'opposite from non-vase' existing on a golden vase
   2. the factor of 'opposite from non-vase' existing on a copper vase
Both appear as if they were the same and you see them as vase.
• To a conceptual consciousness apprehending a vase, there is the
  appearance of ‘opposite from non-vase’. This appearance of ‘opposite from
  non-vase’ appears as if it is the actual vase but it is not a vase.
• This is because, to a conceptual consciousness apprehending vase, the
  appearance as 'opposite from non-vase' is necessarily a permanent
  phenomenon. It is permanent because it is an imputed factor.
• However, to a conceptual consciousness apprehending vase, whatever is an
  appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase' is not necessarily permanent. Why?
• To the conceptual consciousness apprehending vase, there is the
  appearance of ‘opposite from non-vase’ which has two parts:
1. An appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase' that is a SCP, which is the
  vase itself
2. An appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase' that is a GCP that is an
  imputed factor, which is the meaning generality of vase.
How does a vase appear to a conceptual consciousness apprehending a vase?
The way ‘vase’ appears to a conceptual consciousness apprehending vase is
that:
  The meaning generality of vase [which is the appearance as ‘opposite from
  non-vase' that is GCP and is necessarily permanent] to be one mixed with
  'vase' [which is an appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase' that is a
  SCP].

A conceptual consciousness is a mistaken consciousness. Why?
The conceptual consciousness apprehending 'vase' is a mistaken
consciousness because the appearance of ‘opposite from non-vase’ appears as
the actual vase but it is NOT the actual vase.
That is to say that conceptual consciousness is mistaken with respect to:
  The appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase’ that is a meaning generality
  of vase from the appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase’ that is a
  specifically characterized vase.
To this conception, although the appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase'
[that is a meaning generality] appears as the actual vase, but it does not
believe in this appearance of ‘opposite from non-vase’ as the actual vase.
It knows that this is not the actual vase.

Object of engagement, the object of mode of apprehension, and the
determined object for the conceptual consciousness
What does the conceptual consciousness apprehending 'vase' realizes?
It realizes 'vase', it does not realize 'vase generality'.
This is because, what this 'conceptual consciousness apprehending vase' is
getting at is 'vase' itself.
Therefore, the object of engagement, the object of mode of apprehension,
and the determined object is vase.

How does a conceptual consciousness apprehending a vase?
Conceptual consciousness can only apprehend vase by way of the appearance
of ‘opposite from non-vase’. This is because to a conceptual consciousness
apprehending a vase:
   1. the 'actual vase' appears as one mixed with the appearance as
      ‘opposite from non-vase'.
   2. the appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase' appears as if it was the
      actual vase
Therefore, the appearing object (apprehended object) of this conceptual
consciousness apprehending 'vase' is the meaning generality of vase, the
appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase’ to be the actual vase.

A conceptual consciousness apprehending vase is a mistaken consciousness,
because the appearance as ‘opposite from non-vase' appears as 'vase'
Likewise, for an eye consciousness apprehending a reflection of your face
in the mirror, this reflection of your face appears to this eye
consciousness as if it is 'your actual face' but it is not your face.
Even though this reflection of your face in the mirror is not 'your actual
face', nevertheless it appears as if it is your actual face, and you would
not normally believe that reflection of your face in the mirror is your
actual face.
Every thought consciousness is mistaken with respect to its appearing
object.
What is the appearing object of a conceptual consciousness apprehending
vase?
The appearing object is the appearance ‘appearing as vase’. While the
appearance ‘appearing as vase’ is not the actual vase, it appears as the
actual vase.
The conceptual consciousness apprehending vase is not able to realize its
appearing object (the appearance ‘appearing as vase’) is not the actual
vase.
Therefore the conceptual consciousness is mistaken with regards to its
appearing object.
What is the appearing object of a conceptual consciousness apprehending
vase?
The appearing object is the meaning generality (or mental image) of vase.
While the meaning generality of vase is not vase, it appears as vase.
That being the case, it is mistaken with regard to its appearing object

A meaning generality:
- is necessarily a permanent phenomenon in that it does not disintegrate
moment by moment
- is an image which, although it appears to be the actual object being
understood, merely serves as an elimination of what is not that object.

Generally characterized phenomenon are phenomena which are merely imputed
by a term or thought consciousness and are not established as a SCP.
Permanent phenomenon is necessarily GCP because their characters are known
only in a general way by an imputing thought consciousness. Since they
depend on such imputation, they are not established from their own side.
There is no way to realize a permanent or GCP by way of its own specific
entity.

Sevenfold Consciousness on Eye Consciousness Apprehending a Shadow
For an eye consciousness apprehending a shadow, it is valid with respect to
its appearing object, the shadow. This is because shadow exists in the way
it appears. This eye consciousness apprehending shadow realizes and knows
that it is a shadow and is a direct valid cognizer.
At this time the eye consciousness only understand it is a shadow but does
not understand it is the shadow of a bird.
It is valid with respect to the shadow that is appearing to it but this
consciousness is not a valid cognizer with respect to the, for example, the
shadow of the bird.

At the same time, while the eye consciousness apprehending the shadow is
operating, a thought wondering about the nature of the shadow; “Is it a
shadow of a leaf or of a bird?”
This is two-pointed doubting consciousness that is unsure of exactly what
kind of shadow it is. At this moment it has not realized the shadow is the
shadow of the bird; or else the consciousness would not have any doubt.
There are two different consciousnesses operating at the same time when the
eye consciousness sees the shadow and also wondering the kind of shadow:
➢ Valid mind - eye consciousness apprehending the shadow as it is
➢ Non-valid mind - doubt wondering what kind of shadow it is

After some thinking, you arrived at the conclusion; “That is definitely the
shadow of a bird because its shape looks like a shadow of a bird. Therefore
it must be the shadow of a bird.”
The doubt you had earlier is now transformed into a correct assumption
(correctly assuming consciousness). In reality it is true that it is a
shadow of a bird and you have concluded correctly that it is a shadow of a
bird. Nevertheless this conclusion is a mere assumption as it has not yet
realized it is a shadow of the bird. So it is not a valid cognition of this
object being the shadow of a bird.

You can make a correct assumption that accords with reality and you can
also make assumptions with various incorrect reasons. A correctly assuming
consciousness is a mind that arrives at a decisive conclusion that accords
with the reality of that object but that conclusion may not necessarily
reached with a correct reason. Therefore, a correctly assuming
consciousness is a factually concordant determinative knower that is
controvertible with regard to determining its object.

It is possible for the correctly assuming consciousness to be transformed
into a valid cognizer. Subsequently when you look up, you can see a bird
and there is the shadow of that bird on the ground. Based on this, you are
now very sure that the shadow is the shadow of the bird. Your eye
consciousness is apprehending the shadow of a bird and, in your mind you
know that it is the shadow of a bird.

After you have generated the valid cognition realizing that the shadow is
the shadow of a bird, from the second moment of that valid cognition
onwards, when you remember the shadow of the bird, the second moment of
that valid cognition is a subsequent cognizer - a knower that realizes what
has already been realized.

Another scenario is that, while it is a shadow of a bird, you apprehend it
to be a shadow of a leaf. The mind that apprehends the shadow of a leaf is
a wrong consciousness because the principal object of that mind is the
shadow of a leaf, which makes this consciousness engage its object
erroneously.

Presentation of Mind and the Twelve Links of Dependent Arising
In the twelve links of dependent-arising, the four projected results are
the following four links:
   1. name and form
   2. six sources
   3. contact
   4. feeling
-The link of name and form refer to the five contaminated aggregates that
are projected by karma and the afflictions.
The five contaminated appropriated aggregates are:
   1. Aggregate of form
   2. Aggregate of feeling
   3. Aggregate of discrimination
   4. Aggregate of compositional factor
   5. Aggregate of consciousness
-The link of the six sources refer to the time when the six sense powers
are established up to but before the generation of the respective
consciousnesses that depend on them.
-The link of contact refers to the moment when the following three
conditions meet:
   1. The object
   2. The sense power
   3. The consciousness
-The link of feeling arise upon the meeting of the three conditions.
Feeling is the result that arises from contact, without which, the link of
feeling cannot arise.
-Just as how the link of contact is posited to come about, example, to
generate an eye consciousness apprehending vase, the three conditions must
come together:
   1. The vase
   2. The eye sense power
   3. The consciousness
-Earlier, we have studied that the production of a sense consciousness is
due to the three conditions.
Example: An eye consciousness apprehending vase is produced in dependence
on the three conditions:
   1. The observed object condition:          the vase
   2. The uncommon empowering condition:      the eye sense power
   3. The immediately preceding condition:    a consciousness
-You can posit a cause and effect relationship here. In dependence on the
causes, the three conditions, leads to the effect, an eye consciousness
apprehending vase is produced.
  - the vase can be posited as a cause of the eye consciousness
  apprehending vase
  - the consciousness apprehending vase is the result of its observed
  object condition, the vase
-With the exception of the Vaibhashika (GES), Sutra School and the tenets
above it accept that cause and effect are sequential, i.e. cause and effect
cannot occur simultaneously.
-As cause and effect are sequential, so cause has to precede effect. The
vase is the cause of the eye consciousness apprehending vase, so the vase
has to precede the eye consciousness apprehending vase.
- So in dependence on the vase as its observed object condition, the eye
consciousness apprehending vase is generated into the aspect of vase.
-The GES actually assert that the eye consciousness apprehending vase
functions as if like a hand holding the vase. For them, both physical sense
power (the eye sense power) and consciousness (eye consciousness) do know
its object and realize its object. But these are not the assertions of the
Sutra School and the tenets above it.
-Based on our ordinary experience, when we see something, we don’t feel
that there is sequence of events. When we look at a vase, we feel that the
eye consciousness apprehending vase and the vase seem to exist
simultaneously, but in reality, this is not the case.
-This is not what the Sutra School and the tenets above it assert because
there has to be a sequence to cause and effect.  This being the case, cause
and effect cannot be simultaneous.
Khen Rinpoche positing a thesis
-Positing a thesis that relates the generation of the eye consciousness
apprehending vase to the link of contact in the twelve links of dependent-
arising
-An eye consciousness apprehending vase is generated when the three
conditions meet:
   1. The object       - vase
   2. The sense power  - eye sense power
   3. The consciousness      - consciousness
-The eye consciousness apprehending vase is produced after the gathering of
the three conditions.
-But there is a problem of saying that only after the gathering of the
three conditions then there is the production of the eye consciousness
apprehending vase.
-When this eye consciousness apprehending vase is produced, the vase that
existed earlier through meeting with the eye sense power and consciousness,
would have ceased to exist at this moment, since the vase is a momentary
phenomenon.
-How then are you going to explain the production of an eye consciousness
apprehending vase when the vase has already ceased to exist?
-Sutra School and the tenets above it assert that consciousnesses are
aspected, but the GES asserts that consciousnesses are non-aspected.
• At the moment when the three conditions (vase, eye sense power, and
  consciousness) meet, the consciousness that is in the entity of clear and
  knowing is generated into the aspect of vase. This consciousness has the
  aspect of the apprehended.
• At this time, there is no eye consciousness apprehending vase because
  cause has to precede effect.
• In the next moment, this consciousness then transforms into the entity of
  the eye consciousness apprehending vase. Only then does the eye
  consciousness realize vase.
Analogy: Is like placing a vase in front of a mirror. The vase is reflected
in the mirror. Likewise, when you bring the vase to the attention of the
mind, the vase is reflected on the mind; that means the consciousness is
generated into the aspect of the vase.
-By explaining in this way, then there is no problem in saying that when
the eye consciousness apprehending vase is generated, the vase (the
observed object condition) has already ceased to exist because cause
precedes effect.
-At the time when the three things come together, the consciousness is
generated into the aspect of vase. In the next moment, this then transforms
into the entity of the eye consciousness apprehending vase.
- So using the example of a vase and the eye consciousness apprehending
vase, it is asserted that something comes between these two things.  That
something is asserted to be the aspect.
-The link of contact is posited to be the moment of the gathering of the
three things.
-Only after that comes the link of feeling.

How are these five sense direct perceivers differentiated?
All five are produced in dependence upon a physical sense power that is
their uncommon empowering condition but the specific physical sense powers
for these five types of direct perceivers are different.
• Example, a sense direct perceiver apprehending form is a sense direct

  sense power which is the eye sense power.
• The observed object condition is a visible form.
• Sense direct perceiver has to be a knower that is free from conceptuality
  and is non-mistaken.
When a consciousness has all these characteristics, it is called a sense
direct perceiver apprehending form.
Why are there five sense direct perceivers?
(1) They have different observed object conditions.
(2) Although all their uncommon empowering conditions are physical sense
powers, but are different for the respective sense consciousness.
-When a direct perceiver engages its object, e.g., an eye consciousness
apprehending vase, it engages the object in its totality. When we say that
a collective engager engages its object collectively or in totality, it
means everything about the vase appears to the eye consciousness
apprehending it which includes the impermanence of the vase.

Mental Direct Perceiver
Definition of a mental direct perceiver is:
1) that which is generated in dependence on a mental sense power which is
its own uncommon empowering condition and
2) a consciousness that is an other-knower which is free from conceptuality
and non-mistaken.
-The sense power of the mental consciousness is not physical and thus has
no shape.
-A former moment of any of the six consciousnesses acts as the sense power
of a mental consciousness. Example: when one pays attention to a color, an
eye consciousness acts like a sense power in that it gives the mental
consciousness the ability to perceive a visible object.
What determines a mental direct perceiver?
A mental direct perceiver possesses the following four characteristics:
• It is produced in dependence on its own uncommon empowering condition, a
  mental sense power.
• It is free from conceptuality, i.e., it is non-conceptual.
• It is non-mistaken.
• It is a consciousness that is an other-knower.

Why ‘other-knower’ is specified in the definition of a mental direct
perceiver?
In order to show that the self-knower experiencing a mental consciousness
is a self-knower and not the mental consciousness, “other-knower” is
included in the definition of a mental direct perceiver.
An other-knower is a consciousness that engages an object other than an
internal consciousness. It is a consciousness directed outward.

-Mental direct perceiver has three divisions:
1. Valid cognizers that are mental direct perceivers
2. Subsequent cognizers that are mental direct perceivers
3. AAAs that are mental direct perceivers
Therefore, there are no mental direct perceivers that are: inferential
valid cognizers, correctly assuming consciousnesses, doubting
consciousnesses & wrong consciousnesses.

Self-knowing direct perceiver
Definition of self-knower is: that which has the aspect of an apprehender,
is free from conceptuality, and is non-mistaken.

The Buddhist tenets that assert self-knowers
-Tenet schools that assert self-knower: SS, MOS, AMWS
-Tenet schools that do not assert self-knower: GES, CMWS














-Why do some tenets assert self-knowers?
-The reason for those who assert self-knowers is to account for memory.
Memory explains why we can remember things.
-Example: An eye consciousness apprehending the object blue. Later one is
able to remember, “I saw blue.” The reason why one is able to remember
having seen blue in the past is because there was a valid cognizer that
realized blue. One also remembers the mind that saw blue.
That memory conception consists of the memory of remembering:
 (1) the object, blue, and
 (2) the subject, the consciousness that experienced blue.
Therefore, when one thinks, “I remember seeing blue,” one remembers both
the object and the subject.
-All the tenets including CMWS assert a memory.
-The proponents of SS, MOS, and AMWS assert that there must be a self-
knower, an experiencer of the eye consciousness apprehending blue at the
time when the eye consciousness was apprehending blue.
-Without this self-knower that experienced the eye consciousness
apprehending blue at that time, you would not be able to remember the eye
consciousness that saw blue earlier.
-The self-knower experiencing the eye consciousness apprehending blue is of
one collection with the eye consciousness apprehending blue.
-The self-knower that experiences the eye consciousness apprehending blue
has the factor of clarity and knowing.
A self-knower is:
 • that which has the aspect of an apprehender
 • is an experiencer of a consciousness
 • is of one collection with the consciousness that it experiences
 • is a consciousness that is directed inwards and not directed outwards
 • is neither a main mind nor a mental factor
-How does a self-knower experience the consciousness that comes along with
it?
Self-knower experiences that consciousness non-dualistically, i.e., through
the subsidence of duality.
When a self-knower experiences the consciousness that is of one collection
with it, it does not experience the consciousness with any sense of duality
such as the experiencer is here and the experience is there. So there is no
duality.
-What verifies the existence of a valid cognizer?
 • What verifies an existent? A valid cognizer verifies an existent. So a
   valid cognizer has to exist.
 • Then what verifies the existence of a valid cognizer? It is the self-
   knower, the mind that exists in one collection with the valid cognizer
   that it is experiencing.
 • The self-knower that verifies the existence of a valid cognizer cannot
   be another valid cognizer that is of a different collection from the
   valid cognizer that it is verifying.
 • It would be a problem if you were to posit a separate valid cognizer
   verifying the existence of a valid cognizer. This is because, if the
   'verifying' self-knower is of a separate collection from the valid
   cognizer it is experiencing and verifying, then you would need another
   self-knower to verify this valid cognizer that verifies the self-knower
   that verifying the first valid cognizer. One would then incur the
   fallacy of infinite regression.





-Although self-knowers do not exist in reality, nevertheless, those
asserting self-knowers maintain that one of the essential reasons to assert
self-knowers is to verify that valid cognizers do exist; which verifies the
existence of phenomenon.

-The appearing object of this inferential cognizer that realizes sound to
be impermanent is the appearance of impermanent sound or the appearance of
opposite from non-impermanent sound.
-These two appearances, while they are not impermanent sound, appear as
impermanent sound. For this reason, it is mistaken.
Using iPhone as an example
An inferential cognizer realizing an iPhone is a valid cognizer. It is a
mistaken consciousness.
This inferential cognizer realizing an iPhone is mistaken with regard to
its appearing object.
The appearing object of the inferential cognizer realizing an iPhone is:
    ➢ the appearance of an iPhone
    ➢ appearance of opposite of non-iPhone
The inferential cognizer realizing an iPhone is mistaken with regard to its
appearing object because the appearance of opposite of non-iPhone appears
to be the actual iPhone although it is not the actual iPhone. Therefore it
is mistaken.

Elements of a Syllogism
-When stating a correct sign:
    ➢ First there must first be a subject that you are trying to analyze
    ➢ There must be a predicate to be proven
    ➢ Then comes the correct sign.
-When stating a reason for proving something, that sign/ reason could be
correct or incorrect, so it is important to identify what constitutes a
correct sign for the inferential valid cognizer to be generated.
[pic]

-Here product is stated as the sign/reason to prove that sound is an
impermanent phenomena.
When you put forth a syllogism, you can either try to prove that:
- the subject is something or
- the subject has something (such as some properties or characteristics)
Example: The subject, sound, is an impermanent phenomenon, because of being
a product.
In this case, you are trying to prove that the subject is something i.e. an
impermanent phenomenon
Example: There is a fire in the mountain pass because there is smoke.
Here you are trying to establish the subject is something; there is fire in
the mountain pass because of the sign/reason, smoke.
Likewise, you can also try to establish that the subject is not something
or the subject does not have something.
Example: Someone sees a cow with horns but does not know that it is a cow,
then wonders: “Is that a horse?”
You then advices him: “That is not a horse, because it has horns”. [A horse
does not have horns]
Example: The subject, the consciousness to which one moon appears as two
moons, is a wrong consciousness because it is a knower that engages its
object erroneously.
Here we are trying to establish that the sense consciousness to which one
moon appears as two moons is a wrong consciousness by the reason that of
that consciousness being a knower that engages its object erroneously.

Whatever a subsequent cognizer is realizing is induced through the force of

Why 'by its own power' is inserted in the definition?
There is a main mind that is concomitant with the mental factor of doubt.
In the retinue of this main mind, there are also other mental factors like
feeling, discrimination and so on. This main mind and its mental factors in
its retinue are also knowers that have qualms two pointedly. Although doubt
is concomitant with them, they do not have qualms two pointedly by their
own power, but arise through the power of doubt (which does so by its own
power).
Doubting consciousness has three divisions:
1. Doubt tending toward the factual
2. Doubt tending toward the non-factual
3. Equal doubt

Illustrations:
Doubt tending toward the factual is doubt which thinks that sound is
probably impermanent. In reality, sound is impermanent. This mind has doubt
think: “Sound is probably impermanent.” Although, a doubt, it is factual as
it concordant with how the object sound exist.
Doubt tending toward the non-factual is doubt which thinks that sound is
probably permanent. In reality sound is not permanent.
Is there a common locus between doubt and wrong consciousness?
According to Sera-Je's position, there is a common locus between doubt and
wrong consciousness such as a mind thinking: “reincarnation probably does
not exist.”
Such a mind is doubt tending toward the non-factual, and is also a wrong
consciousness. This knower engages in its object 'reincarnation'
erroneously.
Some assert that wrong consciousness and doubt are mutually exclusive
because, a wrong consciousness is decisive about its object, whereas doubt
is two-pointed about its object.

- implicitly realizes the selflessness of persons.

Summarized from Debate in Tibetan Buddhism
The boundaries of pervasion or extension of a phenomenon is its range –
what it pervades, what it includes, and what it excludes. By understanding
clearly a phenomenon’s boundaries of pervasion one is able to ascertain the
scope of that phenomenon. The essential tool in this investigation is the
analytical comparison of phenomenon. By comparing two phenomena and
establishing their relative boundaries of pervasion, the limits of each
phenomenon in relation to the other, one comes to understand the points of
similarity and dissimilarity between them. The comparison of phenomenon
presupposes that those phenomena are different in the sense that they are
not exactly the same from the point of view of name and meaning.
Phenomena which are not different are one. A pot is the only thing which is
ONE with the pot in name and meaning.
The investigation of differences does not address the cases of phenomena
which are exactly the same. There is no difference between a pot and a pot.
Every existent other than a pot is different from it, e.g. a person, a gold
pot.
Any two different phenomena must compare in one of the five ways; there is
no sixth possible way.

1st case (represented by two circles combined into one –one is superimposed
over the other)
The relative boundaries of pervasion or extensions of the two phenomena are
absolutely concomitant. They pervade exactly the same things.
Whatever is a p is necessarily a q.
Whatever is a q is necessarily a p.

2nd and 3rd cases (represented by a smaller circle wholly included within
the perimeter of a larger one)
There is something (inside the smaller circle) which is both a p and a q.
There is something (inside the larger circle but outside the smaller
circle) which is one but not the other. The larger circle includes at least
one phenomenon the smaller does not)
There is a uni-directional pervasion between these two:
Case 2: Whatever is a p is necessarily a q, but whatever is a q is not
necessarily a p
Case 3: Whatever is a q is necessarily a p, but whatever is a p is not
necessarily a q









4th case (represented by two overlapping circles)
There is an intersection of the relative extensions of the two phenomena,
but there is no pervasion between the two.
1. There is something which is both a p and a q (in the overlapping area).
2. There is something which is a p but not a q.
3. There is something which is a q but not a p.

5th case (represented by two separate circles)
The relative extensions of the two phenomena do not correlate at all. There
is neither any pervasion between the two nor is there any overlapping at
all.
There is nothing which is both a p and a q.
Whatever is a p is necessarily not a q.
Whatever is a q is necessarily not a p

Mutually Inclusive Phenomena
Mutually inclusive phenomena are represented by Case 1.
Things which are mutually inclusive are:
    - Being different phenomena and
    - Have all the 8 approaches of pervasion. (see note below)
Mutually inclusive phenomena are different only in the sense of not having
exactly the same name, for their meanings –the objects which are included
within the extension of each- are exactly the same.
E.g. Product and impermanent phenomenon are mutually inclusive, and there
is a common locus of these two. For example, a pot is such a common locus.
A pot is a product and it is also an impermanent phenomenon.

Three Possibilities
If there are three possibilities between two phenomena,
- There must be a common locus of the two and
- There are two points of difference, at least one of which is something
which is one of the two phenomena but not the other.

Phenomena having three possibilities compare in two ways:
The first way represents the three possibilities (Case 2 & case 3)
   1. something which is both,
   2. something which is one but not the other, and
   3. something which is neither

Mutually exclusive phenomena are those which are different and a common

Example: Definition and definiendum
There is nothing that is both definition and definiendum.
Whatever is a definition is necessarily not a definiendum
Whatever is a definiendum is necessarily not a definition

The 8 Approaches of Pervasion that exist between P & Q are:
   1. Whatever is P is necessarily Q
   2. Whatever is Q is necessarily P
   3. Whatever is not P is necessarily not Q
   4. Whatever is not Q is necessarily not P
   5. If P exists, then Q necessarily exists
   6. If Q exists, then P necessarily exists
   7. If P does not exist, then Q necessarily does not exist
   8. If Q does not exist, then P necessarily does not exist.
