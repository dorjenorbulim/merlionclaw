Khen Rinpoche: In the twofold division of object and object possessor, the
object possessor is necessarily consciousness.
Within this twofold division of object and object possessor, for person and
expressive sound belong to the object.
If you are asked: In general, aren’t person and expressive sound object
possessor?
Yes, in general, person and expressive sound are object possessor.
The topic that we are looking at in this module, the presentation of mind,
is a challenging topic, because we are talking about the mind. Even just to
have an idea of the presentation of the mind is difficult. In general, this
is not something that any normal person will pay attention to. So this is
kind of a new subject for most people. You have to understand that whenever
we look at the presentation of mind, this subject is not an easy one. It is
not something that can be explained by using simple words. If you are
expecting this subject can be explained by using simple words and then
thinking you can get it by hearing just the one or two simple words, please
understand that is impossible. As I have mentioned previously, all the
various topics are closely related to what we are learning here. The
presentation of the mind is closely related to the topic of the next module
Tenets, a Buddhist philosophy. In fact, whatever subjects you can name, be
it the Stages of the Path to Enlightenment, or Engaging in the Bodhisattva
Deeds, all these topics are interlinked. So it is extremely important to
understand what it means by saying we realize something directly. It is
also extremely important to understand how a conceptual consciousness
realizes its object. This is an extremely important topic and it is
something we have to continue to learn, to think about and to discuss.
We have talked about specifically characterized phenomenon. Why is it
called a specifically characterized phenomenon? A specifically
characterized phenomenon refers to any object which is specifically
characterized phenomenon as having the potential from its own side to cast
an aspect of its uncommon nature to the consciousness perceiving it. The
power of the word ‘specifically characterized’ is that it has the potential
from its own side to cast an aspect of its uncommon nature to the
consciousness that perceives it.
On the other hand, generally characterized phenomenon is not like that.
Generally characterized phenomenon does not have the potential to cast an
aspect of its uncommon nature from its own side to the consciousness
perceiving it, but rather it is something whose nature is completed by the
mind imputing it. The mind has to impute.
So here you can see that there is a difference between specifically
characterized phenomenon and generally characterized phenomenon.
Definition:
Specifically characterized phenomenon: A phenomena which is established by
way of its own character without being merely imputed by a term or thought
consciousness
Generally characterized phenomenon: A phenomena which is merely imputed by
a term or thought consciousness and is not established as a specifically
characterized phenomenon
During the time when you are looking at blue, blue is appearing very
clearly to a consciousness.
Then close your eye and think of blue. What comes to the mind?
When you close your eyes and thinking of blue, the blue that appears to the
mind thinking about it is not as clear as the blue that appears to the eye
consciousness that directly perceiving it.
We will talk about this later. This topic is about trying to see the
difference between conceptual and non-conceptual consciousness. It very
important but is very challenging because it is something that you have to
take a lot of time thinking about it to get clarity.
A conceptual consciousness can only get at its object via the medium of a
meaning generality or mental image. A conceptual consciousness cannot
perceive its object nakedly as it is; i.e. it cannot do it directly. It has
to do it through a medium of a mental image; unlike direct perception.
When you close your eye and think about blue, there has to be an appearance
of blue before we conceive blue.
So when we conceive blue, the appearance of blue has to arise before the
thought is able to engage blue.
This appearance as blue to the thought consciousness (or conceptual
consciousness) apprehending blue consists of two parts: one part of it is
blue and another part of it is non-blue.
Handout Page 1: When object possessors are divided, there are three:
person, awareness and expressive sound
Person, awareness and expressive sound are all object possessor because
they possess their respective objects.
The definition of person is: a being imputed in dependence upon any of its
five aggregates.
Illustration of a person: A being who possesses a basis of one of the three
realms.
The definition of a person is a being imputed in dependence upon any of its
five aggregates; it doesn’t say in dependence upon all five aggregates.
A person is not necessarily a being imputed in dependence upon the five
aggregates. Therefore, the definition of a person is a being imputed in
dependence upon any of its five aggregates. That is the reason for saying
“any.”
Question: Why a person is not a being imputed in dependence upon the five
aggregates?
By looking at the given illustration, one should be able to understand.
The illustration of a person is a being who possesses a basis of one of the
three realms. The three realms are the desire realms, form and formless
realms.
For beings of the formless realm, you cannot posit a form aggregate because
in the formless realms there is no form. Therefore, a person of a formless
realm can only be a being that is imputed in dependence upon the remaining
four aggregates.

Khen Rinpoche: Here the definition of a person is a being imputed in
dependence upon any of its five aggregates. This is how all the Buddhist
tenets would identify a person. To them a person is a being imputed in
dependence upon any of its five aggregates.  If you were to look at the
assertion of the respective tenets as to what the illustration of a person
is, there are different illustrations being posited by the different
tenets. Many of them assert that the illustration of a person is
consciousness because vast majority of the tenets believe that the imputed
object when sought after can be found. A lot of them give the consciousness
as the illustration of a person, which means to say that a person is
consciousness. If that is the case, a person cannot be a non-associated
compositional factor. Then how should we look at the definition of a person
that is being given here? You could say that this is the general procedure
of all the tenets. One can also look at it as being the actual way in which
a person exists. For those tenets who posit the illustration of a person to
be consciousness, then you will have to say a person is consciousness.
This will be covered in more detail in the future. There is no way to
understand this right now because if you were to think about what a person
is, it is very profound. In order to understand what a person is then you
have to understand the assertions of the various different Buddhist
philosophical systems or tenets. Depending on the tenet system in question,
the illustration of the person will be different. Some assert the person to
be the body, and many others assert the person to be consciousness and
within consciousness, there are different types of consciousness; it is the
mental consciousness. So basically what exactly is the person? Most people
will think it is the mental consciousness. There is one particular school
which says that it is not the mental consciousness but it has to be
something different which is called the mind-basis-of-all. But all these
assertions are not the final view because through using reasons to negate
all these lower schools assertion, you will arrive at the assertion of the
highest school -the Consequence Middle Way School (CMWS). For them, a
person is a non-associated compositional factor; a person is the “I”. What
is the person of the “I”? The ‘I’ is that which is merely imputed in
dependence upon the aggregates. All these will be discussed further in the
next module; Tenets.
Handout Page 2: Awareness can be divided into a valid cognizer and non-
valid awareness.
What is a valid cognizer? Definition of a valid cognizer: new
incontrovertible knower.
In order for an awareness to be a valid cognizer, three features must be
present. They are:
   1. New
   2. Incontrovertible
   3. Knower
If an awareness possesses these three features then that awareness is a
valid cognizer.
“Incontrovertible” means knowing the object as it is.
This definition of a valid cognizer - a new incontrovertible knower- is
accepted by the vast majority of the tenets, starting from the Autonomy
Middle Way School (AMWS) and the tenets below it. The AMWS and all the
tenets below it assert that in order for an awareness to be a valid
cognizer, it has to be new.
“New” refers only to the initial first moment of realizing that object.
On the other hand, the CMWS asserts that as long as the awareness realizes
its object, it is a valid cognizer i.e. a valid cognizer is not restricted
to just the very initial first moment of realizing its object.
In the definition of valid cognizer -new incontrovertible knower- each
eliminates something:-
“New” eliminates that subsequent cognizers are valid cognizers.
“Incontrovertible” eliminates that correctly assuming consciousnesses are
valid cognizers.
“Knower” eliminates that physical sense powers are valid cognizers.
In order to really understand what these three different terms means, you
first have to understand what subsequent cognizer n correctly assuming
consciousnesses are and these will come later.
All other awarenesses that are not valid cognizer are non-valid
awarenesses.
If the definition of valid cognizer is a new incontrovertible knower, then
the non-valid awareness is a knower that is not new and incontrovertible;
basically that which is not valid cognizer.

               1. Direct valid cognizer
               2. Inferential valid cognizer.

The definition of a direct valid cognizer is a new incontrovertible knower
that is free of conceptuality. A direct valid cognizer is a valid cognizer;
but that is not sufficient. It is a valid cognizer and at the same time it
is free of conceptuality; which means that a direct valid cognizer is
necessarily non-conceptual.
The other valid cognizer is an inferential valid cognizer which is a new
incontrovertible determinative knower that is directly produced in
dependence on a correct sign which is its basis.
A valid cognizer that is conceptual in nature is an inferential valid
cognizer.
Example: There is a thought thinking that there is a fire in the mountain
pass. You don’t see the fire directly but you realize that there is a fire
in the mountain pass in dependence upon seeing smoke. You don’t see the
fire but you see the presence of smoke. In dependence on seeing smoke as
the reason, you realize there is a fire in the mountain pass. So this
thought realizing there is a fire in the mountain pass in dependence upon
seeing smoke is an example of inferential valid cognizer.
This inferential valid cognizer is not a direct perception. It is
conceptual thought but it does realize fire. This inferential valid
cognizer does not perceive fire nakedly but it gets at fire via a mental
image (meaning generality) of fire.
A subsequent cognizer is a knower which realizes what has already been
realized. It realized what has been realized by a valid cognizer. A
subsequent cognizer does not refer to the first moment or the initial
comprehension of the object. It is just realizing what has been realized
earlier by a valid cognizer. So it is the subsequent realization of the
same object.
A subsequent cognizer is not a valid cognizer because it is not the initial
comprehension of the object; but rather a knower that realizes something
that has been realized. So it is not new and therefore it is not a valid
cognizer.
Awareness to which an object appears and is not ascertained (AAA) is a
knower that is a common locus of
(1) having clear appearance of the specifically characterized phenomenon
which is its object of engagement and (2) being unable to induce
ascertainment with respect to the specifically characterized phenomenon
which is its object of operation
Illustration of AAA: Imagine your mind is completely engrossed with a
particular object due to attachment, or if you are just concentrating on
someone that you are mad with. During that time, when you are either very
mad or very engrossed with a particular object and your mind is completely
absolved in it, a visible form can appear to your eye consciousness or
sound can appear to your ear consciousness. While they appear, you didn’t
ascertain what appears to your eye consciousness or the sound that fell
onto your ear at that time.
Khen Rinpoche spoke in English: When my mom watches TV, some kind of Indian
serial which she likes so much, she will totally focus on the TV. Even if
somebody walks in front of her, nothing distracts her. If somebody says
something, she hears and nods her head but I don’t know. Even somebody walk
in front of her, she don’t know. So that is AAA. Not all kind of TV she
likes, it is part of the Indian serial, family drama or something like
that. Maybe like some Chinese people likes so much to follow the Korean
drama. So I noticed that when my mom is watching these, nothing can disturb
her.
Correctly assuming consciousness is a factually concordant determinative
knower which is controvertible with regard to determining its object.
Illustration: There is a person who has not realized the impermanence of
sound, but this person firmly believes that sound is impermanent although
without any reasons.
A correctly assuming consciousness is necessary a conceptual consciousness.

Doubting consciousness is a knower which has qualms two-pointedly by its
own power.
Illustration: A mind which wonders “is sound impermanent or permanent?”
Doubting consciousness is necessarily a conceptual consciousness.
Wrong consciousness is a knower which engages its object erroneously.
Illustration:  A mind which firmly believes that sound is permanent.
This is the sevenfold division of consciousness. We have just looked at the
enumeration of it.
