Questions for Sunday (26 Aug) discussion:

1. The definition of a doubting consciousness is a knower that has qualms
   two-pointedly by its own power. What is the reason for including, “by
   its own power” in the definition?
Definition: Doubting consciousness is a knower which has qualms two-
pointedly by its own power.
The reason for saying 'by its own power’ is that doubt is a mental factor
and this qualification eliminates that the main mind and other mental
factors which have similarity with it are doubt.

[Handout dated 7 Aug Page 4]A mind which  possesses  similarity  with  doubt
and the feelings, etc., which are  accompaniers  of  that  [mind  possessing
similarity with doubt] have qualms by the power of doubt, but  do  not  have
qualms two pointedly by their own power.

The main mind and other factors accompanying  the  mental  factor  of  doubt
also have qualms with regard to their object, but not by  their  own  power;
rather,  it  is  through  the  force  of  the  doubting  consciousness  that
accompanies them.


'By its own power' is inserted in the definition because, there is a main
mind that is concomitant with the mental factor of doubt.
In the retinue of this main mind, there are also other mental factors like
feeling, discrimination and so on. This main mind and its mental factors
are also knowers that have qualms two pointedly.
However, this main mind and its mental factors in its retinue that doubt is
concomitant with, although having qualms two-pointedly, nevertheless, do
not have qualms two pointedly by their own power, but arise through the
power of doubt, which does so by its own power. Therefore, this main mind
itself that doubt is concomitant with, as well as its retinue mental
factors, are not the mental factor of doubt/ doubting consciousness.

Definition: Doubting consciousness is a knower which has qualms two-
pointedly by its own power.
The meaning of 'two pointedly’ is that the mind is thinking, 'Is it this  or
is it that?'
'Qualms' means it is undecided.
Doubt is a 'knower' because it is a consciousness.
The division of awarenesses and knowers into seven is not in terms of  minds
and mental factors, but a general one.  When  doubting  consciousnesses  are
defined as knowers which by their own power have qualms two-pointedly,  this
means that they are mental factors and anything else is eliminated.
However, the term 'doubting consciousness' in general  includes  both  minds
and mental factors.

2. If it is a mental factor that has qualms two-pointedly, is it
   necessarily doubt?
Among the six root afflictions, there is one called doubt. That doubt is
necessarily a mental factor and is necessarily afflicted. However, doubt in
general is not necessarily either a mental factor or afflicted. It can be
posited as of three types: virtuous, non-virtuous, and neutral
Virtuous doubting consciousness - one wonders whether or not the effects of
actions/karma exist and thinks that probably they do, or one that wonders
whether or not former and later lives exist and thinks that probably they
do.
Non-virtuous doubting consciousnesses - one wonders whether or not the
effects of actions/karma exist and thinks that they probably do not
Neutral doubt - wondering when one wake up in the morning whether it will
be sunny or not

3. Is a doubting consciousness and a wrong consciousness mutually exclusive
   or is there a common locus between them?
Doubting consciousness is a knower which has qualms two-pointedly by its
own power.
Wrong consciousness is a knower which engages its object erroneously.
How about doubt tending toward the non-factual e.g. doubt which thinks that
sound is probably permanent?
This consciousness engages its object erroneously.

According to Sera-Je's position, there is a common locus between doubt and
wrong consciousness:
Example, a mind thinking: “reincarnation probably does not exist.”
Such a mind is doubt, which tend towards the non-factual, and also a wrong
consciousness. It is a wrong consciousness because it is a knower which
engages in its object erroneously. The object of this mind is
'reincarnation', which is engaged erroneously by this mind.

There are those that assert that wrong consciousness and doubt are mutually
exclusive. This is because, a wrong consciousness is decisive about its
object, whereas doubt is two-pointed about its object.

4. Can a sense consciousness become a conceptual consciousness? Can a
   direct perceiver become a conceptual consciousness?
Sense consciousnesses are necessarily non-conceptual.
Mental consciousnesses can be either conceptual or non-conceptual.
Mental, self-knowing, and yogic direct perceivers are all non-conceptual
mental consciousnesses.
Inference, correct assumption, doubt, and so forth are conceptual mental
consciousnesses.
A conceptual consciousness is necessarily a mental and not a sense
consciousness.
[Handout dated 10 July Page 1] Definition of an awareness which is a direct
perceiver is: knower which is free from conceptuality and non-mistaken
 • “Free from conceptuality”: that which is free from being a determinative
   knower that apprehends a sound generality and meaning generality as
   suitable to be mixed.
 • All conceptual consciousnesses are determinative knowers. The meaning of
   “determinative” is that such a consciousness thinks, “this is such and
   such,” “that is such and such.”
 • All direct perceivers are non-determinative.

5. Is the subsequent consciousness induced by the eye consciousness
   apprehending blue necessarily a sense consciousness?
The second moment of eye consciousness apprehending blue is a sense
consciousness.
The second moment of a self-knowing direct perceiver that experiences an
eye consciousness apprehending blue is a mental consciousness.
(Note: Mental, self-knowing, and yogic direct perceivers are all non-
conceptual mental consciousnesses)


6. What is the Sutra School’s reason for positing self-knowers? Why are
   self-knowers not main minds and mental factors?
-The reason is to account for memory. Memory explains why we can remember
things.
-Example: An eye consciousness apprehending the object blue. Later one is
able to remember, “I saw blue.” The reason why one is able to remember
having seen blue in the past is because there was a valid cognizer that
realized blue. One also remembers the mind that saw blue.
-Why a self-knower is neither a main mind nor a mental factor?
 • The presentation of the main mind and the mental factor is from the
   perspective of the aspect of the apprehended.
 • The main mind realizes the entity of the object it is apprehending.
 • The mental factor realizes the characteristics of the object.
 • Through the sense direct perceiver, mental direct perceiver, and yogic
   direct perceiver, one explains the mind and mental factors.
 • A self-knower is just a mere experiencer of the entity of the
   consciousness, therefore has the aspect of an apprehender. So self-
   knower is neither a main mind nor a mental factor.
Consciousness = Main mind + Mental factor
