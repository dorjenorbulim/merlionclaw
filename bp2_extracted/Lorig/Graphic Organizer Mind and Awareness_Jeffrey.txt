Cognition                   Purpose of Studying Mind and Awareness (Lorig)  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 We engage in the practice of Dharma by studying, reflecting, and then meditating. 
This is undertaken in this particular sequence. Studying is a practice of the Mahayana 
and is undertaken in order to improve and strengthen the  excellent minds of direct , 
inferential , and subsequent  cognition, as well as diminish and eliminate the 
inferior minds of correct assumption , doubt , and the various wrong 
consciousnesses . The mind to which the object appears but is not ascertained , 
does n ot bring any benefit nor does it harm us, so we can set it aside for now.  
 
Therefore, we engage in our studies in order to eliminate wrong consciousnesses 
and to diminish other inferior minds. Another way to express this, is that we engage 
in studies so as to change wrong consciousnesses into doubts, and then to change 
doubts into correct assumptions, and then to change all correct assumptions, into 
the excellent minds of prime cognition.  
 
This is the purpose of our studies, and the resulting reflections and meditations. Through 
study, you will be able to understand what samsara is, and what the  causes of samsara are. 
Through understanding this, you will know that the very root cause of cyclic existence is 
ignorance, to which ther e is also an antidote. This means that you can be liberated from 
samsara. By understanding this, you can eradicate your doubts and arrive at excellent minds, 
realising minds, with regard to these topics.  
 
 
We said that there were three excellent minds: dir ect prime cognizers, inferential prime 
cognizers, and subsequent prime cognizers. First, you will generate an inferential prime 
cognizer that realises the actual situation with regard to your doubts. From this you will 
understand how you can become complet ely liberated from samsara, how there is an end to 
samsara. After the generation of an inferential prime cognizer, you will generate a subsequent 
prime cognizer. As your understanding develops and becomes more refined, finally you will 
have a direct realis ation of all the aspects of samsara and what samsara means. This will 
enable you to generate a fully -fledged mind of definite emergence, which will enable you to 
eventually generate the antidote to cyclic existence, the wisdom directly realising emptiness.  
All of these excellent minds are generated in this sequence:

under what circumstances it is established: it is established by a valid awareness. Since there is 
a need to depend on a valid awareness, it must be either a direct perceiver or a conceptual 
consciousness. Only on the basis of these two valid minds c an we establish object of 
knowledge and established basis. Existence and non -existence are established by these two. 
Therefore, the object is posited in dependence on the object -possessor, an awareness.

The definition of an object is that which can be cognized by mind. Everything that exists 
is an object because object of knowledge,  established base, and existent thing, are synonyms, 
they are things that  are known by a prime cognizer, realized by a prime cognizer, and 
perceived  by a prime cognizer.  
 
The many aspects of mind include correct minds and incorrect minds. Correct minds are of 
two types: prime cognizers and subsequent cognize rs. Incorrect minds include other aspects 
of mind such as wrong consciousness, presumption, doubt, and inattentive cognition. These 
minds are incorrect because they do not realize their objects properly.

 Apprehended and Engaged Object  
For the mental consciousness that directl y perceives the impermanence of the color blue, 
the impermanence of blu e is the engaged object and the apprehended object. The referent 
object is the color blue itself. Although the apprehended, beheld, and  appearing object to 
the visual consciousness apprehending blue are the same thing, for that visual 
consciousness "appearing object" does not on ly refer to the color blue. The appearing 
object is everything that came into existence tog ether with the color blue - its substance, 
its impermanence , and so on.  
 
 For a direct perception, such as the visua l consciousness that apprehends blue, the 
color blue is both the apprehende d and the engaged object. It is also the referent, 
the appearin g, and the beheld object.  
 
It is "apprehended" because the visual consciousne ss grasps at the color blue, and it 
is "engaged" because the visual consciousness focuses upon the color blue. 
Grasping and engaging refer to d ifferent ways in which the mind becomes involved 
with its object.  
 
Althou gh the ways of apprehension are different, the object remains the same.
