What is the difference between studying Awareness and Knowledge & Minds and
Mental Factors?
-Both study the mind and its functions.
-Awarenesses and Knowers focused primarily on the study of knowledge, what
are valid ways of knowing and what are invalid.
-Minds and mental factors is a more psychological study of mind, examining
the mind in terms of its wholesome and unwholesome aspects, etc.

Why study minds and mental factor?
The extensive presentation of Mind and Mental factors are given to the
intelligent beings who have in mind their welfare in future lives; not
merely concerned about this life alone. Such intelligent ones who are
looking beyond this life should investigate what is the cause of oneself
circling endlessly in the three realms of cyclic existence. Having
contemplated on the root cause of cyclic existence, he must come to
understand that these sufferings of cyclic existence neither arise without
cause, nor arise from a discordant cause; rather, they arise from their own
causes – actions and afflictions.

Factor of appearance
Whatever appears to the mind, there is the factor of appearance. The object
appears in a particular way but we believe in that appearance. We believe
that the object exists in the way that it appears to us. Due to this belief
and because we hold on to this appearance to be real, we accumulate karma:
➢ Even though the object appears to the mind, without the mind, one would
  not have such an appearance.
➢ Without such an appearance, one would cease to grasp at the appearance.
➢ Without grasping at such an appearance, one would cease to accumulate
  actions/ karma.
➢ Without the mind, that factor of appearance cannot appear to the mind.
➢ Therefore, in dependence on this factor of appearance to the mind, one
  apprehends that factor to be utterly real, to exist in the way it
  appears, one then accumulates actions.
When we believe in whatever is appearing to our mind to exist in the way it
appears, that is when we get emotionally involved with that appearance. We
then accumulate actions that lead to the result, suffering. So we have to
understand and experience how all these different appearances to the mind
arise from the mind itself. Ultimately everything is created by the mind,
including the suffering that we do not want. We have to understand how
suffering arises from our mistaken belief in these appearances to the mind.

Minds and mental factors
Maitreya's Discrimination of the Middle and the Extremes says:
 ➢ That which sees an object is a primary consciousness (mind).
 ➢ That which sees its attribute is a mental factor.

What is the difference between a main mind and a mental factor?
A main mind engages merely in the entity of the object by its own power.
A mental factor engages in the specific features/characteristics of an
object by its own power.
Example: Eye primary consciousness apprehends the mere entity of blue.
Main mind: Eye primary consciousness apprehending the mere object blue
Mental factor of mindfulness (in the retinue of this eye primary
consciousness) induces a memory holding on to this blue.  This mindfulness
performs the function of holding on to an object without forgetting it.
Because it engages the object by way of its attributes, it is a mental
factor.
The eye primary consciousness apprehending blue and the mental factor of
mindfulness in its retinue are different (i.e. not one) because:
   - mind is not its mental factor
   - its mental factor is not mind
These two are not mutually inclusive. They are not one but nevertheless one
entity i.e. one entity but different isolates.
What does it mean to be one entity?
Example: Vase and the vase’s impermanence, these two are one entity but
different isolates.
The vase’s impermanence is one entity with vase. This doesn’t mean that
vase’s impermanence is vase.
Does vase’s impermanence exists on a non-vase?
Vase’s impermanence is only found on vase. You cannot posit vase’s
impermanence on a non-vase.
Vase’s impermanence cannot be posited as separate from vase.
You will not be able to find a vase’s impermanence that is of a different
entity from the vase itself.
Abhidharmakosa says: “A mind and its mental factor are definitely
simultaneous”, so the main mind and its mental factor are one entity.
One will not be able to posit mental factor that is of a separate entity
from the main mind; and just as one cannot posit a main mind that is of a
separate entity from its mental factor.

What are Minds?
They are the six primary consciousnesses:
  1. eye primary consciousness
  2. ear primary consciousness
  3. nose primary consciousness
  4. tongue primary consciousness
  5. body primary consciousness
  6. mental primary consciousness
What are Mental Factors?
-A primary mind and its attendant mental factors always operate in
conjunction with one another.
-An object is never cognized by a primary mind devoid of any mental factors
nor by a mental factor unaccompanied by a primary mind.

   1. Aggregate of form
   2. Aggregate of feeling

All impermanent phenomena can be subsumed under these five aggregates. If
it is an impermanent phenomenon, it is one of these aggregates.

Functioning thing can be divided into:
   1. Matter
   2. Consciousness
   3. Non-associated compositional factor (NACF)
All phenomena that are in the category of matter can be put under the
category of aggregate of form.
All form is subsumed by the aggregate of form.
Consciousness can be looked in term of:
   1. Main minds
   2. Mental factors
-All the main minds are classified under the aggregate of consciousness.
-In general, there are 51 mental factors.
  - The mental factor of feeling comes under the aggregate of feeling
  - The mental factor of discrimination comes under the aggregate of
  discrimination.
  - The remaining 49 mental factors come under the aggregate of
  compositional factors. The third division of functioning thing – the non-
  associated compositional factor- comes under the aggregate of
  compositional factors.
Why are the mental factor of feeling and the mental factor of
discrimination highlighted separately?
The two reasons why these two mental factors are listed separately because
they are:
(1) the bases for conflict and
(2) the primary causes for the perpetuation of cyclic existence.

Four possibilities between sentience and mental consciousness
1) Something which is a sentience but not a mental consciousness: Eye
primary consciousness. It is a sentience but not a mental consciousness
2) Something which is mental consciousness but not a sentience: Mental
factor of feeling that is concomitant with a mental primary consciousness.
It is a mental consciousness and not a sentience because it is a mental
factor.
3) Something which is both sentience and mental consciousness: mental
primary consciousness. It is a sentience and also a mental consciousness.
4) Something which is neither sentience nor mental consciousness: mental
factor of feeling that is concomitant with an eye primary consciousness: It
is not a sentience because it is a mental factor. It is also not a mental
consciousness because it is a sense consciousness.
Three possibilities between a sense consciousness and sense primary
consciousness:
1) Something which is a sense consciousness but not a sense primary
consciousness: mental factor of feeling in the retinue of an eye
consciousness apprehending blue. It is a sense consciousness as it is in
the retinue of a sense consciousness but not a primary sense consciousness
as it is a mental factor.
2) Something which is both a sense consciousness and a sense primary
consciousness: an eye primary consciousness apprehending blue.
3) Something which is neither: mental factor of feeling in the retinue of
conceptual consciousness apprehending blue.

states the definitions:
 ➢ A main mind is that which is concomitant by way of the five
   similarities with the mental factors in its retinue
 ➢ A mental factor is that which is concomitant by way of the five
   similarities with its main mind.
Example: An eye consciousness apprehending blue and the mental factor of
feeling in its retinue.
These two are concomitant by way of the five similarities.
Regarding the modes of these five similarities, there are two explanations
with slight different presentations:
  – one from the Vasubandhu’s Treasury of Knowledge

Five Omnipresent Mental Factors







The five omnipresent mental factors accompany all minds; thus are explained
as “omnipresent”.
Whenever any one among these five is not complete, enjoyment of the object
is not complete:
–without feeling, the experiencing of pleasure, pain, and so forth, does
not arise;
–without discrimination, designation of verbal conventions does not occur;
–without intention, engagement of the object does not occur;
–without mental engagement, directing the mind to the object of observation
does not occur;
–without contact, feeling –pleasure, pain, and so forth –is not generated
Feelings are the cause of the afflictions
-pleasant feelings ( attachment
-unpleasant feelings ( aversion/ hatred
-neutral feelings ( ignorance
