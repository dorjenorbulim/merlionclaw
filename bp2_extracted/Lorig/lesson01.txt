The subjects that are covered in this Basic Program are meant to be the
foundation for studying and understanding the great treaties in the future.
So by studying and understanding the subjects taught in the Basic Program,
this will become the foundation for you to gain an unmistaken comprehension
or understanding of the great treaties which you may have the opportunity
to look into in the future. When you are able to gain an unmistaken
comprehension or understanding of these great teachings in the future, then
you can share your understanding of the teachings with others. By doing so,
you will be able to bring benefits to others. Due to the unmistaken
understanding of those great Buddhist teachings in the future, as an
individual, especially when you have developed the conviction and have
gained an ascertainment of the validity of the facts of those teachings,
you will also benefit because you will be able to use those teachings to
effect a change in your mind.
In the Buddhist circle, we always make prayers for the Buddha’s teachings
to flourish but it is not sufficient to just make the prayers alone. As an
individual, we have to understand that we also have a responsibility to
cause the Buddha’s teaching to flourish. What do we mean to cause the
Buddha’s teaching to flourish? This refers to the fact that we should cause
both the scriptural teachings and the realizational teachings to flourish.
How do the scriptural teachings flourish?
As an individual, on the basis of having learned and gained an unmistaken
comprehension and understanding of the Buddha’s teachings and then share
the knowledge with others, this is how one causes the Buddha’s scriptural
teachings to flourish.
As an individual, on the basis of having gained an unmistaken comprehension
and understanding of Buddha’s teachings, and then put them into practice
which effect some positive changes in your heart in your mind, that is what
we mean by the flourishing the realizational teachings.
Just as we continue to pray for the Buddha’s teachings to flourish, we
should also put into action by learning the Buddha’s teachings, by
listening, by studying and analysing those teachings to gain an
understanding. Therefore, it is very important to make a constant effort in
learning and reflecting on the teachings.
As I have often mentioned, we all accept that if we recite Om Mani Padme
Hung, we do accumulate merits. We also accept that if we circumambulate
holy objects, we do accumulate merits. So, if we think about this by
applying logic and reasoning, when we engage in these activities we will
create merits. Likewise, if we study the Buddha’s teachings, we will also
create merits. Just by reading the Buddha’s teaching itself is a virtuous
activity and it brings about accumulation of merits.  On the basis of
learning and gained an understanding of the Buddha’s teachings, the merits
we accumulate will even be greater. We have to remember this all the time.
So by keeping this in mind, we should think; “Every time I read the text
and I study, I am creating virtues and accumulating merits. By keeping this
in the mind, I should strive to come to class”. Of course all of you have
busy lives. By the fact that all of us have obtained this human life and
now have the opportunity to find out and to learn about Buddha’s teaching,
we should seize the opportunity to learn.
His Holiness the Dalai Lama always says that we all have to be 21st century
Buddhist. What does he mean by that? He always says that it is not
sufficient to just have faith and belief in the Buddha’s teachings, but
rather, we should study and actually discover what the Buddha has said and
the kind of faith that we should develop is the faith that arises from
understanding what the Buddha had taught. This is very important.
The Buddha’s teachings are such that if you learn or study them, you will
be able to understand them. From studying the Buddha’s teaching, you will
be able to discover for yourself that the Buddha's teachings is really
something different and is a class of its own. It is something very
different from other instructions or teachings you can find in this world.
But this can only come if you do actually study the Buddha’s teaching and
discover it for yourself. Once you see that, then you will have the
understanding and feeling of how special and unique the Buddha is. With
this comes a very strong faith in the Buddha. Once you have found this kind
of faith in the Buddha, then gaining the positive experiences of the path
and realizations would be easy.
Another thing to note is that in the course of our learning, it is possible
that you may feel there is not much use in studying as you don’t see the
purpose and its usefulness. It is really possible for you to think like
this along the way.  You may not see the point in learning some of things
that were taught but you have to keep in mind of what I have mentioned
earlier. The whole point of learning the subjects in the Basic Program is
to enable you to be able to learn and understand those profound teachings
and treaties in the future. So keep this goal in the mind. Then whenever
there is something you don’t understand including new terminologies and so
forth, you just have to tell yourself that this is something you have to
learn and memorize as this will becomes the foundation and the tools to
study those advanced subjects in the future.

Handout Page 1: ESTABLISHED BASES
The broadest possible category, including all existents and non-existents
as well, is that of the selfless. All Buddhist tenet systems assert
selflessness as the main principle of their philosophies. What is selfless
is empty of or lacks a certain type of self. A fundamental assertion in
Buddhism is that only through understanding selflessness, or emptiness, can
one gain liberation and thereby be relieved of powerless rebirth.
In order to understand selflessness, it is necessary to understand the
phenomena that are the bases of selflessness. Therefore, in the beginning,
students are taught what exists and how one can know those existents.
Established base is mutually inclusive with object  of  knowledge,  existent
and object of comprehension.
(Mutually inclusive means they have different names  but  are  referring  to
the same thing and same meaning).
Basically, established base, object of knowledge,  existent  and  object  of
comprehension refer to  the  phenomenon  that  is  established  by  a  valid
cognizer.
(Definition: Valid cognizer is  a  correct,  certifying  consciousness  that
incontrovertibly realize its object)
What is the difference between existent and non-existent? What determine
what is existent and what is non-existent?
An existent exists because it can be realized by  a  valid  cognizer  (valid
mind). Whatever exists  is  realized  (verified)  as  existing  by  a  valid
cognizer.
A non-existent is something  that  does  not  exist  because  it  cannot  be
verified by any valid cognizer. Its existence is not  realized  by  a  valid
cognition, therefore it does not exist; it is non-existent.
Established base can be divided into:
      -impermanent
      -permanent
Impermanent  phenomenon  is  mutually  inclusive  with  functioning   thing,
product, ultimate truth, specifically characterized phenomenon and  composed
phenomenon.
What is an impermanent phenomenon?
It is called impermanent phenomenon because it  undergoes  momentary  change
i.e. it undergoes change in each and every single moment.
What is a permanent phenomenon?
It is a permanent phenomenon because while it is an existent,  it  does  not
undergo momentary change.
If a phenomenon is an existent, it is either an impermanent phenomenon or  a
permanent phenomenon.
For an existent, if it is not an impermanent  phenomenon,  it  is  necessary
has to be a permanent phenomenon. It is  exhaustive.  There  isn’t  a  third
possibility whereby there is a  phenomenon  that  is  an  existent  that  is
neither impermanent nor permanent.
An impermanent phenomenon can also be referred to as product.
It is called a product because it is a created or produced phenomenon.
An impermanent phenomenon can be known as composed phenomenon.
It is  composed  phenomenon  because  that  phenomenon  comes  about  or  is
composed through causes and conditions.
What is a specifically characterized phenomenon?
Specifically characterized phenomenon is a phenomenon which  is  established
by way of its own  character  without  being  imputed  by  term  or  thought
consciousness (or conceptuality).
An ultimate truth is a  phenomenon  which  ultimately  able  to  performs  a
function.
What we have mentioned so far is based on the view of Sutra School.
You have to  understand  that  these  different  terms  (functioning  thing,
product, ultimate truth, specifically characterized phenomenon and  composed
phenomenon) are the various ways of talking  about  impermanent  phenomenon.
They are not separate from impermanent phenomenon  and  just  the  different
ways of saying the same thing.
Impermanent  phenomenon,  functioning  thing,   product,   ultimate   truth,
specifically characterized phenomenon and composed phenomenon  are  mutually
inclusive.
Permanent  phenomenon  covers  every   single   phenomenon   that   is   not
impermanent.
Question: If it is not impermanent, is it necessary permanent?
Geshela (spoke in English): Those who say “yes”, there is  another  question
for you.
Question: Is sky-flower permanent phenomenon?
The point is: Just because it is not impermanent, that does not  necessarily
make it  permanent.  If  it  is  not  impermanent,  it  is  not  necessarily
permanent. However, if you say it is an existent  and  is  not  impermanent,
then that is necessarily permanent.
Permanent phenomenon is mutually inclusive with  non-produced,  conventional
truth, generally characterized phenomenon and uncomposed phenomenon.
A permanent phenomenon does not undergo momentary change.
A permanent phenomenon can also be known as non-produced phenomenon.
A non-produced phenomenon is a non-created phenomenon.
An uncomposed phenomenon is so-called because that phenomenon does not  come
into existence through being put together by causes and conditions.
Generally characterized phenomenon is a phenomenon that  is  merely  imputed
by term or thought consciousness. It is not established  as  a  specifically
characterized phenomenon.
Conventional truth refers to phenomenon which ultimately unable  to  perform
a function.

Impermanent  phenomenon  refers  to  phenomenon  that  undergoes   momentary
change.
Impermanent phenomenon can be divided into:
   1. Matter
   2. Consciousness
   3. Non-associated compositional factor
So, if it is an impermanent phenomenon, it is necessary either one of  these
three: matter, consciousness or non-associated compositional factor.
According to the Sutra School, one of the Buddhist tenets, form  and  matter
are mutually inclusive.
Matter  is  atomically  established  i.e.  it  is  established  through  the
gathering of many atoms.
Consciousness is an impermanent phenomenon but is not matter.  Consciousness
is non-obstructive; it does not have color and shape yet it is able to  have
clear apprehension of object appearing to it.
Consciousness (shes pa), awareness (blo) and knower (rig  pa)  are  mutually
inclusive.
For non-associated compositional factor, if it is an impermanent  phenomenon
but is neither matter nor consciousness,  then  it  is  necessarily  a  non-
associated compositional factor.
What we have discussed so far is basically a presentation  of  what  reality
is. In technical Buddhist language, it is called the  basis.  Therefore,  it
is the understanding of what is the basis or reality.
Matter and consciousness are of different  entities.  Matter  is  atomically
established and is composed of many particles or atoms. On the  other  hand,
consciousness is non-obstructive; no color and no shape.  It  is  empty  and
has the factor of clarity and knowing.
How does form or matter is established? Matter or form comes into  existence
by depending on  concordant  cause  (cause  of  the  similar  type).  It  is
established by former moment of form or matter. Likewise,  consciousness  is
established in dependence on concordant cause  (the  cause  of  the  similar
type).
So, whether it is matter or consciousness, both is  impermanent  phenomenon,
therefore they are product. The fact that they are product means  that  they
are being produced.
Matter and consciousness  are  also  composed  phenomena  because  they  are
composed by causes and conditions. Therefore, according  to  the  Buddhist’s
view, if it is an impermanent phenomenon, it cannot exist without  depending
on causes and conditions.
So, if it is an impermanent phenomenon, it has to come into being by:
 -depending on causes and conditions and
- also depend on the concordant cause.
Therefore,  whether  it  is  matter  or  consciousness,  they  have  to   be
established through causes and conditions.
Causes are of two types:
   1. Substantial causes
   2. Cooperative conditions
Form or matter  cannot  act  as  the  substantial  cause  of  consciousness.
Likewise, consciousness cannot act as  the  substantial  causes  of  matter.
This is because in reality,  matter  or  consciousness  are  established  as
distinct entity i.e. of different entities. Therefore they  must  come  from
their respective causes. Therefore,  matter  has  to  come  to  existent  in
dependence on the former moment of matter. Likewise,  consciousness  has  to
come from the previous moments of consciousnesses. Based  on  this  line  of
reasoning of  the  substantial  cause  of  consciousness,  this  is  how  we
establish reincarnation and there are past lives and there  will  be  future
lives. This is  something  that  we  have  to  think  about.  Based  on  our
observation of reality, matter and consciousness,  which  are  of  different
entities,  come  about  through  their  own  causes.   On   the   basis   of
understanding what constitute reality, then we can conclude  that  there  is
reincarnation, there is past lives and there  will  be  future  lives.  From
this you can see the importance of understanding the reality.
In the case of consciousness,  consciousness  has  to  come  into  being  in
dependence on some other factors other than  itself  i.e.  it  has  to  come
about as the result of a cause.  It  cannot  come  about  in  dependence  on
discordant cause but in dependent on concordant cause. From  this,  you  can
establish reincarnation, past and future lives. So you have to have  a  good
understanding on the basis or reality in order for you to figure all  these.
You also  have  to  understand  how  matter  and  consciousness  with  their
distinct entities exist in  reality.  An  ascertainment  of  how  liberation
exist, how the Buddha exist and how enlightenment exist can only be  arrived
based on the fundamentally understanding of the basis  or  what  constitutes
reality.
As I have mentioned earlier, there is no way out other than  learning  these
terms. In preparation for the studies of the future  subject,  you  have  to
memorize these terms and understand  them.  You  must  think  big  here  and
understand what the goal is. The goal is what you want  to  achieve  in  the
future. This is the preparation to  help  you  to  understand  the  Buddha’s
teachings in the future. Please keep this in mind. On the  basis  of  making
the preparation now, when you do gained some understanding in  the  Buddha’s
teaching in the future, you will get the mental satisfaction and  happiness.
Only then you can say that you have made this human  life  of  freedoms  and
endowments meaningful.

A substantial cause  is  a  cause  which  produces  an  effect  which  is  a
continuation of its own substantial entity.
A cooperative condition produces an effect which is not  a  continuation  of
its own substantial entity.
Whatever is an effect  necessarily  has  both  a  substantial  cause  and  a
cooperative condition. For instance, the substantial cause of a clay pot  is
the mud which serves as a cause for the pot, and a cooperative condition  of
a pot is a person who molds the clay and so forth.
