Verse 34
When conceptual construction rises up as pollution demons and evil spirits,
it is the weapon of my own evil deeds turned upon me for sinning against
the gods and mantras. From now on I shall crush all conceptual
constructions.
(In the Tibetan edition: When experiencing spirit harms and also
accumulating negativities from having degenerated or broken the spiritual
vows and commitments, it is the weapon of my evil deeds turned upon me for
sinning against the gods and mantras. From now on I shall crush all
conceptual constructions. )
Sometimes when one experiences harms from an external spirit, it could be
due to transgressing or breaking one’s vows and samaya. Due to that
transgression or breaking of vows and samaya, it appears as external spirit
which causing harm. So, whatever the specific karmic cause might be, in
general, if we analyze the main cause of receiving spirit harm, at the end
of the day, it always comes back to our own afflictions.
There is a verse, I don’t remember exactly who said it, perhaps is Lama
Atisha, which goes like this: If your mind is subdued, the external spirit
cannot harm you. But if your mind is not subdued, then that external spirit
acts as a condition for your own mind to be burned.”
So, in the context of this verse, what it is saying is that “Due to the
degeneration of one’s samaya or vows, that negativities then arise in the
form of an external source of harm, non-human entities, spirit or whatever
which then causes you problems. This is due to having sinned against the
deities or the gods in the past.
“The deities” here could be a worldly god (deity) or it could be the
transcendental deity. Whatever it is, one may have despised them, put them
down or criticized them in the past.
“Sinning against mantras” - mantra is Sanskrit word and when translated
into Tibetan, it means “mind protection” or “protecting the mind”. So
mantra means protecting the mind (mind protection). In this case, we can
take it to mean protecting the mind from non-virtue.  The entire dharma can
be condensed into the practices of the path of the three types of persons:
   1. The path of a person of small capacity
   2. The path of a person of middle capacity
   3. The path of a person of great capacity
Whatever dharma it maybe, starting from the dharma of a person of small
capacity, it is a method which protects our minds from the non-virtuous
states of mind.
So criticizing, despising, disregarding, or putting down such a dharma
would lead to the accumulation of negativities which then ripens in the
form of an external source of harm like a spirit which causes us problem.
What do we do henceforth? “From now on I shall crush all negative
conceptions”. In the translation, it says “conceptual constructions”
whereas in the Tibetan it says “niantuk??” which means negative concepts
(negative thoughts).
Ven Gyurme: I don’t think “conceptual construction” is correct.
“From now on, I shall crush all negative concepts (negative thoughts)” -
the thoughts that we should vanquish or we should try our best to reduce
are those thoughts that do not accord with dharma. So here, one is kind of
undertaking an oath or a pledge to vanquish such negative thought - thought
that do not accord with the dharma.
We have all kinds of discursive thought or negative thought that arise from
our anger and attachment which themselves are induced by ignorance. All
these are non-beneficial negative thoughts. So, we should try to overcome
them.
Verse 35
When I wander far from home like a helpless person, it is the weapon of my
own evil deeds turned upon me for driving spiritual teachers and other from
their homes. From now on I shall not expel anyone from their home.
This verse gives the example of being expelled from a place. Sometimes you
may be living in an environment that is quite pleasant but somehow somebody
who has more power comes along and forces you to leave that particular
place. Sometimes at work when somebody who is more powerful comes along and
does something that make you leave the job. Or sometimes in a community or
an organization, somebody who is more powerful comes along and for whatever
reason expels you from that community or that organization. These things
cause us to suffer.
If you are Buddhist, the way to think when encountering such situation is
to remember karma - “Whatever I am experiencing is the result of my own
actions”. The way is not about looking for the source of the problem
outside, and not to point finger at somebody out there of such situation,
but rather to remember that whatever it is, it is the result of our own
karma. So that is the approach of a Buddhist. This is the way to experience
the situation without the mind getting disturb or upset, basically is to
make peace in our hearts. This way of thinking is the best way.
If you were to ask: “What kind of karma did I accumulate to experience
this?” The teachings tell us that this is the result of having expelled or
driven people away from their home.
The verse mentioned “spiritual teachers” and that is just one of the
examples, essentially motivated by anger, jealousy, or competitiveness, you
expel someone from their abode or from the place they live.
Henceforth, if you don’t want to experience something like that in the
future, the solution is then one need to avoid expelling anybody from where
they live or from a community and so forth, motivated by anger, jealousy,
or competitiveness, especially when one has the power and authority to do
so.
Verse 36
When calamities occur like frost and hail, it is the weapon of my own evil
deeds turned upon me for not properly guarding my my vows and moral
conduct. From now on I shall keep my vows and moral conduct pure.
(Ven Gyurme read “vows” as samayas and “moral ethical” as ethical conduct)
There is nothing much to say about that as they are pretty much the same.
[BP1 M6] Let us say you are a farmer. You  are  expecting  to  harvest  your
crops  soon.   When  harvest  time  arrives,  however,  suddenly   tornados,
hurricanes, frost and hail come and  destroy  your  crops.  Such  calamities
happen due to the negative karma of not properly guarding  your  commitments
in relation to your gurus and your practice of ethics. This karma ripens  in
the form of damage to your  possessions,  crops  and  so  forth.  When  such
damage occurs, you suffer. Therefore,  from  now  on  you  should  keep,  as
purely as possible, your samaya with your gurus and guard your ethics well.
When we talk about having pure samaya with our gurus, we have to  understand
this very well, as this is extremely important. At  the  very  beginning  of
the Lamrim, there  are  very  extensive  explanations  of  the  practice  of
correctly relying on a guru or spiritual  master.  At  the  very  beginning,
before you rely on anyone, whether you want to rely on a  particular  person
as your teacher or not is completely up to you. It is  your  own  individual
choice. But once you have taken that person as your guru, from  that  moment
on, if you do not properly keep your samaya, eg. by  criticizing  or  giving
up your guru as the object of refuge from the heart, your samaya  with  your
guru will degenerate.  When  this  happens,  it  is  taught  that  one  will
accumulate an immeasurable amount of negative karma. Therefore, one  has  to
be careful from the very beginning.
Verse 37
When I, a greedy person, lose my wealth, it is the weapon of my own evil
deeds turned upon me for not giving charity or making offerings to the
Three Jewels. From now on I will zealously make offerings and give charity.
[In the Tibetan edition “When I, a greedy person, “losing my wealth or
experiencing poverty or have lack of wealth” it is the weapon of my own
evil deeds turned upon me for not giving charity or making offerings to the
Three Jewels. “From now on I will strive in making offerings and practicing
generosity.”]
This does happens to people. Generally, people are looking for material
gain or profits. Then they work very hard and do all kinds of things. They
may even invest by putting in all their money, time and effort. But despite
of having work so hard and doing whatever they can, still they don’t meet
with success. Not only that, they even lose what they have invested and end
up with really nothing. So these things do happen.
If we experience something like that, then we will be very upset. At that
time when we experience problems, suffering, or unhappiness, it is
important to bring karma to mind. We have to think that is the result of
our own karma. Which particular karma? Here it says: This is due to not
having practice giving in the past nor making offerings to the Three
Jewels.
It is said that not meeting with success in your work, business, career,
not making profits, and not making money out of those ventures is the
result of having been tightfisted in the past and being very miserly. Even
though one has the resources and money, one didn’t practice giving nor make
offerings to the Three Jewels.
What is the solution if we don’t want to experience the similar problem in
the future? The solution is to practice giving and to make offerings to the
Three Jewels.
It is mentioned in the Stages of the Path literature that having perfect
resources, perfect body, perfect enjoyment, perfect helpers and perfect
environment are the result of generosity, joyous perseverance, ethical
discipline and patience. As explained clearly in the Lam Rim, the Stages of
the Path literature, that having perfect resources, having perfect
enjoyments, having people to help you, having a perfect body and having a
perfect environment are the result of having practiced generosity, ethical
discipline, patience and joyous perseverance in the past.
But having these alone are not sufficient. If one has some kind of
meditative stabilization, or some kind of concentration and more
importantly wisdom, then having a perfect resources, having helpers, having
a perfect body and so forth could be useful and beneficial. Otherwise, if
we don’t have some kind of concentration, in particular some kind of
wisdom, just having money, having lot of helpers just because you have a
nice physical appearance, just because you have a nice abode and live in a
nice environment, that doesn’t necessarily mean you will end up happy. This
is because those things that you possess could actually be the condition
for you to get more and more afflicted. It is condition for negative
emotions to arise. So having those things without concentration and in
particular wisdom is not sufficient.
If we do not have wisdom that enables us to discriminate between what is
good and bad, what is right and wrong, what is harmful and what is
beneficial, and in particular if we do not have the wisdom that understands
karma and its effects, and if we don’t have wisdom, then even though we are
rich and have a lot of money and a lot of possessions, we will just using
up our merit because whatever enjoyments that we own, whatever enjoyments
that we partake, we are just exhausting our merit. If we are not topping up
at the same time, as we are not accumulating merit, we are just depleting
them. So this will happen when we don’t have wisdom. And also if we don’t
have wisdom, then having money, wealth and possessions actually become a
cause for us to get more emotional. Often it just stirs up more attachment,
more anger and more ignorance.
Just as we have wealth and prosperity now, we know that we need to do
something to ensure we will continue to have wealth and prosperity that in
the future. So if we have the wisdom that understands karma, then we will
do something to create the cause to experience wealth and prosperity in the
future. We will then happily practice giving and happily make offerings to
the Three Jewels. So if we have wisdom, then this will happen.  The
explanation from the Stages of the Path to Enlightenment literature (Lamrim
Chenmo) in the section dealing with the introduction to the perfections is
very good. That is really effective and is really very good advice. So we
should get acquainted with this advice. You should think about it.
In the Tibetan text, it says “From now on, I shall strive in practicing
giving and making offerings purely.” The word “purely” that is mentioned in
the Tibetan text indicates the purity of the intention.  So whatever we are
giving or making offerings, it has to be done sincerely and with a pure
motivation. Although it says here -“If you want wealth and prosperity, you
need to practice giving and make offerings” and this doesn’t mean you just
go about doing it in any slipshod manner such as “just give or just make
offering.” It is not like that. When we are making offerings, it must be
done with the purity of intention. That means we don’t make offerings
motivated by partisanship, bias or looking for fame, power and reputation,
or expecting something in return or “people look at me” and so forth. It is
important to make offerings or to practice giving purely. If we look at the
practice of giving from the perspective of a bodhisattva, it is mentioned
that bodhisattvas should give without expecting anything in return or
expect any kind of reward. Neither do they even expect to experience the
ripening effect of giving.
Making offerings to the Three Jewels and practicing giving without any
hopes of reward or even to experience the positive ripening effect of those
actions is the practice of the bodhisattva. But this doesn’t mean that
there is anything wrong with practicing giving just looking for future life
happiness. It is okay and it is good.  It is okay even if you practice
giving just looking to be rich and wealthy in the future life. But if you
are talking about the practice of a bodhisattva, then bodhisattva give
without any slightest hope of expectation as they don’t expect anything.
There is a lot of discussion about karma in the Buddhist teachings such as
the benefits of giving, the practices of ethical discipline, for example,
from giving come resources and from ethical discipline comes good rebirth.
These things are explained in detail. That is the theory part and that is
what is being mentioned many times in the teachings. However, if we look at
the actual reality in the practical world, if we look at the Buddhist
community at large, generally speaking, we don’t do as well as the
followers of the other religious traditions when it comes to extending
practical assistance to the society like helping the poor, constructing
schools for people and so forth. Although we talk so much, but action wise,
generally speaking we paled in comparison to the followers of the other
religious traditions. So we have to think for ourselves, if we have
sufficient resources and have enough to give, then we should help others.
So when we talk about giving, the practice of giving is essentially mainly
to those who are destitute, to the poor and the underprivileged.
The advice here is that we should practice giving and make offerings purely
as much as possible within our own ability and our own situation. We should
implement this and try to put it into practice.
Verse 38
When my companions mistreat me for being ugly, it is the weapon of my own
evil deeds turned upon me for venting my rage by erecting ugly images. From
now on I shall erect images of the gods and be slow to anger.
[In Tibetan edition: It is the weapon of my own evil deeds turned upon me
for erecting holy objects improperly, making bad representations of them,
includes the mind is disturbed in relation to holy objects]
As mentioned in the Lamrim, one of the eight fruitional effects is having a
good physical appearance in the context of benefitting sentient beings.
Having that quality could be useful because just by having a pleasant
appearance, people are attracted, and then it becomes easier to benefit
those sentient beings.
It is stated clearly in the Stages of the Path literature that if one
acquires the special body of the high status that possesses the eight
fruitional effects (eight special qualities) that will make quite a big
difference to one’s progress on the path to enlightenment. The eight
qualities stated in the Lamrim literature are:
   1. Having a long life
   2. Having a good body
   3. Having resources,
   4. Being born into a high lineage
   5. Having powerful speech, body and mind
   6. Having power, in general
   7. Being born as a male
   8. Having a strong body and mind.
What kind of karma do you need to accumulate to be born as a male?
Ven Gyurme: If you are not interested, you won’t have to worry about
accumulating the cause, right?
You will find in many sutras on the advantage of having a male body. Many
sutras say that one need a male body to achieve the Path of Seeing.
Khen Rinpoche (In English):  Fourteen years ago I went to a conference in

There were some discussions about whether female can get enlightenment or

in a female body; must be a man. One Kagyu nun, Tenzin Palmo, was against
that. I think it is not true, woman can get enlightenment. There is a lot

All I said was many sutras stated that one need a male body to achieve
enlightenment. But in tantra, it is mentioned that one can achieve
enlightenment on the basis of a female body. Anyway, this is not a critical
issue. It is not an important issue. We have to be aware that the Buddha
had said something like this and in other situation he said something
different. We have to understand what were Buddha’s intent and the purpose
for saying different things at different time to different people. As such,
therefore when we talk about the four reliances, one of which is do not
rely on the provisional meaning but rely on the definitive meaning. That is
very important.
In order to achieve a special body of high status that possesses the eight
fruitional effects or the eight fully ripened qualities such as the one
that we have mentioned earlier, it is not sufficient just to keep the
ethical discipline for refraining from the ten non-virtues. There are many
qualities, so there are many different karmas need to be accumulated such
as reducing pride, practicing patience and so forth.
Here, in order to achieve the qualities of having good body which include a
pleasant physical appearance, the cause for that is practicing patience. It
is also mentioned in Lamrim that constructing holy objects such as
constructing the statue of Buddha and making offering of garments to the
statue of the Buddha are also causes for achieving a good body. So, if one
does make a statue of Buddha or a holy object or one does commission a holy
object, it is important to do it properly. The statue or the holy object
must be made properly with the proper proportion, dimension, measurement
and so forth. The final product should be appealing to people so that when
people look at it, they find it very appealing. That is important. If you
achieve that then that become a cause to have a good body in the future.
You may already know that there is the practice of making tsa-tsa, i.e.
making images of the Buddha using clay or plaster. What I personally think
is that the number is not the emphasis. It is the quality that is the
important, mainly for the reason that we have looked at so far. So the
quality should be the priority. It should be make well such that the final
product is appealing to people when people look at it. That leaves a
positive impression on the people. With regards to the material for the
holy object, according to one’s ability, one should use the best material
if one can afford it.
If you think about it, by erecting a holy image of Buddha using clay or
plaster versus using gold, which will helps to get more merits? If you can
do it well, then just by doing one, it can be the cause to get good body in
the future. So, it is important to make holy objects properly with the
proper measurement, proportion, dimension and not missing anything out.
Sometimes you see maybe people make holy object or tsa-tsa and sometimes in
the final product, the eyes are missing, the hands are missing or the hands
and the legs are being out of proportion or crooked.  These are deformities
in the products. These are the causes to be born in the future with
incomplete sense faculty, sense power or missing parts in the body and so
forth. So, one has to be very careful. There are also people who disrespect
holy objects such as statues, dharma texts and so forth because of anger.
There are people who destroy holy objects or destroy dharma texts such as
burning them motivated by anger and some afflictions. These are causes to
have bad body in the future. Engaging in these kinds of actions would
result one having a bad physical body.
In the society we do see people who are ugly in the eyes of the society or
people who are not physical attractive. People who are ugly tend to have a
harder time. As people make fun of them, jeer at them, and say all kinds of
nasty things. It is not pleasant to experience that.
The solution to this is “I should erect holy objects properly, and be
careful when one is making holy objects and also be slow to anger, i.e. to
be patient.”
Here it says “be slow to anger” that means not to be short temper. There
are people who are very short temper, it doesn’t matter who they meet, they
just get upset almost immediately for whatever right or wrong reason. So,
we should try not to be liked that. We should be slow to anger.
Verse 39
When lust and hate are stirred up no matter what I do, it is the weapon of
my own evil deeds turned upon me for hardening my malevolent, evil mind.
Obstinacy, from now on I shall totally extirpate you!
Let’s say we may be aware of the defects of being attached to people of our
own side - the people who we considered to be our side, our loved one and
so forth.  We also aware of the defects or the disadvantages of having
anger towards those who we considered not to be on our side. Although we
may be aware and we may even try to stop such a bias attitude, but somehow
we don’t succeed. We experience difficulty in doing so. It is said that
such is the result of having come under the control of our self-cherishing
in the past. Henceforth, by recognizing the source of the problem which is
our self-cherishing, then we undertake to vanquish our self-
cherishing;basically promise ourselves to expel this self-cherishing.
Answering Compiled Questions
Q1. Does the developmental lineage include the wisdom directly realizing
emptiness which is the cause for dharmakaya, in particular, the cause for
the wisdom truth body?  Is the wisdom realizing emptiness the cause of the
both wisdom truth body and nature body?
Answer: The wisdom directly perceiving emptiness is the substantial cause
of the wisdom truth body; but it is not the cause for the natural purity.
The natural purity that is a nature body. The emptiness of the wisdom
directly perceiving emptiness is that which becomes the natural purity;
that is the nature body.
Q2. Is the Tathagata Essence an extremely hidden phenomenon? If it is then
it cannot be proved but rather one can only understand it based on the
testimony of somebody else.
Answer: No, the Tathagata Essence which is the emptiness of the mind is not
an extremely hidden phenomenon. One can come to understand it initially, at
least, through reasoning. As such, it is not an extremely hidden
phenomenon. For example, one can realize the emptiness of another person
through reasoning i.e. that person is not truly existence, the person does
not exist inherently because he is dependent arising. So, through the
reason of dependent arising, by depending on reason, one comes to
understand that the person does not exist inherently.
The Tathagata Essence which is the emptiness of the mind is not an
extremely phenomenon. But it is not an obvious phenomenon or manifest
phenomenon. It is a slightly hidden phenomenon. Object of knowledge or
object of comprehension when divided, there are three:
   1. Manifest (Obvious, Evident) phenomenon
   2. Slightly hidden phenomenon
   3. Extremely hidden phenomenon
Of these three, the Tathagata Essence is the slightly hidden phenomenon.
It is good to know a little bit about the ways of categorizing phenomena.
Manifest phenomenon is phenomenon that you can understand directly without
the need for reasoning such as the pillar or the flower that we see. We
experience the flowers directly. We see flower directly. We understand it
directly. We don’t need to depend on any thinking or reasoning to realize
it is flower. So, phenomenon that can be experienced directly without
depending for reasoning are called manifest phenomenon.
Slightly hidden phenomena are those phenomena that we cannot at least
initially realize directly. At least initially we have to realize it
through reasoning. At least to understand it initially we have to depend on
reasoning. So, emptiness of inherently existence is an example of slightly
hidden phenomenon. Subtle impermanent is another example of slightly hidden
phenomenon.
There are things such as the subtle presentation of karma and its effect
such as the one that we see in the teaching, if we do this then we will get
this particular result, and these are example of the extremely hidden
phenomenon. There is no way we can understand it or prove it through
reasoning. We can only understand it based on testimony. In this case, the
mind that realizes extremely hidden phenomenon is basically an inferential
cognizer generated through belief; in short, inference through belief.
Although we can only understand and accept extremely hidden phenomenon
based on the testimony of somebody’s else, and in our case we have to base
on scripture. It is not straightforward. It doesn’t mean whatever is said
in the scripture then we have to accept it. Yes, ultimately we accept it
because the Buddha said so. Or because it was mentioned in the scripture,
but there is a whole line of reasoning that has to precede that acceptance
of that scripture. Essentially we have to subject that scripture, the
Buddha’s words, to analysis. There are three kinds of analysis. If the
Buudha’s words or scripture is not harmed by the three kinds of analysis
then we can accept the testimony, that scripture and the Buddha’s words.
