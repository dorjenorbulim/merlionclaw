## Book Structure

The book is organized into **10 major parts** following the logical progression of the BP2 curriculum:

### Part I: Tenet Systems
- Introduction to the four Buddhist philosophical schools
- Vaibhashika, Sautrantika, Cittamatra, Madhyamaka
- The two truths: conventional and ultimate
- Emptiness and dependent arising

### Part II: Lorig (Mind and Cognition)
- Buddhist epistemology and theory of knowledge
- Types of awareness and consciousness
- Valid cognizers (direct perception and inference)
- The 51 mental factors
- Logical reasoning and debate methodology

### Part III: Lamrim Chenmo (Stages of the Path)
- Complete commentary on the Great Treatise
- Relying on a spiritual master
- Precious human rebirth
- Death and impermanence
- Lower realms and refuge
- Karma and its effects
- Renunciation and the determination to be free
- Bodhicitta: the mind of enlightenment
- The six perfections
- Calm abiding and special insight

### Part IV: 37 Aspects of Path to Enlightenment
- Four close placements of mindfulness
- Four perfect efforts
- Four bases of miraculous power
- Five powers and five forces
- Seven factors of enlightenment
- Eightfold noble path

### Part V: Six Perfections
- Generosity
- Ethical discipline
- Patience
- Joyous effort
- Concentration (meditative stability)
- Wisdom (realizing emptiness)

### Part VI: Engaging in the Deeds of a Bodhisattva
- Study of Shantideva's Bodhicaryavatara
- Chapter-by-chapter commentary
- Practical application of the Bodhisattva way of life

### Part VII: Heart Sutra
- The essence of the Perfection of Wisdom teachings
- Line-by-line commentary
- The meaning of emptiness
- Practice guidance

### Part VIII: Tathagata Essence (Buddha Nature)
- The Uttaratantra Shastra commentary
- The seven vajra points
- Buddha nature teachings
- The three kayas

### Part IX: Tantra
- Introduction to the four classes of tantra
- Highest Yoga Tantra
- Generation and completion stages
- Grounds and paths in tantra
- The resultant state of enlightenment

### Part X: Wheel Weapon (Mind Training)
- Lojong teachings
- Transforming adverse circumstances
- Sending and taking (tonglen)
- Practical mind training instructions
