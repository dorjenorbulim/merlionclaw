## Section 1: Ethics & Professional Practice

### Question 1
**Scenario:** During your second coaching session, a client discloses that they have been experiencing severe depression and are considering medication. They ask if you think they should see a psychiatrist.

**What is the most appropriate response?**

A. Share your opinion on medication based on your personal experience and encourage them to try coaching first.
B. Listen empathetically, acknowledge their courage in sharing, and suggest they consult with a mental health professional while offering to continue coaching alongside treatment.
C. Offer to extend the session to help them process their feelings about medication.
D. Tell them that coaching can replace therapy and suggest a goal of "feeling happier within 3 months."

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice), 1.2 (Embodies Coaching Mindset)  
**Rationale:** Mental health treatment decisions are outside coaching scope. Refer to appropriate professional while maintaining coaching relationship if client wishes.

---

### Question 2
**Scenario:** A client emails you after a session asking for detailed notes about everything they said, concerned their employer might request access.

**What should you do?**

A. Provide the notes since the client requested them and this shows transparency.
B. Explain your confidentiality policy, clarify that notes are private, and offer to discuss their concerns about employer access.
C. Refuse to provide notes citing confidentiality, ending the conversation there.
D. Suggest they speak with their employer first before you take any action.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice), 2.1 (Establishes Agreements)  
**Rationale:** Transparency includes explaining policies AND addressing underlying concerns. Simply providing or refusing notes misses the coaching opportunity.

---

### Question 3
**Scenario:** You discover that a potential client is the direct competitor of another client you coach. Both work in the same industry.

**What is your ethical obligation?**

A. Accept the new client but keep the two relationships completely separate.
B. Decline the new client to avoid any conflict of interest.
C. Disclose the potential conflict to both clients and let them decide.
D. Accept both but only coach on personal development, not business strategy.

**Answer: C**
**Competency:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice) — Conflict of Interest  
**Rationale:** Transparency is key. Disclose and let clients decide. If either is uncomfortable, refer the new client.

---

### Question 4
**Scenario:** In an AI-assisted coaching practice, a client is not informed that AI tools are used to generate session summaries and accountability reminders.

**What ICF principle is violated?**

A. Professional Conduct only
B. Confidentiality only  
C. Both Transparency and Professional Conduct
D. No violation if the AI improves outcomes

**Answer: C**
**Competencies:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice), 2.1 (Establishes Agreements)  
**Rationale:** ICF Code of Ethics requires transparency about the coaching process AND honest representation of how coaching is conducted.

---

### Question 5
**Scenario:** Your AI tool suggests that a client is "clearly afraid of commitment" based on conversation patterns.

**What should you do?**

A. Present this interpretation to the client as an insight.
B. Disregard the AI suggestion entirely.
C. Notice the pattern yourself and share it as your own observation.
D. Share the pattern observation neutrally and invite the client to make meaning from it.

**Answer: D**
**Competencies:** 3.2 (Evokes Awareness), 3.3 (Reflects), AI Ethics  
**Rationale:** AI can notice patterns but client creates meaning. Share observation, not interpretation. Client decides what it means.

## Section 2: Co-Creating the Relationship

### Question 6
**Scenario:** At the start of a new coaching engagement, a client says "I want you to tell me exactly what to do to fix my career." They seem frustrated and want direct advice.

**What is your best response?**

A. Provide clear action steps since this is what the client wants and needs.
B. Explain the coaching approach, explore what "fix" means to them, and partner to discover their own solutions.
C. Suggest they hire a consultant instead since they want advice, not coaching.
D. Agree to give advice for the first few sessions, then transition to coaching.

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 1.2 (Embodies Coaching Mindset), 2.1 (Establishes Agreements)  
**Rationale:** Coaching is non-directive. Clarify the process while honoring client's desire for change. Don't abandon coaching model but don't reject client either.

---

### Question 7
**Scenario:** A client from a different cultural background shares that direct questioning feels disrespectful in their culture.

**What demonstrates cultural responsiveness?**

A. Maintain your coaching style since ICF competencies are universal.
B. Ask permission before asking questions and explore what feels supportive.
C. Suggest they find a coach from their own culture.
D. Use only statements instead of questions throughout sessions.

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 2.2 (Cultivates Trust and Safety), 2.1 (Establishes Agreements)  
**Rationale:** Competencies are universal but application is culturally responsive. Partner with client to find what works for them.

---

### Question 8
**Scenario:** During a session, you feel yourself getting frustrated with a client who keeps making excuses for not completing actions.

**What should you do?**

A. Express your frustration directly to help them see the pattern.
B. Ignore your feelings and continue with the planned coaching questions.
C. Acknowledge your emotion internally, recenter, and explore what the "excuses" reveal about underlying challenges.
D. End the session early since you're not in the right headspace.

**Answer: C**
**Competencies:** 1.2 (Embodies Coaching Mindset), 2.3 (Maintains Presence)  
**Rationale:** Self-management is part of coaching presence. "Excuses" may reveal important information about obstacles or misaligned goals.

---

### Question 9
**Scenario:** An AI-assisted coaching session is underway. The client becomes emotional discussing a recent loss. The AI suggests a goal-setting exercise to "move them forward."

**What should the human coach do?**

A. Follow the AI suggestion since forward movement is the goal.
B. Allow space for the emotion, validate the client's experience, and ask what they need in this moment.
C. Pause to ask the client if they want to continue with AI suggestions.
D. Switch to therapy mode to help them process the grief.

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 2.3 (Maintains Presence), 2.2 (Cultivates Trust)  
**Rationale:** AI cannot read emotional nuance or presence. Human must override AI when emotion needs space. Coaching holds space; therapy treats grief. Know the difference.

## Section 3: Communicating Effectively

### Question 10
**Scenario:** A client says "I just don't think I can do this" with slumped shoulders and downcast eyes, but their words are vague about what "this" refers to.

**What is the most effective coach response?**

A. "Yes you can! I believe in you!" (encouragement)
B. "When you say 'this,' what specifically are you referring to?" (clarification)
C. "I notice your shoulders dropped and your voice became quiet. What's happening for you right now?" (full presence)
D. "What would it mean if you actually could do it?" (reframing)

**Answer: C**
**Competency:** 3.1 (Listens Actively) — verbal AND non-verbal communication  
**Rationale:** Deep listening includes body language and energy shifts. Addressing what is observed creates awareness about what's really happening.

---

### Question 11
**Scenario:** A client talks for 15 minutes about a work situation without pausing. They seem to be processing important information.

**What is most appropriate?**

A. Interrupt with a powerful question to move the coaching forward.
B. Let them continue until they naturally pause.
C. Wait 2 minutes then gently redirect.
D. Summarize what you've heard to check understanding.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 3.1 (Listens Actively), 2.3 (Maintains Presence)  
**Rationale:** Processing is part of coaching. Unless off-topic, allow space. Trust the client's process and the coaching relationship.

---

### Question 12
**Scenario:** An AI tool generates the following "powerful question" based on session data: "Why do you keep sabotaging yourself?"

**What is the issue with this AI-generated question?**

A. Nothing — it's direct and addresses the pattern.
B. It's judgmental and assumes negative intent.
C. It's too short to be a powerful question.
D. AI shouldn't generate questions at all.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 3.2 (Evokes Awareness), AI Ethics  
**Rationale:** "Sabotage" is judgmental framing. Better: "What pattern are you noticing in how you approach these situations?" AI can suggest; human must curate.

---

### Question 13
**Scenario:** You share an intuition: "I'm sensing there's something deeper here about feeling valued." The client says "No, that's not it at all."

**What do you do?**

A. Defend your intuition since you felt it strongly.
B. Apologize for being wrong and move on.
C. Thank them for the correction and invite them to share what IS true.
D. Ask them to explain why your intuition was wrong.

**Answer: C**
**Competency:** 3.3 (Reflects and Interprets)  
**Rationale:** Client is always the expert on their own experience. Rejection of an observation is information, not failure. Stay curious.

---

### Question 14
**Scenario:** In an AI-assisted session, the client says "I think I'm ready to quit my job." The AI flags this as a "goal" and suggests action steps.

**What should the human coach do first?**

A. Support the goal since client seems clear.
B. Explore what "ready" means and what has led to this moment.
C. Ask the AI for more context about the client's history.
D. Suggest they sleep on it before making such a big decision.

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 3.2 (Evokes Awareness), 3.1 (Listens Actively)  
**Rationale:** "I think I'm ready" invites exploration, not action planning. Understand the full picture before supporting goals.

## Section 4: Cultivating Learning and Growth

### Question 15
**Scenario:** A client completes a major goal they set in coaching. They say "I'm done now, I don't need coaching anymore."

**What is the most coach-like response?**

A. Agree immediately and schedule a termination session.
B. Celebrate their success, ask what they learned, and explore if there are new areas of growth.
C. Suggest they continue to maintain momentum.
D. Ask them to commit to 3 more sessions to consolidate learning.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 4.1 (Facilitates Client Growth)  
**Rationale:** Celebrate, learn, and offer without attachment. Client decides. Coaching is client-directed; don't be attached to continuation.

---

### Question 16
**Scenario:** A client consistently fails to complete action items between sessions. When asked about it, they say "I've just been too busy."

**What is the most effective approach?**

A. Hold them accountable by pointing out the pattern.
B. Explore what "busy" means and whether the actions are truly aligned with their values.
C. Suggest smaller action items that are easier to complete.
D. Accept that they're busy and don't bring up actions again.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 4.1 (Facilitates Client Growth), 4.2 (Partners in Goal-Setting)  
**Rationale:** "Too busy" often masks misalignment. Explore rather than blame or accept. Let client discover what the pattern reveals.

---

### Question 17
**Scenario:** An AI coaching tool tracks that a client has achieved 80% of their goals for 3 months straight.

**What should the human coach do with this data?**

A. Congratulate them on the metric and set more ambitious goals.
B. Celebrate progress AND ask how the client feels about the achievement.
C. Let the AI send an automated congratulations message.
D. Ignore the data since metrics aren't important in coaching.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 4.1 (Facilitates Client Growth)  
**Rationale:** Data supports awareness but human connection celebrates. Ask how they FEEL, not just what they achieved. Coaching is about the person, not just performance.

---

### Question 18
**Scenario:** A client sets a goal to "be more confident" but struggles to articulate what that looks like in observable terms.

**What is the best coaching approach?**

A. Accept that confidence is subjective and can't be measured.
B. Explore what "confidence" means to them and what others would notice.
C. Assign homework to research confidence-building techniques.
D. Suggest they focus on a different, more concrete goal.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 4.2 (Partners in Goal-Setting)  
**Rationale:** Partner to make the abstract concrete. "What would others notice?" makes the goal observable and actionable without losing the essence.

## Section 5: AI Coaching Ethics Scenarios

### Question 19
**Scenario:** Your AI coaching platform has a data breach. Client session notes are potentially exposed.

**What are your obligations under ICF ethics?**

A. Only tell clients if they ask.
B. Notify affected clients immediately, explain what was exposed, and steps you're taking.
C. Fix the breach and move on since it wasn't your direct fault.
D. Refer clients to the AI platform's support team for questions.

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice), Confidentiality  
**Rationale:** You are responsible for your practice's data security. Transparency, even when uncomfortable, maintains trust.

---

### Question 20
**Scenario:** A client asks "How much of our work is done by AI versus you?" They seem concerned.

**What is the most ethical response?**

A. Reassure them that AI is just a tool and you're always in control.
B. Give exact percentages of AI vs. human involvement.
C. Explain specifically what AI does, what you do, and invite questions about their concern.
D. Ask why they want to know and deflect the question.

**Answer: C**
**Competencies:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice), 2.1 (Establishes Agreements)  
**Rationale:** Specific transparency builds trust. Address the concern behind the question while being clear about roles.

## Section 6: Scope & Referral

### Question 21
**Scenario:** A client discloses they were recently diagnosed with bipolar disorder and are working with a psychiatrist. They want coaching for career goals.

**Can you coach this client?**

A. No — mental health diagnosis means coaching is inappropriate.
B. Yes — if they're stable, in treatment, and coaching goals are separate from treatment.
C. Only if their psychiatrist approves in writing.
D. Only if you have training in bipolar disorder.

**Answer: B**
**Competency:** 1.1 (Demonstrates Ethical Practice) — Scope  
**Rationale:** Coaching can coexist with treatment if client is stable, goals are coaching-appropriate, and boundaries are clear. Referral capacity is key.

---

### Question 22
**Scenario:** A client says "I want to leave my marriage but I'm afraid of the financial impact." They ask "Should I get a divorce?"

**What is the coaching response?**

A. Share your opinion if you've been through divorce.
B. Explore their values, fears, and what they truly want without advising.
C. Refer to a marriage counselor and end coaching.
D. Suggest they consult a lawyer before deciding.

**Answer: B**
**Competencies:** 1.2 (Embodies Coaching Mindset), 3.2 (Evokes Awareness)  
**Rationale:** Major life decisions are coaching territory — but non-directively. Support clarity, not advice. Refer to couples therapist if they want to work on the marriage.
