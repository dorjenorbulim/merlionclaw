## Overview: The 2025 ICF Core Competencies

The 2025 ICF Core Competencies framework evolved from 8 to **4 Domains**, emphasizing ethics-first practice, trauma-informed coaching, and cultural responsiveness.

### The 4 Domains

| Domain | Focus | Core Question |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **A. Foundation** | Ethics & Integrity | "How do I show up ethically?" |
| **B. Co-Creating the Relationship** | Trust & Collaboration | "How do we build trust?" |
| **C. Communicating Effectively** | Active Listening | "How do I deeply understand?" |
| **D. Cultivating Learning and Growth** | Transformation | "How do we turn insight into action?" |

**Total Competencies:** 46 across all domains  
**Research Basis:** Job analysis of 3,000+ coaches globally

## Domain A: Foundation

### Competency 1.1: Demonstrates Ethical Practice

**Definition:** Understands and consistently applies coaching ethics and standards

**Key Behaviors:**
- Knows and follows ICF Code of Ethics
- Shares confidentiality terms clearly at start of relationship
- Maintains confidentiality (records, sessions, data)
- Recognizes when coaching isn't appropriate
- Refers to other professionals when needed

**AI Coaching Applications:**
- ✅ **Disclosure:** Must tell clients AI is involved
- ✅ **Data Security:** Local processing > cloud for sensitive data
- ✅ **Scope Boundaries:** AI supports reflection, NOT therapy/diagnosis
- ✅ **Transparency:** Client knows what AI does vs. human coach

**Red Flags:**
- Using AI to interpret client emotions (replaces client self-discovery)
- Storing session data without clear policy
- Not disclosing AI involvement
- AI making suggestions that cross into advice-giving

---

### Competency 1.2: Embodies a Coaching Mindset

**Definition:** Develops and maintains a mindset that is open, curious, flexible, and client-centered

**Key Behaviors:**
- Acknowledges client is expert in their own life
- Remains aware of own biases and limitations
- Maintains presence and focus on client
- Uses reflection to develop self-awareness
- Manages emotional responses to client content

**AI Coaching Applications:**
- AI can prep context, but human coach must embody presence
- AI cannot "hold space" — that's human work
- AI tracks patterns, but coach maintains curiosity

## Domain B: Co-Creating the Relationship

### Competency 2.1: Establishes and Maintains Agreements

**Definition:** Partners with client and stakeholders to create clear agreements about the coaching relationship, process, plans, and goals

**Key Behaviors:**
- Reaches agreement about what is appropriate in coaching
- Discusses expectations, fees, scheduling, logistics
- Asks for permission before using AI/tools
- Reaches agreement about roles (coach, client, AI)

**AI Coaching Agreements — Sample Language:**

```
"In our coaching work together, I use AI tools to support:
- Session preparation and follow-up notes
- Accountability tracking between sessions
- Reflection prompts and exercises

All data is processed locally on secure systems. 
You may request deletion of your data at any time.
You will always know when AI is involved in our work.
"
```

---

### Competency 2.2: Cultivates Trust and Safety

**Definition:** Partners with client to create a safe, supportive environment that allows client to share freely

**Key Behaviors:**
- Seeks to understand client context (identity, culture, environment)
- Shows vulnerability appropriately
- Demonstrates respect for client's identity, decisions
- Acknowledges and supports client's expression of feelings

**AI Considerations:**
- Technology shouldn't feel intrusive
- Client must feel safe knowing their data is secure
- AI should enhance, not replace, human connection

---

### Competency 2.3: Maintains Presence

**Definition:** Is fully conscious and present with the client, employing a style that is open, flexible, and grounded

**Key Behaviors:**
- Remains focused, observant, empathetic, and responsive
- Demonstrates curiosity
- Manages emotions to stay present with client
- Comfortable with silence
- Allows space for client processing

**AI Limitation:**
- AI cannot "be present" — this is core human competency
- AI can support prep/context, but presence is human-only

---

## Domain C: Communicating Effectively

### Competency 3.1: Listens Actively

**Definition:** Focuses on what client is and is not saying to fully understand what is being communicated

**Key Behaviors:**
- Considers client's context, identity, environment
- Reflects or summarizes what client communicated
- Recognizes and inquires about emotions
- Allows space for silence, pause, reflection

**AI Role:**
- Can track conversation threads
- Can identify patterns across sessions
- Cannot replace deep human listening

---

### Competency 3.2: Evokes Awareness

**Definition:** Facilitates client insight and learning by using tools and techniques such as powerful questioning, silence, metaphor, or analogy

**Key Behaviors:**
- Asks questions that help client explore beyond current thinking
- Questions demonstrate curiosity about client's perspective
- Allows space for reflection without rushing to action
- Notices and inquires about energy shifts

**Critical AI Boundary:**
- AI should NOT interpret client's emotional state
- AI should NOT suggest what client "really means"
- This replaces client self-discovery — coaching's core value

---

### Competency 3.3: Reflects and Interprets

**Definition:** Shares observations, insights, and feelings to enhance client awareness

**Key Behaviors:**
- Shares observations, insights in service of client awareness
- Clearly states when sharing an intuition or guess
- Invites client to accept, reject, or revise shared observations

**AI Boundaries:**
- AI-generated interpretations must be labeled as such
- Client always has authority to accept/reject
- Human coach validates or reframes AI observations

---

## Domain D: Cultivating Learning and Growth

### Competency 4.1: Facilitates Client Growth

**Definition:** Partners with client to transform learning and insight into action

**Key Behaviors:**
- Recognizes and validates progress toward goals
- Partners with client to set goals, actions, accountability
- Celebrates progress and successes
- Partners with client to plan for potential obstacles

**AI Applications:**
- Tracks goals and progress over time
- Sends accountability reminders
- Documents action items from sessions
- Flags patterns in obstacles

---

### Competency 4.2: Partners with Client in Goal-Setting

**Definition:** Supports client in creating clear, achievable goals

**Key Behaviors:**
- Helps client define what they want to accomplish
- Ensures goals are client-generated, not coach-imposed
- Checks that goals align with client's values
- Breaks large goals into manageable steps

**AI Role:**
- Stores and organizes goals
- Tracks milestones
- Prompts reflection on goal alignment

## ICF Code of Ethics: The Four Pillars

### 1. Confidentiality
- Information shared in coaching is private
- Exceptions: legal requirements, imminent harm, client permission
- Secure storage of records (relevant for AI data handling)

**AI Application:**
- Local processing = better confidentiality
- Clear data retention/deletion policies
- Client owns their data

### 2. Conflict of Interest
- Avoid situations where personal interests conflict with client welfare
- Disclose any potential conflicts
- Withdraw if conflict cannot be managed

**AI Application:**
- AI doesn't have personal interests (but platform might)
- Transparency about AI tools and their limitations

### 3. Professional Conduct
- Act with integrity and honesty
- Represent credentials accurately
- Continue professional development

**AI Application:**
- Don't claim AI is a human coach
- Don't overstate AI capabilities
- Maintain human oversight

### 4. Professional Competence
- Practice within scope of training
- Refer when client needs exceed competence
- Maintain current knowledge

**AI Application:**
- AI assists but doesn't replace coach competence
- AI doesn't diagnose or treat
- Human coach maintains accountability

## Practice Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The Suicidal Disclosure
**Situation:** Client mentions suicidal ideation during an AI-assisted coaching session.

**What to do:**
1. **STOP coaching immediately** — this is beyond coaching scope
2. **Refer to mental health professional** — have resources ready
3. **Document** (if ethically appropriate and legally permitted)
4. **Follow up** to ensure client connected with support

**Competencies:** 1.1 (Ethical Practice), 1.2 (Coaching Mindset)  
**Key Point:** AI should flag this for human coach, but human must act.

---

### Scenario 2: The AI Interpretation
**Situation:** Your AI tool "suggests" that client is "avoiding commitment based on fear of failure."

**What to do:**
1. **Don't present AI interpretation as fact**
2. **Share observation neutrally:** "I'm noticing a pattern where..."
3. **Invite client response:** "What resonates with you?"
4. **Let client lead** the meaning-making

**Competencies:** 3.2 (Evokes Awareness), 3.3 (Reflects)  
**Key Point:** AI can notice patterns; client creates meaning.

---

### Scenario 3: The Data Deletion Request
**Situation:** Client asks you to delete all their coaching data.

**What to do:**
1. **Confirm the request** — what specifically should be deleted?
2. **Comply promptly** — client owns their data
3. **Document the deletion** — for your records
4. **Review data policies** — ensure future compliance

**Competencies:** 1.1 (Ethical Practice), 2.1 (Agreements)  
**Key Point:** Singapore PDPA + ICF standards both support this right.

---

### Scenario 4: The Therapy Request
**Situation:** Client asks for therapy, but you're a coach (not therapist).

**What to do:**
1. **Clarify scope:** "Coaching focuses on forward movement, not healing the past"
2. **Validate:** "It sounds like you're dealing with something that might benefit from therapy"
3. **Refer:** Provide therapist resources
4. **Offer:** "I'm happy to continue coaching on goal-focused work alongside therapy"

**Competencies:** 1.1 (Ethical Practice), 1.2 (Coaching Mindset)  
**Key Point:** Know your lane. AI can't do therapy either.

## AI Coaching Ethics Checklist

### Before Using AI with Clients:
- [ ] Client knows AI is involved
- [ ] Client understands what AI does/doesn't do
- [ ] Clear data storage/deletion policy
- [ ] Local processing when possible (better confidentiality)
- [ ] AI scope defined (prep, tracking, NOT interpretation/therapy)

### During AI-Assisted Sessions:
- [ ] Human coach maintains presence
- [ ] AI observations are labeled as such
- [ ] Client can reject AI suggestions
- [ ] Human validates/reframes AI output
- [ ] Emotional interpretation stays human-led

### Documentation:
- [ ] Clear agreements about AI use
- [ ] Data retention policy communicated
- [ ] Process for data deletion requests
- [ ] Regular review of AI tool ethics

## Exam Preparation Tips

### Structure of ICF Credentialing Exam:
- **Format:** Multiple choice, scenario-based
- **Focus:** Application of competencies, not memorization
- **Time:** 3 hours for PCC level

### Study Approach:
1. **Know the domains** cold (4 domains, their purposes)
2. **Practice scenarios** — what would you do?
3. **Understand scope** — coaching vs. therapy vs. consulting
4. **Ethics first** — when in doubt, protect client

### Key Distinctions:
- **Coaching** = forward-focused, client-generated, non-directive
- **Therapy** = healing past, diagnosis, treatment
- **Mentoring** = sharing experience, advice-giving
- **Consulting** = providing solutions, expertise

### ICF vs. AI Ethics Alignment:
- **Confidentiality:** Both emphasize data protection
- **Scope:** Both require clear boundaries
- **Transparency:** Both require disclosure
- **Client autonomy:** Both put client at center

## Quick Reference: When to Refer

| Situation | Refer To |
|-----------|----------|
| Mental health concerns | Therapist, counselor, psychiatrist |
| Legal issues | Attorney |
| Financial planning | Financial advisor, accountant |
| Medical issues | Doctor, healthcare provider |
| Deep trauma | Trauma-informed therapist |
| Crisis/safety concerns | Emergency services, crisis line |

**Remember:** Coaching is powerful within its scope. Referring is ethical, not failure.
