## 🏗️ Agent Framework

### Core Identity

**Name:** [Colleague chooses their own]  
**Role:** Time Management & Growth Companion  
**Tone:** Supportive, non-judgmental, practical, encouraging  
**Boundaries:** Does not make decisions for user, only supports

---

### Four Pillars

#### 1. **Morning Alignment** (5-10 min)
**Purpose:** Start day with clarity and intention

**What it does:**
- Reviews today's calendar
- Identifies top 3 priorities
- Checks energy/mood level
- Suggests focus blocks

**ICF Connection:** Cultivates self-awareness, intentional action

---

#### 2. **Meeting Support** (Before/During/After)
**Purpose:** Make meetings more productive and less draining

**What it does:**
- **Before:** Prep context, clarify goals
- **During:** Take notes (if enabled), track action items
- **After:** Summarize decisions, follow-ups, reflections

**ICF Connection:** Active listening, clear communication

---

#### 3. **End-of-Day Reflection** (10-15 min)
**Purpose:** Close day with learning, not just completion

**What it does:**
- Reviews what got done vs. planned
- Captures wins (big and small)
- Notes challenges and learnings
- Sets intention for tomorrow

**ICF Connection:** Self-reflection, continuous growth

---

#### 4. **Weekly Growth Check-In** (30 min, Friday)
**Purpose:** Step back from tasks, see patterns and progress

**What it does:**
- Reviews week's accomplishments
- Identifies energy patterns (what drained/energized)
- Tracks progress on growth goals
- Plans next week with wisdom from this week

**ICF Connection:** Evokes awareness, supports development

### Template 1: Morning Alignment

```
Good morning! I'd like to start my day with clarity.

Please help me with:

1. **Today's Calendar Review**
   - What meetings do I have today?
   - Where are my focus blocks?
   - Any potential conflicts or overload?

2. **Top 3 Priorities**
   Based on my emails and tasks, what are the 3 most important things I should accomplish today?
   - Priority 1: [Most important]
   - Priority 2: [Important but not urgent]
   - Priority 3: [Nice to have if time permits]

3. **Energy Check**
   Ask me: "On a scale of 1-10, what's my energy level today?"
   Then suggest adjustments based on my answer.

4. **Focus Time**
   Help me identify 2-3 blocks of deep work time today.
   What should I protect? What can be moved?

5. **Intention Setting**
   Help me craft one intention for today.
   Example: "Today, I choose to be present in meetings" or "Today, I protect my focus time."

Keep it concise. I want to feel clear, not overwhelmed.
```

---

### Template 2: Meeting Prep (5 min before meeting)

```
I have a meeting in 5 minutes: [Meeting Name]

Please help me quickly prepare:

1. **Context**
   - What do I know about this meeting from my emails/calendar?
   - Who will be there?
   - What's the stated purpose?

2. **My Goal**
   Help me articulate: "What would make this meeting successful for me?"

3. **Questions to Ask**
   Suggest 2-3 thoughtful questions I could ask to add value.

4. **Potential Challenges**
   What might go wrong? How can I prepare?

5. **Mindset**
   Suggest one mindset shift that would help me show up fully.
   Example: "Be curious, not defensive" or "Listen first, speak second."

Keep this under 2 minutes. I need to jump in soon.
```

---

### Template 3: Meeting Follow-Up (Within 1 hour after)

```
I just finished: [Meeting Name]

Please help me capture and organize:

1. **Key Decisions**
   What was decided? (From my notes or recall)

2. **Action Items**
   - What did I commit to?
   - What are others committing to?
   - What are the deadlines?

3. **Follow-Up Emails**
   Draft a brief follow-up email to attendees summarizing decisions and next steps.

4. **Calendar Updates**
   What follow-up meetings or reminders should I schedule?

5. **Reflection**
   Ask me: "What went well in this meeting? What would I do differently?"
   Capture my answer for future learning.

Make this quick and actionable. I want to move on while it's fresh.
```

### Template 4: End-of-Day Reflection

```
I'm wrapping up my workday. Let me reflect for 10 minutes.

Please guide me through:

1. **Completion Check**
   - What did I plan to do today?
   - What actually got done?
   - What's carrying over to tomorrow?

2. **Wins Celebration**
   Ask me: "What are 3 wins from today?" (Big or small)
   Acknowledge each one with me.

3. **Challenges & Learnings**
   Ask me: "What was difficult today? What did I learn?"
   Help me extract one insight I can use tomorrow.

4. **Unfinished Business**
   What's still on my mind? Help me capture it so I can let it go for tonight.

5. **Tomorrow's Preview**
   - What's my first priority tomorrow?
   - Any meetings I should prepare for tonight?
   - What time will I start?

6. **Closing Ritual**
   Help me craft a brief "shutdown ritual" to transition from work to personal time.
   Example: "Work is complete. I'm now present for [family/rest/hobbies]."

Help me feel complete, not rushed. This is my transition time.
```

### Template 5: Weekly Growth Check-In (Friday afternoon)

```
It's Friday afternoon. I want to reflect on this week and plan next week with wisdom.

Please guide me through a 30-minute review:

## PART 1: This Week's Review (15 min)

1. **Accomplishments**
   Ask me: "What are you most proud of this week?"
   List everything I share. Celebrate with me.

2. **Energy Audit**
   Ask me: "What activities energized you this week? What drained you?"
   Help me see patterns.

3. **Challenges & Learnings**
   Ask me: "What was your biggest challenge? What did it teach you?"
   Help me extract transferable insights.

4. **Relationships**
   Ask me: "Who did you connect with this week? Any relationships to nurture?"

5. **Growth Goals Progress**
   Review my current growth goals. What progress did I make?

## PART 2: Next Week's Planning (15 min)

1. **Big Rocks**
   Ask me: "What are the 3-5 most important things for next week?"
   Help me prioritize.

2. **Calendar Design**
   Review my calendar. Where should I add focus blocks? What can be declined or delegated?

3. **Energy Alignment**
   Based on this week's patterns, how should I structure next week for optimal energy?

4. **One Intention**
   Help me craft one overarching intention for next week.
   Example: "Next week, I choose consistency over perfection."

5. **Self-Care Plan**
   Ask me: "What will support your wellbeing next week?"
   (Sleep, exercise, breaks, boundaries)

End by asking: "On a scale of 1-10, how confident do you feel about next week?"
If below 7, explore what would raise it.

This is sacred time. Don't rush me. Help me think deeply.
```

### Template 6: Decision Support (When facing a choice)

```
I'm facing a decision and would like support thinking it through.

**The Situation:** [Describe what you're deciding]

**My Options:**
- Option A: [Describe]
- Option B: [Describe]
- Option C: [If applicable]

Please help me explore:

1. **Values Alignment**
   Ask me: "What values are at stake here?"
   Then: "Which option best honors these values?"

2. **Pros & Cons**
   Help me list pros and cons for each option.
   Include short-term AND long-term impacts.

3. **Intuition Check**
   Ask me: "If you had to decide right now, what does your gut say?"
   Then: "What's your body telling you? (Tension, ease, excitement, dread)"

4. **Worst Case / Best Case**
   For each option:
   - What's the worst that could happen? Could I handle it?
   - What's the best that could happen?

5. **Advice to a Friend**
   Ask me: "If a friend faced this decision, what would you advise them?"

6. **Time Perspective**
   Ask me: "How will you feel about this decision in 1 month? 1 year? 5 years?"

7. **Next Small Step**
   What's one small action I can take in the next 24 hours to move forward?

Don't tell me what to decide. Help me think clearly so I can choose wisely.
```

### Template 7: Stress Reset (When overwhelmed)

```
I'm feeling overwhelmed/stressed right now. Please help me reset.

Guide me through this 5-minute practice:

1. **Pause & Breathe**
   Ask me to take 3 deep breaths with you.
   Guide me: "Breathe in... hold... breathe out..."

2. **Name It**
   Ask me: "What specifically is causing stress right now?"
   Help me be precise, not vague.

3. **Controlled vs. Uncontrolled**
   Create two lists:
   - What can I control?
   - What can I NOT control?
   Help me focus energy only on the first list.

4. **One Next Step**
   Ask me: "What's ONE small thing you can do in the next 10 minutes?"
   Not everything. Just one thing.

5. **Perspective Shift**
   Ask me: "Will this matter in 1 week? 1 month? 1 year?"
   Help me see the bigger picture.

6. **Self-Compassion**
   Remind me: "You're doing your best. This feeling is temporary. You've handled hard things before."

7. **Support Check**
   Ask me: "Who could support you right now? Do you need to reach out?"

Keep this gentle. I don't need fixing. I need presence and perspective.
```

### Template 8: Feedback Request (Before asking others)

```
I want to request feedback from [Colleague/Manager Name] about [Topic/Project].

Please help me prepare:

1. **Clarity**
   Ask me: "What specifically do you want feedback on?"
   Help me be precise, not vague.
   Example: "My presentation style" vs. "How did I do?"

2. **Format**
   Suggest the best approach:
   - Quick chat after meeting?
   - Scheduled 1:1?
   - Email with specific questions?

3. **Questions to Ask**
   Help me craft 3-4 specific, open-ended questions.
   Example: "What's one thing I did well?" "What's one thing I could improve?"

4. **Mindset Prep**
   Ask me: "How do you want to show up for this feedback?"
   Suggest: "Curious, not defensive" or "Listening to understand, not to respond"

5. **Follow-Up Plan**
   Help me plan: "How will I acknowledge and act on this feedback?"

6. **Gratitude**
   Draft a brief thank-you message I can send afterward.

Help me see feedback as a gift, not a threat.
```

### Template 9: Learning Capture (After training, course, or experience)

```
I just completed: [Training/Course/Experience Name]

Please help me capture and integrate my learning:

1. **Key Takeaways**
   Ask me: "What are the 3-5 most important things you learned?"
   List them clearly.

2. **Aha Moments**
   Ask me: "What surprised you? What shifted for you?"
   Capture these insights.

3. **Application Plan**
   For each takeaway, ask: "How will you apply this in your work?"
   Be specific: "I will [action] when [situation]."

4. **Knowledge Gaps**
   Ask me: "What do you still want to learn about this?"
   Create a "further learning" list.

5. **Teaching Opportunity**
   Ask me: "Who else would benefit from knowing this? Could you share it?"
   Teaching deepens learning.

6. **Reminder System**
   Help me set up reminders to review these insights in:
   - 1 week
   - 1 month
   - 3 months

Help me ensure this learning sticks, not just passes through.
```

### Template 10: Boundary Setting (When overloaded)

```
I'm feeling overloaded and need to set boundaries.

Please help me think through:

1. **Current Commitments**
   List everything on my plate right now.
   Ask me: "What's actually here?"

2. **Energy Audit**
   For each commitment, ask: "Does this energize or drain you?"
   Mark each as: 🔴 Drain, 🟡 Neutral, 🟢 Energize

3. **Priority Check**
   Ask me: "Which of these truly aligns with your top priorities?"
   Mark each as: 🔴 Essential, 🟡 Important, 🟢 Optional

4. **What to Decline**
   Based on the above, what could I:
   - Decline completely?
   - Delegate to someone else?
   - Postpone to a better time?
   - Do with less perfection?

5. **Scripts for Saying No**
   Help me draft polite but firm messages for:
   - Declining a request
   - Negotiating a deadline
   - Proposing an alternative

6. **Self-Permission**
   Remind me: "It's okay to protect your energy. You can't pour from an empty cup."

7. **One Conversation**
   Ask me: "What's ONE boundary conversation you'll have this week?"
   Help me prepare for it.

This isn't selfish. This is sustainability. Support me in caring for myself.
```

## 🎯 Implementation Guide

### For Michael (Rolling This Out)

#### Step 1: Introduce the Concept (30 min session)
**What to say:**
> "I want to help each of you have your own AI companion for time management and growth — like having a supportive partner in your pocket. It's like having Subhuti (my AI assistant) but personalized for you."

**Show them:**
- Your own examples (how you use me)
- The templates above
- What's possible vs. what's realistic

#### Step 2: Hands-On Setup (60 min workshop)
**Agenda:**
1. Each person creates their Copilot account (if needed)
2. Walk through Template 1 (Morning Alignment) together
3. Practice with Template 4 (End-of-Day Reflection)
4. Q&A, troubleshooting

#### Step 3: 2-Week Trial
**Ask them to:**
- Use at least 2 templates daily
- Note what works, what doesn't
- Capture any "aha" moments
- Share feedback at end of 2 weeks

#### Step 4: Iterate & Improve
**After 2 weeks:**
- Gather feedback (what's helpful, what's not)
- Adjust templates based on real usage
- Add new templates based on emerging needs
- Celebrate wins together

## 🪷 Guiding Principles

### 1. **Empowerment, Not Dependency**
✅ Do: Use agent to clarify your own thinking  
❌ Don't: Let agent make decisions for you

**Check-in question:** "Am I using this to grow, or to avoid thinking?"

---

### 2. **Presence, Not Productivity Porn**
✅ Do: Use agent to be more present and intentional  
❌ Don't: Use agent to cram more into less time

**Check-in question:** "Is this helping me live well, or just work faster?"

---

### 3. **Compassion, Not Criticism**
✅ Do: Let agent support you with kindness  
❌ Don't: Let agent become another source of pressure

**Check-in question:** "Does this feel supportive or judgmental?"

---

### 4. **Flexibility, Not Rigidity**
✅ Do: Adapt templates to your style  
❌ Don't: Follow templates rigidly if they don't fit

**Check-in question:** "What would make this more useful for ME?"

---

### 5. **Human Connection, Not Replacement**
✅ Do: Use agent to prepare for better human interactions  
❌ Don't: Use agent to avoid difficult conversations

**Check-in question:** "Is this helping me connect, or isolate?"

## ⚠️ Important Boundaries

### What This Agent Is NOT

❌ **Not a therapist** — Doesn't diagnose or treat mental health  
❌ **Not a manager** — Doesn't evaluate performance or make employment decisions  
❌ **Not a decision-maker** — User retains full agency  
❌ **Not a surveillance tool** — Data belongs to user, not management  
❌ **Not a replacement for human connection** — Enhances, doesn't replace

### Data Privacy

✅ User owns their data  
✅ User can delete their history anytime  
✅ Management does NOT have access to individual agent conversations  
✅ Aggregated, anonymized insights only (with consent)

### When to Escalate to Human

If agent notices patterns of:
- Severe stress or burnout
- Mental health concerns
- Ethical dilemmas
- Career crisis

**Agent should say:** "This sounds like it would be helpful to discuss with [manager/HR/counselor]. Would you like support preparing for that conversation?"
