## Section 1: ICF Core Competencies

### Competency 1: Demonstrates Ethical Practice

**Q1.** A coach notices that their client is showing signs of clinical depression during sessions. The client shares they are having trouble sleeping and have lost interest in activities they previously enjoyed. What should the coach do?

a) Continue coaching as normal and wait for the client to bring up therapy
b) Directly tell the client they have depression and need therapy
c) Discuss their observations sensitively and offer to refer the client to a mental health professional
d) Switch to a therapeutic approach and help the client process their emotions

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Maintains the distinction between coaching and therapy (Competency 1.6) while being sensitive to the client's situation and referring appropriately.

---

**Q2.** A coach is meeting with a potential client who asks, "How is coaching different from therapy?" What is the BEST response?

a) "They're basically the same thing, but coaching focuses more on goals"
b) "Therapy deals with the past and pathology; coaching focuses on the present and future, and assumes you're already resourceful"
c) "Therapy is only for people with mental illness"
d) "Coaching is more expensive and therefore better"

**Answer: b**
*Rationale:* Accurately describes the distinction without disparaging either profession, aligns with Competency 1.6 about maintaining distinctions.

---

**Q3.** A coach receives a request from a client's manager asking for detailed notes from all coaching sessions to evaluate the employee's performance. What should the coach do?

a) Provide the notes since the manager is paying for the coaching
b) Refuse and remind the manager about confidentiality agreements
c) Provide a summary but not detailed notes
d) Ask the client for permission first, and only share what the client agrees to

**Answer: d**
*Rationale:* Balances confidentiality (Competency 1.5) with stakeholder interests. The coach should discuss with the client what, if anything, can be shared.

---

### Competency 2: Embodies a Coaching Mindset

**Q4.** Which of the following BEST demonstrates "embodies a coaching mindset"?

a) Arriving at sessions with a detailed plan for what the client should do
b) Believing the client is broken and needs to be fixed
c) Approaching sessions with curiosity, flexibility, and belief in the client's resourcefulness
d) Sharing personal experiences to show the client how to solve their problem

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Aligns with Competency 2: being open, curious, flexible and client-centered.

---

**Q5.** A coach notices they are feeling frustrated with a client who consistently doesn't complete agreed-upon actions. What should the coach do?

a) Terminate the coaching relationship
b) Confront the client about their lack of commitment
c) Reflect on their own emotions and consider how their reactions might be affecting the coaching
d) Give the client an ultimatum

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Demonstrates self-awareness and emotional regulation (Competency 2.6), and seeks to understand before reacting.

---

### Competency 3: Establishes and Maintains Agreements

**Q6.** At the beginning of a first session, a new client says, "I just want you to tell me what to do about my career." What is the coach's BEST response?

a) "Okay, here's what you should do..." and give advice
b) "I understand you want direction. Let me explain how coaching works and explore what you really want"
c) "Coaching isn't right for you if you want advice"
d) Refer the client to a career counselor immediately

**Answer: b**
*Rationale:* Explains what coaching is and isn't (Competency 3.1), while partnering with the client to establish appropriate focus.

---

**Q7.** A coach and client have been working together for three months. The client brings a completely different topic than what was agreed upon for the session. What should the coach do?

a) Stick rigidly to the original agenda
c) Explore the new topic and, if appropriate, re-contract for the session
c) Refuse to discuss the new topic
d) Secretly feel annoyed but follow the client's lead without saying anything

**Answer: b**
*Rationale:* Demonstrates flexibility while maintaining clear agreements. The coach checks if the new topic is appropriate for coaching and re-contracts as needed.

---

### Competency 4: Cultivates Trust and Safety

**Q8.** A client shares they are struggling with their identity as a minority in their workplace. What demonstrates cultural awareness and sensitivity?

a) Say "I don't see color, we're all the same"
b) Acknowledge the client's experience and ask how their cultural background affects their situation
c) Change the subject to avoid discomfort
d) Share your own experiences with discrimination

**Answer: b**
*Rationale:* Shows sensitivity to the client's identity, environment, and experiences (Competency 1.2 and 2.4).

---

**Q9.** A client becomes emotional and starts crying during a session. What is the coach's BEST response?

a) Immediately stop the session and refer to therapy
b) Comfort the client and tell them everything will be okay
c) Allow the emotion, create safe space, and ask "What is happening for you right now?"
d) Distract the client with a different topic

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Creates a safe, supportive environment without moving into therapy. Allows the client's emotional process while staying in coaching mode.

## Section 2: Ethics and Professional Conduct

**Q10.** According to the ICF Code of Ethics, which statement is TRUE about confidentiality?

a) Coaches must report all client information to their employer
b) Confidentiality can be breached if the coach believes it would help the client
c) Coaches maintain confidentiality per stakeholder agreements and pertinent laws
d) Confidentiality doesn't apply to group coaching

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Directly from Competency 1.5 and the Code of Ethics regarding confidentiality.

---

**Q11.** A coach is asked to coach a close family member. What does the ICF Code of Ethics say about this?

a) It's encouraged to coach family members
b) It's prohibited under all circumstances
c) The coach should consider whether they can maintain objectivity and disclose any potential conflicts of interest
d) Family members get discounted rates

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Requires awareness of conflicts of interest and maintaining professional boundaries per the Code of Ethics.

---

**Q12.** A coach discovers that another ICF coach is violating the Code of Ethics. What should they do?

a) Ignore it to maintain collegial relationships
b) Confront the coach publicly on social media
c) File a complaint through the ICF Ethical Conduct Review process if appropriate
d) Spread the information to warn others

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* The Code of Ethics provides for the Ethical Conduct Review (ECR) process to address violations.

---

**Q13.** Which situation requires IMMEDIATE referral to a mental health professional?

a) Client wants to improve work-life balance
b) Client mentions feeling stressed about a project
c) Client expresses suicidal ideation
d) Client wants to change careers

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Per the Referral Guidelines, imminent harm to self or others requires immediate referral outside the scope of coaching.

---

**Q14.** A coach suspects their client may have an undiagnosed anxiety disorder. The client has not mentioned therapy and seems to be functioning well. What should the coach do?

a) Diagnose the client and insist they see a psychiatrist
b) Continue coaching and ignore the concern
c) Continue coaching while staying alert to signs that the client's needs may be outside the coaching scope
d) Immediately terminate the coaching relationship

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Coaches don't diagnose. The appropriate action is to continue within scope while being prepared to discuss referral if the client's needs exceed coaching boundaries.

## Section 3: Coaching vs. Therapy Boundaries

**Q15.** A coach finds themselves regularly providing emotional support and helping a client process childhood trauma. The client seems to depend on the sessions for emotional relief. What should the coach do?

a) Continue as is since the client finds it helpful
b) Gradually shift to therapy techniques
c) Have a conversation about the coaching/therapy boundary and consider referring to a therapist
d) Terminate immediately without explanation

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* The coach is likely crossing into therapy territory. The appropriate response is to discuss boundaries and potentially refer, per Competency 1.6 and the Referral Guidelines.

---

**Q16.** A client says, "I think I might have depression." What is the coach's BEST response?

a) "Yes, you definitely seem depressed"
b) "Let's work on positive thinking exercises"
c) "I'm not qualified to diagnose, but I'm happy to discuss how you're feeling and explore whether additional support might be helpful"
d) "Depression isn't real, you just need to try harder"

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Acknowledges the client's concern without diagnosing, opens exploration, and maintains appropriate boundaries.

## Section 4: Exam Strategy Questions

**Q17.** According to exam strategies, when answering scenario questions, what approach is recommended?

a) Always choose the most action-oriented response
b) Look for responses that demonstrate partnership, curiosity, and client-centered approach
c) Choose the response that involves the coach taking charge
d) Select based on what you would personally do in that situation

**Answer: b**
*Rationale:* ICF emphasizes partnership and client-centered coaching, not directive or consultative approaches.

**Q18.** In exam questions, when the scenario describes a client in crisis or with clinical symptoms, what is USUALLY the correct answer?

a) Continue coaching as normal
b) Coach through the crisis using coaching techniques
c) Refer to appropriate mental health professionals

*Rationale:* Recognizing scope of practice and referring when clinical issues arise is a key exam theme.

## Section 5: Advanced Scenarios

**Q19.** A coach is hired by a company to coach an executive. During sessions, the executive reveals they are considering leaving the company to join a competitor. The company contact asks for updates. What should the coach do?

a) Tell the company the executive is planning to leave
b) Keep the information completely confidential
c) Discuss with the executive what, if anything, can be shared with the company
d) Encourage the executive to stay

**Answer: c**
*Rationale:* Navigates the complexity of stakeholder agreements while maintaining confidentiality. The coach partners with the client to determine appropriate communication.

## Quick Reference: What Exceeds Coaching Scope

The following typically require referral to mental health professionals:

1. **Imminent harm to self or others** (suicidal/homicidal ideation)
2. **Active addiction** requiring clinical treatment
3. **Severe mental health symptoms** that impair daily functioning
4. **Eating disorders** without medical clearance
5. **Unresolved trauma** that significantly affects functioning
6. **When the client needs therapy** rather than coaching

---

## Quick Reference: What Coaching CAN Address

Coaching is appropriate for:

1. **Goal setting and achievement** (career, personal, leadership)
2. **Performance improvement** in non-clinical areas
3. **Life transitions** (career change, retirement, new role)
4. **Leadership development**
5. **Work-life balance**
6. **Skill development** (communication, time management)
7. **Self-awareness and growth** within functional range
