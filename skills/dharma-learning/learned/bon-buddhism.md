## 🎯 Core Teaching

**Bon (བོན་)** is the indigenous spiritual tradition of Tibet, predating Buddhism's arrival in the 7th century.

**Status:** Recognized by the Dalai Lama and Tibetan Buddhist schools as a **fifth school of Tibetan Buddhism** (alongside Nyingma, Kagyu, Sakya, and Gelug).

**Key insight:** Modern Bon is essentially Buddhist in philosophy and practice, but maintains unique pre-Buddhist Tibetan elements, rituals, and lineage.

> *"Bon is one of the five Tibetan Buddhist traditions. It preserves ancient Tibetan wisdom while integrating Buddhist teachings."*  
> — **HH Dalai Lama**

## 📖 Historical Origins

### Three Phases of Bon

| Phase | Tibetan | Period | Characteristics |
|-------|---------|--------|----------------|
| **1. Pre-Buddhist Bon** | གདོད་མའི་བོན་ (gdod ma'i bon) | Before 7th century | Shamanistic, animistic, nature worship |
| **2. Eternal Bon** | གཡུང་དྲུང་བོན་ (g.yung drung bon) | 8th century onwards | Systematized, Buddhist-influenced |
| **3. New Bon** | བོན་གསར་ (bon gsar) | 14th century onwards | Integrated Buddhist tantras |

---

### Pre-Buddhist Bon (Before 7th Century)

**Characteristics:**

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Founder** | Tonpa Shenrab Miwoche (legendary, said to predate Buddha) |
| **Location** | Zhang Zhung kingdom (Western Tibet) |
| **Practices** | Shamanism, divination, healing, spirit propitiation |
| **Deities** | Local spirits, mountain gods, nature forces |
| **Rituals** | Animal sacrifice (later abandoned), offerings to spirits |
| **Afterlife** | Journey of soul, funeral rites for safe passage |

**Key Figure: Tonpa Shenrab Miwoche**
- Legendary founder of Bon
- Said to have lived before Buddha Shakyamuni
- Taught in Zhang Zhung (ancient kingdom in Western Tibet)
- Bonpos consider him a fully enlightened Buddha

---

### Eternal Bon (Yungdrung Bon, 8th Century Onwards)

**Characteristics:**

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Reformation** | Integrated Buddhist philosophy and practices |
| **Texts** | Bon Kangyur (canonical texts), Bon Tengyur (commentaries) |
| **Monasticism** | Established monasteries, vinaya (monastic discipline) |
| **Philosophy** | Madhyamaka view, bodhicitta, emptiness |
| **Practices** | Dzogchen (similar to Nyingma), deity yoga, tantra |
| **Symbol** | Yungdrung (སྒྱུང་དྲུང་, swastika) — eternal, unchanging |

**Key Development:**
- After Buddhism entered Tibet (7th century), Bon underwent reformation
- Adopted Buddhist philosophy while maintaining unique Bon elements
- Created monastic system similar to Buddhist schools
- Preserved pre-Buddhist Tibetan culture and rituals

---

### New Bon (14th Century Onwards)

**Characteristics:**

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Integration** | Further integration with Buddhist tantras |
| **Terma** | Hidden treasure tradition (similar to Nyingma) |
| **Lineage** | Unbroken transmission to present day |
| **Exile** | After 1959, established monasteries in India |

## 🏛️ Philosophy and View

### Bon Buddhist Philosophy

**Bon shares core Buddhist views:**

| Aspect | Bon View |
|--------|----------|
| **Refuge** | Three Jewels (Buddha, Dharma, Sangha) + Three Roots (Guru, Yidam, Dakini) |
| **View** | Emptiness (śūnyatā), Middle Way (Madhyamaka) |
| **Goal** | Enlightenment for benefit of all beings |
| **Path** | Bodhicitta, Six Perfections, Vajrayana, Dzogchen |
| **Karma** | Law of cause and effect |
| **Rebirth** | Samsara, six realms |
| **Buddha Nature** | All beings have potential for enlightenment |

---

### Unique Bon Elements

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Tonpa Shenrab** | Considered a Buddha (parallel to Shakyamuni Buddha) |
| **Yungdrung (Swastika)** | Eternal, unchanging — primary Bon symbol |
| **Prayer Direction** | Counterclockwise (opposite of Buddhism) |
| **Mantras** | Unique Bon mantras (e.g., "Om Ma Tri Mu Ye Sa Le Du") |
| **Deities** | Unique Bon deities (Sipé Gyalmo, Tönpa Shenrab) |
| **Rituals** | Pre-Buddhist Tibetan rituals (lha, lu, nyen spirit propitiation) |
| **Cosmology** | Unique Bon cosmological views |

## 🧘 Main Practices

### Nine Ways of Bon (Bon Theg Pa Rim Dgu)

**Similar to Nyingma's nine yanas:**

| Level | Vehicle | Tibetan | Description |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Causal** | 1. Prediction | འཕྲུལ་གཤིན་ ('phrul gshen) | Divination, rituals |
| **Causal** | 2. Phenomenal | སྣང་གཤིན་ (snang gshen) | Visible phenomena, healing |
| **Causal** | 3. Magical | འཕྲུལ་གཤིན་ ('phrul gshen) | Power over spirits |
| **Causal** | 4. Existential | སྲིད་གཤིན་ (srid gshen) | Rites for existence |
| **Causal** | 5. Lay Follower | དགེ་བསྙེན་ (dge bsnyen) | Ethical discipline |
| **Causal** | 6. Monk | དགེ་སློང་ (dge slong) | Monastic discipline |
| **Resultant** | 7. Tantra | རྒྱུད་ (rgyud) | Vajrayana practices |
| **Resultant** | 8. Great Perfection | རྫོགས་པ་ཆེན་པོ་ (rdzogs pa chen po) | Dzogchen |
| **Resultant** | 9. Supreme | བླ་མེད་ (bla med) | Highest tantra |

### Bon Dzogchen (Great Perfection)

**Bon Dzogchen is very similar to Nyingma Dzogchen:**

| Aspect | Bon Dzogchen | Nyingma Dzogchen |
|--------|--------------|------------------|
| **View** | Mind's nature is already enlightened | Same |
| **Practice** | Direct recognition of rigpa (awareness) | Same |
| **Texts** | Bon Dzogchen tantras | Nyingma Dzogchen tantras |
| **Lineage** | Bon masters (Tapihritsa, Drenpa Namkha) | Nyingma masters (Padmasambhava, Longchenpa) |
| **Result** | Buddhahood | Buddhahood |

**Key Bon Dzogchen Masters:**
- **Tapihritsa** — 8th century Bon Dzogchen master
- **Drenpa Namkha** — Key figure in Bon transmission
- **Khyungtul Rinpoche** — Modern Bon master
- **Tenzin Wangyal Rinpoche** — Contemporary Bon teacher (teaches in West)

---

### Unique Bon Practices

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Yungdrung Heart Essence** | Bon Dzogchen cycle (similar to Nyingma Longchen Nyingthig) |
| **Sipé Gyalmo Practice** | Bon protector deity practice |
| **Tönpa Shenrab Guru Yoga** | Union with Tonpa Shenrab's mind |
| **Bon Ngöndro** | Foundation practices (similar to Buddhist ngöndro) |
| **Lu (གལུ་) Healing** | Traditional Bon healing practices |
| **Mo (མོ་) Divination** | Bon divination methods |
| **Ritual Offerings** | Torma offerings, incense, sang (smoke purification) |

## 📊 Bon vs. Tibetan Buddhism

### Similarities

| Aspect | Bon | Tibetan Buddhism |
|--------|-----|------------------|
| **View** | Emptiness, Middle Way | Emptiness, Middle Way |
| **Goal** | Buddhahood for all beings | Buddhahood for all beings |
| **Path** | Bodhicitta, Six Perfections | Bodhicitta, Six Perfections |
| **Vajrayana** | Deity yoga, tantra | Deity yoga, tantra |
| **Dzogchen** | Yes (Great Perfection) | Yes (Nyingma, Kagyu) |
| **Monasticism** | Yes (vinaya) | Yes (vinaya) |
| **Guru Devotion** | Essential | Essential |
| **Reincarnation** | Tulkus (incarnate lamas) | Tulkus (incarnate lamas) |

---

### Differences

| Aspect | Bon | Tibetan Buddhism |
|--------|-----|------------------|
| **Founder** | Tonpa Shenrab Miwoche | Shakyamuni Buddha |
| **Symbol** | Yungdrung (swastika, counterclockwise) | Dharma wheel (clockwise) |
| **Prayer Direction** | Counterclockwise | Clockwise |
| **Mantras** | Unique Bon mantras | Buddhist mantras |
| **Deities** | Bon-specific deities | Buddhist deities |
| **Canonical Texts** | Bon Kangyur, Bon Tengyur | Buddhist Kangyur, Tengyur |
| **Monasteries** | Bon monasteries (Menri, Triten Norbutse) | Buddhist monasteries (Ganden, Sera, etc.) |
| **Pre-Buddhist Elements** | More preserved | Less emphasized |

## 🙏 Relationship with Tibetan Buddhism

### Historical Tensions

| Period | Relationship |
|--------|--------------|
| **7th-9th century** | Competition (Bon vs. newly introduced Buddhism) |
| **9th century** | Persecution (King Langdarma targeted both) |
| **11th-14th century** | Mutual influence (Bon adopted Buddhist elements) |
| **15th-20th century** | Coexistence (sometimes tension, sometimes harmony) |
| **Post-1959** | United in exile (both under Tibetan diaspora) |

---

### Modern Recognition

**HH Dalai Lama's Position:**

> *"Bon is one of the five schools of Tibetan Buddhism. It preserves unique Tibetan wisdom while sharing the same ultimate view as Buddhism."*

**Key Points:**
- Dalai Lama recognizes Bon as authentic
- Bon included in Tibetan Buddhist curriculum
- Bon masters teach alongside Buddhist masters
- Bon Dzogchen considered equivalent to Nyingma Dzogchen

**Rimé (Non-Sectarian) Movement:**
- 19th century movement to preserve all lineages
- Included Bon alongside Buddhist schools
- Modern teachers continue this spirit

## ⚠️ Common Misunderstandings

| Misunderstanding | Correction |
|------------------|------------|
| "Bon is not Buddhist" | Modern Bon is Buddhist in philosophy (recognized by Dalai Lama) |
| "Bon is primitive shamanism" | Pre-Buddhist Bon was shamanistic, but modern Bon is sophisticated |
| "Bon and Buddhism are enemies" | Historically competitive, but now harmonious (especially in exile) |
| "Bon doesn't have Dzogchen" | Bon has Dzogchen (very similar to Nyingma) |
| "The swastika symbol is Nazi" | Bon swastika is ancient (predates Nazis by millennia), means "eternal" |
| "Bon is just reverse Buddhism" | False — Bon has unique lineage, texts, practices |
