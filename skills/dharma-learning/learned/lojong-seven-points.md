## 🎯 Core Teaching

**Lojong (བློ་སྦྱོང་, blo sbyong)** = **Mind Training**

**The Seven Point Mind Training** is a foundational Tibetan Buddhist practice for transforming self-cherishing into cherishing others — the essence of the Bodhisattva path.

**Origin:** Atisha Dipamkara (11th century) → transmitted through Kadampa lineage → integrated into all Tibetan Buddhist schools.

**Core insight:** All suffering comes from self-cherishing. All happiness comes from cherishing others.

**Famous slogan:**
> *"All Dharma agrees at one point: Transform adversity into the path."*

## 🔄 The Seven Points

### Point 1: Preliminary Practices (Foundation)

**What to do:**
- Recognize precious human rebirth (rare, has great potential)
- Be mindful of death (impermanence — practice NOW, not later)
- Take refuge in Three Jewels
- Observe law of cause and effect (karma)

**Practice:**
- Clean practice space (visualize purifying mind)
- Arrange body, speech, mind (statues, offerings)
- Sit in meditative posture (7-point posture)
- Nine-round breathing (neutralize mind)
- Generate motivation: "I practice for all sentient beings"

**Key Slogans:**
- *"First, train in the preliminaries."*
- *"Regard all dharmas as dreams."*
- *"Examine the nature of unborn awareness."*

**Why this matters:** Without foundation, advanced practices become ego-driven.

---

### Point 2: Train on Ultimate Bodhicitta (Wisdom)

**What to realize:**
- All phenomena lack inherent existence (emptiness)
- Subject (mind) and object (phenomena) are both empty
- Between sessions: view everything as illusion, dream

**Practice:**
- Meditate on selflessness (no truly existent "I")
- When grasping arises: "This is like a dream"
- Understand: emptiness ≠ nothingness (things function dependently)

**Key Slogans:**
- *"Between sessions, be like in a dream — or be like an illusion."*
- *"Meditate on whatever provokes resentment."*
- *"The essence of short-term practice is to apply the remedy."*

**Why this matters:** Without wisdom, compassion becomes sentimental attachment.

---

### Point 3: Train on Relative Bodhicitta (Compassion)

**The Main Practice** — Two parts:

#### A. Equalizing Self and Others

**Recognize:**
- All beings want happiness, none want suffering (equal)
- Friend/enemy/stranger distinctions are temporary (past lives)
- All beings have been your mother (countless rebirths)

**Practice:**
1. **Equanimity:** Level your mind toward all beings (no discrimination)
2. **Recognize all as mother:** Meditate gradually (this life's mother → father → relatives → all beings)
3. **Remember kindness:** How they cared for you (in this life and past)
4. **Repay kindness:** Help them attain happiness, remove suffering

**Key Slogans:**
- *"Begin the sequence of sending and taking with yourself."*
- *"When the world is filled with evil, transform all mishaps into the path of bodhi."*
- *"Drive all blames into one."* (The one = self-cherishing)

---

#### B. Exchanging Self for Others (Tonglen)

**Taking Visualization:**
- Inhale: Take on suffering of all beings (black smoke)
- Visualize it crushing your self-cherishing mind
- Start with yourself → parents → locality → world → all realms

**Giving Visualization:**
- Exhale: Give your merit, body, possessions (white light)
- Visualize beings receiving what they need
- Feel joy that they are happy

**Key Slogans:**
- *"Begin the sequence of sending and taking with yourself."*
- *"Three objects, three poisons, three seeds of virtue."*
- *"By sending and taking, begin the exchange with yourself."*

**Why this matters:** This directly undermines self-cherishing — the root of samsara.

---

### Point 4: Transform Adverse Conditions into the Path

**When suffering arises:**

1. **Accumulate merit** (generosity, ethics, patience)
2. **Purify negativities** (confession, prostrations)
3. **Offer to spirits/protectors** (request they ripen others' suffering on you)
4. **Invoke protectors** (ask for blessings to practice)

**Mindset:**
- Suffering = opportunity to practice Tonglen
- Criticism = chance to reduce pride
- Poverty = less attachment, more Dharma
- Spirits/hindrances = teachers destroying self-cherishing

**Key Slogans:**
- *"When the world is filled with evil, transform all mishaps into the path of bodhi."*
- *"Drive all blames into one."*
- *"Be grateful to everyone."*
- *"When praised — I am not happy. When criticized — I am happy. When suffering — I am comfortable. Spirits are my teachers."*

**Why this matters:** This transforms poison into medicine — the hallmark of a Bodhisattva.

---

### Point 5: Precepts for Practice (Guidelines)

**What to maintain:**

1. **Practice continually** (no breaks, even when traveling)
2. **Practice with mindfulness** (not mechanically)
3. **Antidote to self-cherishing** (not new form of ego)
4. **Accept harm** (do not retaliate)
5. **Not for material gain** (not for income, food, clothing)
6. **Practice with all beings** (not just friends)
7. **No attachment to outcomes** (not happy when praised, not sad when suffering)
8. **Abandon attachment** (visualize taking all beings' attachment onto yourself)

**Key Slogans:**
- *"Always maintain only a joyful mind."*
- *"If you can practice even at the risk of your life, always maintain only a joyful mind."*
- *"Do not let the god become a devil."* (Don't let practice become ego)
- *"Do not seek others' suffering as your own happiness."*

**Why this matters:** Without ethical guidelines, practice becomes corrupted.

---

### Point 6: Commitment to Practice (Vows)

**Three inseparables:**
- Body → not separated from deity form
- Speech → not separated from mantra recitation
- Mind → not separated from bodhicitta

**Five Powers at Death:**

| Power | Practice |
|-------|----------|
| **Familiarity** | Practice mind training repeatedly (now, so it's automatic at death) |
| **White seed** | Give away possessions before dying (no attachment) |
| **Determination** | Never be separated from bodhicitta |
| **Repudiation** | Confess broken vows |
| **Prayer** | Pray to take others' suffering, not go to Pure Land |

**Key Slogans:**
- *"Always maintain the three inseparable."*
- *"Train wholeheartedly."*
- *"Meditate on whatever provokes resentment."*
- *"At the time of death, have no regrets. The five powers are the real transference of consciousness."*

**Why this matters:** Death is the ultimate test — practice must be internalized, not just intellectual.

---

### Point 7: All Activities Become Training

**Transform daily life:**

| Activity | Transformation |
|----------|----------------|
| Eating | "May this feed organisms in my body so they generate bodhicitta" |
| Washing | "Water is wisdom washing away obscurations" |
| Opening door | "Opening door to liberation" |
| Closing door | "Shutting door to hell realms" |
| Lighting fire | "Burning self-cherishing with flame of compassion" |
| Sleeping | "May all beings awaken from ignorance" |
| Walking | "Leading all beings to liberation" |
| Sitting | "Sitting in the heart of enlightenment" |

**Key Slogans:**
- *"All conduct and activities become the training."*
- *"Two witnesses: the world and yourself."*
- *"Constantly train in the three difficulties."*
- *"Take on the three principal causes."*

**Why this matters:** Dharma is not just cushion practice — it's 24/7 transformation.

## 💡 Daily Life Application

### How to Practice Lojong (Modern Context)

#### Morning Practice (10-15 min)

1. **Clean space** (sweep, visualize purifying mind)
2. **Set motivation:** "Today, I practice for all sentient beings"
3. **Nine-round breathing** (neutralize mind)
4. **Read 1-3 slogans** (contemplate meaning)
5. **Set intention:** "I will transform all conditions into the path"

---

#### During the Day (As needed)

**When something difficult happens:**
- Pause
- Remember: "Transform adversity into the path"
- Ask: "How can this practice my self-cherishing?"
- Practice Tonglen if possible (even 3 breaths)

**When meeting someone difficult:**
- Remember: "Be grateful to everyone"
- See them as a teacher (destroying your self-cherishing)
- Wish them happiness (even if they harmed you)

**When praised:**
- Remember: "When praised — I am not happy"
- See praise as empty (dependently arisen)
- Dedicate merit to all beings

**When criticized:**
- Remember: "When criticized — I am happy"
- See criticism as reducing your pride
- Look for truth in the criticism

---

#### Evening Review (10-15 min)

1. **Review day:** When did self-cherishing arise?
2. **Tonglen practice:** Take suffering, give merit (5-10 min)
3. **Dedication:** "May merit benefit all beings"
4. **Sleep meditation:** "May all beings awaken from ignorance"

## 🧘 Lojong Meditation Practice

### Tonglen (Taking and Sending) — 15-20 min

1. **Settle the mind** (5 min breathing)

2. **Equalize self and others** (2 min):
   > "All beings want happiness, none want suffering — equal to me."

3. **Taking (Inhale)** (5 min):
   - Visualize suffering of all beings (black smoke)
   - Inhale it into your heart
   - Visualize it crushing your self-cherishing mind
   - Start with yourself → loved ones → strangers → enemies → all beings

4. **Sending (Exhale)** (5 min):
   - Visualize your happiness, merit, body (white light)
   - Exhale it to all beings
   - Visualize them receiving what they need
   - Feel joy that they are happy

5. **Dedication** (2 min):
   > "By the merit of this practice, may all beings be free from suffering and its causes, and attain happiness and its causes."

## ⚠️ Common Misunderstandings

| Misunderstanding | Correction |
|------------------|------------|
| "Lojong is just positive thinking" | No — it's profound transformation of self-cherishing |
| "I need to be happy all the time" | No — "joyful mind" means commitment to practice, not forced happiness |
| "Drive all blames into one = self-blame" | No — blame self-cherishing (not yourself as a person) |
| "Tonglen is dangerous" | No — start small, build gradually (with proper motivation) |
| "Lojong is only for monks" | No — laypeople can practice (especially relevant in modern life) |
| "I need to memorize all 59 slogans" | No — start with a few, internalize them |
| "Lojong negates self-care" | No — healthy self-care supports practice (not self-cherishing) |
